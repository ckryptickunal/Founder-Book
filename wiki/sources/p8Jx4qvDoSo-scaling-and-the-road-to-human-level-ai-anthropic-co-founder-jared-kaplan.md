# Scaling and the Road to Human-Level AI | Anthropic Co-founder Jared Kaplan

## Summary

Jared Kaplan, co-founder of Anthropic, discusses the role of scaling laws in the development of human-level AI. He explains that predictable improvements in AI performance, driven by scaling compute and data in both pre-training and reinforcement learning (RL), are the primary engines of progress. Kaplan emphasizes the shift toward longer-horizon tasks, the importance of memory and oversight in AI development, and the potential for AI to act as an autonomous agent in complex workflows.

## Key Ideas

- Scaling laws provide a predictable, empirical foundation for AI progress across compute, data, and model size.
- AI training consists of two main phases: pre-training (predicting the next token) and reinforcement learning (optimizing for helpfulness, honesty, and harmlessness).
- The 'time horizon' of tasks AI can perform is doubling roughly every 7 months.
- Future AI development requires better memory, nuanced oversight, and the ability to handle long-horizon, multi-step tasks.
- Human-AI collaboration is evolving from simple co-piloting to end-to-end workflow automation.
- Physics-based heuristics—specifically looking for macro trends and asking simple, precise questions—are highly effective for AI research.

## Topics

- [[topics/scaling-laws|Scaling Laws]]: The empirical observation that AI performance improves predictably with increased compute and data.
- [[topics/reinforcement-learning-rl|Reinforcement Learning (RL)]]: The process of optimizing models to be helpful, honest, and harmless using feedback.
- [[topics/long-horizon-tasks|Long-Horizon Tasks]]: The ability of AI to execute tasks that span days, weeks, or months rather than just seconds or minutes.
- [[topics/ai-interpretability|AI Interpretability]]: The effort to understand the internal mechanisms of neural networks, compared by Kaplan to neuroscience.
- [[topics/human-ai-collaboration|Human-AI Collaboration]]: The shift toward using AI as an agent that can be managed and sanity-checked by humans.

## Notable Claims

- The time horizon of tasks AI can perform is doubling every 7 months. Evidence: Referenced as an empirical finding by the organization 'Meter'.
- Scaling laws are as precise as trends found in physics or astronomy. Evidence: Observed consistency across many orders of magnitude in compute and data.
- AI progress is driven more by scaling than by sudden breakthroughs in researcher intelligence. Evidence: The systematic application of scaling laws has consistently yielded performance gains.
