# Do It Yourself. Here's how REAL movements start, then change the world.

## Summary

Garry Tan explores the mechanics of how movements—whether in technology, music, or social change—are born from a 'Do It Yourself' (DIY) ethos. By drawing parallels between punk rock and startup culture, he argues that individuals can influence the world by rejecting the need for permission, embracing authenticity over perfection, and building momentum through early followers.

## Key Ideas

- The world is malleable and can be shaped by individuals who realize that existing systems were created by people no smarter than themselves.
- Movements start as fringe, informal efforts to solve a specific problem, often characterized by 'extra-institutional conflict'.
- Leadership requires a first follower to validate the idea, and a second follower to create a crowd, which then triggers momentum.
- The DIY (Do It Yourself) aesthetic prioritizes authenticity and immediate action over waiting for perfect conditions or resources.
- Startups and social movements share a common trajectory: starting small, solving a personal problem, and scaling to impact millions.
