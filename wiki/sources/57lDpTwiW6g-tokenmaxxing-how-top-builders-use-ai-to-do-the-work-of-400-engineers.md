# Tokenmaxxing: How Top Builders Use AI To Do The Work Of 400 Engineers

## Summary

Garry Tan, CEO of Y Combinator, discusses his return to coding by leveraging AI agents to achieve massive productivity gains. He introduces the concept of 'tokenmaxxing'—the strategy of aggressively using AI tokens to automate complex tasks, research, and testing—to allow builders to act as 'time billionaires' by offloading work to machine intelligence.

## Key Ideas

- Tokenmaxxing: The practice of spending heavily on AI tokens to maximize output and completeness in software development and research.
- The 'Ferrari' analogy: Modern AI tools are powerful like Ferraris but require the user to be a 'mechanic' capable of fixing them when they break.
- Human-in-the-loop: AI should handle the heavy lifting, but humans must provide the agency, taste, and product direction.
- The shift from 'coding' to 'directing': Developers are evolving into operators who manage multiple AI agents to ship code.
- Personal AI as the next PC revolution: Individuals must maintain control over their own AI stacks to avoid corporate-controlled algorithms.
- Boiling the ocean: Using AI to perform exhaustive, completionist research that would be impossible for a human to do manually in a reasonable timeframe.
