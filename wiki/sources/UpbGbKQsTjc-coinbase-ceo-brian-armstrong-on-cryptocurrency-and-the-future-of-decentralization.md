# Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong on Cryptocurrency and the Future of Decentralization

## Summary

In this conversation, Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong and investor Garry Tan discuss the early days of Coinbase, the philosophy of building startups, and the future of decentralization. Armstrong reflects on his transition from working at Airbnb to founding Coinbase, the importance of persistence, and how he views the evolution of crypto from a speculative asset to a foundational internet platform.

## Key Ideas

- The importance of 'believing before others believe' and the role of early supporters in a founder's journey.
- The 'builder' mindset: focusing on solving real-world problems rather than just chasing trends.
- The evolution of crypto: from digital gold (Phase 1) to DeFi (Phase 2) to decentralized internet applications (Phase 3).
- The concept of DAOs as the 'Delaware C-Corp of the cloud' for future organizations.
- The necessity of balancing centralized corporate structures with decentralized protocols to ensure accountability.
- The '10% rule' at Coinbase: dedicating resources to internal innovation to prevent stagnation and encourage entrepreneurship.

## Notable Claims

- Most startups fail, and founders should expect to iterate through many ideas before finding success. Evidence: Armstrong notes he tried creating about 10 startups, with only Coinbase achieving significant success.
- The internet is transforming the human race into a 'hive mind' through increased information sharing. Evidence: The rise of instant communication platforms like WhatsApp and Signal allows for unprecedented global collaboration.
- DAOs will become the standard organizational structure for the future of the internet. Evidence: DAOs allow for stakeholder governance and are more resilient to tyranny because they can be forked.
