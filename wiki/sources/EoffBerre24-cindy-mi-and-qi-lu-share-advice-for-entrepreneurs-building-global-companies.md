# Cindy Mi and Qi Lu Share Advice for Entrepreneurs Building Global Companies

## Summary

Qi Lu, partner at Y Combinator, interviews Cindy Mi, founder and CEO of VIPKid, about her journey in building a global education company. They discuss the importance of lifelong learning, the challenges of finding product-market fit in a hyper-competitive market, the necessity of focusing on customer success, and the future of education technology.

## Key Ideas

- Education should be reimagined as a global, personalized classroom that connects cultures.
- Product-market fit is achieved by deeply understanding the pain points of both sides of a marketplace (students/parents and teachers).
- Customer success must be the core focus, even for founders, to drive long-term growth.
- Technology, specifically AI and data, should be used to empower teachers and personalize learning rather than just replacing human interaction.
- Building a global company requires a 'global from day one' mindset, including cross-cultural team management and respecting time zones.

## Notable Claims

- Chinese parents spend approximately 50% of their household income on education, compared to 2% in the United States. Evidence: Cited by Cindy Mi as a cultural driver for the demand for high-quality education services in China.
- A teacher can make or break a student's learning curiosity. Evidence: Based on Cindy Mi's personal experience as a student and educator.
