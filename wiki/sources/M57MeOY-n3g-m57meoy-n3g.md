## Summary

Arty founders Robin and Jacqueline discuss their real-time data streaming platform, which helps companies move production data (like Postgres) into data warehouses (like Snowflake) with low latency. The company recently raised a $12 million Series A after successfully scaling to process over 700 billion rows of data, overcoming significant technical challenges in distributed systems and change data capture.

## Key Ideas

- Real-time data streaming is essential for modern AI workloads and operationalizing data.
- Building reliable, production-ready data connectors is a massive engineering challenge that most companies should not attempt in-house.
- Founders should focus on solving high-stakes problems for large enterprises rather than building generic SaaS tools.
- Maintaining a lean team and keeping founders close to sales and customer feedback accelerates product iteration.
- The 'married co-founder' dynamic can enhance communication and feedback loops if managed with clear professional boundaries.

## Notable Claims

- Building a custom Postgres-to-Snowflake connector in-house typically takes a team of 5-7 engineers 6-12 months to reach production readiness. Evidence: The founders' experience at previous companies like Zenus and OpenDoor, where internal attempts failed or required massive resources.
- Arty processed over 700 billion rows of data in the last 12 months. Evidence: Stated by the founders during the discussion of current business scale.
- The founders reached $1 million ARR with only four people (two founders and two engineers). Evidence: Stated by the founders as a testament to their lean growth strategy.
