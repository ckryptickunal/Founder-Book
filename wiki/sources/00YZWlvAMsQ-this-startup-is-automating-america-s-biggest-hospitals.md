# This Startup Is Automating America's Biggest Hospitals

## Summary

Keshav, co-founder of Lumina, discusses his journey from a Rubik's Cube prodigy in India to leading an AI transformation company for US health systems. He explains how Lumina automates manual operational workflows, such as processing patient referrals via fax at institutions like the Cleveland Clinic, converting unstructured data into structured data to improve efficiency and patient care. Keshav shares his unconventional path, including 'hacking hackathons' to survive in Silicon Valley and methodically pivoting Lumina's focus to healthcare. He emphasizes the importance of personal storytelling and building deep relationships in enterprise sales, and advises aspiring entrepreneurs to embrace boundless ambition.

## Key Ideas

- Lumina's core mission is to be the AI transformation partner for US health systems, automating operational workflows.
- Manual processes, like fax-based patient referrals, create significant administrative waste and slow down patient care in large hospitals.
- Keshav's background as a Rubik's Cube world record holder and his academic struggles in India shaped his meritocratic and competitive drive.
- He survived in Silicon Valley by strategically winning prize money from hackathons, demonstrating an early knack for understanding decision-makers' motivations.
- Lumina's pivot to focus specifically on healthcare automation was a strategic decision based on market data and the need for specialization.
- Enterprise sales are fundamentally about selling to an individual 'champion' within an organization, requiring personal storytelling, mission alignment, and building deep relationships.
- Companies must continuously reinvent themselves and specialize as they grow to remain relevant and impactful.
- Entrepreneurs should embrace ambition and aggression far beyond what they initially perceive as possible, as the playing field is increasingly level.
