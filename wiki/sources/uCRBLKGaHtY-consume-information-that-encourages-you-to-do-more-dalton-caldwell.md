# Consume Information That Encourages You To Do More - Dalton Caldwell

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell of Y Combinator advises aspiring founders to be highly selective about the information they consume, warning that excessive focus on fundraising, venture capital, and 'thought leadership' can be discouraging and distracting. He emphasizes that founders should prioritize building products and solving problems over seeking external validation or building a personal brand in the early stages.

## Key Ideas

- Aspiring founders should consume content that encourages building and creating rather than content focused on fundraising and valuation.
- Excessive consumption of fundraising-related media can create a false belief that external permission or capital is required to start a business.
- The vast majority of successful YC-funded founders have little to no public presence or 'thought leadership' in their early years.
- Thought leadership is often a form of content marketing for established companies, not a prerequisite for startup success.
- A founder's mindset is heavily influenced by their primary information sources, similar to how one's personality is shaped by their social circle.
