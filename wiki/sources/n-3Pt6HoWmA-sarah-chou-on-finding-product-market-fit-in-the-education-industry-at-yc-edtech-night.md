## Summary

Sarah Chou, CEO and co-founder of Informed K12 (formerly Chalk Schools), discusses her journey from working in school administration to building a platform that digitizes paperwork for school districts. She emphasizes the importance of finding product-market fit through direct user feedback, the necessity of pivoting when initial ideas fail, and the value of building deep, trust-based relationships with school district stakeholders.

## Key Ideas

- The importance of 'getting on the ground' and immersing oneself in the school environment to understand real user pain points.
- The necessity of pivoting when user testing reveals that a product does not solve a critical problem or fit into the user's workflow.
- The value of doing things that don't scale, such as manual support and on-site visits, to build trust and gather insights.
- The challenge of identifying the correct buyer in a complex, systemic market like public education.
- The importance of maintaining a consistent, well-rehearsed narrative for both hiring and fundraising.

## Notable Claims

- Teachers are often too polite to give negative feedback directly to founders. Evidence: The founders recorded teachers using a prototype in a room without the founders present, capturing the candid comment: 'I would never use this.'
- School districts are highly contextual, meaning solutions must be tailored to local needs. Evidence: The speaker notes that every district claims to be unique and often has specific, localized processes that require deep understanding.
