# Lessons From The Founders Scaling Their Startup In A War Zone

## Summary

Stacy and Roman, founders of the design platform Assamic, share their experience of maintaining and scaling their startup while navigating the Russian invasion of Ukraine. The video highlights their transition from normal operations to emergency evacuation, the implementation of safety protocols for their team, and the decision to continue business operations to support the Ukrainian economy.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must prioritize the physical safety and evacuation of their team during a crisis.
- Having a pre-existing emergency plan allows founders to shift into 'robot mode' and execute necessary tasks during high-stress situations.
- Maintaining business operations during a war serves as a form of economic resistance.
- The 'startup mode' mentality—agility, resilience, and collective problem-solving—can be applied to national survival.
- The emotional toll of war, including trauma from news of atrocities, is a significant challenge for leadership.
