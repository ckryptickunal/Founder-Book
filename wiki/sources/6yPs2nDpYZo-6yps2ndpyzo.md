## Summary

Tavus founders discuss their journey from a YC-backed startup to an AI research lab building 'AI humans.' The company has pivoted from simple personalized video generation to developing foundational models for real-time, multimodal, and agentic AI that can see, hear, and respond with human-like emotional intelligence.

## Key Ideas

- Transitioning from command-line AI interfaces to natural, human-like interaction.
- The importance of 'perception' in AI—teaching machines to interpret facial expressions and emotional context.
- The necessity of low-latency (under 200ms) for natural human-machine interaction.
- Moving from an SDK-based model for developers to consumer-facing 'Tavus Pals'.
- The philosophy that AI should augment human capability rather than just replace existing, inefficient machine-based processes.
- The 'Crucible moment' of pivoting away from being an AI sales company to focusing on foundational research.

## Notable Claims

- High-quality human-machine interaction requires a response latency of less than 200 milliseconds. Evidence: The founders cite this as the threshold for natural, fluid conversation.
- AI can provide a 'good enough' experience for services like therapy where human access is otherwise non-existent. Evidence: The founders argue that for many, the alternative to AI therapy is no therapy at all.
