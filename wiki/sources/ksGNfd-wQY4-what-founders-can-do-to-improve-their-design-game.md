# What Founders Can Do To Improve Their Design Game

## Summary

Raphael Schaad, designer and founder of Cron (acquired by Notion), discusses the intersection of design, engineering, and entrepreneurship. He argues that design is a critical, often overlooked, competitive advantage for startups and encourages founders to develop 'design taste' and technical fluency to better execute their product visions.

## Key Ideas

- Design is not just aesthetics; it is how a product works, how it is built, and how it solves user problems.
- Founders should aim to be comfortable in the medium they are building in, meaning designers should learn to code and engineers should develop design literacy.
- Design taste can be acquired by surrounding oneself with high-quality, well-designed objects and by actively analyzing why certain designs work or fail.
- The 'AI age' shifts design focus from static UI elements (nouns) to dynamic interactions and agentic behaviors (verbs).
- Paper sketching remains a powerful, unconstrained tool for initial ideation before moving to high-fidelity digital prototyping.
- Everything is designed; founders should be intentional about their design choices rather than leaving them to chance.

## Topics

- [[topics/design-led-entrepreneurship|Design-Led Entrepreneurship]]: The philosophy that designers make excellent founders because they inherently focus on user desirability and problem-solving.
- [[topics/design-taste|Design Taste]]: The ability to distinguish between well-designed and poorly designed objects, which can be cultivated through observation and critical analysis.
- [[topics/prototyping|Prototyping]]: The process of moving from abstract ideas to tangible products, emphasizing the value of sketching and building in the actual medium.

## Notable Claims

- Designers are uniquely positioned to build successful companies because they focus on 'making something people want'. Evidence: The intersection of desirability (design), feasibility (technology), and viability (business) is the core of a successful product.
- Software design and software engineering should not be treated as separate disciplines. Evidence: Just as a sculptor must understand their material, a software designer must understand the code and constraints of the medium they are building in.
