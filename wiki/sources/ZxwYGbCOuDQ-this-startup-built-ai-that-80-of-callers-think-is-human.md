## Summary

Will Bodewes, founder and CEO of Phone AI, discusses his company's mission to optimize voice AI agents for businesses, moving beyond just answering the phone to continuously improve customer outcomes. Phone AI, which handles millions of calls monthly across hundreds of verticals, recently secured a $16 million Series A funding round led by Bessemer. Bodewes shares his unique background, the evolution of Phone AI from a small business product to an enterprise solution, and the technical differentiators like custom LLMs and fast inference hardware. He also touches on the increasing indistinguishability of AI from human agents, ethical considerations for disclosure, and offers advice for aspiring founders.

## Key Ideas

- Phone AI optimizes voice AI agents for businesses, focusing on continuous improvement of customer outcomes rather than just basic call answering.
- The company differentiates itself through proprietary LLMs, data-driven optimization tools, and extensive experience in deploying voice AI in production environments.
- Phone AI serves call centers, home services, and insurance, handling millions of calls monthly and aiming for 50 million plus.
- Technical innovation includes using smaller, specialized models on fast inference hardware (like Groq) to reduce latency and cost while maintaining quality.
- AI agents are becoming highly sophisticated, with 80% of Phone AI's customers' callers unaware they are speaking to an AI, projected to reach 100% soon.
- Ethical considerations around AI disclosure are discussed, with a preference for disclosure in outbound calling, but a belief that consumers will increasingly prefer AI for certain tasks.
- Will Bodewes' entrepreneurial journey includes an AI PhD, an audio startup, and ultra-endurance athletics, with the idea for Phone AI stemming from his father's business needs.
- Phone AI secured a $16 million Series A funding round led by Bessemer, highlighting investor confidence in their approach.
- Future improvements in voice AI focus on interruption handling, endpoint detection, transcription, and refining edge cases.
- Founding a company is a continuous 'fight' requiring persistence, hard work, and the ability to 'create your own luck'.
