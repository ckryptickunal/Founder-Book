# Delivering Founder Happiness

## Summary

Garry Tan reflects on the life and philosophy of the late Zappos CEO Tony Hsieh, using Hsieh's four-part framework for happiness—perceived control, perceived progress, connectedness, and meaning—to explain the psychological journey and challenges of being a startup founder.

## Key Ideas

- Founder happiness is derived from four pillars: control, progress, connectedness, and meaning.
- The startup journey involves two phases: 'catching lightning in a bottle' (early stage) and scaling/managing (later stage).
- Founding a company is a way to achieve maximum personal control and autonomy, though it comes with high responsibility.
- Progress for a founder is directly tied to delighting customers rather than satisfying corporate hierarchies.
- Connectedness is essential for building high-performing teams and maintaining organizational health.
- Meaning is the ultimate driver that sustains founders through the hardest challenges.
