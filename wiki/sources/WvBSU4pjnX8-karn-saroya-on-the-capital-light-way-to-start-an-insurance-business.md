# Karn Saroya on the Capital-Light Way to Start an Insurance Business

## Summary

Karn Saroya, CEO of Cover, discusses the company's evolution from a mobile-first insurance distributor to a sophisticated, technology-driven insurance platform. He highlights the importance of capital-light operations, leveraging computer vision for underwriting, and maintaining a focus on product-market fit and key performance indicators.

## Key Ideas

- Insurance can be approached as a capital-light business by focusing on distribution and technology before taking on underwriting risk.
- Computer vision can act as a sophisticated frontline underwriter, improving loss ratios and simplifying the claims process.
- Founders should prioritize 'making' over 'advising' and focus on building durable businesses rather than relying on marketing arbitrage.
- Product-market fit is validated by customer retention and high-value transactions, not just user acquisition.
- Maintaining a cohesive co-founder team with clear divisions of responsibility is critical for long-term success.
- For early-stage startups, focus exclusively on a single KPI and avoid distractions like conferences or premature hiring.

## Notable Claims

- Computer vision improves loss ratios in insurance. Evidence: By using visual proof of property existence and condition, adjusters have less room to dispute claims.
- A purely lead-generation insurance business is not durable. Evidence: Marketing arbitrage opportunities dissipate, and the business fails to build brand equity or customer loyalty.
- Buying a premium domain name can be a sound investment. Evidence: For companies with high lifetime value (LTV) customers, minor improvements in conversion rates pay for the domain cost quickly.
