# What We Look for in Founders

## Summary

Paul Graham outlines the five essential qualities Y Combinator looks for in startup founders: determination, flexibility, imagination, naughtiness, and friendship.

## Key Ideas

- Determination is more critical than raw intelligence once a certain threshold is met.
- Founders must balance determination with the flexibility to pivot their ideas.
- The best startup ideas often seem crazy or bad initially.
- Successful founders often possess a 'naughty' streak, meaning they enjoy hacking systems rather than following arbitrary rules.
- Strong, pre-existing friendships between co-founders are vital to surviving the intense pressure of a startup.

- Startups are likely to destroy weak founder relationships. Evidence: The intense pressure of a startup acts like a stress test on the interpersonal bond.
