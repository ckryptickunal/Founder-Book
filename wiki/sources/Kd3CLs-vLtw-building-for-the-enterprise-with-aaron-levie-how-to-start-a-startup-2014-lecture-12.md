# Building for the Enterprise with Aaron Levie (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 12)

## Summary

Aaron Levie, CEO and co-founder of Box, discusses the evolution of enterprise software, arguing that it is currently the most opportune time to build in this sector. He highlights the shift from IT-led to user-led adoption, the impact of cloud computing, and the necessity of maintaining consumer-grade design while solving complex business problems.

## Key Ideas

- Enterprise software is undergoing a massive transformation due to cloud computing, mobile devices, and the shift to user-led adoption.
- Startups should focus on 'wedges'—small, specific problems that can be solved elegantly before expanding into broader enterprise architecture.
- Consumer DNA (great UX, ease of use) is a competitive advantage in the enterprise market.
- The best way to enter a market is to identify technology disruptions that make previously impossible or expensive tasks feasible.
- While the product should sell itself, a consultative sales team is still necessary to help large organizations navigate implementation and complex ecosystems.
- Avoid building custom solutions for every client; instead, build modular platforms with open APIs.

- [[topics/market-disruption|Market Disruption]]: How changing technology factors (storage costs, mobile, internet speed) create opportunities for new startups.

- [[topics/sales-strategy|Sales Strategy]]: Balancing self-service product adoption with consultative sales for large-scale enterprise deployment.

## Notable Claims

- Enterprise software is a more attractive market than consumer software due to the massive scale of IT spending. Evidence: Levie cites $3.7 trillion in annual enterprise IT spending versus $170 billion in consumer app/ad spending.
- The best time to start a company is when the cost of underlying technology drops significantly. Evidence: Historical trends in storage costs and browser capabilities enabled Box to succeed where previous file-sharing attempts failed.
- User-led adoption is the most significant shift in modern enterprise software. Evidence: Mobile devices allow individual employees to bring their own tools into the workplace, bypassing traditional top-down IT procurement.

## Quotes

> My job is to try and convince you that everybody else who speaks throughout this sort of whole class is wrong and that you actually want to build an Enterprise software company.
> Any Market that has a significant change in the underlying either raw materials or enabling factors is an environment that's about to change in a very significant way.
> Listen to your customers but don't always build exactly what they're telling you.
