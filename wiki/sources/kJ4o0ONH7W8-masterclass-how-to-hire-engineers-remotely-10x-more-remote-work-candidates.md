# Masterclass: How to hire engineers remotely (10X more remote work candidates)

## Summary

Garry Tan and Ammon Bartram discuss best practices for conducting remote software engineering interviews, emphasizing the shift from credential-based hiring to skills-based assessment. They highlight the benefits of remote hiring for expanding the talent pool and introduce Triplebyte Screen as a tool to normalize and objectify the candidate evaluation process.

## Key Ideas

- Remote interviewing expands the potential talent pool by 10x to 100x.
- Inter-rater reliability in traditional interviews is low, similar to movie reviews, due to personal preference and noise.
- Brain teasers are poor predictors of skill and introduce unnecessary noise; continuous, process-oriented tasks are better.
- Remote interviews provide a more accurate facsimile of the actual job, which involves digital communication and remote collaboration.
- Credentialism (focusing on schools or past employers) often excludes qualified, diverse candidates.
- The current market offers a unique opportunity for companies to hire high-caliber talent that is more accessible than in previous years.

## Notable Claims

- Brain teasers are ineffective for assessing engineering skill. Evidence: They rely on 'hard' knowledge or specific insights that candidates may have encountered before, introducing noise rather than measuring problem-solving ability.
- Remote interviews are a better test of job readiness than in-person interviews. Evidence: Since the job itself is remote, the interview process acts as a direct simulation of the candidate's ability to communicate and work via digital tools.

## Quotes

> The goal of an interview is to judge: does this person have the skills to join my company and be productive?
> The core challenge of interviewing is trying to find strategies to minimize that noise and just more accurately get a repeatable signal.
> Credentialism is actually holding back hundreds of thousands if not millions of people from being welcomed with open arms into technology.
