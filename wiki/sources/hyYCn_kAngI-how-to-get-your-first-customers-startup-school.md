## Summary

Gustav from Y Combinator explains the essential process of acquiring initial startup customers by 'doing things that don't scale.' He emphasizes that founders must personally handle sales to deeply understand customer problems, iterate on the product, and build a repeatable sales funnel before hiring a sales team.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must personally perform sales to learn about customer pain points and validate the product.
- Early-stage startups should focus on 'doing things that don't scale' rather than relying on automated marketing or SEO.
- Sales is a numbers game; founders must track conversion rates at every stage of the funnel to identify bottlenecks.
- Startups are the easiest customers to sell to due to shorter decision-making cycles.
- Avoid free trials in B2B; use money-back guarantees or opt-out clauses to ensure customers are serious.
- Outreach should be personalized, plain-text, and brief, focusing on the specific problem the customer faces.

## Notable Claims

- Founders should not hire a sales team until they have mastered sales themselves. Evidence: Only by doing it themselves can founders know what 'good' looks like and understand if the product is actually solving a problem.
- Startups are the easiest category of customers to sell to. Evidence: They have shorter decision-making lines and lack the bureaucratic processes of larger corporations.
- Most startups fail because they don't do enough outreach. Evidence: Founders often stop too early, failing to reach the necessary volume to achieve statistically significant conversion data.
