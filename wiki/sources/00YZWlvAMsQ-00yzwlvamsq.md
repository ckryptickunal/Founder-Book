## Summary

Keshav, founder of Lumina, discusses his journey from a competitive Rubik's Cube champion in India to building a successful AI transformation partner for US health systems. He details how his unconventional background, persistence in hackathons, and strategic approach to enterprise sales helped him secure major clients like the Cleveland Clinic, ultimately raising $60 million to automate administrative healthcare workflows.

## Key Ideas

- Lumina acts as a data transformation layer, converting unstructured data like faxes into structured data for hospital operational workflows.
- The healthcare industry suffers from over $1 trillion in administrative waste, much of which is driven by manual, paper-based processes.
- Enterprise sales is a deeply relational game; success often comes from finding a 'champion' within an organization rather than selling to the institution as a whole.
- Personal narratives and mission-driven storytelling are powerful tools for young founders to build credibility with large, bureaucratic institutions.
- The 'pivot' process in startups should be methodical, focusing on mission-alignment and deep customer discovery to identify high-value use cases.
- The playing field for entrepreneurship is increasingly level, allowing individuals to pursue massive, multi-decade goals regardless of their background or academic history.
