## Summary

Emergent founders Mukund and Madav Jha discuss their AI-powered app building platform, which allows users to create production-ready web and mobile applications using natural language prompts. The company has seen rapid growth, reaching 1.7 million users and 2.5 million apps built within three months of launch, and recently secured $23 million in funding from Lightspeed.

## Key Ideas

- Emergent uses a multi-agent architecture to handle complex software engineering tasks, including design, coding, testing, and deployment.
- The platform differentiates itself by providing a tightly coupled infrastructure that allows for a faster feedback loop between the AI agent and the code.
- The founders focus on 'power users'—entrepreneurs and domain experts—who have serious, long-term ideas rather than just casual users.
- Influencer marketing was a key strategy for their initial growth, allowing them to track conversion data methodically.
- The founders believe AI is a 'big reset' for the next 20 years, comparable to the early days of Bitcoin.
