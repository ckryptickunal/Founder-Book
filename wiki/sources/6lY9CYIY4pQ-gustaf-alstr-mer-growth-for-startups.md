## Summary

Gustaf Alströmer, former Airbnb growth team member, outlines a framework for startup growth, emphasizing that early-stage founders must prioritize 'doing things that don't scale' over premature optimization. He details how to measure product-market fit through retention metrics, explains when and how to implement growth channels, and discusses the role of data-driven experimentation in scaling decision-making.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must prioritize 'doing things that don't scale' in the early stages to gain initial traction.
- Retention is the most reliable, unbiased metric for determining product-market fit.
- Growth channels and tactics should only be pursued after achieving product-market fit.
- Conversion rate optimization (CRO) involves analyzing and improving every step of the user funnel.
- Paid marketing should only be utilized once a company has revenue and a clear understanding of Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC).
- A culture of experimentation and A/B testing helps remove bias from product decision-making as a company scales.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The state where a product satisfies a strong market demand, best measured by repeat usage/retention.
- [[topics/growth-tactics|Growth Tactics]]: Strategies for scaling user acquisition, including SEO, paid marketing, and viral loops.
- [[topics/conversion-rate-optimization|Conversion Rate Optimization]]: The process of improving the percentage of users who complete a desired action within a product funnel.
- [[topics/experimentation|Experimentation]]: Using A/B testing to make data-driven product decisions rather than relying on intuition or hierarchy.

## Notable Claims

- Net Promoter Score (NPS) is a poor metric for measuring product-market fit. Evidence: Many world-class products and companies have historically low NPS scores.
- Founders should not spend money on online marketing until they have revenue. Evidence: Without revenue, it is impossible to validate if the Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is sustainable against the Lifetime Value (LTV).
- Doing things that don't scale is necessary for early-stage startups. Evidence: Airbnb founders manually visited hosts to take photos and fix bugs, which built the foundation for their growth.
