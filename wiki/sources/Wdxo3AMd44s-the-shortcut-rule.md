# The Shortcut Rule

## Summary

The 'shortcut rule' describes a phenomenon where focusing exclusively on a single metric of success leads to achieving that specific goal while sacrificing broader, unmeasured objectives, effectively hitting the target but missing the point.

## Key Ideas

- Optimizing for a single metric often results in the degradation of unmeasured variables.
- The pursuit of specific goals can distract from the original, broader intent behind those goals.
- Historical AI research in chess was intended to decode human intelligence but ultimately succeeded only in game mastery without providing insights into cognition.
