# RFS: LLMs for manual back office processes in legacy enterprises

## Metadata

## Summary

Y Combinator highlights a significant opportunity for startups to use LLMs to automate complex, manual back-office compliance and auditing processes within legacy industries like banking, insurance, and healthcare.

## Key Ideas

- Large enterprises rely on massive human teams to perform manual compliance spot-checks on customer service interactions.
- Current automation efforts focus on customer-facing interactions, leaving a gap in internal auditing and compliance workflows.
- Industries with high regulatory burdens, such as banking, insurance, and healthcare, are prime targets for LLM-based process automation.
- Startups should focus on replacing repetitive, complex manual tasks rather than just simple customer-facing chatbots.

## Notable Claims

- LLMs are highly effective at performing compliance spot-checks on large volumes of text-based customer service interactions. Evidence: The speaker notes that these tasks involve reviewing reams of text to ensure specific rules are followed, which aligns with LLM capabilities.
