# New RFS -- Breakthrough Technologies

## Summary

Sam Altman outlines Y Combinator's strategic shift to prioritize funding 'breakthrough technology' companies—startups tackling long-term, high-impact problems in science and engineering that were previously the domain of government-funded research.

## Key Ideas

- Startups can now leverage technology to achieve what previously required national-level resources.
- The YC model supports long time horizons and high-risk ventures by focusing on compounding small wins.
- Economic viability is essential: new energy and technology solutions must be cheaper than existing alternatives without subsidies.
- A common failure mode for ambitious startups is attempting an initial project that is too large and expensive.
- The current scientific funding model is broken, favoring political skill over scientific genius.

- [[topics/biotech|Biotech]]: The emerging field of 'hacking biology' to fight disease, slow aging, and merge humans with computers.
- [[topics/healthcare|Healthcare]]: Focusing on preventative care and data-driven solutions to address the unsustainable growth of US healthcare spending.

## Notable Claims

- The cost of energy is directly correlated with the quality of life. Evidence: Historical examples like the steam engine reducing energy costs and raising living standards.
- The current scientific funding model is broken. Evidence: It favors political skill over scientific genius and fails to support basic research effectively.
