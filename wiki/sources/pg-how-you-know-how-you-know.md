# How You Know

## Summary

Paul Graham explores the paradox of forgetting the details of books while still benefiting from them, proposing that reading and experience function like a 'compiled program' that updates one's mental model of the world even after the source material is forgotten.

## Key Ideas

- The value of reading is not in the retention of facts, but in the refinement of one's mental models.
- The mind acts like a compiled program where the source code (specific memories) is lost, but the functionality (wisdom/intuition) remains.
- Rereading books is valuable because the 'compilation' process depends on the state of the brain at the time of reading.
- Future technology may allow for the indexing and editing of life experiences, potentially changing how we learn from our past.
