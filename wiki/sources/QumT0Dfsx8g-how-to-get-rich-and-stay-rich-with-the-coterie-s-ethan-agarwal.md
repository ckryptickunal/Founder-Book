## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Ethan Agarwal, CEO of The Coterie, to discuss the evolving definition of wealth, the unique financial needs of modern 'built-wealth' individuals, and how software can democratize access to high-level financial tools and investment opportunities previously reserved for the ultra-wealthy.

## Key Ideas

- Wealth is increasingly viewed as an 'unlock' for freedom and creative pursuit rather than an end goal for retirement.
- Modern wealth creation differs from the past; it is driven by career mobility, equity-based compensation, and entrepreneurial ambition rather than long-term corporate tenure.
- Traditional wealth management firms fail to scale and often ignore younger, high-earning individuals because of high marginal service costs.
- Software-driven financial platforms can provide high-quality services (investing, lending, estate planning) to a broader demographic of accredited investors.
- Access to 'Alpha' (outperforming the market) through venture capital funds is a critical differentiator for wealth growth, which is currently gated by high minimums and exclusive networks.
- Second-generation immigrants often possess a 'survivor' mindset that drives them to build wealth, often as a byproduct of their ambition rather than a primary goal.

## Notable Claims

- Approximately one-third of wealth in the U.S. is inherited, one-third is from stock/investing, and one-third is created. Evidence: Cited by Ethan Agarwal as a general breakdown of U.S. wealth sources.
- Software can reduce the marginal cost of servicing financial clients to near zero. Evidence: The Coterie's business model uses digital distribution to service clients at lower wealth tiers than traditional firms.
- Estate planning should be done before assets appreciate in value. Evidence: Legal strategy to minimize tax impact and provide asset protection early.
