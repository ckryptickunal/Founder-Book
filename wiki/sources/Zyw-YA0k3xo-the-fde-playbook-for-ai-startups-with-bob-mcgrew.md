# The FDE Playbook for AI Startups with Bob McGrew

## Summary

Bob McGrew, former Chief Research Officer at OpenAI and early Palantir executive, explains the 'Forward Deployed Engineer' (FDE) model. He details how this strategy—originally developed at Palantir to bridge the gap between complex software and specific customer needs—has become the dominant organizational structure for modern AI agent startups. The model emphasizes deep, high-touch product discovery, iterative customization, and solving high-value problems for enterprise clients.

## Key Ideas

- The FDE model is essentially 'doing things that don't scale' at scale.
- FDEs act as the bridge between raw product capabilities and specific, high-value customer outcomes.
- Successful FDE teams consist of 'Echo' teams (domain experts/account managers) and 'Delta' teams (rapid prototyping engineers).
- The goal of the FDE model is to move from custom, high-touch deployments to a generalized platform by identifying common patterns across customers.
- AI agent startups are adopting this model because there is no incumbent product, necessitating intense product discovery within the enterprise.
- The FDE model is a 'learning' strategy; it requires organizational discipline to avoid devolving into a pure, non-scalable consulting firm.
- Pricing in the FDE model should be based on the value of the outcome delivered rather than traditional SaaS metrics like seats or usage.

## Topics

- [[topics/forward-deployed-engineering|Forward Deployed Engineering]]: A technical strategy where engineers are embedded at customer sites to build custom solutions that eventually inform core product development.
- [[topics/product-discovery|Product Discovery]]: The process of identifying what a customer truly needs, often best achieved by solving problems from the inside rather than selling from the outside.
- [[topics/ai-agents|AI Agents]]: A new market category characterized by rapid capability growth but a significant gap in enterprise adoption.
- [[topics/organizational-scaling|Organizational Scaling]]: The challenge of transitioning from high-touch, custom deployments to a scalable, generalized software platform.

## Notable Claims

- The FDE model is the dominant way AI agent startups are currently organizing. Evidence: Over 100 YC startups are hiring for 'Forward Deployed Engineer' roles, up from zero three years ago.
- FDEs are not just consultants; they are product discovery engines. Evidence: They identify high-value problems that can be generalized into platform features, turning 'gravel roads' into 'paved highways'.
- AI capabilities are racing ahead of enterprise adoption. Evidence: The rate of progress from GPT-4o to o3 is extremely fast, yet real-world adoption remains in the early stages.
