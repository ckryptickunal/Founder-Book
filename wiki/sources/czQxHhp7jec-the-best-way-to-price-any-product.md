# The best way to price any product

## Summary

This video explains the fundamental relationship between cost, price, and value, advocating for value-based pricing over cost-plus pricing to drive business growth.

## Key Ideas

- The margin (gap between cost and price) drives the seller's incentive to sell.
- The gap between price and value drives the customer's incentive to buy.
- Cost-plus pricing is based on internal expenses, while value-based pricing is based on the perceived benefit to the customer.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and guidance to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/pricing-strategy|Pricing Strategy]]: The methodology of determining the optimal price point for a product or service.
- [[topics/value-based-pricing|Value-Based Pricing]]: A strategy that sets prices primarily based on a consumer's perceived value of the product.
- [[topics/cost-plus-pricing|Cost-Plus Pricing]]: A strategy that adds a markup to the cost of producing a product to determine the selling price.

## Notable Claims

- A larger gap between price and cost increases the seller's incentive to sell. Evidence: Defined as the margin, which serves as the primary financial driver for the business.
- A larger gap between price and value increases the customer's incentive to buy. Evidence: Customers are more likely to sign up or use a product when they perceive the value to be significantly higher than the price.

## Quotes

> The gap between price and cost that is your margin that is your incentive to sell.
> This gap here between price and value is incentive to buy.
