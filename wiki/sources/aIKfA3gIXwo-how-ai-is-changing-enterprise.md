# How AI Is Changing Enterprise

## Summary

Aaron Levie, CEO of Box, joins Y Combinator's 'The Light Cone' podcast to discuss the AI revolution in the enterprise. He argues that AI is not just a 'wrapper' but a transformative technology that will commoditize intelligence, expand software markets, and drive productivity. Levie emphasizes that enterprises care about outcomes and workflows rather than underlying models, and that the true value lies in the software stack built around AI to solve specific business problems.

## Key Ideas

- The 'AI wrapper' meme is largely a misunderstanding; value is created by building software around workflows, data, and business logic, not just the model itself.
- In B2B, enterprises prioritize outcomes (e.g., customer support, contract automation) over the specific underlying AI model.
- The cost of intelligence is trending toward zero, making it a commodity similar to cloud storage or compute.
- AI will significantly expand the Total Addressable Market (TAM) for software by enabling automation of tasks that were previously too expensive or operationally intensive to perform.
- Enterprises distinguish between 'context' (standardized, non-competitive functions like HR or ERP) and 'core' (unique value propositions), with most AI adoption occurring in the former via ISVs.
- Competitive pressure forces companies to reinvest AI-driven efficiency gains back into growth and product improvement rather than just taking higher profit margins.
- The ultimate potential of AI is to drive abundance by lowering the cost of goods and services, thereby improving global standards of living.

## Topics

- [[topics/jevons-paradox|Jevons Paradox]]: The economic theory that technological progress that increases efficiency of a resource can increase the consumption of that resource rather than decreasing it.
- [[topics/software-tam-expansion|Software TAM Expansion]]: How AI makes software economically viable for tasks that were previously handled by manual labor or not done at all.
- [[topics/core-vs-context|Core vs. Context]]: A framework for businesses to decide whether to build internal AI solutions (core) or buy external software (context).

## Notable Claims

- The cost of intelligence will converge to the cost of bare metal. Evidence: Historical trends in cloud storage and compute pricing, combined with the competitive nature of model providers.
- AI will increase the total spend on software by 5x over the next decade. Evidence: AI enables the automation of tasks that were previously untapped by software, creating additive revenue streams.
- Enterprises do not care about the underlying AI model. Evidence: Successful B2B startups have switched models multiple times without their enterprise customers noticing or caring, as long as the outcome is delivered.
