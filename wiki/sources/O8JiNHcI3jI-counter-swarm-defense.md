## Summary

Y Combinator is actively seeking startups to develop a 'counter-swarm' defense stack, arguing that current defense systems are too expensive and fragmented to combat the rising threat of cheap, autonomous, and coordinated drone swarms.

## Key Ideas

- The cost asymmetry between expensive interceptors and cheap drones favors attackers.
- Current counter-drone systems are siloed and ineffective against coordinated swarms.
- Drone defense is evolving into a real-time distributed systems problem rather than a traditional weapon operation.
- Future defense requires non-kinetic solutions and software that integrates all sensors into a unified operational picture.

## Topics

- [[topics/cost-asymmetry-in-warfare|Cost Asymmetry in Warfare]]: The economic imbalance where attackers use low-cost drones to overwhelm high-cost defensive assets.
- [[topics/distributed-systems-in-defense|Distributed Systems in Defense]]: The application of real-time data fusion and software orchestration to manage complex defensive environments.

## Notable Claims

- The cost advantage in modern drone warfare currently lies entirely with the attackers. Evidence: A Patriot missile costs $3 million, while an FPV drone costs approximately $500.
- Radio jamming is becoming an obsolete method for drone defense. Evidence: The speaker notes the rise of jam-resistant autonomous drone technology.
