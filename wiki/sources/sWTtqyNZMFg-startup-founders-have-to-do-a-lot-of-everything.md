# Startup founders have to do a lot of… everything.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sWTtqyNZMFg`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-01-02T17:17:30Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sWTtqyNZMFg

## Summary

This video explains that early-stage startup founders must embrace 'low-status' operational work rather than emulating the visionary roles of high-profile CEOs, emphasizing the 'do things that don't scale' philosophy.

## Key Ideas

- Founders at pre-product-market fit startups must handle all operational tasks personally.
- The 'visionary CEO' archetype is inappropriate for early-stage startups.
- Founders must be personally accountable for the end-to-end functionality of their system.
- Success requires embracing unglamorous, low-status work.

## Notable Claims

- Early-stage founders should perform low-status work rather than focusing on high-level vision. Evidence: The speaker argues that founders must make the entire system run end-to-end personally at the pre-product-market stage.

## Quotes
