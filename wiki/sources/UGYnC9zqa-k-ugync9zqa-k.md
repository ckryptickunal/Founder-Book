## Summary

Shomik, a repeat YC founder, discusses his new startup Sasabi, an AI-native observability platform. After his first venture, Opkit, he realized the importance of building a company aligned with his personal expertise and passion. Sasabi aims to replace traditional observability tools by using AI to analyze logs as the sole source of telemetry, simplifying the debugging process for engineering teams.

## Key Ideas

- Logs are all you need: AI can now extract sufficient insights from unstructured logs, potentially replacing the traditional 'three pillars' (logs, metrics, traces) approach.
- Founders should build companies aligned with their personal expertise and past experiences rather than chasing 'MBA-style' opportunities in unfamiliar industries.
- Observability is about preparing to respond to production issues, as static analysis and testing cannot predict all real-world failures.
- The 'AI era' of software development requires a shift from manual dashboard monitoring to AI-driven, self-healing systems.
- Building a company should be fun; alignment with personal interests and working with peers makes the difficult journey of a startup more sustainable.
