## Summary

Manis is a new general-purpose multi-agent AI platform that utilizes a hierarchical architecture to decompose complex tasks into subtasks, managed by specialized sub-agents. By leveraging foundational models like Claude 3.7 Sonnet and integrating with tools like Browser-use and E2B, it achieves high performance on the GAIA benchmark while offering users greater transparency and control compared to centralized AI services.

## Key Ideas

- Manis uses a multi-agent architecture where an executive agent coordinates specialized sub-agents to perform tasks.
- The platform employs 'chain of thought injection' to allow agents to reflect and update plans during execution.
- Manis functions as an 'AI wrapper' that integrates foundational models with proprietary orchestration, UI, and toolsets.
- The platform offers significant cost efficiency, averaging $2 per task, and provides a transparent file system view of agent operations.
- Sustainable differentiation for AI startups requires proprietary evaluations, deep workflow integration, or exclusive data access rather than just model-wrapping.
