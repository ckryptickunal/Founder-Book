## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the critical issue of co-founder conflict, which he identifies as the leading cause of startup failure. He shares his personal experience with conflict avoidance at his first startup, Posterous, and provides actionable frameworks for managing both too little and too much conflict, emphasizing the importance of accountability, active listening, and professional coaching.

## Key Ideas

- Co-founder conflict is the number one cause of early-stage startup failure.
- Conflict avoidance is often mistaken for a healthy relationship, but it leads to resentment and eventual breakdown.
- When conflict is too low, founders must force resolution through dedicated time and honest communication.
- When conflict is too high, it is often due to poorly defined roles; assigning clear ownership for every task is essential.
- The 'disagree and commit' principle allows teams to move fast once a decision is made by the designated owner.
- Active listening, specifically using 'steel man' arguments, is vital for resolving fundamental disagreements.
- Founders should not hesitate to hire executive coaches or seek help from boards and advisors when conflicts become intractable.
