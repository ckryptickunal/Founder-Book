## Summary

Sam Altman provides a comprehensive guide for startup founders on the critical importance of hiring, emphasizing that building a team is the most significant task a founder performs. The essay covers sourcing, interviewing, auditioning, cultural fit, compensation, and the necessity of firing quickly when hires do not work out.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should spend 33-50% of their time on hiring.
- Use auditions (paid trial work) rather than just interviews to assess candidates.
- Prioritize personal networks for sourcing; avoid job boards and recruiters when possible.
- Hire for intelligence, effectiveness, and cultural alignment; never compromise on quality.
- Equity should be generous, while salaries should be market-competitive or slightly lower.
- Fire quickly if a hire is not working out; it is better for both the company and the individual.
- Maintain a small team size and only hire when absolutely necessary.

## Notable Claims

- A single bad hire left unfixed can kill a company. Evidence: Great people attract other great people; mediocre hires cause this phenomenon to unwind.
- Founders should not hire remote employees in the early days. Evidence: Culture is still gelling and requires everyone to be in the same building.
- Personal networks are the best source for candidates. Evidence: They outperform job boards and recruiters by at least a 10x margin.
