# How to be a hustler or a builder

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan provides actionable advice for aspiring entrepreneurs, distinguishing between the 'hustler' archetype, focused on marketing and sales, and the 'builder' archetype, focused on technical execution and problem-solving.

## Key Ideas

- Hustlers should focus on proactive marketing and sales by providing tangible value to founders.
- Builders should prioritize shipping a first version of a product that solves a real problem rather than waiting for the perfect idea.
- Cold outreach is most effective when accompanied by actual work or demonstrated value.
- Self-reliance is a critical trait for builders to avoid dependency on others.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): CEO of Y Combinator and investor

## Topics

- [[topics/entrepreneurship|Entrepreneurship]]: The fundamental approaches to starting a business through either sales-driven or product-driven paths.
- [[topics/cold-outreach|Cold Outreach]]: The strategy of contacting founders by offering unsolicited improvements or bringing them new customers.

## Notable Claims

- Founders will always respond to individuals who bring them new customers. Evidence: The speaker asserts that providing direct revenue or growth is a guaranteed way to get a founder's attention.
- The specific problem being solved matters less than the act of building a functional solution. Evidence: The speaker notes that many beginners stall by waiting for the 'perfect' problem, whereas shipping a real solution is more important.

## Quotes

> Marketing is about helping other people communicate their problem and how their solution is the best.
> There's power in being able to do that yourself and not rely on others.
> It just has to be a real problem that your software can actually solve.
