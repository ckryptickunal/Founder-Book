## Summary

Sam Altman reflects on his experience with the anonymous social app 'Secret,' arguing that anonymous platforms inherently trend toward toxicity and 'burn book' behavior, ultimately predicting their inevitable decline.

## Key Ideas

- Anonymity on the internet frequently correlates with increased meanness and toxic behavior.
- Unlike platforms like Facebook or Twitter, which improve with scale, anonymous social networks tend to degrade as they grow.
- There is a fundamental tension between the desire for anonymous confessionals and the tendency for such platforms to become hubs for personal attacks.
- The sustainability of an anonymous app depends on its ability to curb targeted harassment.
