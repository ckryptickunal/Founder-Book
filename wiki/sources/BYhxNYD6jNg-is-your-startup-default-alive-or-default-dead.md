# Is your startup default alive, or default dead?

## Summary

This video explains the 'default alive' versus 'default dead' framework for startups, a concept introduced by Paul Graham to help founders assess their financial sustainability without relying on future fundraising.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must confront the uncomfortable reality of their startup's financial survival.
- The framework is binary: a startup is either default alive or default dead.
- Default alive is distinct from being currently profitable; it focuses on the trajectory of revenue growth relative to cash burn.
- A startup is default alive if its revenue growth rate ensures profitability before cash reserves are exhausted.

- A startup is default alive if its revenue growth rate will lead to profitability before the bank account hits zero. Evidence: The speaker defines default alive as a state where growth trajectory outpaces cash burn.
- Default alive is not the same as being profitable. Evidence: The speaker notes that a company can be burning money today and still be default alive if the growth rate is sufficient.
