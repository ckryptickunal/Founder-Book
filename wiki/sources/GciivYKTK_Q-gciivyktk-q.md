## Summary

The founders of Parahelp, Anker and Mass, discuss their journey from building consumer apps in Denmark to creating a leading agentic AI company for customer support. They detail their Y Combinator experience, their pivot to B2B SaaS, and the development of their next-generation agentic architecture that automates ticket resolution and internal knowledge management for fast-growing software companies.

## Key Ideas

- Parahelp uses agentic AI to resolve complex customer support tickets by performing actions in external tools like Stripe.
- The company employs a 'forward-deployed engineering' approach that has been scaled through AI, allowing the system to self-configure and test.
- The 'Parahelp Assistant' acts as an AI-driven engineer that manages policies, runs evaluations, and optimizes performance without manual intervention.
- A continuous feedback loop (flywheel) allows the system to learn from human interactions and automatically improve resolution rates.
- The founders emphasize the importance of playing offense and failing fast, a mindset they cultivated despite the risk-averse culture in Denmark.
