# Elon Musk On Fear

## Metadata

## Summary

Elon Musk discusses his relationship with fear, explaining that he experiences it intensely but manages it through a sense of purpose and the application of fatalistic probability analysis.

## Key Ideas

- Fear is a normal human emotion, not a signal to avoid action.
- High-stakes goals can override the paralyzing effects of fear.
- Fatalism, or accepting the high probability of failure, can diminish the anxiety associated with risk.
- Focusing on the potential progress of an industry, even if a specific venture fails, provides motivation.

## Topics

- [[topics/risk-management|Risk Management]]: The psychological process of evaluating and proceeding with high-risk ventures despite fear.
- [[topics/fatalism|Fatalism]]: A mindset of accepting the inevitability of failure to reduce the emotional burden of risk.

## Notable Claims

- The odds of success for SpaceX were less than 10% at the start. Evidence: Musk's personal assessment of the venture's viability during its inception.
- Feeling fear is normal and not a reason to avoid an action. Evidence: Musk argues that a lack of fear would be indicative of a mental health issue.

## Quotes

> I actually think I feel fear quite strongly... but there are times when something is important enough you believe in it enough that you do it in spite of the fear.
> If you just accept the probabilities, then that diminishes fear.
> If we could just move the ball forward even if we died, maybe some other company could pick up the baton and keep moving it forward.
