# Should you work on that startup idea?

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the critical process of validating a startup idea by moving beyond mere vision to practical execution. He emphasizes the necessity of building a 'moat'—a unique competitive advantage—and provides a framework of questions for founders to evaluate the feasibility, market need, and defensibility of their ventures.

## Key Ideas

- Ideas are insufficient; founders must build the 'spaceship' to realize their vision.
- A startup needs a 'moat'—a unique advantage that prevents others from easily copying the product.
- Moats can be built through proprietary technology, exclusive customer relationships, or innovative go-to-market strategies.
- Founders should rigorously test their ideas against questions regarding audience, feasibility, cost, and existing alternatives.
- Simple technical innovations, even those not involving complex AI, can serve as effective moats if they solve a perceived impossibility.

## Notable Claims

- The best moat for a startup is often pure technology. Evidence: Cites Standard Cognition's ability to provide cashierless checkout at a fraction of the cost of competitors.
- Simple technical solutions can be as effective as complex ones for building a moat. Evidence: Cites Posterous using simple 'IF' statements to solve email security concerns, which competitors deemed impossible.
