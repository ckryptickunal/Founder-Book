# 10 People + AI = Billion Dollar Company?

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses Jensen Huang's controversial claim that programming is no longer a necessary skill due to AI. They conclude that while AI will automate junior-level coding tasks and increase productivity, learning to code remains essential for developing the 'taste' and logical thinking required to build complex, high-quality software products.

## Key Ideas

- AI will likely automate junior developer tasks, potentially leading to smaller, more efficient teams.
- The 'Jevons Paradox' suggests that as programming becomes more efficient, demand for software and programmers will increase rather than decrease.
- Learning to code is not just about writing syntax; it is a fundamental way to develop logical thinking and 'good taste' in product design.
- The real-world application of software involves messy, complex systems that AI cannot yet fully encapsulate, requiring human oversight.
- The goal of AI in programming is to enable more people to go from zero to one, potentially leading to a surge in the number of billion-dollar companies.
- Founders should view their companies as engineering problems, treating people and processes with the same optimization mindset used in coding.

## Notable Claims

- Learning to code makes you smarter. Evidence: Studies show that LLMs learn to think logically by processing code, suggesting a correlation between coding and logical reasoning.
- AI will not reduce the number of programmers. Evidence: Historical data (Jevons Paradox) shows that making a task more efficient increases demand for the output of that task.
- Companies will not necessarily get smaller. Evidence: Despite decades of infrastructure improvements, companies have not shrunk; they have simply expanded their ambitions and output.
