## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the dual nature of building world-changing companies, contrasting the innovative 'zero-to-one' phase of the McDonald's brothers with the scaling expertise of Ray Kroc.

## Key Ideas

- Founding a company requires two distinct skill sets: initial innovation and subsequent scaling.
- The 'zero-to-one' phase demands bottom-up resourcefulness and a willingness to challenge established norms.
- The scaling phase requires the ability to take a unique product and dominate a competitive market.
- Ray Kroc should not be viewed solely as a villain, but as a master of the scaling phase.
