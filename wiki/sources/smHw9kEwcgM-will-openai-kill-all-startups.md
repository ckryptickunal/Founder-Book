# Will OpenAI Kill All Startups?

## Metadata

## Summary

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell of Y Combinator discuss the fear that OpenAI will render startups obsolete. They argue that history shows major technological shifts—like the internet, cloud computing, and mobile—actually create more opportunities for startups than they destroy. They emphasize that OpenAI's primary goal is AGI, leaving significant room for founders to build specialized, high-quality products that solve real customer problems.

## Key Ideas

- Major technological shifts historically favor startups over incumbents due to agility.
- There is a critical distinction between 'cargo culting' AI (using it superficially for hype) and using AI to solve genuine customer problems.
- OpenAI's focus on AGI leaves a massive gap for startups to build vertical-specific applications.
- Many successful companies, such as Dropbox, began as 'thin wrappers' on top of existing infrastructure (like AWS).
- The current AI landscape is comparable to the early days of the App Store or cloud computing, representing a prime moment for innovation.
- Second-order effects of AI—similar to how the iPhone enabled Uber—will likely produce the most significant future businesses.
