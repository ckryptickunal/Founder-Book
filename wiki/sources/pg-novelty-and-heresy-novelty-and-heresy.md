# Novelty and Heresy

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-novelty-and-heresy`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: November 2019
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-novelty-and-heresy

## Summary

Paul Graham explores the inherent link between discovering novel ideas and being labeled a heretic, arguing that valuable, non-obvious ideas often challenge deeply held, mistaken assumptions.

## Key Ideas

- Truly novel ideas are often hidden behind mistaken assumptions that society is emotionally attached to.
- Challenging these assumptions inevitably leads to accusations of heresy.
- Suppressing heresy creates a 'dead zone' of unexplored ideas, stifling innovation.
- The existence of heresy can be used as a heuristic to identify fertile ground for new discoveries.

## Notable Claims

- Suppressing heresy prevents the discovery of any ideas that indirectly imply a protected assumption is false. Evidence: The existence of a 'dead zone' of unexplored ideas surrounding every cherished mistaken assumption.

## Quotes

> If you discover something new, there's a significant chance you'll be accused of some form of heresy.
> Every cherished mistaken assumption has a dead zone of unexplored ideas around it.
> If you're looking for new ideas, one way to find them is by looking for heresies.
