## Summary

A panel of business development leaders from major life science companies (Genentech, J&J, Medtronic, Novartis) discusses the strategic approach to partnering with early-stage startups, emphasizing the importance of long-term relationship building, scientific alignment, and realistic deal expectations in the current market.

## Key Ideas

- Start building relationships with large companies early, even before a formal partnership is needed.
- Focus on scientific dialogue and data transparency rather than rigid deal structures.
- Understand that large company governance processes are standard; delays are often caused by the startup's own legal or advisory teams.
- Avoid 'cold' outreach to CEOs; instead, engage with regional business development leads or scientific counterparts.
- Current market conditions require startups to be more realistic about their ability to build commercial organizations and maintain financial solvency.
- Large companies are increasingly cautious about option-based deals due to concerns over startup capital sustainability.

## Notable Claims

- Large companies are often not the bottleneck in deal speed; startups and their external legal counsel frequently cause delays. Evidence: Panelists noted that they are often waiting on the other party to provide feedback or finalize documents.
- Reaching out to a CEO of a large company rarely speeds up a deal. Evidence: Stacy Feld noted that such actions often create confusion and force the request back down the chain of command.
- Financial solvency of partners has become a critical due diligence factor. Evidence: Ahmet noted that due to the current market, they now scrutinize a startup's ability to fund its own operations.
