## Summary

Chase Adam, co-founder of Watsi, shares the origin story and early growth of his nonprofit crowdfunding platform for healthcare. He discusses the challenges of transitioning from a Peace Corps volunteer to a startup founder, the importance of radical transparency, and the process of securing funding through Y Combinator.

## Key Ideas

- Watsi functions as a 'Kickstarter for healthcare,' allowing donors to fund specific medical treatments for patients globally.
- Nonprofits face unique challenges compared to for-profits, specifically regarding feedback loops and measuring success.
- Radical transparency, a 100% donation model, and a flat organizational structure are core to Watsi's operational philosophy.
- Fundraising for a nonprofit is most effective when selling a clear, simple vision rather than just emotional stories or complex impact models.
- The '1,000-day' persistence period is a common reality for startups, and success is not guaranteed even with initial traction.

## Notable Claims

- Nonprofits are often more risk-averse than for-profits. Evidence: Philanthropists and foundations are afraid of being burned by new, unproven organizations.
- Watsi's 100% model increases donor trust. Evidence: By ensuring 100% of donations go to medical care, they remove the friction of overhead concerns.
- Focusing on a single metric (weekly donations) accelerated growth. Evidence: By ignoring other distractions, they increased weekly donations by 30% consistently during YC.
