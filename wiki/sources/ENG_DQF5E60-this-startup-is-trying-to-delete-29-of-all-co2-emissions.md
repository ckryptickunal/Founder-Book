# This Startup Is Trying To Delete 29% Of All CO2 Emissions

## Summary

Remora, a startup founded by Paul Gross, is developing mobile carbon capture technology designed to retrofit semi-trucks and freight trains. By capturing CO2 directly from exhaust systems using specialized pellets, the company aims to reduce transportation emissions—the largest sector of US emissions—while producing beverage-grade CO2 for commercial resale.

## Key Ideas

- Point-source carbon capture is more energy-efficient than direct air capture because CO2 concentrations are higher in exhaust.
- Retrofitting existing heavy-duty vehicles is a strategic way to address hard-to-electrify sectors like long-haul trucking and rail.
- Vertical integration and in-house manufacturing allow for faster iteration cycles in hardware development.
- Choosing a location based on talent density (e.g., Detroit for mechanical/automotive engineering) is critical for hardware startups.
- The business model creates a dual benefit: reducing environmental impact and generating revenue by selling captured CO2 to industrial customers.

## Notable Claims

- Transportation accounts for 29% of all US emissions. Evidence: Cited by the founder as the primary motivation for targeting the trucking and rail industries.
- Remora's device can reduce a vehicle's emissions by at least 80-90%. Evidence: Stated by the founder regarding the performance of their retrofitted systems.
- Point-source capture is more efficient than direct air capture. Evidence: The founder explains that air is too dilute (0.04% CO2), requiring significantly more energy to process compared to concentrated exhaust.
