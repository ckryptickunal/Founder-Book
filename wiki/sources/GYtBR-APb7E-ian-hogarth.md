## Summary

Ian Hogarth, co-founder of Songkick, discusses the realities of building a startup, specifically within the entertainment industry. He outlines the 'three engines' of startup success—gratification, growth, and economics—and emphasizes the importance of resilience, team dynamics, and understanding industry consolidation.

## Key Ideas

- Startups in entertainment require navigating highly consolidated markets by partnering with incumbents rather than trying to disrupt them.
- A startup's success is defined by three interconnected engines: Gratification (product value), Growth (user acquisition), and Economics (revenue model).
- Survival is a growth strategy; staying in the game allows you to benefit from platform shifts (e.g., the rise of mobile).
- Founders should perform a 'five whys' analysis on their motivations to maintain resilience during difficult periods.
- Data quality and comprehensive coverage are often the 'linchpin' for user gratification in consumer apps.
