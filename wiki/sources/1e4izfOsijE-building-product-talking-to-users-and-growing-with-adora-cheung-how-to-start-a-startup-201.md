# Building Product, Talking to Users, and Growing with Adora Cheung (How to Start a Startup 2014: 4)

## Summary

Adora Cheung, co-founder of Homejoy, shares practical advice on taking a startup from zero to many users. She emphasizes the importance of deep industry immersion, manual processes before automation, direct user feedback, and data-driven growth strategies (sticky, viral, and paid).

## Key Ideas

- Immerse yourself in the industry you are disrupting to understand inefficiencies.
- Build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that solves a specific problem, not just a set of features.
- Manual processes are necessary in the early stages to learn what to automate later.
- Focus on one growth channel at a time until it is exhausted or proven ineffective.
- Customer retention and cohort analysis are critical metrics for long-term sustainability.
- Avoid the 'Frankenstein approach' of building every feature requested by users without understanding the underlying problem.
- Pivot if you experience three to four weeks of no growth or negative growth despite hard work.

## Notable Claims

- Manual processes are better than automation in the early stages of a startup. Evidence: Homejoy manually interviewed cleaners to identify key performance indicators before building an automated application system.
- Paid products provide more honest user feedback than free products. Evidence: Users who pay for a service are more likely to complain if the value proposition is not met, providing clearer data on product quality.
- You should focus on one growth channel at a time. Evidence: Trying multiple strategies simultaneously prevents founders from accurately identifying which channel is actually working.
