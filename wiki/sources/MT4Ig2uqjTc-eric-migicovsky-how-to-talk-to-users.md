## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, a Y Combinator partner and founder of Pebble, provides a tactical guide for founders on how to conduct effective user interviews. He emphasizes that maintaining a direct connection with users is a core responsibility of founders, regardless of their role, and outlines strategies to extract actionable data rather than biased feedback.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must maintain a direct, ongoing connection with users throughout the company's lifespan.
- The goal of a user interview is to extract data, not to pitch or sell the product.
- Avoid hypothetical questions; focus on past experiences and specific problems the user has encountered.
- Use the 'Mom Test' approach: avoid asking leading questions that elicit polite but useless compliments.
- Prioritize potential customers based on the frequency of their problem, the cost of the problem, and their budget/authority to solve it.
- Use quantitative metrics, such as the 'how would you feel if this product disappeared' survey, to measure progress toward product-market fit.

## Notable Claims

- Founders should not outsource user research to sales or product teams. Evidence: The best companies are built by founders who maintain a direct connection to users to extract unfiltered information.
- If customers aren't already trying to solve their problem, it might not be a burning enough issue. Evidence: If a problem is painful, users will naturally experiment with workarounds or existing tools.
- A 40% 'very disappointed' response rate is a strong indicator of product-market fit. Evidence: Analysis of successful companies shows that those with high retention and growth often hit this threshold in user surveys.
