## Summary

Nick, founder of Sphere, discusses his journey from a failed edtech startup through 'pivot hell' to building a successful AI-powered global tax compliance platform. He emphasizes the importance of domain expertise, selling before building, and maintaining resilience during extreme personal and professional adversity.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should focus on domains they understand deeply rather than chasing 'hot' market trends.
- Selling before building (using high-fidelity prototypes) validates demand and de-risks product development.
- Resilience is a mental game; success often requires pushing through periods where external support and resources vanish.
- Modular product design allows for 'land and expand' strategies, making it easier for customers to adopt new solutions.
- True AI-native solutions involve rebuilding core engines rather than just applying AI to ancillary tasks.

## Notable Claims

- Selling before building is a viable strategy for early-stage founders. Evidence: The founder sold 10 binding contracts based solely on a Figma prototype.
- Focusing on a known domain leads to better compounding learnings. Evidence: The founder's shift to finance-focused problems allowed him to iterate faster and build a more effective product.
- Sphere is the first AI-native tax engine. Evidence: Unlike competitors who use AI for peripheral tasks, Sphere uses AI to collect, codify, and monitor global tax law.
