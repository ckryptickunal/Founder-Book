# Don't make the investor your customer.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `YU0kYWNuMXo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-05-10T21:25:40Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YU0kYWNuMXo

## Summary

Y Combinator partners discuss the trend of 'conformist' founders who prioritize seeking investor approval over building actual value for customers, warning that this mindset is detrimental to startup success.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often mistakenly treat investors as their primary customers rather than the actual users of their product.
- Conformist founders seek validation and approval from the venture capital ecosystem rather than focusing on solving real-world problems.
- The desire to impress investors leads to superficial behaviors, such as curating resumes or seeking 'secret' formulas for fundraising success.
- True startup success requires a shift in focus from status-seeking to genuine product-market fit.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/founder-psychology|Founder Psychology]]: The behavioral patterns and motivations of startup founders, specifically the distinction between value-driven and status-driven founders.
- [[topics/fundraising-strategy|Fundraising Strategy]]: The common pitfalls founders face when approaching investors and the importance of prioritizing customer acquisition over investor approval.

## Notable Claims

- The highest status job in the early-stage startup world is currently that of an investor. Evidence: Observed behavior where founders prioritize meeting, talking to, and seeking approval from investors above other activities.
- Conformist founders often treat investors as their customers. Evidence: Founders focus on acting in ways that investors like and seeking 'secret' advice on how to gain investor approval rather than building a product.

## Quotes

> Don't make the investor your customer.
> They want us to tell them the secret to get investors to like them they hope that we will tell them the secret words to whisper to an investor to get approval.
