## Summary

Mariya Nurislamova, founder of Scentbird, shares the challenging journey of building her fragrance subscription company, detailing early pivots, fundraising struggles, the impact of Y Combinator, and key lessons on leadership and growth.

## Key Ideas

- The importance of talking to customers early rather than building in isolation.
- The necessity of pivoting business models when initial monetization strategies fail.
- The power of mentorship and seeking advice from people who have achieved what you want.
- The 'rule of scarcity' in fundraising: creating urgency by framing rounds as nearly closed.
- The value of hiring '10x' employees over cheaper, less experienced talent.
- The importance of setting and communicating audacious goals early.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-pivoting|Startup Pivoting]]: The process of iterating through failed business models (recommender system, discovery kits, try-before-you-buy) to find a sustainable subscription model.
- [[topics/fundraising-strategy|Fundraising Strategy]]: Techniques for raising capital, including overcoming investor rejection and using scarcity to close rounds.
- [[topics/hiring-and-culture|Hiring and Culture]]: The shift from hiring for cost to hiring for high-impact '10x' talent.

## Notable Claims

- Top-tier talent is 10 times more effective than average employees in a startup environment. Evidence: Nurislamova's observation that she could have achieved more with fewer, higher-quality hires.
- You should only take advice from people who have what you want. Evidence: Nurislamova's experience ignoring discouraging advice from a successful hedge fund manager who did not understand the startup world.
