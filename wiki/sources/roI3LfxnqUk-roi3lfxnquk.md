## Summary

Albert and Santi, co-founders of the AI-native FP&A platform Alf, discuss their journey from Y Combinator to securing $29 million in Series B funding. They emphasize their product philosophy of 'meeting finance teams where they are' by integrating directly into spreadsheets, their commitment to rapid time-to-value, and the importance of maintaining a low-ego, customer-centric culture.

## Key Ideas

- Finance professionals prefer working in spreadsheets, so building tools that integrate directly into that environment is a key competitive advantage.
- Startups should focus on 'doing things that don't scale' in the early days to ensure customer success and build deep empathy for user pain points.
- Founders must be willing to reinvent their roles as the company grows, shifting from individual contributors to leaders who set direction and strategy.
- Most startups fail due to internal issues ('suicide') rather than external competition ('murder'), making internal focus and customer feedback more important than competitor obsession.
- AI is most effective in the finance space when applied pragmatically to solve specific workflow bottlenecks rather than just being a feature add-on.
