# Signs Your Company Is Recovering From ZIRP

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell and Michael Seibel discuss the 'ZIRP (Zero Interest Rate Phenomenon) Experience' (ZERP) hangover, identifying key signs that a company is successfully pivoting from the era of excess and low accountability back to a culture of productivity, hard work, and intentionality.

## Key Ideas

- ZERP (Zero Interest Rate Phenomenon) created a culture of infinite resources, low accountability, and 'fake work' that many companies are still struggling to unwind.
- Executive turnover is often a positive sign that a company is removing 'big company' managers who thrived in excess but are ineffective in a scaling startup environment.
- Founders becoming more involved and 'getting real' about operations is a critical indicator of recovery.
- Return-to-office mandates, while unpopular, signal that management is prioritizing productivity and team cohesion over individual convenience.
- The removal of excessive HR perks and vanity projects indicates a shift toward focusing on real products and customer value.
- Employees should view the shift toward higher work intensity as an opportunity to be on the 'right side' of a company's recovery, which ultimately leads to more professional growth and equity value.

## Topics

- [[topics/zerp-zero-interest-rate-phenomenon|ZERP (Zero Interest Rate Phenomenon)]]: A period of economic excess where capital was cheap, leading to bloated organizations, vanity projects, and a lack of focus on profitability.
- [[topics/corporate-culture-recovery|Corporate Culture Recovery]]: The process of shifting an organization from a state of low-accountability, high-perk environments back to a high-performance, product-focused culture.
- [[topics/return-to-office|Return to Office]]: The strategic decision by management to require physical presence to foster collaboration, productivity, and deeper professional connections.

## Notable Claims

- Executive turnover is a positive indicator of company recovery. Evidence: Many executives hired during ZIRP are accustomed to infinite resources and lack the skills to manage a scaling startup in a constrained environment.
- Physical office presence increases productivity. Evidence: Cities and physical proximity have historically been the primary drivers of human productivity and deeper professional relationships compared to remote work.
- Vanity projects are often used to justify promotions and headcount growth. Evidence: Employees often initiate projects not for customer value, but to build a team and budget, which serves as a self-perpetuating mechanism for personal advancement.
