## Summary

Kat Mañalac, a partner at Y Combinator, challenges the common misconception that a product launch is a singular, high-stakes event. She advocates for a strategy of continuous launching, where founders treat every new feature, update, or community engagement as an opportunity to gather feedback, refine their pitch, and build momentum.

## Key Ideas

- Launching is a continuous process, not a one-time event.
- Early-stage startups should avoid spending months on 'pixel-perfect' perfection before shipping.
- Silent launches (simple landing pages) are sufficient to start collecting user interest.
- Different channels (friends/family, strangers, online communities, blogs) serve different purposes in the validation lifecycle.
- When engaging online communities like Hacker News or Reddit, avoid marketing jargon and speak like a human.
- Building an email list from day one provides a powerful distribution channel for future announcements.
- Avoid 'pay-to-play' influencer or blogger schemes in the early stages.

## Notable Claims

- Spending six months preparing for a single launch often leads to failure. Evidence: Startups that wait too long to ship often find no one cares about their product, and they lack the runway to try again.
- Technical products are not the only ones that succeed on Hacker News. Evidence: Non-technical companies like '70 Million Jobs' have seen high engagement on the platform by leaning into intellectual curiosity.
- You do not need a fully functioning product to launch. Evidence: Companies like Lugg validated their business model by manually performing the service before building the software.
