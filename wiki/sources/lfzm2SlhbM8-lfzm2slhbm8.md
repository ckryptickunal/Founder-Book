## Summary

Dominic Vituchi, founder of Onshore, discusses the disruption of the traditional accounting industry through AI. He explains how his company automates complex tasks like R&D tax credits, which were previously handled by manual, billable-hour-driven processes at large firms. He argues that the 'Big Four' accounting model is structurally resistant to innovation due to misaligned incentives and a reliance on billable hours, and that AI will eventually shift the industry toward project-based, high-efficiency outcomes.

## Key Ideas

- The traditional accounting industry's reliance on billable hours creates a disincentive for efficiency and technological adoption.
- AI models now possess the capability to outperform junior and mid-level accounting staff in repetitive, data-heavy tasks.
- Selling AI software to established accounting firms is difficult because it threatens their core business model; selling directly to corporations is a more effective strategy.
- The 'Big Four' accounting firms are structurally incentivized to prioritize senior partner retirement payouts over long-term technological investment.
- Future professional services will likely shift toward higher revenue-per-employee models, with AI agents handling the bulk of execution.
- There is a significant market opportunity for a modern, AI-integrated alternative to Microsoft Excel that handles complex workflows better than traditional spreadsheets.
