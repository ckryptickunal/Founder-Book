## Summary

Jessica Mah, founder and CEO of inDinero, shares her candid journey of building her company, detailing the initial failures of her startup, the importance of pivoting, and the personal growth required to transition from an arrogant founder to a grounded, successful leader.

## Key Ideas

- The danger of vanity metrics and focusing on PR over product-market fit.
- The necessity of pivoting when a business model is fundamentally flawed.
- The importance of having the courage to fire underperforming employees.
- The value of maintaining 'unapologetic confidence' despite external criticism or gender-based biases in the VC industry.
- The shift from focusing on fundraising and hype to focusing on real revenue and customer needs.

## Notable Claims

- Focusing on vanity metrics like user counts instead of revenue can lead to a false sense of success. Evidence: The speaker describes having billions in assets under management but failing to convert users into paying customers.
- Hiring and firing are critical components of building a high-performing team. Evidence: The speaker recounts the difficulty of firing her first employee and the subsequent realization that she needed to remove underperformers.
- Working harder is not always the solution to a failing business model. Evidence: The speaker explains that taking a break to gain perspective was more effective than trying to 'work out of the box'.
