## Summary

Amir, co-founder of SIM.AI, discusses the company's journey from its inception to its rapid growth as a leading open-source platform for building AI agents. He shares insights into their pivot from an initial failed idea (Scalemont) to SIM.AI, driven by their own need for better agent orchestration. The conversation covers SIM.AI's current success (60k+ developers, 17.5k+ GitHub stars, Series A funding), their open-source philosophy, strategies for achieving GitHub trending, and advice for founders on pivoting, maintaining urgency, hiring, and long-term vision.

## Key Ideas

- SIM.AI is an AI agent building platform with a visual, Figma-like interface, enabling users to create workflows via drag-and-drop or natural language.
- The company pivoted from an initial complex and poorly understood idea (Scalemont) to SIM.AI, born out of their own pain points in orchestrating numerous AI agents.
- SIM.AI's rapid growth is attributed to its open-source nature, strategic community engagement (Hacker News, Twitter), and a deliberate focus on achieving GitHub trending.
- The founders emphasize the importance of being 'truly open source,' believing that vision, not code, is proprietary, and fostering a strong developer community.
- Maintaining a high tempo of consistent product launches (every two weeks) is crucial for sustained growth and urgency, a philosophy carried over and amplified from their YC experience.
- Hiring focuses on individuals passionate and curious about the future of AI, prioritizing drive and a long-term vision over specific technical stacks.
- Founders should optimize for long-term learning and growth, be honest with themselves, and pursue ideas they would genuinely enjoy working on for many years, even without immediate success, while maintaining personal well-being.

## Notable Claims

- SIM.AI is one of the fastest-growing open-source workflow builders. Evidence: Just crossed 60,000 developers on their cloud platform and 17.5k+ GitHub stars.
- Code is no longer the proprietary part of a company. Evidence: Belief that LLMs/AGI will soon be able to replicate entire applications within minutes, making the platform's interconnectedness and vision more valuable than the code itself.
- Most software will be orchestrated inside of workflows rather than just in pure code in the future. Evidence: Observation of the trend towards higher abstraction in programming (e.g., Cursor generating code) and the belief that workflows represent the next paradigm for shipping software easily.
- SIM.AI grew much faster outside of YC than inside YC. Evidence: Ended the batch with ~5,000 developers, grew to 60,000 developers in just 4 months post-batch by consistently launching and applying YC philosophies.
- AGI is an engineering challenge, not a scientific one. Evidence: Amir's personal belief that all necessary scientific problems for AGI have been solved, and the remaining hurdle is democratizing its implementation for developers and non-developers.
