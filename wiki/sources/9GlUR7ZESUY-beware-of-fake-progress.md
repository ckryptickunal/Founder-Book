# Beware of fake progress.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `9GlUR7ZESUY`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2022-12-21T16:23:07Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9GlUR7ZESUY

## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator emphasizing that startup success is driven exclusively by revenue or active user growth, warning founders against 'fake progress' activities that distract from core product development.

## Key Ideas

- Primary KPIs for startups should be limited to revenue or active users.
- Weekly goals must be set to move the needle on primary KPIs.
- High-leverage activities are strictly limited to talking to users and building/iterating the product.
- Fake progress includes attending conferences, winning awards, networking, and optimizing vanity metrics.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-growth|Startup Growth]]: The process of focusing resources on metrics that directly correlate to business viability.
- [[topics/fake-progress|Fake Progress]]: Activities that feel productive but do not contribute to the primary growth KPIs of a startup.

## Notable Claims

- The highest leverage activities for a startup are talking to users and building/iterating the product. Evidence: Direct correlation between product-market fit and growth metrics.
- Attending conferences and networking events is generally a bad use of time for early-stage founders. Evidence: These activities are several steps removed from delivering real value to the customer.

## Quotes

> Your primary KPI almost always is either Revenue or active users.
> Nothing else [besides talking to users and building/iterating] is in direct contrast with fake startup progress.
> While you may convince yourself these are good things to focus on, there are actually many steps away from delivering real value to your customer.
