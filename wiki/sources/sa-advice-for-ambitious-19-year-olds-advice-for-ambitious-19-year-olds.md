# Advice for ambitious 19 year olds

## Summary

Sam Altman provides strategic advice for ambitious 19-year-olds, emphasizing that the path chosen—college, joining a company, or starting a startup—is less important than the commitment to building tangible projects and surrounding oneself with smart, driven people.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize building things and networking with smart people regardless of the chosen path.
- Redefine risk: wasting productive years is riskier than taking a chance on a startup you love.
- When joining a company, choose one on a 'breakout trajectory' rather than focusing on salary.
- Only start a company if you are genuinely passionate about the idea, not just for the sake of being a founder.
- Maintain a low personal burn rate and minimize commitments to remain agile for future opportunities.
- Operational experience is more valuable for future career paths, including venture capital, than climbing corporate ladders.

## Notable Claims

- Starting a failed startup is less damaging to a career than being an employee at a failed company. Evidence: The founder learns more from the experience and is not penalized by the market for the failure of a project they were passionate about.
- Salary should not be a primary factor in choosing a job at a young age. Evidence: High salary at an established company often comes at the cost of working on uninteresting projects and missing out on the growth trajectory of a breakout company.
