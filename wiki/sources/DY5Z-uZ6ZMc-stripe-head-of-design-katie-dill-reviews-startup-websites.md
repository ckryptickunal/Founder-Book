## Summary

Katie Dill, Head of Design at Stripe, joins Y Combinator's Aaron Epstein to critique the landing pages of several startups. The review focuses on first impressions, clarity of value propositions, user journey, visual hierarchy, and the balance between creative design and functional usability.

## Key Ideas

- Websites must communicate value and purpose immediately above the fold.
- Avoid 'scroll hijacking' as it frustrates users and hinders navigation.
- Every design element, including animations and graphics, should serve a clear purpose and align with the brand's intended emotional impact.
- Consistency in typography, casing, and visual details is essential to maintaining user trust.
- B2B companies can be fun and creative, but not at the expense of usability or clarity.
- User testing is critical to ensure the intended message is actually received by the target audience.

## Notable Claims

- Scroll hijacking negatively impacts user experience. Evidence: Users have limited time and expect intuitive navigation; forcing a specific scroll behavior creates friction.
- Inconsistent design details erode user trust. Evidence: Poor grammar, inconsistent casing, or misaligned elements suggest a lack of attention to detail that may extend to the product itself.
- B2B websites can successfully use fun or flashy designs. Evidence: Design can be energetic and joyful as long as it does not obscure the core value proposition or make the site difficult to use.
