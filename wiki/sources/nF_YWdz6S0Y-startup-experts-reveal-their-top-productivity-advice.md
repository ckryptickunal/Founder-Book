# Startup Experts Reveal Their Top Productivity Advice

## Summary

Y Combinator partners discuss the core principles of startup productivity, emphasizing that true productivity is about prioritization, customer focus, and saying 'no' to distractions rather than using complex tools or productivity hacks.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should prioritize customer needs and product development above all else.
- Avoid 'fake work'—tasks that feel productive but do not move the needle on key business metrics.
- The 'Maker vs. Manager' schedule is a critical framework for balancing deep work with administrative tasks.
- Stack ranking tasks is an effective forcing function to ensure focus on the most important goals.
- Productivity is not about tools or life-hacking; it is about the willingness to work hard on the right things.
- Successful founders often avoid 'productivity porn' and focus organically on their business.

## Topics

- [[topics/prioritization|Prioritization]]: The practice of identifying the single most important metric or goal and ruthlessly eliminating tasks that do not contribute to it.
- [[topics/maker-vs-manager-schedule|Maker vs. Manager Schedule]]: A time management framework that separates deep, uninterrupted work (Maker) from meeting-heavy, collaborative work (Manager).
- [[topics/fake-work|Fake Work]]: Activities that feel like work but do not contribute to the startup's primary goals, often used as a distraction from harder, more essential tasks.

## Notable Claims

- Multitasking is ineffective for founders. Evidence: The speakers note that the best founders are hyper-focused on one thing at a time rather than splitting their attention.
- Productivity tools often hinder rather than help. Evidence: Founders often use complex tools as a form of procrastination, similar to 'cleaning your room to avoid homework'.

## Quotes

> Focus is saying no.
> The most important middle model for time management is not what you decide to do, it's what you decide to not do.
> Fake work is one of the most dangerous things to fall into as a startup founder.
