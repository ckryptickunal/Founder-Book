## Summary

Alf, an AI-native FP&A platform, recently secured $29 million in Series B funding led by Kla. Co-founders Albert (CEO) and Santi (CTO) discuss their journey, emphasizing Alf's unique approach of integrating with spreadsheets and delivering rapid time-to-value, contrasting with traditional solutions. They share insights into early customer acquisition through extensive research, maintaining product vision, navigating challenges like prioritization, and building trust through robust security and a dedicated customer success team. Looking ahead, Alf plans to leverage its established "plumbing" to build more advanced AI-driven features, focusing on accuracy and productivity for finance teams, while fostering a low-ego, collaborative, and empathetic company culture.

## Key Ideas

- Alf is an AI-native FP&A platform that centralizes finance data and provides tools for analysis and decision-making.
- The product meets finance professionals where they are by integrating directly with spreadsheets, a unique market differentiator.
- Alf offers significantly faster time-to-value and implementation (days/weeks) compared to industry standards (months).
- Extensive customer research and empathy for the problem (from Albert's own experience) were crucial for early customer acquisition and product development.
- The core product concept and foundational building blocks have remained consistent since inception, avoiding major pivots.
- Building trust with sensitive financial data was paramount, demonstrated by early SOC 2 certification and a dedicated customer success team that ensured client success even with manual effort.
- AI is viewed pragmatically as a means to an end, focusing on practical applications to enhance productivity and accuracy in finance workflows.
- Founders' roles evolve from individual contributors to leaders who amplify team efforts, with new hires significantly impacting company trajectory.
- Startups should focus on internal development and adding customer value rather than being overly distracted by competitors, as most startups fail due to internal issues.

## Notable Claims

- ALF is an AI native FPNA platform that centralizes all finance information and provides tools for analysis. Evidence: Albert's direct statement: 'ALF is an AI native FPNA platform. So what we do is we centralize in one place all of information that finance team needs to make decisions and then we give them some tools to work with these and do analysis.'
- Alf's product integrates directly with spreadsheets, meeting finance people where they are. Evidence: Albert's statement: 'finance people live and breathe spreadsheets. So part of our product is actually within spreadsheets and it's an addin that people can use to work with that data.'
- Alf recently closed a $29 million Series B funding round led by Kla, with participation from all existing investors. Evidence: Albert's statement: 'the main highlight over the last last couple weeks is the announcement of our series B uh 29 million led by Kla participation from all existing investors.'
- Alf offers significantly faster time to value and full implementation (days to weeks) compared to the market standard (3-6 months). Evidence: Albert's statement: 'it doesn't make sense for a tool like a left to take 6 months to implement. But that's the standard today in the market... we are, you know, first time to value within a day or less... and then we're fully implemented in like one, two weeks.'
- Alf achieved SOC 2 certification very early in its existence, even with limited funds. Evidence: Albert's statement: 'We were sock certified when we were like three people in the company... we spent you know 10 of those 100k in like our sock 2 certification.'
- Alf has consistently had product-market fit, with growth being a matter of building out the product. Evidence: Albert's statement: 'we always had product market fit and it was more of a matter of like the amount of product that we needed to build in order to materialize that and to actually like build a business around that early product market fit.'
- AI has huge potential to improve the productivity of Alf's customers in finance. Evidence: Santi's statement: 'AI which I think you know has a huge potential to to really improve the the productivity of our customers.'
