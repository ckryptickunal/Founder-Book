# Build housing in weeks, not years: Fight the California housing crisis with Abodu

## Summary

Abodu is a startup focused on addressing the California housing crisis by providing pre-approved, factory-built accessory dwelling units (ADUs) that can be installed in homeowners' backyards in as little as 12 weeks, significantly faster and cheaper than traditional construction.

## Key Ideas

- ADUs (granny flats) are a politically palatable solution to the housing crisis that benefits homeowners, communities, and cities.
- Factory-built construction allows for predictable timelines, higher quality control, and minimal disruption to homeowners compared to on-site building.
- Pre-approved permitting plans in specific cities (e.g., San Jose, Palo Alto) drastically reduce the time and complexity of the approval process.
- Abodu manages the entire end-to-end process, including permitting, site work, and installation, to reduce homeowner stress.
- The company offers various unit sizes (studio, 1-bedroom, 2-bedroom) to accommodate different family needs or rental income opportunities.

## Topics

- [[topics/california-housing-crisis|California Housing Crisis]]: The severe shortage of affordable housing in California and the legislative efforts to streamline development.
- [[topics/accessory-dwelling-units-adus|Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs)]]: Small, secondary housing units built on the same lot as a primary residence, often used for family or rental income.
- [[topics/prefabricated-construction|Prefabricated Construction]]: Building housing units in a factory setting to improve speed, quality, and predictability.

## Notable Claims

- Abodu can deliver a housing unit in as little as 12 weeks. Evidence: Factory production takes 10 weeks, and pre-approved permitting can be completed in as little as an hour.
- Abodu units are significantly cheaper than traditional mid-rise apartment units. Evidence: Abodu units start at $189,000, compared to approximately $600,000 per unit for mid-rise apartment developments.
- Factory-built units are higher quality than on-site builds. Evidence: Units are built to be more rigid to withstand transportation, with walls 50% thicker than industry standards.
