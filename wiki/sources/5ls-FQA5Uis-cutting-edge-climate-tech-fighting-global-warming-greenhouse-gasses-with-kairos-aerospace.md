## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Ari Gesher, former Palantir employee and current co-founder of Kairos Aerospace, about their mission to reduce methane emissions using advanced imaging technology and the lessons learned from scaling deep tech startups.

## Key Ideas

- Kairos Aerospace uses aerial spectroscopy to detect methane leaks in oil and gas infrastructure, targeting 'super emitters' to significantly reduce anthropogenic warming.
- The company's business model focuses on working with the oil and gas industry rather than being adversarial, providing actionable data to fix leaks efficiently.
- Scaling a deep tech startup requires hitting specific plateaus: building a working sensor, operationalizing data collection, and achieving density in survey areas.
- Campus recruiting can be a powerful engine for growth if approached with a long-term strategy, including building brand presence and moving quickly on top talent.
- Diverse life experiences and non-traditional career paths often provide unique perspectives that lead to better problem-solving in engineering and entrepreneurship.

## Notable Claims

- Shutting down major methane leaks could reduce total anthropogenic warming by 12.5%. Evidence: Based on research regarding the impact of methane emissions on global warming and IPCC targets.
- Kairos Aerospace's environmental impact from detected leaks already exceeds that of all Teslas on the road. Evidence: Internal data comparison between methane reduction and electric vehicle carbon displacement.
