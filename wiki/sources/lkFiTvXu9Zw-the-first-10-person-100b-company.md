# The First 10-person, $100B Company

## Metadata

## Summary

Y Combinator explores the emerging paradigm where AI tools enable small, high-agency teams to build massive, multi-billion dollar companies with minimal capital and headcount, prioritizing revenue per employee over traditional scaling models.

## Key Ideas

- AI tools are the modern equivalent of cloud computing, drastically reducing the capital and labor required to scale.
- The future of high-growth startups is defined by small, high-agency teams.
- Revenue per employee is the primary metric for measuring the efficiency and success of modern startups.
- Small teams avoid the bureaucratic friction, politics, and lack of focus that typically plague large, bloated organizations.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/ai-driven-scaling|AI-Driven Scaling]]: The use of artificial intelligence to automate tasks and reduce the need for large human workforces in high-growth companies.
- [[topics/high-agency-founders|High Agency Founders]]: Founders who demonstrate extreme initiative, speed, and execution capabilities, essential for small-team success.
- [[topics/organizational-efficiency|Organizational Efficiency]]: The shift away from traditional headcount-heavy growth toward lean, high-revenue-per-employee structures.

## Notable Claims

- It is now possible for a 10-person team to build a $100 billion company. Evidence: The evolution of AI tools allows for massive scaling with significantly fewer human resources compared to previous eras.
- Small teams have a competitive advantage over incumbents. Evidence: They avoid the politics, excessive meetings, and lack of focus that slow down large, bloated organizations.

## Quotes

> The best high agency startups of the future will all optimize for one metric, revenue per employee.
> We want to fund these high agency founders to help them build the first 10person hundred billion dollar company.
