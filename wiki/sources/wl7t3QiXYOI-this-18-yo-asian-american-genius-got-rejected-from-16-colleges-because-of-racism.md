# This 18 yo Asian American genius got rejected from 16 colleges because of racism

## Metadata

## Summary

The video discusses a lawsuit filed by Nanzong and his son, Stanley Zong, against the University of California system, alleging systemic racial discrimination against Asian-American applicants. Despite Stanley's exceptional technical qualifications—including being hired by Google at an L4 level—he was rejected by 16 of the 18 colleges to which he applied. The video argues that this case is part of a broader, documented pattern of bias where Asian-American students are held to higher standards, and calls for transparency, third-party oversight, and an end to the 'black box' of college admissions.

## Key Ideas

- High-achieving Asian-American students are frequently rejected by top universities despite exceptional qualifications.
- The current college admissions process is described as a 'black box' that lacks transparency and accountability.
- The lawsuit against the UC system seeks to expose and reform systemic bias that persists despite bans on affirmative action.
- Many students internalize these rejections as personal failures, when they are actually the result of systemic discrimination.
- There is a 'chilling effect' on students who wish to speak out against admissions practices due to hostility on college campuses.
