## Summary

Jeff Clavier and Andrea Zurek, experienced venture investors, discuss the fundamentals of angel investing, portfolio construction, and personal branding for startup investors at Y Combinator's Startup Investor School.

## Key Ideas

- Angel investing is a long-term, high-risk game that typically requires 8-10 years to see significant returns.
- Investors should only invest money they are prepared to lose entirely.
- Portfolio construction involves defining factors like check size, investment pace, sectors, and geography to manage risk.
- Building a personal brand as an investor helps attract high-quality deal flow and establishes trust with founders.
- Investors should aim for high conviction in every deal; 'gateway' deals to build a network are generally not worth the risk of a bad investment.
- Diversification across time and sectors is crucial to avoid being wiped out by market trends or failed business models.

## Topics

- [[topics/portfolio-construction|Portfolio Construction]]: The strategic process of determining check sizes, number of investments, and sector allocation to manage risk and maximize returns.
- [[topics/personal-branding-for-investors|Personal Branding for Investors]]: Developing a reputation and identity that attracts founders and establishes trust within the startup ecosystem.

## Notable Claims

- Most angel investments will take 8 to 10 years to reach a massive outcome. Evidence: Jeff Clavier's 14 years of experience and observation of the industry.
- A brand is what other people say about you when you're not in the room. Evidence: Attributed to Jeff Bezos by Andrea Zurek.
- Only 4 or 5 companies in a 40-company portfolio will typically return the fund. Evidence: Jeff Clavier's analysis of venture capital return distributions.
