## Summary

Artist and UCLA professor Lauren McCarthy discusses her art practice, which explores human interaction, social rituals, and the impact of technology on personal identity. She details projects that use technology to facilitate or manipulate social situations, such as 'Social Triggers' and 'Lauren,' while also highlighting her work on the p5.js creative coding framework.

## Key Ideas

- Art can be used to explore and challenge the limits of personal identity and social norms.
- Technology, including AI and home automation, is fundamentally changing how humans interact and perceive their environment.
- Humor and absurdity are effective tools for making complex, potentially unsettling technological concepts more approachable.
- The 'human-in-the-loop' model, where humans perform tasks behind automated systems, remains a critical area of study in the age of AI.
- Creating inclusive, welcoming spaces in open-source software is as important as the technical functionality of the tools themselves.

## Notable Claims

- Technology can be used to loosen the narrow boxes we place around our identities. Evidence: McCarthy's 'Social Triggers' project, where strangers directed her behavior on dates, allowed her to act outside her normal personality without negative consequences.
- The design of software tools can directly influence the diversity of their user base. Evidence: McCarthy's work on p5.js focuses on documentation and accessible language to ensure the tool is welcoming to non-expert developers.
- Physical objects are more effective than digital apps at changing human behavior in social settings. Evidence: McCarthy notes that physical objects like the 'Conversa Cube' provide a tangible excuse for people to break normal social patterns.
