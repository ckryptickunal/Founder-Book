# Board Members

## Summary

Sam Altman discusses the shifting power dynamics between founders and investors, arguing that while founder control is important, the trend of excluding outsiders from boards is detrimental to long-term company success and operational discipline.

## Key Ideas

- Founders are increasingly excluding investors from board seats to maintain control.
- Experienced board members provide critical value in strategic thinking, executive hiring, and operational discipline.
- Board members offer stability and support during crises that advisors typically do not.
- The ideal early-stage board structure includes a mix of founders, investors, and independent outsiders.
- Bad board members can be disastrous, making thorough reference checks essential.

## Notable Claims

- Companies without outsiders on their boards often lack operational discipline. Evidence: Altman's personal experience observing startup performance and cadence.
- The most impactful companies consistently have excellent board members. Evidence: Historical observation of high-value companies.
