# It's Surprising How Much Small Teams Can Get Done - Sam Chaudhary of ClassDojo

## Summary

Sam Chaudhary, co-founder of ClassDojo, discusses the company's journey from a small startup to a global education platform. He emphasizes the importance of deep user empathy, building a grassroots community, and maintaining a lean, high-performing team. He also addresses the challenges of operating in the sensitive EdTech space, including privacy, communication, and the philosophy of supporting human-centric education systems rather than replacing them with technology.

## Key Ideas

- Education is a human-centric product; technology should serve and empower teachers rather than replace them.
- Small, autonomous teams can achieve significantly more than large, top-down organizations through shared context and empowered execution.
- Growth in education often happens through grassroots, organic adoption by teachers rather than top-down administrative mandates.
- Building a 'community of practice' among users (mentors) is a powerful engine for product growth and improvement.
- Transparency and clear communication, especially regarding data privacy and business models, are critical when serving children and parents.
- Embracing 'emergence'—allowing ideas to bubble up from the team rather than dictating them from the top—leads to better innovation.

## Notable Claims

- Small teams are more effective than large teams due to reduced communication overhead and increased autonomy. Evidence: ClassDojo scaled to 200,000+ schools with a team of only 30 people.
- Education is a human-centric system, not a technical one. Evidence: Success in education depends on the interaction between teachers and learners, not just the software used.
- Grassroots adoption is more effective than top-down sales in education. Evidence: ClassDojo grew organically through teacher-to-teacher recommendations rather than selling to school districts.
