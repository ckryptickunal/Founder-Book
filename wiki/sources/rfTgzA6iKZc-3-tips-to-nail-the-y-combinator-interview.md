## Summary

Garry Tan, former Y Combinator partner, provides strategic advice for startups preparing for the 10-minute YC interview, emphasizing the importance of clear communication, deep knowledge of business metrics, and articulating the potential for long-term market expansion.

## Key Ideas

- The YC interview is a 10-minute conversation focused on three pillars: the problem, the solution, and the potential scale.
- Communication must be simple and direct; explain your business as if to a first-grade teacher.
- Founders must master their unit economics, including gross margins, burn rate, and cohort retention.
- Use the 'inverted pyramid' communication style to answer questions directly before providing supporting details to handle interruptions effectively.
- Startups should focus on a 'thin edge of the wedge'—a small, solvable initial problem that can expand into a massive market.
- Practice with YC alumni or friends, specifically training for interruptions.
