# Mark Zuckerberg On Yahoo's Billion Dollar Offer

## Summary

Mark Zuckerberg discusses the internal turmoil and leadership challenges Facebook faced in 2006 when Yahoo offered to acquire the company for $1 billion, highlighting the importance of mission alignment over short-term financial gain.

## Key Ideas

- The Yahoo acquisition offer was a pivotal moment that forced Zuckerberg to clarify the company's long-term mission.
- Lack of communication regarding the company's mission led to significant internal friction and the departure of the entire management team.
- Turning down the offer was difficult, but the subsequent success of Newsfeed and opening the platform to the public validated the decision.
- Founders must ensure that early employees are aligned with the long-term vision rather than just seeking a financial exit.
