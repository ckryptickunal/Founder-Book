## Summary

David Lieb, co-founder of the startup Bump, recounts his journey from a failed startup venture to spearheading the creation of Google Photos, and how a life-altering leukemia diagnosis shifted his perspective from corporate product leadership to mentoring the next generation of founders at Y Combinator.

## Key Ideas

- Building a product for yourself allows you to trust your own intuition.
- The importance of identifying a problem that genuinely bugs you before building a solution.
- The necessity of looking at quantitative data and retention curves rather than relying solely on user feedback.
- The 'low frequency, low value' trap is the primary cause of startup failure.
- Persistence and risk-taking are essential, even when facing internal corporate resistance.
- A near-death experience can fundamentally reorient one's career priorities toward mentorship and impact.

## Notable Claims

- Google Photos became the fastest-growing product of all time at the time of its growth. Evidence: Reached over a billion users in less than four years.
- The Bump team made classic startup mistakes by raising too much money and hiring too quickly. Evidence: The team burned through $15 million without a clear business model or high-frequency usage.
