# Financial Misstatements

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-financial-misstatements`

- Published: 2015-08-21 16:25:44

## Summary

Sam Altman warns startup founders against the common and dangerous practice of misusing financial terminology when communicating with investors, emphasizing that precision is essential to avoid legal and professional repercussions.

## Key Ideas

- First-time founders often make mistakes due to a lack of financial literacy.
- Misrepresenting financial metrics like GMV as revenue is a serious ethical and legal issue.
- Founders are responsible for ensuring the accuracy of their financial statements.
- Investors expect precise definitions; over-explaining terminology is safer than being vague or inaccurate.

## Notable Claims

- Misrepresenting GMV as revenue or an LOI as a contract is a felony. Evidence: Altman notes that these misrepresentations occur frequently and carry severe legal consequences.

## Quotes

> When in doubt, you will never get in trouble for defining the way you’re using a financial term too precisely.
> I’ve seen people use GMV for revenue or refer to an LOI as a contract many times in the past year when talking to investors. This is a felony.
