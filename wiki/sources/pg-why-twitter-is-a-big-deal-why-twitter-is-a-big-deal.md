# Why Twitter is a Big Deal

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that Twitter's significance lies in its nature as a rare, successful messaging protocol that functions without specified recipients, noting that its lack of early monetization helped it feel like an open standard.

## Key Ideas

- Twitter is fundamentally a new messaging protocol rather than just a social network.
- Successful new protocols are extremely rare in the history of the internet.
- Twitter's lack of aggressive monetization allowed it to feel like an open, decentralized protocol, facilitating its rapid adoption.
- The ownership of a protocol by a private company is an unusual and significant development.

## Notable Claims

- Twitter is a new messaging protocol where recipients are not specified. Evidence: The platform's architecture allows for broadcast-style messaging without pre-selecting a specific audience.
- The founders' slow approach to monetization helped Twitter's growth. Evidence: By not controlling the platform too strictly, it maintained the feel of an open protocol, which encouraged wider adoption.
