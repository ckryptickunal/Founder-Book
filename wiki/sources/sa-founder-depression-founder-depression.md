# Founder Depression

## Summary

Sam Altman discusses the pervasive, often hidden issue of depression among startup founders, driven by the pressure to maintain a facade of constant success and the isolation inherent in leadership roles.

## Key Ideas

- Founders face immense pressure to project optimism and strength, even during crises.
- The responsibility for employees, investors, and families creates a unique, isolating burden.
- Depression is a common, yet rarely discussed, experience among founders.
- Peer support networks, such as Y Combinator, are crucial for mitigating founder isolation.
- Reframing startups as 'just work' rather than life-or-death matters can help reduce psychological pressure.

## Topics

- [[topics/founder-mental-health|Founder Mental Health]]: The psychological toll of running a startup and the prevalence of depression among entrepreneurs.
- [[topics/startup-culture|Startup Culture]]: The societal and professional pressure on founders to maintain a facade of constant growth and success.

## Notable Claims

- Most founders experience periods of depression during their startup journey. Evidence: Altman notes that most founders he knows have had 'seriously dark times'.
- Talking about struggles with peers significantly improves a founder's mental state. Evidence: Altman states that founders are often surprised by how much better they feel when they stop pretending everything is 'crushing it'.

## Quotes

> There is a huge amount of pressure as a founder to never show weakness and to be the cheerleader in all internal and external situations.
> But startups are not life-and-death matters—it’s just work.
> You’ll be surprised how much better you feel just by talking to people about the struggles you’re facing instead of saying 'we’re crushing it'.
