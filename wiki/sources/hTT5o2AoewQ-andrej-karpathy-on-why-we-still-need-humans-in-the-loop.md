# Andrej Karpathy on why we still need humans in the loop

## Summary

Andrej Karpathy discusses the necessity of human oversight in AI development, emphasizing that visual interfaces are superior for auditing AI outputs and that humans remain the ultimate bottleneck in software engineering despite AI's ability to generate code rapidly.

## Key Ideas

- Graphical User Interfaces (GUIs) are essential for auditing AI because they leverage human visual processing.
- Reading raw text is cognitively effortful, whereas visual information is processed more efficiently by the brain.
- AI agents should be kept on a 'leash' to maintain control over system outputs.
- Human oversight is the primary bottleneck in software development, regardless of how much code an AI can generate.

## Entities

- [[entities/andrej-karpathy|Andrej Karpathy]] (person): AI researcher and former Director of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI.

## Topics

- [[topics/human-in-the-loop|Human-in-the-loop]]: The necessity of human intervention and oversight in AI-driven workflows to ensure accuracy and safety.
- [[topics/user-interface-design|User Interface Design]]: The role of visual interfaces in facilitating efficient human interaction with complex AI systems.

## Notable Claims

- Humans are the bottleneck in software development even when AI generates code instantly. Evidence: The need for human verification to ensure code correctness and prevent the introduction of bugs.
- Visual representations are more efficient for auditing AI than text. Evidence: Human visual processing is described as a 'highway to the brain' compared to the effortful nature of reading text.

## Quotes

> We have to keep the AI on the leash.
> I'm still the bottleneck, right? Even though that 10,000 lines come out instantly, I have to make sure that this thing is not introducing bugs.
