# Once you identify the problem and fix it, you can always launch again.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `PHgqbdcWTuk`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-09-22T17:31:19Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PHgqbdcWTuk

## Summary

This video provides a tactical framework for founders whose product launches have failed to gain traction, emphasizing an analytical, iterative approach to diagnosing and fixing growth problems.

## Key Ideas

- Treat a lack of product traction as an analytical problem rather than a personal failure.
- Systematically isolate and tweak variables in the sales and marketing funnel to identify the root cause of low engagement.
- Iterate on messaging, target audience, and outreach methods week by week.
- Successful founders maintain a learning mindset, viewing setbacks as data points for improvement.

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-growth|Startup Growth]]: The process of identifying why a product is not gaining users and systematically adjusting strategy to improve adoption.
- [[topics/iterative-problem-solving|Iterative Problem Solving]]: The methodology of changing one variable at a time to test hypotheses and optimize business outcomes.

## Notable Claims

- Founders can always relaunch a product after identifying and fixing the underlying problem. Evidence: The speaker suggests that by diagnosing drop-off points and tweaking variables like messaging or target audience, founders can dig themselves out of a lack of traction.

## Quotes

> Tweaking those variables one by one is like how you kind of dig yourself out.
