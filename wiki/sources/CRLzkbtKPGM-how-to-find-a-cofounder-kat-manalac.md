## Summary

Kat Manalac, a partner at Y Combinator, provides guidance on finding a co-founder, emphasizing the statistical advantages of having a partner, where successful teams typically meet, and the importance of equal equity splits for long-term commitment.

## Key Ideas

- While solo founders can succeed, the vast majority of top-tier startups have co-founders.
- The most common places to meet co-founders are school (undergrad) and work, as these environments provide context for performance and communication styles.
- YC prefers teams that are well-balanced, capable of both building and selling, and have a history of working together.
- An equal equity split is strongly recommended to ensure long-term commitment and mutual respect over the 8-10 year lifespan of a company.
- Teams that have worked together previously demonstrate higher stability and potential for longevity.

## Notable Claims

- Only 6% of the top 50 YC companies by valuation were started by solo founders. Evidence: Analysis of YC's top 50 companies by valuation.
- The majority of successful co-founding teams met in school or at work. Evidence: Data from YC companies with valuations over $100 million showing 50% met in school and 20% met at work.
