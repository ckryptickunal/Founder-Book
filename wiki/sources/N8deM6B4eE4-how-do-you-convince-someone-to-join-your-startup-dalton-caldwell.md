# How Do You Convince Someone to Join Your Startup? - Dalton Caldwell

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell of Y Combinator explains that the fundamental prerequisite for convincing co-founders, employees, and customers to join a startup is for the founder to be deeply convinced of their own idea. He argues that genuine obsession and belief are necessary to survive the inevitable setbacks of the startup journey.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must be fully convinced of their mission before they can persuade others.
- Obsession with an idea acts as a survival mechanism during difficult times.
- It is equally difficult to succeed with a boring idea as it is with an ambitious one, so founders should choose the ambitious path.
- External validation from side projects can help build the necessary conviction over time.
- If you are not excited about your startup, you are likely to quit when you hit a rough patch.

## Notable Claims

- It is equally hard to succeed in a startup you don't care about as one you do. Evidence: Caldwell argues that the difficulty of building a business is constant regardless of the idea's nature, so one should choose the more ambitious path.
- Teams that do not believe in their mission give up when they hit rough patches. Evidence: Observation of Y Combinator companies shows that deep conviction is the primary factor in surviving pivots, financial issues, and team turnover.
