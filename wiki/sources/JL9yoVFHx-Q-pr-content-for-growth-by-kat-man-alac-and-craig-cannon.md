# PR + Content for Growth by Kat Mañalac and Craig Cannon

## Summary

Kat Mañalac and Craig Cannon from Y Combinator provide a strategic framework for early-stage startup founders on how to approach content marketing and public relations. They emphasize that both should only be pursued after achieving product-market fit, and that founders should treat content as a product and PR as a relationship-building exercise rather than outsourcing it to expensive firms.

## Key Ideas

- Content marketing and PR are not scalable user acquisition strategies and should not replace the core work of building a product people want.
- Content should be treated like a product: identify your target audience, understand where they hang out, and create high-quality, valuable material for them.
- PR should be treated like business development: build long-term relationships with reporters rather than relying on cold outreach or PR firms.
- Founders should be the primary storytellers; outsourcing PR early is generally ineffective and expensive.
- Use 'exclusive' pitches to increase the likelihood of coverage from top-tier publications.
- Avoid 'marketing robot' language; be authentic, succinct, and prepared with clear answers to standard reporter questions.

## Notable Claims

- Press is not a scalable user acquisition strategy. Evidence: It is difficult to control, not reliable long-term, and not the best way to reach earliest users.
- Warm introductions are significantly more effective than cold emails for PR. Evidence: Cold emails have a low response rate, while warm intros can yield responses half the time.
- Asking to see a draft of a press article is a major faux pas. Evidence: Reporters view it as an insult to their journalistic integrity.
