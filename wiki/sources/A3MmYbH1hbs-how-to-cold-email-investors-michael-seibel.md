## Summary

Michael Seibel, CEO of Y Combinator, provides actionable advice for founders on how to effectively cold email investors. He emphasizes brevity, clarity, and the goal of starting a conversation rather than securing an immediate meeting or investment.

## Key Ideas

- Keep cold emails under 60 seconds to read.
- Focus on raw facts (problem, solution, growth, market size, team) rather than narrative.
- Avoid industry jargon; use simple, accessible language.
- Use a professional company email address to build credibility and enable tracking tools.
- Follow standard Silicon Valley deck formats rather than industry-specific styles.
- The goal of the first email is to get a reply, not to secure a meeting or funding.
- Do not push for in-person meetings immediately; let the investor ask for more information.

## Notable Claims

- Investors are more likely to reply to emails that can be read in under 60 seconds. Evidence: Personal experience as an investor receiving high volumes of emails.
- Using a company email address is essential for credibility and tracking. Evidence: Investors use tools like Rapportive or Superhuman to verify sender information.
