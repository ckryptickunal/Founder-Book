# This Is The Next Industry AI Will Disrupt

## Summary

Dominic Vituchi, CEO and founder of Onshore, discusses the impending "tectonic shift" in the accounting industry driven by AI. He argues that traditional accounting firms, particularly the "Big Four," are resistant to adopting AI due to their outdated billable-hour model and senior partners' short-term incentives. Onshore, initially attempting to sell AI software to these firms, pivoted to directly serving corporations, automating tasks like R&D tax credits with significantly higher efficiency. Vituchi predicts a future where accounting firms will have fewer junior staff (replaced by AI agents) and a higher revenue per employee, focusing on high-level expertise and sales. He also suggests a "better Microsoft Excel" as a potential future startup idea, emphasizing the need to reimagine professional workflows currently constrained by general-purpose tools. While AI's technical capabilities are rapidly advancing, he believes mass unemployment in knowledge work is likely 5-7 years away, with an initial phase of acceptance and integration.

## Key Ideas

- The accounting industry is on the cusp of a "tectonic shift" driven by AI, leading to a "post Big Four" world.
- Traditional accounting firms are inherently resistant to AI adoption due to their billable-hour model and the short-term incentives of senior partners.
- AI models, even in their current state, can outperform junior and mid-level accounting staff and are approaching the capabilities of senior technical experts.
- Directly serving corporations with AI-powered accounting solutions (like Onshore) is a more viable path than selling software to resistant accounting firms.
- The future accounting firm will be leaner, with AI handling repetitive tasks, leading to significantly higher revenue per employee and a focus on high-level expertise and sales.
- A "wedge" strategy, focusing on excelling at one specific task before expanding, is crucial for successful AI-driven disruption in professional services.
- There's a need for a reimagined spreadsheet tool that addresses the misuse of Excel in professional services.
- While AI's technical capabilities are rapidly advancing, mass unemployment in knowledge work is likely 5-7 years away, with an initial phase of acceptance and integration.

## Notable Claims

- The accounting industry is facing a 'functional and tectonic shift' towards a 'post big four, post accounting firm world' due to AI. Evidence: AI models are already outperforming junior and mid-level staff, and Onshore's rapid growth by directly serving clients with AI-automated services demonstrates a viable alternative.
- Traditional accounting firms (e.g., Big Four) have not 'earned the right to maintain the reverence' they've been granted. Evidence: Their work often involves repetitive, low-value tasks (like copying data in spreadsheets) and they resist adopting technology that could improve efficiency.
- Large accounting firms are resistant to true AI transformation, despite public claims of investment. Evidence: Examples include Deloitte buying Copilot licenses without effective use, Grant Thornton having minimal software engineering talent, and senior partners prioritizing short-term gains over long-term AI investment.
- Selling software to accounting firms is a 'losing game' because their employees are 'the least motivated group of people on the planet' to automate their own jobs. Evidence: Dominic's two years of failed attempts to sell Onshore's software to firms, where junior staff feared job loss due to automation.
- The accounting industry will make more revenue in 10 years but employ a similar number of people, with revenue per employee significantly increasing. Evidence: AI will automate the 'fat bottom layer' of junior staff, allowing a smaller, more technical workforce to handle higher-value tasks and leverage AI tools, leading to increased efficiency and profitability.
- The contention that software programmers will no longer write code, which sounded crazy nine months ago, is now 'orthodoxy.' Evidence: Observation of trends in the software engineering world, where professional engineers are increasingly embracing AI tools.
- Mass realization that AI can do knowledge work jobs as well or better than humans will occur by the end of this year (2026), but mass unemployment will take 5-7 years. Evidence: Current trends in AI capability and increasing acceptance from customers, but the lag in societal and economic shifts.
