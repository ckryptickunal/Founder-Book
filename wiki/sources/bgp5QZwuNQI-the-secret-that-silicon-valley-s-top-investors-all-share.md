# The Secret That Silicon Valley's Top Investors All Share

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell and Michael Seibel from Y Combinator explain the apparent contradiction between venture capitalists publicly criticizing YC while privately investing heavily in YC-backed companies. They argue that top investors use YC as a high-quality filter to identify promising startups, despite preferring to be the 'first check' themselves to avoid competition and maintain better terms.

## Key Ideas

- Top VC firms frequently invest in YC companies despite public rhetoric suggesting otherwise.
- YC acts as a 'filter' or 'purveyor' that provides VCs with pre-vetted, higher-quality, and more technical deal flow.
- VCs prefer to be the first check to avoid competition, maintain control, and secure lower valuations.
- Seed funds often discourage YC participation because YC companies typically command higher valuations post-demo day, reducing the seed fund's potential ownership stake.
- Founders should prioritize advice from investors who are actually offering to write a check over those who offer unsolicited advice while passing on the investment.
