# Time to Take a Stand

## Metadata

## Summary

Sam Altman argues that the tech industry must take a public stand against the Trump administration's executive orders, specifically the travel ban, warning that silence facilitates the erosion of civil rights and democratic norms.

## Key Ideas

- Tech companies possess significant power and influence and should use it to oppose discriminatory government policies.
- The executive order on immigration is a breach of the social contract with immigrants and a dangerous precedent.
- Employees have leverage to pressure their companies to take a public stance and concrete action.
- It is crucial to avoid alienating Trump voters while resisting the administration's specific policies.
- Individuals should define their own 'line in the sand' to prevent the normalization of unacceptable government actions.

## Notable Claims

- The executive order 'Protecting the Nation From Foreign Terrorist Entry Into the United States' is effectively a Muslim ban. Evidence: Altman characterizes the broad-strokes targeting of a specific religious group as a discriminatory solution.
- Tech employees have the power to force their companies to take political action. Evidence: Altman notes that companies go to great lengths to recruit and retain talent, giving employees significant leverage.
