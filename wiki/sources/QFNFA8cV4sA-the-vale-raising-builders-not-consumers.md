## Summary

Garry Tan interviews author Abigail about her novel 'The Veil,' a story centered on a young coder in Silicon Valley who uses an AI-generated virtual world to save his family from financial ruin. The book explores themes of startup culture, the necessity of resilience in the face of failure, and the ethical implications of AI development.

## Key Ideas

- The 'eight too late great' concept illustrates the cycle of startup failure and the importance of learning from it.
- AI development is a reflection of the creator's values; ethical people are required to build ethical technology.
- The novel serves as a metaphor for how AI models ingest and learn from human stories and data.
- Silicon Valley culture often involves navigating office politics and ensuring one's intellectual contributions are properly credited.
- Early exposure to technology and AI can empower the next generation of builders.
