# Focus on WEALTH, not MONEY

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the fundamental distinction between money and wealth, arguing that money is merely an accounting tool for economic energy, while true wealth lies in the creation and ownership of value-generating systems.

## Key Ideas

- Money is an abstract representation of wealth, not wealth itself.
- Focusing solely on money leads to becoming a replaceable cog in an existing system.
- True value comes from creating and owning the 'engine' of production rather than seeking incremental career advancement.
- Money is essentially digital data in a database, whereas wealth is defined by actual goods and services.

## Notable Claims

- Money is not wealth; it is an accounting of available economic energy. Evidence: Comparison of money to words, which represent meaning but are not the physical world itself.
- Focusing on money leads to becoming a cog in the machine. Evidence: The pursuit of promotions and upward mobility traps individuals in existing systems rather than encouraging the creation of new ones.
