# How Psychedelics will Change Society for the Better | with Dillan DiNardo of Mindstate Design Labs

## Summary

Dillan DiNardo, founder of Mindstate Design Labs, discusses the transition of psychedelics from counterculture stigma to legitimate clinical tools. The conversation highlights how controlled, repeatable psychedelic therapy can treat severe mental health conditions like PTSD and depression by altering brain connectivity and consciousness, while emphasizing the importance of moving away from the 1960s 'moral panic' toward a structured, medicalized framework.

## Key Ideas

- Psychedelics are being successfully deployed in clinical settings to treat treatment-resistant mental health conditions.
- The mechanism of action involves changing brain connectivity patterns, allowing different regions to communicate in novel ways.
- The 1960s counterculture movement and figures like Timothy Leary caused a moral panic that stalled legitimate research for decades.
- Mindstate Design Labs uses a combination of biochemical data and subjective experiential data (from pioneers like Sasha and Ann Shulgin) to map and design specific, therapeutic states of consciousness.
- Psychedelics are not monolithic; different compounds induce vastly different states, such as the memory-focused experience of ibogaine versus the ego-dissolution of 5-MeO-DMT.

## Notable Claims

- 67% of patients with treatment-resistant PTSD no longer qualified for the diagnosis after three MDMA-assisted therapy sessions. Evidence: Cited as a result from long-term nonprofit clinical research.
- Psychedelics can make people 'sane' rather than 'crazy' when used in controlled, clinical environments. Evidence: Supported by emerging clinical data showing efficacy against PTSD and depression.
