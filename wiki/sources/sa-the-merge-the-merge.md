# The Merge

## Summary

Sam Altman argues that the 'merge' between humans and machines is not a future singularity, but a gradual, ongoing process that has already begun through our reliance on algorithms, social media, and AI-driven technology.

## Key Ideas

- The merge is a gradual process of co-evolution rather than a single, sudden event.
- Humans are already integrated with AI through smartphones, search engines, and social media algorithms.
- Attention hacking is a significant societal issue, comparable to a 'sugar epidemic'.
- Superhuman AI and genetic enhancement are inevitable due to scientific advancement and exponential growth in computing power.
- Merging with AI is a preferable survival strategy compared to competing with it for dominance.
- Humanity is the first species to design its own descendants, necessitating careful coordination.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Author of the essay and former leader of OpenAI.

## Topics

- [[topics/technological-singularity|Technological Singularity]]: The hypothetical point where machine intelligence surpasses human intelligence, now viewed by the author as a gradual merge.
- [[topics/co-evolution|Co-evolution]]: The reciprocal relationship where humans improve AI systems, which in turn influence human behavior and biological evolution.
- [[topics/attention-economy|Attention Economy]]: The impact of algorithmic content delivery on human attention spans and psychological well-being.

## Notable Claims

- The merge between humans and machines has already begun. Evidence: Our daily reliance on phones, social media feeds, and search engines to dictate behavior and thought.
- AI breakthroughs are highly correlated with increases in computing power. Evidence: Observations made by the author during his time working at OpenAI.
- Attention hacking will be the 'sugar epidemic' of this generation. Evidence: The author's personal experience with decreased attention span and the addictive nature of dopamine-driven digital feedback loops.

## Quotes

> I believe the merge has already started, and we are a few years in.
> We will be the first species ever to design our own descendants.
> I believe attention hacking is going to be the sugar epidemic of this generation.
