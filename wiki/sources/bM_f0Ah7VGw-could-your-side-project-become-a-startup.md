# Could your side project become a startup?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `bM_f0Ah7VGw`

## Summary

A brief guide from Y Combinator on evaluating the startup potential of a side project, emphasizing the value of deep user engagement over broad, superficial interest.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize building a product that a small group of users loves over one that many users are indifferent toward.
- A single passionate user of a rough prototype is more valuable than a million waitlist signups for a non-existent product.
- A strong indicator of startup potential is when a user changes their behavior significantly because of your product.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The importance of finding a small group of users who find significant value in a product.
- [[topics/startup-validation|Startup Validation]]: Using early user feedback and behavior to determine if a side project is worth scaling.

## Notable Claims

- It is better to have a few people love your product than many people be indifferent to it. Evidence: The assertion that deep user reaction is a stronger indicator of potential than broad, shallow interest.
- A single passionate user of a prototype is more valuable than a million waitlist signups. Evidence: The distinction between actual usage of a functional product versus speculative interest in a non-existent one.

## Quotes

> It's better to make a product that a few people really love than one a lot of people are indifferent towards.
> A single passionate user of a crude prototype that was hacked together in a weekend means so much more than a million sign ups on a wait list for something that doesn't exist yet.
