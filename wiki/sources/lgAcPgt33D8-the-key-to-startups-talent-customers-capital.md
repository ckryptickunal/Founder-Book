# The Key to Startups: Talent, Customers & Capital

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the fundamental mechanics of a successful startup, emphasizing the creation of wealth over mere profit through three interconnected virtuous cycles: talent acquisition, customer advocacy, and capital reinvestment.

## Key Ideas

- Startups should prioritize building 'wealth'—the underlying engine of value—rather than just accumulating money.
- Talent attracts more talent, creating a self-reinforcing cycle of human capital.
- Satisfied customers drive organic growth through word-of-mouth, creating a cycle of user acquisition.
- Capital should be reinvested into the business to accelerate the growth of both talent and customer cycles.
- Product-market fit is the result of successfully integrating these three virtuous cycles.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): CEO and President of Y Combinator, startup investor and advisor.

## Topics

- [[topics/virtuous-cycles|Virtuous Cycles]]: The concept that growth in talent, customers, and capital creates self-reinforcing loops that drive startup success.
- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The state where a startup's product effectively satisfies market demand, fueled by the integration of talent, customers, and capital.

## Notable Claims

- A great startup creates wealth, not just money. Evidence: Wealth is defined as the reinvestment of capital into the engine that generates more value and more money.
- Talent attracts talent. Evidence: High-quality teams create an environment that naturally draws in other high-performing individuals.

## Quotes

> Don't focus on the money, focus on the wealth.
> The more talented your team is, the more likely more talented people will come to work with you.
> When customers and users love you, they tell everyone else who could possibly use you that they should.
