# Relentlessly Resourceful

## Summary

Paul Graham defines the core quality of a successful startup founder as being 'relentlessly resourceful,' contrasting it with being 'hapless' (passive). He argues that this trait is essential for navigating the novel obstacles inherent in startups, distinguishes it from the curiosity required in creative fields, and suggests it is a teachable skill.

## Key Ideas

- The defining trait of a successful startup founder is being 'relentlessly resourceful'.
- Hapless is defined as passive, whereas resourcefulness involves actively overcoming external obstacles.
- Startups require resourcefulness because obstacles are novel and unpredictable, unlike creative fields which require curiosity to overcome internal obstacles.
- The ability to be relentlessly resourceful can often be taught, especially to those previously constrained by rigid authority.
- The number of potential startups is limited by the pool of people who possess or can develop this resourcefulness, not by economic factors.
- Relentless resourcefulness is the tactical method for achieving the goal of 'making something people want'.

## Notable Claims

- The quality of being 'relentlessly resourceful' can be taught to many people. Evidence: The author cites four years of experience observing founders and notes that while not everyone can learn it, many can, especially those breaking free from rigid authority.
- There is no economic upper bound on the number of startups that can exist. Evidence: The author argues there is no limit to the wealth consumers can absorb, making the pool of potential founders the only limiting factor.
