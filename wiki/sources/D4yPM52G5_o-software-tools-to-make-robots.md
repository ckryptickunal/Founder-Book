# Software Tools To Make Robots

## Metadata

- Video ID: `D4yPM52G5_o`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-05-13T05:57:42Z

## Summary

Y Combinator expresses interest in funding startups developing software tools that enable the creation of robots, citing the convergence of foundation models and robotics as a catalyst for a 'ChatGPT moment' in the industry.

## Key Ideas

- Robotics is approaching a breakthrough similar to the rise of generative AI.
- Previous robotics failures were due to high costs, brittleness, and limited environmental adaptability.
- Foundation models provide the missing link for human-level perception and judgment in robots.
- B2B and industrial applications are more immediate and viable than consumer robotics.
- Y Combinator is specifically seeking founders building the infrastructure and software tools to facilitate robot development.

- [[entities/bear-flag-robotics|Bear Flag Robotics]] (company): A company that builds autonomous tractors, acquired by John Deere.
- [[entities/john-deere|John Deere]] (company): A major manufacturer of agricultural machinery.

## Topics

- [[topics/robotics-infrastructure|Robotics Infrastructure]]: The development of software tools and platforms that simplify the creation and deployment of robotic systems.
- [[topics/foundation-models|Foundation Models]]: Large-scale AI models that provide robots with advanced perception and judgment capabilities.
- [[topics/industrial-automation|Industrial Automation]]: The application of robotics to B2B sectors like infrastructure inspection and agriculture.

## Notable Claims

- Robotics is on the verge of a 'ChatGPT moment' due to advancements in foundation models. Evidence: Rapid improvements in AI models are finally enabling human-level perception and judgment in robots.
- B2B and industrial use cases are more immediately addressable than consumer robotics. Evidence: Successful examples like Gecko Robotics and Bear Flag Robotics demonstrate clear market viability in industrial and agricultural sectors.

## Quotes

> Robotics hasn't had its ChatGPT moment yet, but we think it is almost here.
> With the rapid improvements in foundation models, it is finally possible to make robots that have human level perception and judgment.
> We're interested in funding people building software tool to help others make robot.
