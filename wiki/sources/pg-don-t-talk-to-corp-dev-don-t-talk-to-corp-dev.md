# Don't Talk to Corp Dev

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-don-t-talk-to-corp-dev`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: January 2015
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-don-t-talk-to-corp-dev

## Summary

Paul Graham advises startup founders to avoid meetings with Corporate Development (corp dev) teams unless they are actively seeking to sell their company, warning that these interactions are often manipulative, distracting, and detrimental to startup morale and growth.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should only engage with corp dev if they are ready to sell immediately.
- Engaging with corp dev prematurely is a major distraction that undermines founder morale.
- Corp dev professionals are incentivized to acquire companies as cheaply as possible using manipulative tactics.
- Refusing a meeting with corp dev does not offend large companies; it often commands respect.
- The 'first drink' rule (avoiding the first meeting) is the best defense against being drawn into a grueling and potentially demoralizing M&A process.

## Notable Claims

- Talking to corp dev when you aren't ready to sell is a mistake. Evidence: It consumes founder attention, undermines morale, and exposes founders to manipulative negotiation tactics.
- Refusing a meeting with corp dev will not offend the large company. Evidence: Founders of large companies respect focus and often view the refusal as a sign of a serious, ambitious entrepreneur.
- Corp dev tactics are often more aggressive than those of investors. Evidence: The author notes that corp dev professionals are measured solely on acquisition price, leading to tactics like lowballing and last-minute deal renegotiations.

## Quotes

> Do you want to sell your company right now? Not eventually, right now. If not, just don't take the first meeting.
> Conversations with corp dev are the worst sort of distraction, because as well as consuming your attention they undermine your morale.
> The biggest mistake founders make in dealing with corp dev is not doing a bad job of talking to them when they're ready to, but talking to them before they are.
