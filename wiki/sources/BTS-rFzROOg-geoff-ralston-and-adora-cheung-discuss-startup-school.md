# Geoff Ralston and Adora Cheung Discuss Startup School

## Summary

Geoff Ralston and Adora Cheung discuss the 2018 iteration of Y Combinator's Startup School, a free 10-week online course designed to help early-stage founders globally. The program emphasizes community, mentorship, and practical curriculum to lower the barriers to starting a company, offering participants access to advisors, exclusive deals, and a chance at a $10,000 equity-free grant.

## Key Ideas

- Startup School functions as a MOOC (Massively Open Online Course) to democratize access to startup knowledge.
- The program provides a 'forcing function' through weekly advisor meetings and peer groups to keep founders focused and accountable.
- The curriculum covers the full startup lifecycle, including product-market fit, growth, fundraising, and the psychological challenges of entrepreneurship.
- Participants can choose to audit the course or apply for the advisor-led track, which includes exclusive perks and eligibility for grants.
- The program aims to help founders improve their startups, which in turn increases their chances of getting into the core Y Combinator program.
- The course is designed to be accessible to founders regardless of their location, reducing the necessity of being in Silicon Valley.

## Notable Claims

- Startup School significantly increases the likelihood of a startup's success. Evidence: The program provides a forcing function through group accountability and access to high-quality mentorship.
- The program is effective at preparing companies for the core YC accelerator. Evidence: 38 companies from the previous Startup School batch were accepted into the core Y Combinator program.
- The $10,000 grant is intended to prevent promising startups from failing due to lack of capital. Evidence: The grant is awarded to 100 companies based on progress and advisor feedback to help them reach viability.
