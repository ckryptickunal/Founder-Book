# The Patent Pledge

## Summary

Paul Graham proposes a voluntary 'Patent Pledge' for technology companies to refrain from using software patents against small startups (fewer than 25 employees) to curb anti-competitive litigation and foster innovation.

## Key Ideas

- Patent reform is slow through government channels, so private, downstream solutions are necessary.
- Established companies using patents to suppress small, innovative competitors is a major threat to progress.
- A public pledge creates social and reputational pressure, making companies that litigate against startups stand out negatively.
- The pledge is not legally binding but acts as a moral standard similar to Google's 'Don't be evil'.
- Top talent and investors are attracted to companies that compete through product quality rather than litigation.

## Notable Claims

- Companies that use patents to sue startups are more damaging to innovation than patent trolls. Evidence: Patent trolls are parasites, but companies suing startups do so with the explicit goal of removing competing products from the market.
- The most productive employees prefer to work for companies that hold themselves to higher standards than the law requires. Evidence: Observation of talent attraction patterns in the technology industry.
