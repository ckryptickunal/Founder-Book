# Tim Brady - How Much Equity Should I Give My First Employees?

## Summary

Tim Brady, a veteran of Silicon Valley, provides practical guidance on how startup founders should approach equity distribution for early employees, emphasizing that it is an art rather than a science based on risk, effort, and the company's growth stage.

## Key Ideas

- Early employees deserve more equity due to higher personal risk and the chaotic nature of early-stage startups.
- Startups should typically reserve 10-20% of total equity for an employee option pool.
- Equity distribution should be planned strategically based on future hiring needs, such as executive roles.
- The first employee typically receives between 1% and 2% equity.
- Equity should be viewed as a tool to incentivize success and foster ownership rather than a fixed pie to be divided.
- Founders should balance cash salary versus equity based on the candidate's risk tolerance and the company's current cash position.
