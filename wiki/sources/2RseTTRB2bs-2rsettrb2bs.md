## Summary

Kaarel Kotkas, founder and CEO of Veriff, discusses the evolution of his identity verification platform from a startup built in Estonia to a global trust infrastructure provider. The conversation covers his early challenges with cap tables and Y Combinator rejection, the company's growth during the crypto boom and COVID-19, and the current arms race against AI-driven fraud and deepfakes.

## Key Ideas

- Identity verification is shifting from a compliance checkbox to a critical tool for fighting fraud and building trust.
- Effective identity verification requires more than just static images; it needs thousands of data points, including behavioral analysis and device telemetry.
- A 'trust infrastructure' should answer three questions: Is the person who they claim to be? Are they trusted? Is the same trusted person still behind the account?
- Founders should focus on solving a specific, high-conviction problem rather than chasing trending technologies like AI or blockchain.
- The future of identity is portable trust, where a user's history of good behavior across platforms (Uber, Airbnb, etc.) creates a stronger identity than government documents alone.
