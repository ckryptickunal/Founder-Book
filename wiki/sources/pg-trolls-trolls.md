## Summary

Paul Graham explores the nature of online 'trolling,' identifying its causes—distance, social discomfort, incompetence, and community culture—and discusses the strategies used by Hacker News (News.YC) to maintain high-quality discourse.

## Key Ideas

- Trolling has evolved from deliberate provocation to general antisocial behavior.
- Distance and anonymity in digital communication reduce empathy and social inhibition.
- Technical communities often struggle with trolling due to the social awkwardness of their members.
- Trolling is often a manifestation of incompetence, where insults replace substantive argument.
- Gresham's Law applies to online forums: trolls drive out thoughtful users, leading to a decline in community quality.
- Community moderation and reputation systems (like karma) are effective tools for maintaining civil discourse.

## Notable Claims

- Trolling is often a result of incompetence. Evidence: It is easier to insult someone than to formulate a logical, substantive disagreement.
- Reputation systems (karma) discourage trolling. Evidence: Users often delete rude comments when they see their reputation score decrease.
- Gresham's Law applies to online forums. Evidence: Thoughtful users leave forums dominated by trolls, while trolls are indifferent to the presence of thoughtful users.
