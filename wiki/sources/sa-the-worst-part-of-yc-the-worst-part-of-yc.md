# The Worst Part of YC

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-the-worst-part-of-yc`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2014-04-16 01:29:16
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-the-worst-part-of-yc

## Summary

Sam Altman discusses the emotional difficulty of rejecting startup applications for YC, acknowledges the limitations of the selection process, and encourages founders to persist despite rejection.

## Key Ideas

- Rejecting startup founders is the most difficult aspect of managing YC.
- YC's selection process is imperfect and prone to missing high-potential companies.
- The quality of startup applications is consistently increasing over time.
- Persistence is crucial for founders, regardless of YC's funding decision.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Former President of Y Combinator and author of the essay.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-funding|Startup Funding]]: The process and challenges of selecting early-stage companies for investment.
- [[topics/founder-resilience|Founder Resilience]]: The importance of continuing to build a business despite rejection from investors.

## Notable Claims

- The average quality of startup applications is increasing. Evidence: Partners at YC independently noted a significant rise in quality compared to previous batches.
- YC's rejection does not mean a startup will fail. Evidence: The best startups often look unpromising at the early stage, and YC admits to making mistakes in their selection.

## Quotes

> The worst part of our job at YC is rejecting companies.
> The best startups often look bad at this stage, and we make mistakes.
> If you’re working on something that users love, you like working on it, and you have a plan for how to build a business around it, then please don’t let us deter you.
