# Dylan Field describes design as “art as it applies to problem solving.”

## Metadata

- Video ID: `b_NdMJHX_aY`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-03-20T17:49:30Z

## Summary

Dylan Field discusses the current limitations of AI models in design, defining design as 'art as it applies to problem solving' and noting the current disconnect between generative art models and logical problem-solving LLMs.

## Key Ideas

- Design requires a synthesis of artistic capability and logical problem-solving.
- Current AI models struggle with design because they treat art (diffusion) and problem-solving (LLMs) as separate, unintegrated domains.
- Effective design relies on deep user context and research, which goes beyond simple prompt-based problem statements.
- Design involves understanding human needs, emotions, and first principles rather than just executing tasks.

## Notable Claims

- AI models are currently poor at design because they lack the integration of artistic diffusion and logical reasoning. Evidence: The speaker notes that diffusion models handle the art side while LLMs handle the logic, but they have not been successfully married for design tasks.
- Design is fundamentally about deep user research and context, not just solving a simple prompt. Evidence: The speaker emphasizes that designers must understand user feelings and needs from first principles.

## Quotes

> Design is art as it applies to problem solving.
> On the art side you've got diffusion. On the problem solving side, you've got LLMs. And it's not clear that people have figured out how to marry techniques together yet.
