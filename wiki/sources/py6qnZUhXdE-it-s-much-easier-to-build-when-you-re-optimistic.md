# It’s much easier to build when you’re optimistic.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `py6qnZUhXdE`
- Channel: Y Combinator

## Summary

A short discussion from Y Combinator emphasizing that optimism is a necessary trait for effective builders and change agents, contrasting it with the unproductive cynicism often perceived as 'cool' or 'edgy'.

## Key Ideas

- Optimism is a functional requirement for solving difficult problems.
- Cynicism and snark are often used as a shield to avoid the effort of building.
- True change agents and revolutionaries are typically driven by the belief that their efforts can yield results.
- Building the future requires an investment of self that is only possible through an optimistic outlook.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/optimism-in-entrepreneurship|Optimism in Entrepreneurship]]: The psychological necessity of believing in the possibility of success to sustain the effort required for innovation.
- [[topics/cynicism-vs-productivity|Cynicism vs. Productivity]]: The critique of performative negativity as a barrier to meaningful contribution and problem-solving.

## Notable Claims

- It is more fruitful to be optimistic than cynical when building things. Evidence: Optimism allows individuals to invest the necessary effort to solve problems, whereas cynicism leads to inaction.
- Historical change agents were default optimistic. Evidence: They had to believe that their efforts were not a waste of time in order to commit to their causes.

## Quotes

> It’s much easier and more fruitful to be optimistic and think about all the things you can do.
> If you really care about those problems you have to be optimistic to try to solve them.
> None of your heroes were the cool snarky folks who never did anything and just bitched about how the world was.
