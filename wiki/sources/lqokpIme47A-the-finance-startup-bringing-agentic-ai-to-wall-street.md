## Summary

Arie and Chaz Englander, serial entrepreneurs and Y Combinator alumni, discuss their latest venture, Model ML, an agentic AI workspace designed for financial services. The company provides a suite of tools that automate manual tasks like data gathering and document creation by integrating with internal and external data sources, effectively acting as an AI-powered 'brain' for financial analysts.

## Key Ideas

- Model ML functions as an agentic AI workspace that mirrors a human's digital access to files, emails, and CRM systems to automate repetitive financial tasks.
- The financial sector has shifted from 'testing' AI in proof-of-concepts to 'using' it in production, leading to significant contract growth.
- Founders emphasize the importance of perseverance, customer-centricity, and hiring people who genuinely enjoy their work.
- The founders advocate for 'first principles' thinking and moving fast, often unlearning corporate habits when transitioning to a startup environment.
- Building a company is a high-stakes, difficult journey that requires extreme dedication, often involving seven-day work weeks in the early stages.
- The founders highlight the unique value of the Y Combinator network and the San Francisco ecosystem for ambitious startups.

## Notable Claims

- Model ML's AI models are already more accurate than humans at certain data gathering and presentation tasks. Evidence: The founders observe that models can pull from multiple data sets simultaneously, reducing human error in financial reporting.
- The financial sector has moved from testing AI to full-scale adoption in the last 12 months. Evidence: The founders report a massive increase in contract signings compared to previous quarters.
- Working in a startup requires unlearning corporate habits like slow, slide-heavy decision-making. Evidence: The founders note that new hires from finance often need to shift to a faster, 'first principles' approach.
