## Summary

Kevin Song, founder of withco, discusses the systemic challenges small business owners face regarding commercial real estate and how his company provides a 'lease-to-own' model to help entrepreneurs achieve property ownership, thereby stabilizing communities and preventing displacement.

## Key Ideas

- Small businesses often face displacement when they become successful because rising property values lead to rent hikes they cannot afford.
- Traditional commercial leases are often counterproductive for long-term business growth, while traditional mortgages are inaccessible to many small business owners.
- withco offers a lease-to-own model where a portion of rent payments acts as a down payment credit, allowing businesses to eventually purchase their buildings.
- Data-driven underwriting allows withco to assess the financial health of small businesses more accurately than traditional banks, opening access to credit and ownership.
- Small business ownership is a cornerstone of community stability and economic health.

## Notable Claims

- Small business owners often displace themselves by being successful. Evidence: Success leads to increased neighborhood property values, which results in higher rents that the original business can no longer afford.
- Traditional mortgages and leases do not have product-market fit for most small businesses. Evidence: Leases are short-term and counterproductive to growth, while mortgages are often inaccessible due to rigid bank criteria.
