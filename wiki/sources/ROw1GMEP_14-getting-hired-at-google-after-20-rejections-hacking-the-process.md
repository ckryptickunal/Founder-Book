# Getting hired at Google after 20 rejections — Hacking the process

## Metadata

- Video ID: `ROw1GMEP_14`

## Summary

Michael Brown shares his journey of transitioning from a failed business owner to a Google employee by bypassing traditional hiring channels. After facing 20+ rejections through standard applications, he secured a low-level contracting role and used a proactive, data-driven 'owner' mindset to demonstrate value, eventually earning a promotion to the corporate headquarters.

## Key Ideas

- When the front door (standard application process) is locked, look for a window (alternative entry points like contracting).
- Adopting an 'owner' mindset involves identifying problems and providing solutions even when not explicitly asked.
- Data-driven contributions are highly valued in meritocratic corporate cultures like Google's.
- Persistence and proactive work can overcome a lack of traditional credentials or resume gaps.
- Large organizations are composed of individuals; getting your work in front of the right person is key to advancement.

## Notable Claims

- Standard job application portals are often ineffective for candidates without 'central casting' backgrounds. Evidence: Michael Brown applied to 20-30 jobs at Google and received only rejections before changing his strategy.
- Proactive, unsolicited work can lead to internal recognition. Evidence: Michael performed time-and-motion studies and process optimizations while working as a grocery picker, which eventually led to a meeting with management.
