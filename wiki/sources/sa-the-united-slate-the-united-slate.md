# The United Slate

## Summary

Sam Altman announces the launch of 'The United Slate,' an initiative to support political candidates and ballot measures in the 2018 California elections, centered on technological progress, economic fairness, and housing affordability.

## Key Ideas

- The need for a new social contract to address the impacts of the automation revolution.
- Addressing California's housing crisis through ballot initiatives.
- Promoting prosperity through a combination of technological growth and economic equity.
- Moving away from stagnant political strategies toward new, experimental policy ideas.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Entrepreneur, investor, and author of the essay.
- [[entities/the-united-slate|The United Slate]] (project): A political initiative focused on supporting candidates and ballot measures in California.

## Topics

- [[topics/automation-revolution|Automation Revolution]]: The belief that technological shifts will be as transformative as the industrial revolution, requiring a new social contract.
- [[topics/affordable-housing|Affordable Housing]]: A critical issue in California identified as a priority for legislative and ballot-based reform.
- [[topics/economic-policy|Economic Policy]]: Focusing on wealth inequality, economic growth, and the cost of living.

## Notable Claims

- The automation revolution will be as significant as the agricultural or industrial revolutions. Evidence: Historical comparison to previous major economic and technological shifts.
- Current political and economic strategies in California are failing to address wealth inequality and cost of living. Evidence: Observation of spiraling cost of living and stagnant economic growth for the average person.

## Quotes

> I believe in creating prosperity through technology, economic fairness, and maintaining personal liberty.
> We need to figure out a new social contract, and to ensure that everyone benefits from the coming changes.
