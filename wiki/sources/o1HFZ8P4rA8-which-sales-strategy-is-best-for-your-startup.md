# Which Sales Strategy Is Best For Your Startup?

## Summary

Pete Koomen, Y Combinator visiting group partner and Optimizely co-founder, explains the two primary sales strategies for B2B startups: top-down (selling to executives) and bottom-up (product-led growth). He details the mechanics, advantages, and drawbacks of each, emphasizing that the choice depends on whether the product solves problems for individual contributors or corporate leadership.

## Key Ideas

- Top-down sales target executives to solve strategic problems, often resulting in higher retention but requiring expensive sales teams.
- Bottom-up sales rely on viral adoption by individual users, allowing for more efficient sales cycles once a critical mass is reached.
- Top-down sales have a high floor price (typically $10k-$100k) due to the cost of maintaining an enterprise sales force.
- Bottom-up strategies require extreme focus on reducing friction, optimizing onboarding, and using freemium models to drive adoption.
- Neither strategy is inherently superior; the choice should be dictated by who experiences the pain point the product solves.

## Notable Claims

- Startups that start with top-down sales often have better early retention metrics. Evidence: Observation of startup performance data by the speaker.
- Scaling an enterprise sales team creates a floor price for products. Evidence: The cost of maintaining a sales force necessitates a minimum contract value of $10k-$100k to remain profitable.
- Bottom-up sales still require a sales team. Evidence: Even viral products like Slack employ large sales teams to convert existing users into enterprise contracts.
