# You can only learn by DOING

## Summary

Garry Tan argues that practical experience through building projects is superior to formal credentials, emphasizing that the act of creating and iterating is the most effective way to learn and achieve professional success.

## Key Ideas

- Learning is best achieved through the process of 'doing' rather than theoretical study.
- Initial projects do not need to be the final business idea; they serve as a training ground for building skills.
- A portfolio of impressive, functional work is more valuable to founders and employers than academic credentials like a Stanford degree.
- Iteration is the key to turning a simple starting project into a high-quality, successful product.

## Notable Claims

- Creating a product that users love is more valuable than having a prestigious degree. Evidence: The speaker notes that top-tier founders often start with simple projects like gradebook software to learn the craft before building billion-dollar companies.
- Building a portfolio of work sets an individual in the top 10% of their field. Evidence: The speaker asserts that a beautiful app or elegant code serves as proof of competence that bypasses the need for traditional credentials.
