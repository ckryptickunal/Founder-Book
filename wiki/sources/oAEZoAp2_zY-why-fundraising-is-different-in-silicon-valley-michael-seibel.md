# Why Fundraising Is Different In Silicon Valley - Michael Seibel

## Summary

Michael Seibel explains why fundraising dynamics differ significantly between Silicon Valley and other regions, noting that Bay Area investors are more open to early-stage ideas because they have historically missed out on successful companies, whereas investors in less active ecosystems often rely on a track record of being 'right' by saying no.

## Key Ideas

- Investors in the Bay Area are conditioned to dig deeper because they have previously rejected companies that became successful.
- Investors outside of major startup hubs often have a higher 'no' rate that reinforces a pattern of dismissing ideas prematurely.
- Evaluating a team's execution capability is a more reliable metric for success than judging the quality of an idea.
- Founders discouraged by local fundraising should not necessarily view it as a failure of their business idea, but rather a limitation of their local investor ecosystem.

## Topics

- [[topics/fundraising|Fundraising]]: The process of securing capital and the psychological differences in investor behavior across different geographic regions.
- [[topics/investor-psychology|Investor Psychology]]: How past experiences and deal flow volume influence an investor's willingness to take risks on new companies.
- [[topics/execution-vs-idea|Execution vs. Idea]]: The shift in focus from evaluating the novelty of an idea to evaluating the team's ability to execute.

## Notable Claims

- Bay Area investors are more likely to give founders the benefit of the doubt than investors in smaller startup communities. Evidence: Because Bay Area investors have historically missed out on successful companies after saying no, they are more cautious about dismissing new ideas.
- Evaluating a team's execution is more effective than evaluating the quality of an idea. Evidence: Seibel notes that after reviewing hundreds of thousands of YC applications, his ability to judge execution is significantly sharper than his ability to judge the inherent quality of an idea.

## Quotes

> I much rather use the is this team executing bar to choose companies than the idea bar.
