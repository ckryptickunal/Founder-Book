# How to Convert Between Wealth and Income Tax

- Published: Unknown

## Summary

Paul Graham explains the mathematical equivalence between wealth taxes and income taxes, arguing that a 1% wealth tax is effectively a 20% income tax based on a 5% risk-free rate of return. He contends that politicians often fail to grasp this relationship, leading them to underestimate the severity of proposed wealth tax policies.

## Key Ideas

- A 1% wealth tax is mathematically equivalent to a 20% income tax when assuming a 5% rate of return.
- The conversion between wealth and income tax is calculated by dividing the wealth tax rate by the rate of return on capital.
- Wealth taxes should be compared using the risk-free rate of return because the tax liability is certain.
- Politicians often frame wealth taxes as 'mere' percentages without realizing they represent significant increases in total tax burden.
- Adding a 1% wealth tax could push total marginal tax rates in some US states to levels exceeding the highest in the world.
