## Summary

Y Combinator explores the opportunity for an 'AI-native' product management tool that mirrors the efficiency of coding agents like Cursor, focusing on the discovery and decision-making phase of product development rather than just implementation.

## Key Ideas

- Coding agents have revolutionized implementation, but product discovery remains fragmented.
- Product management involves synthesizing user feedback, market data, and problem prioritization.
- Current PM artifacts (PRDs, Jira, Figma) are designed for human communication, not AI-driven workflows.
- There is a need for a system that ingests raw data (interviews, usage logs) to generate actionable feature specs and technical requirements.
- Future product development will shift toward defining intent for AI agents rather than writing code manually.

## Notable Claims

- Writing code is only a fraction of building a successful product. Evidence: The most critical phase is identifying the right problems to solve through user feedback and market analysis.
- Current product management artifacts are optimized for human engineers, not AI. Evidence: Artifacts like Jira tickets and Figma mocks are designed to communicate intent to humans, creating a bottleneck for AI-driven development.
