# Please Fund More Science

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-please-fund-more-science`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2020-03-30 17:46:36
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-please-fund-more-science

## Summary

Sam Altman argues that the COVID-19 pandemic requires a massive increase in scientific funding and private sector support to achieve a return to normalcy through vaccines, treatments, or robust public health infrastructure.

## Key Ideas

- There are three primary paths to ending the pandemic: vaccines, effective treatments, or comprehensive testing and tracing.
- The federal government is currently underfunding critical R&D efforts.
- The biotech and research sectors are operating at unprecedented speeds, demonstrating the power of focused innovation.
- Private investors and philanthropists must step in to provide the capital and connections scientists need to solve the crisis.
- Investing in pandemic defense is a necessary shift for long-term global stability.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Entrepreneur, investor, and author of the essay.

## Topics

- [[topics/scientific-funding|Scientific Funding]]: The necessity of increasing public and private investment in R&D to address global health crises.
- [[topics/pandemic-response|Pandemic Response]]: The strategies required to mitigate the impact of COVID-19 and return to societal normalcy.
- [[topics/innovation-speed|Innovation Speed]]: The observation that focused, high-stakes environments can accelerate scientific research by over 10x.

## Notable Claims

- The speed of biotech and research labs has increased by more than 10x during the pandemic. Evidence: Altman's observation of the unprecedented ferocity and focus of the scientific community.
- The world will be permanently changed until we develop the ability to defend against new viruses quickly. Evidence: The assertion that this is a 'before-and-after' moment in global history.

## Quotes

> Scientists can get us out of this. What they need are money and connections.
> It’s the best of the spirit of innovation, and it’s inspiring to see what these companies and research labs are doing.
> Please consider shifting some of your focus and capital to scientific efforts addressing the pandemic.
