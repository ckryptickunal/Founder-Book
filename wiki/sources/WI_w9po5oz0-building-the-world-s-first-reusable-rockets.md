# Building The World's First Reusable Rockets

## Summary

The founders discuss the foundational motivation behind starting a company to develop the world's first reusable rockets, driven by the industry's wasteful 'expendable' model and a personal conviction that the technology was necessary for the future.

## Key Ideas

- The aerospace industry historically relies on expendable rockets that are lost after a single launch.
- Rapid reusability is the key to making space travel economically viable and frequent.
- The decision to start a deep-tech company from scratch is an immense personal and professional risk.
- Foundational conviction is necessary to justify the 'irresponsible' nature of launching a high-stakes startup.

## Entities

## Topics

- [[topics/rocket-reusability|Rocket Reusability]]: The engineering and economic challenge of creating vehicles that can return to Earth and be relaunched repeatedly.
- [[topics/entrepreneurial-conviction|Entrepreneurial Conviction]]: The psychological process of committing to a high-risk startup idea despite the potential for failure.

## Notable Claims

- Current industry standards treat rockets as single-use vehicles that are lost in the ocean after launch. Evidence: The speakers contrast the proposed reusable model with the existing industry practice of expendable rockets.
