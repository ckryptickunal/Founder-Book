## Summary

Garry Tan provides career advice centered on the framework of 'learning' and 'earning.' He argues that individuals should prioritize learning early in their careers to build skills, eventually transitioning to roles that offer equity or high compensation. He warns against the 'hedonic treadmill' of high-paying but meaningless jobs and advises quitting roles that offer neither growth nor financial reward.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize learning early in your career to build foundational skills.
- The best career path involves both learning and earning; if you have neither, it is time to leave.
- True wealth is best created through equity ownership in a company.
- Avoid the 'hedonic treadmill' where high income is spent on lifestyle inflation rather than investing in future ventures.
- Meaningless, high-paying jobs can be traps that prevent personal growth and fulfillment.
- Creating a tangible product or project is more valuable than traditional credentials.
