# Urska Srsen Speaks at Y Combinator Female Founder Conference 2016

## Summary

Urska Srsen, co-founder of Bellabeat, shares her journey from an art student in Finland to building a successful health-tech hardware company. She discusses the challenges of being a non-technical founder, the importance of moving to Silicon Valley for growth, the necessity of an 'underdog mentality,' and the commitment required to scale a hardware startup.

## Key Ideas

- Founding a company is not about being qualified; it is about following your passion and learning through observation.
- Silicon Valley provides essential access to capital, talent, and a culture of successful role models that are often lacking in Europe.
- Hardware startups require founders to be physically present on the factory floor to ensure successful shipping.
- An 'underdog mentality'—keeping your mouth shut and eyes open—is a powerful tool for learning in a new environment.
- Commitment means prioritizing the company's interests over personal comfort, even when it requires moving to new countries or making difficult personnel decisions.
- A clear vision acts as the 'glue' that holds a company together during periods of rapid growth and uncertainty.
