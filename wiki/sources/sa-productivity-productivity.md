## Summary

Sam Altman outlines his personal productivity philosophy, emphasizing that working on the right problems is more important than optimizing efficiency. He covers prioritization strategies, physical health habits, and the importance of momentum and delegation.

## Key Ideas

- Productivity is meaningless if applied to the wrong tasks; choosing the right work is the most critical step.
- Compound growth applies to career productivity; small daily gains lead to massive long-term advantages.
- Prioritization should focus on momentum, using simple paper lists to manage tasks and reduce mental load.
- Physical health—specifically sleep, heavy weightlifting, and tailored nutrition—is a foundational pillar for sustained high performance.
- Avoid 'productivity porn'—the trap of optimizing systems rather than focusing on high-impact goals.
- Delegation should be based on matching tasks to people who actually enjoy and excel at them.
