# How to build the next great startup with remote work, with Andreas Klinger (fmr CTO of Product Hunt)

## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Andreas Klinger, former CTO of Product Hunt and investor, about the evolution of remote work, the shift toward global digital knowledge work, and the strategic advantages of building distributed, asynchronous teams.

## Key Ideas

- Remote work is a spectrum defined by time zone overlap and proximity to company hubs.
- Asynchronous communication is superior for deep thinking and decision-making compared to constant meetings.
- The 'golden age' of digital knowledge work allows talent to compete globally regardless of location.
- Startups should focus on building products with zero marginal cost rather than selling time as an agency.
- The future of work is 'online work,' with remote-first practices becoming the standard for all tech companies.
- Successful remote teams often leverage international expertise to solve niche, high-impact problems.

- [[topics/career-development|Career Development]]: The importance of building products, equity, and personal brands over selling hourly labor.
- [[topics/investment-philosophy|Investment Philosophy]]: Focusing on 'firsts' and secondary effects of remote work rather than just communication tools.

## Notable Claims

- Writing is essential for high-quality decision-making. Evidence: Asynchronous writing forces deeper thought compared to 'shooting from the hip' in live calls.
- Silicon Valley is no longer a physical place but a network. Evidence: Talent and capital are increasingly distributed globally, and top-tier programs like YC are moving to remote cohorts.
- Startups often overpay for local talent while ignoring global experts. Evidence: Klinger notes that highly effective developers exist globally who are often overlooked by SF-centric hiring biases.
