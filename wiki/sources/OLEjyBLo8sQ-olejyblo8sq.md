## Summary

Ian Fischer, co-founder and co-CEO of Poetic, discusses his company's recent breakthrough in AI performance on the ARC-AGI benchmark. By utilizing a recursive self-improvement loop on top of existing models like Gemini 3, Poetic achieved a 54% success rate on the ARC-AGI private test set, significantly outperforming the base models while maintaining cost efficiency. The team, consisting of six former DeepMind researchers, focuses on automating prompt engineering and agent development to create a 'factory' for AI optimization rather than manual engineering.

## Key Ideas

- Recursive self-improvement (RSI) is a viable and powerful path toward AGI.
- Poetic's system acts as an automated 'factory' for prompt engineering and agent optimization, replacing manual trial-and-error.
- The system uses an ensemble approach where independent model instances are refined and combined via a voting scheme.
- Poetic achieved 54% on ARC-AGI using Gemini 3, outperforming the model's native performance at half the cost of the Gemini 3 Deep Think variant.
- The technology is model-agnostic, showing similar performance gains when applied to Anthropic's Opus model.
- The company is shifting from pure research to commercial applications, aiming to solve real-world business problems.

## Notable Claims

- Poetic's system achieved 54% on the ARC-AGI private test set. Evidence: Reported by Ian Fischer as a significant increase over the base Gemini 3 performance of ~31%.
- Poetic's approach is more cost-effective than Gemini 3 Deep Think. Evidence: Fischer claims a 9-10 percentage point improvement in accuracy at half the cost of the Deep Think model.
- The system is model-agnostic. Evidence: Fischer states they were able to swap Gemini 3 for Anthropic's Opus and achieve similar performance gains.
