# How to Spend Your 20s in the AI Era

## Summary

In this live episode of The Light Cone, Y Combinator partners discuss the evolving landscape of career paths and entrepreneurship in the AI era. They argue that traditional 'credential-maxing' (like seeking stable corporate jobs) is becoming obsolete, while the ability to build, exercise agency, and solve real-world problems using AI tools is becoming the new standard for success. They emphasize that the current environment offers unprecedented speed for startups, favoring those who can find niche markets and build deep domain expertise quickly.

## Key Ideas

- The traditional 'safe' career path (e.g., corporate engineering roles) is increasingly risky due to AI's ability to perform routine tasks.
- Entrepreneurship in the AI era allows for hyper-growth, with some startups reaching significant revenue in months rather than years.
- Domain expertise can be acquired rapidly by 'going undercover' or embedding oneself in specific industries (e.g., dentistry, logistics).
- The most successful startups often start as highly niche products before expanding into broader markets.
- Founders should avoid 'credential-maxing' (seeking external validation like VC funding or social media status) and focus on building utility and real business value.
- AI is shifting the competitive advantage from pure software development to a combination of domain knowledge and technical engineering skill.
