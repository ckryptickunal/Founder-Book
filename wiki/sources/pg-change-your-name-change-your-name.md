# Change Your Name

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-change-your-name`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: August 2015
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-change-your-name

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that US startups without the .com domain for their name should change it, as a marginal domain signals weakness and lack of authority. He posits that founder attachment to names is a psychological bias rather than a strategic necessity and encourages founders to outsource naming or adopt a more pragmatic approach to domain acquisition.

## Key Ideas

- A .com domain is a signal of strength and legitimacy for a startup.
- Founder attachment to a company name is often a result of identity bias rather than the name's intrinsic value.
- Naming is a distinct skill separate from the core competencies required to build a successful startup.
- Most startups can find a suitable, available .com domain if they approach the task with discipline and objectivity.
- Data shows a strong correlation between top-performing YC companies and the ownership of their .com domain.

## Notable Claims

- Not having the .com of your name signals weakness to the market. Evidence: The author notes that unless a company is already massive, a marginal domain suggests a marginal company.
- 100% of the top 20 YC companies by valuation own their .com domain. Evidence: Statistical observation of Y Combinator's top-performing portfolio companies.
- Naming is a skill orthogonal to other startup founder skills. Evidence: The author's experience helping startups find names in 20-minute office hour sessions.
