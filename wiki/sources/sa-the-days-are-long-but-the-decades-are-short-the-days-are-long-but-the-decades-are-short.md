# The days are long but the decades are short

## Metadata

## Summary

Sam Altman reflects on his 30th birthday by sharing 36 pieces of life and career advice, emphasizing the importance of prioritizing relationships, working hard on meaningful projects, maintaining financial discipline, and embracing new experiences while keeping a long-term perspective.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize deep personal relationships over superficial acquaintances.
- Life is short; focus on work that is fulfilling and meaningful rather than status-driven.
- Success requires focus, original thought, and the ability to identify and work with talented people.
- Minimize cognitive load and distractions to focus on high-impact goals.
- Maintain a low personal burn rate to preserve freedom and career flexibility.
- Embrace risk and new experiences to prevent stagnation and keep life interesting.
- Practice empathy, gratitude, and forgiveness to maintain long-term well-being.

## Notable Claims

- You become an average of the people you spend the most time with. Evidence: Observation of social influence and the importance of surrounding oneself with smart, ambitious individuals.
- Doing new things slows down the perception of time and increases happiness. Evidence: Psychological observation on how novelty prevents mental calcification.
- Most people are too risk-averse, and advice is often biased toward conservative paths. Evidence: Observation of human behavior regarding career and life choices.

## Quotes

> The days are long but the decades are short.
> It really is true that you become an average of the people you spend the most time with.
> Don’t chase status. Status without substance doesn’t work for long and is unfulfilling.
> If you think you’re going to regret not doing something, you should probably do it.
