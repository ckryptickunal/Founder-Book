## Summary

A Y Combinator tech talk event featuring presentations from three climate-tech startups (Charge Robotics, Wright Electric, Impossible Mining) and two quick pitches (Muji Meats, Noya) focused on decarbonization, automation, and sustainable resource extraction.

## Key Ideas

- Electric aviation requires significant advancements in energy storage, specifically targeting narrow-body aircraft for maximum carbon impact.
- Labor shortages in the solar industry can be addressed by automating mechanical installation tasks with robotics.
- Deep-sea mining of polymetallic nodules offers a high-grade source of battery metals but requires autonomous, non-destructive collection methods.
- Retrofitting existing industrial infrastructure, such as cooling towers, provides a scalable path for direct air carbon capture.
- 3D printing technology can enable the mass production of whole-cut meat alternatives, overcoming the limitations of ground-meat products.

## Topics

- [[topics/solar-infrastructure-automation|Solar Infrastructure Automation]]: Using robotics to handle heavy lifting and installation tasks in large-scale solar projects.
- [[topics/sustainable-mining|Sustainable Mining]]: Extracting critical battery minerals from the seabed while minimizing ecological damage.

## Notable Claims

- Small airplanes (under 100 passengers) represent less than 5% of the aerospace industry's carbon footprint. Evidence: Industry research cited by Wright Electric.
- Labor is approximately 30% of total solar project costs, with mechanical installation being half of that. Evidence: Market analysis provided by Charge Robotics.
- An EV requires six times the amount of metal compared to a traditional gas vehicle. Evidence: Industry data cited by Impossible Mining.
