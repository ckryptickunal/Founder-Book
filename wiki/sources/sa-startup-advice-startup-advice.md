# Startup Advice

## Summary

A comprehensive list of 95 actionable principles for startup founders, covering product development, hiring, growth, fundraising, and operational management, emphasizing the necessity of execution and product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize building a product people want over all other activities.
- Growth is the primary indicator of success and solves most startup problems.
- Hiring is the most critical task for a founder; prioritize aptitude and cultural fit.
- Maintain speed and agility by keeping the organization flat and avoiding unnecessary complexity.
- Founders must be relentlessly resourceful and prepared for a decade-long commitment.
- Execution quality and product obsession are more important than perfect strategy.

## Notable Claims

- Growth solves nearly all problems. Evidence: Growth indicates product-market fit and provides the momentum necessary to overcome operational hurdles.
- Speed is a startup's main advantage over large companies. Evidence: Large companies are slowed by bureaucracy and process, whereas startups can iterate and pivot rapidly.
- It is better to sell painkillers than vitamins. Evidence: Products that solve urgent, painful problems are easier to sell and gain adoption than those that are merely 'nice to have'.
