# Why Vertical LLM Agents Are The New $1 Billion SaaS Opportunities

## Metadata

## Summary

Jake Heller, founder of CaseText, discusses the development of CoCounsel, a vertical AI agent for the legal industry. He explains how his company pivoted from a decade of incremental legal tech improvements to building a mission-critical AI agent using GPT-4, ultimately leading to a $650 million acquisition by Thomson Reuters.

## Key Ideas

- Vertical AI agents represent massive SaaS opportunities by automating mission-critical, high-value tasks.
- Achieving product-market fit requires moving from 'vibes-based' prompting to rigorous, test-driven development (TDD) to reach 100% accuracy.
- The 'idea maze' for startups often involves dead ends, but major technological shifts like LLMs can suddenly align a product with market needs.
- Successful AI applications require deep domain expertise, proprietary data, and complex engineering beyond just wrapping an LLM.
- System 2 thinking (reasoning) models like OpenAI's o1 offer new potential for precision in complex, high-stakes domains like law.

## Notable Claims

- Moving from 70% to 100% accuracy is the key to unlocking massive value in vertical SaaS. Evidence: CaseText's transition from incremental tools to a mission-critical agent that lawyers trust.
- Prompt engineering requires a test-driven development approach similar to traditional software engineering. Evidence: CaseText built thousands of tests for individual prompts to ensure reliability in legal research.
- Vertical AI agents are not just 'GPT wrappers'. Evidence: They require complex integrations, proprietary data, and custom workflows to be truly useful.
