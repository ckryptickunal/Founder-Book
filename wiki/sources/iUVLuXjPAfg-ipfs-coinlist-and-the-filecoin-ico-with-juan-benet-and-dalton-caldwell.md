# IPFS, CoinList, and the Filecoin ICO with Juan Benet and Dalton Caldwell

## Summary

This podcast features Juan Benet, founder of Protocol Labs, discussing the development of IPFS, Filecoin, and CoinList. The conversation explores the challenges of building decentralized infrastructure, the role of blockchain in rebalancing internet power, and the evolution of funding models for long-term research projects.

## Key Ideas

- Protocol Labs aims to build foundational internet infrastructure that is decentralized and user-sovereign.
- IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) shifts the internet from location-based addressing to content-based addressing.
- Filecoin incentivizes decentralized storage by creating a market where users can buy storage and providers can earn tokens.
- The 'peer-to-peer winter' occurred due to lack of viable monetization, centralization of cloud services, and lack of digital currency for trustless payments.
- Smart contracts allow software to 'eat' finance and law, enabling complex financial instruments to be created and settled without traditional legal intermediaries.
- The 'credit assignment problem' in open source is a major hurdle; current models fail to reward foundational contributors effectively.
- CoinList was created to provide a compliant, high-signal platform for token sales, bridging the gap between complex legal requirements and decentralized projects.

## Notable Claims

- The internet is currently too centralized and controlled by a few large entities. Evidence: Data control and information flow are currently dominated by companies like Google, Facebook, and Amazon.
- Smart contracts can replace traditional legal and financial intermediaries. Evidence: They allow for automated, trustless execution of financial agreements, such as insurance policies, without human litigation.
- VC funding is ill-suited for long-term, foundational infrastructure projects. Evidence: VCs require 10-year returns with strong adoption in 2-3 years, which is incompatible with multi-decade research cycles.
