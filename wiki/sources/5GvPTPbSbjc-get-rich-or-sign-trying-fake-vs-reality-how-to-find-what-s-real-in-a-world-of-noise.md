# Get RICH or Sign Trying -- FAKE vs. reality & how to find what's REAL in a world of noise.

## Summary

Garry Tan explores the disconnect between 'signal value' (the appearance of success) and actual substance in the modern startup ecosystem. Drawing on Jean Baudrillard’s theory of simulacra and the Keynesian beauty contest, Tan argues that society and the tech industry have become obsessed with performative success, leading to fraud and superficiality. He encourages founders and professionals to prioritize fundamentals and long-term value over the 'fake it till you make it' mentality.

## Key Ideas

- The danger of believing one's own publicity and prioritizing image over substance.
- Baudrillard’s concept of simulacra: modern society is dominated by signs and images that are disconnected from reality.
- The 'Keynesian beauty contest' explains herd behavior in venture capital, where investors fund startups based on what they think others will value, rather than fundamental merit.
- The shift from startups being a fringe, creative endeavor to a commoditized 'power position' driven by prestige.
- The 'weighing machine' metaphor: while markets may be driven by popularity in the short term, they eventually evaluate companies based on true worth and profitability.
- The necessity of focusing on fundamentals to avoid being a 'flash in the pan'.

## Topics

- [[topics/simulacra|Simulacra]]: The replacement of reality with symbols and signs, leading to a society disconnected from the truth.
- [[topics/keynesian-beauty-contest|Keynesian Beauty Contest]]: A decision-making model where participants choose based on what they believe the average opinion will be, rather than objective quality.
- [[topics/startup-culture|Startup Culture]]: The evolution of the startup ecosystem from a creative fringe to a commoditized, prestige-driven industry.
- [[topics/reification|Reification]]: The process by which human beings become dominated by things and lose their sense of authentic reality.

## Notable Claims

- The modern startup ecosystem is increasingly driven by signal value rather than fundamental business success. Evidence: The rise of massive valuations for pre-revenue companies and the focus on prestige over actual product utility.
- Faking success is a short-term strategy that eventually fails against the 'weighing machine' of the market. Evidence: The collapse of Theranos and the long-term market evaluation principles cited by Warren Buffett.
