# What to do if a bubble is starting

## Metadata

## Summary

Sam Altman analyzes the 2013 startup landscape, identifying signs of a potential bubble while advising founders on how to build resilient, lean companies regardless of market cycles.

## Key Ideas

- Early-stage valuations with no traction are unsustainable.
- Macroeconomic indicators like Fed stimulus prolong bubble cycles.
- Investors tend to overreact to market downturns by pulling back capital.
- Founders should operate as if they will never raise another round of funding.
- A market bust can be an opportunity for disciplined companies to gain ground.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-bubbles|Startup Bubbles]]: The phenomenon of inflated valuations and market exuberance in the venture capital ecosystem.
- [[topics/financial-discipline|Financial Discipline]]: The practice of maintaining low burn rates, profitability, and lean operations to survive market volatility.

## Notable Claims

- Investors will overreact to the first sign of trouble by reducing investment when they should be increasing it. Evidence: Historical observation of investor behavior during market downturns.
- The only thing that is cheap during a startup bubble is capital. Evidence: High valuations and easy access to funding make capital abundant, while labor and real estate costs rise.

## Quotes

> The only thing that is cheap during a startup bubble is capital. Everything else is relatively more expensive.
> Make sure you have enough money in the bank, and treat this money as the last money you’ll ever raise.
> Good companies can make incredible strides during a bust.
