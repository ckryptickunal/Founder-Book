# The Need to Read

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-the-need-to-read`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: November 2022
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-the-need-to-read

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that reading remains an essential practice because writing is a fundamental tool for thinking, not just a medium for communication.

## Key Ideas

- Reading is essential because it teaches the craft of writing.
- Writing is a generative process for developing ideas, not just a way to record pre-existing thoughts.
- Complex, ill-defined problems require writing to be solved effectively.
- There is a direct dependency chain: thinking well requires writing well, which requires reading well.

## Entities

- [[entities/paul-graham|Paul Graham]] (person): Essayist, programmer, and venture capitalist.

## Topics

- [[topics/cognitive-science|Cognitive Science]]: The relationship between language, reading, and the process of human thought.
- [[topics/writing-as-thinking|Writing as Thinking]]: The concept that the act of writing is a primary mechanism for discovery and idea generation.

## Notable Claims

- Writing is a way to have ideas, not just convey them. Evidence: The author notes that good writers almost always discover new things during the writing process that they did not know before starting.
- People who are not good at writing are at a disadvantage in solving complex, ill-defined problems. Evidence: The author asserts that writing is a necessary tool for navigating problems that cannot be solved through formal logic or simple visualization.

## Quotes

> Writing is not just a way to convey ideas, but also a way to have them.
> You can't think well without writing well, and you can't write well without reading well.
> People who just want information may find other ways to get it. But people who want to have ideas can't afford to.
