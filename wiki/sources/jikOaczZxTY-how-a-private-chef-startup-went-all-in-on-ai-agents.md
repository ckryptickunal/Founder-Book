## Summary

Siti, founder of WhyHungry, details three impactful AI business use cases. First, an autonomous bug fixer was developed in under four days, resolving over 25 bugs in its first week with a 60-70% one-shot fix rate. Second, Siti leveraged her AI expertise to secure $50,000 worth of free conference slots by teaching AI agent building, effectively marketing WhyHungry's new AI product. Third, WhyHungry is developing an AI product to modernize its private chef booking process, aiming to instantly match customers with chefs and automate administrative tasks for chefs, addressing the current 'old school' system's inefficiencies. The company is undergoing a rapid AI transformation, with all departments adopting AI-native workflows, and Siti emphasizes founder branding through sharing her AI knowledge.

## Key Ideas

- AI can drastically improve engineering efficiency through autonomous bug fixing, achieving high one-shot fix rates.
- Teaching complex AI concepts in plain English serves as a powerful, cost-effective marketing and growth strategy for a company.
- AI is crucial for transforming traditional service industries by automating matching, booking, and administrative tasks, enhancing user experience and operational efficiency.
- Company-wide AI adoption requires significant organizational shifts, including leadership changes and a commitment to AI-native workflows across all departments.
- Founder branding, built on sharing genuine expertise, can generate substantial attention and value that funnels back to the company.
