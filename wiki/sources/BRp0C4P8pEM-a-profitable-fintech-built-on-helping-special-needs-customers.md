# A profitable fintech built on helping special needs customers

## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Kai Stinchcomb, founder and CEO of TrueLink Financial, a profitable neobank designed specifically for individuals with special needs, such as those with Alzheimer's, autism, or addiction recovery needs. The discussion highlights the strategic advantage of targeting underserved niches rather than competing for broad, expensive customer segments.

## Key Ideas

- Startups should focus on unique, underserved customer segments rather than trying to compete with mass-market incumbents.
- Personal experience with a problem often leads to the most authentic and effective product solutions.
- Targeting a specific niche allows for lower customer acquisition costs (CAC) and higher lifetime value (LTV).
- Humility is a critical trait for founders and employees when building products for populations with complex, specialized needs.
- Profitability in fintech is achievable by solving genuine, high-stakes problems that traditional banks overlook.
