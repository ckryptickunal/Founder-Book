# This $1.5 Trillion Industry Still Runs on Paper and Fax Machines

## Summary

Vory, an all-in-one point-of-sale and store management system for supermarkets, recently announced a $22 million Series B funding round led by Cherry Rock Capital. Founded by Brandon, Rob, and Trey, Vory aims to digitize the largely antiquated $1.5 trillion U.S. grocery industry, which still relies heavily on paper and manual processes. Starting with a simple inventory reordering app, Vory expanded into a full-stack operating system covering POS, payments, inventory, shopper marketing, and dynamic pricing. The company emphasizes building trust and relationships with grocers, demonstrating significant impact on their profitability (20-25% lift in net sales, 7-10 points in gross margin, labor efficiency). Vory leverages AI for product features (autonomous agents for inventory, pricing, marketing), internal efficiency, and defensibility, positioning itself to help independent grocers compete with giants like Walmart and Amazon in the race to define the future of retail. Their long-term vision is to create a "self-driving grocery store" and become the clearinghouse for trade in the global food supply chain.

## Key Ideas

- The U.S. grocery industry is a massive, $1.5 trillion market, larger than restaurants and hotels, yet largely undigitized and reliant on antiquated systems.
- Vory provides an all-in-one point-of-sale and store management system to help supermarkets move from paper-based operations to digital, increasing profitability and sustainability.
- The founding insight for Vory came from observing the widespread use of paper-and-pencil systems in grocery stores as recently as 2019/2020, despite advanced technology in other sectors (e.g., SpaceX).
- Vory's initial 'wedge product' was a mobile app for inventory reordering, which helped build trust and demonstrate value to early adopters.
- Scaling in the grocery industry requires persistence, building deep relationships, and understanding customer pain points, often starting with founder-led, door-to-door sales.
- Vory evolved from a simple app to a comprehensive 'compound startup' offering full-stack solutions including hardware and payment processing, driven by the need to address where grocers spend the most money.
- Vory's value proposition is tied directly to a grocer's P&L: significantly increasing sales, gross margin, and labor efficiency, leading to substantial profitability lifts.
- AI is central to Vory's product (autonomous agents for inventory, pricing, marketing), internal operations, and long-term defensibility in a 'real industry' context.
- Vory aims to empower independent grocers to compete with large players like Walmart and Amazon, who are heavily investing in physical retail AI and vying for market dominance.
- Exceptional customer service, including 'superhero acts,' is crucial for building trust and competitive advantage in a relationship-driven industry.
- The future vision for Vory is a 'self-driving grocery store' that augments human work, and to become the clearinghouse for global food supply chain trade, eventually offering financial services.
