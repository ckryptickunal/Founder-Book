# How to Build a Self-Improving Company with AI

## Summary

The speaker argues that traditional hierarchical company structures, akin to Roman legions, are obsolete in the age of AI. Instead of merely using AI for productivity boosts, companies should be reimagined as 'recursive self-improving AI loops.' This involves making all company knowledge legible to AI by recording everything, implementing an AI loop (sensor, policy, tool, quality gate, learning mechanism), and allowing AI to autonomously improve processes like product optimization or customer service. This shift implies 'burning tokens, not headcount,' the obsolescence of middle management, and a focus on individual contributors (ICs) and directly responsible individuals (DRIs). Humans will primarily interface with the real world, handling novel, ethical, or high-stakes situations, while AI manages and self-improves internal operations.

## Key Ideas

- Traditional hierarchical company structures are broken by AI and should be replaced.
- AI should fundamentally reimagine company operations, not just serve as a productivity tool.
- Companies can be structured as 'recursive self-improving AI loops' with distinct layers (sensor, policy, tool, quality gate, learning mechanism).
- All company domain knowledge (emails, Slack, conversations) must be recorded and made 'legible' to AI to enable self-improvement.
- AI can autonomously monitor its performance, identify failures, and update its own tools/code.
- This new model leads to 'burn tokens, not headcount,' significantly increasing revenue per employee.
- Middle management becomes obsolete as AI can handle coordination problems; companies will be built around individual contributors (ICs) and directly responsible individuals (DRIs).
- Humans will focus on interfacing with the real world, handling novel, ethical, or high-stakes situations that AI cannot yet address.
- Internal software and dashboards are ephemeral and can be regenerated by AI, while underlying data and business context are the valuable assets.
