# Lightcone: Consumer is back, What’s getting funded now, The vibes immaculate

## Summary

The Y Combinator partners discuss the Winter 2024 batch, highlighting a significant shift toward AI-driven startups, a resurgence in consumer-focused ideas, and a record-breaking increase in developer tools. They contrast this period with the pandemic era, noting a return to San Francisco, a younger founder demographic, and a renewed sense of optimism driven by a major technology platform shift.

## Key Ideas

- AI is the dominant mega-trend, with nearly 70% of the batch focused on AI-related ideas.
- There is a clear platform shift occurring, making existing SaaS markets vulnerable to disruption.
- Consumer startups are making a comeback after a period where B2B SaaS dominated the landscape.
- Developer tools are experiencing a surge, mirroring the early infrastructure-building phase of previous technology cycles.
- Open-source projects are increasingly being used as a strategy to challenge dominant closed-source incumbents.
- The current batch is the most technical in YC history, with 99% of companies having a technical founder.
- The 'Tech Enabled' business model (like WeWork) has been largely replaced by a focus on pure software and high-margin AI innovation.
