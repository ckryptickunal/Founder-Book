# Real vs. Fake progress

## Metadata

- Video ID: `Lg3n4cChF1M`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-02-14T21:10:52Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lg3n4cChF1M

## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator emphasizing that startup success relies exclusively on talking to users and product iteration, while warning against 'fake progress' activities that do not directly drive growth.

## Key Ideas

- Real startup progress is defined by talking to users and iterating on the product.
- Fake progress involves activities that feel productive but do not directly impact primary KPIs.
- Founders should avoid vanity metrics and activities like attending conferences or winning awards.
- The ultimate goal of a startup is to deliver tangible value to the customer.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-growth|Startup Growth]]: The process of scaling a business by focusing on core value delivery rather than vanity metrics.
- [[topics/product-iteration|Product Iteration]]: The continuous cycle of improving a product based on direct user feedback.

## Notable Claims

- Attending conferences and winning awards are forms of fake startup progress. Evidence: These activities are often many steps removed from delivering direct value to the customer or moving primary KPIs.

## Quotes

> The highest leverage thing you can be doing is talking to users and building and iterating your product.
> The goal is not to optimize startup vanity but actually delivering value to your customer.
