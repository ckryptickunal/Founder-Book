# Cursor CEO: Going Beyond Code, Superintelligent AI Agents, And Why Taste Still Matters

## Summary

Michael Tru, CEO of Anysphere, discusses the evolution of Cursor, an AI-powered coding platform, and his vision for the future of software development. He emphasizes that the ultimate goal is to move beyond traditional coding toward an intent-driven, higher-level paradigm where AI agents handle the labor of implementation, while human 'taste' remains the essential, irreplaceable component of building great software.

## Key Ideas

- The goal of software development is shifting from manual coding to intent-driven creation.
- AI agents are currently helpers, but the future involves moving to higher levels of abstraction where developers define logic rather than writing syntax.
- Taste is the most critical, irreplaceable skill for future software engineers.
- The 'vibe coding' approach is currently limited by the need for reliability and long-term maintainability in professional codebases.
- Product distribution is a key moat; user interaction data allows for continuous improvement of AI models and product features.
- Scaling laws suggest that AI models will continue to improve, making it essential to build products that evolve alongside model capabilities.

- AI will eventually replace traditional coding with a higher-level way of building software. Evidence: The trend of moving from manual syntax to intent-based interaction and the rapid growth of AI capabilities.
- Taste is the only irreplaceable skill for future software engineers. Evidence: AI can handle implementation details, but humans must define what is useful and aesthetically sound.
