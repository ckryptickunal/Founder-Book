# Video Generation as a Primitive

## Summary

This video explores the rapid advancement of generative video technology, positioning it as a fundamental 'primitive' for future software development rather than just a content output, and invites founders to build infrastructure and applications around this shift.

## Key Ideas

- Generative video is evolving into a low-cost, high-fidelity building block for software.
- The technology will disrupt media, entertainment, e-commerce, and gaming.
- Future applications include personalized content, virtual try-ons, and engine-less video games.
- Y Combinator is seeking founders to build the infrastructure and tooling for this new era of limitless video.

## Entities

- [[entities/google|Google]] (company): Technology corporation developing advanced video generation models.
- [[entities/v3|V3]] (product): A generative video model capable of producing photorealistic, sound-on clips.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator seeking founders to build in the generative video space.

## Topics

- [[topics/generative-video|Generative Video]]: The use of AI to create high-quality, photorealistic video content on demand.
- [[topics/computing-primitives|Computing Primitives]]: Treating video generation as a foundational layer for software development rather than a final product.
- [[topics/e-commerce-personalization|E-commerce Personalization]]: Using AI video to simulate product usage and personal appearance for online shoppers.

## Notable Claims

- Video generation will soon approach a marginal cost of zero. Evidence: Rapid improvements in model efficiency and output quality as seen in current models like V3.
- Video generation will enable the creation of video games without traditional game engines. Evidence: The ability to generate real-time, interactive, and photorealistic visual environments via AI.
