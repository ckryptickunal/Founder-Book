# Why I started a VC firm- "Too much money chasing too few good people and ideas" is totally wrong

## Summary

Garry Tan explains his motivation for launching his YouTube channel, emphasizing his desire to provide unfiltered, high-bandwidth insights into the startup ecosystem. He challenges the common venture capital narrative that there is a scarcity of talent and ideas, arguing instead that capital is abundant and that the real challenge lies in connecting untapped talent with the resources needed to solve societal problems.

## Key Ideas

- YouTube serves as a high-bandwidth, direct medium for sharing authentic, unfiltered startup knowledge.
- The venture capital industry's claim that there are 'too few good people and ideas' is fundamentally flawed.
- Capital is currently in oversupply, leading to a desperate search for yield, which makes startup formation a critical outlet.
- Over-reliance on traditional credentials (like elite universities or big tech experience) hinders the discovery of brilliant, non-traditional founders.
- The role of a venture capitalist is to identify 'real' builders and provide them with the community and capital to create value.

## Notable Claims

- There is an oversupply of capital in the global economy. Evidence: The existence of negative interest rates and the difficulty in finding productive uses for capital.
- The belief that there are too few good people to build startups is incorrect. Evidence: There is an abundance of smart people, but they are often overlooked due to an over-reliance on traditional credentials.
