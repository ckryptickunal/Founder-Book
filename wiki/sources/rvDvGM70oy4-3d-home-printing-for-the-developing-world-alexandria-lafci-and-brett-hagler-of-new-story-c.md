# 3D Home Printing for the Developing World – Alexandria Lafci and Brett Hagler of New Story Charity

## Summary

Alexandria Lafci and Brett Hagler, co-founders of the non-profit New Story, discuss their mission to provide housing for families in the developing world. They explain how they leveraged Y Combinator's startup methodology—such as 'make something people want,' radical transparency, and audacious goal-setting—to revolutionize the non-profit sector. The conversation highlights their transition from traditional construction to 3D home printing as a scalable, cost-effective, and high-quality solution to global housing instability.

## Key Ideas

- Apply startup principles (MVP, rapid iteration, data-driven decision making) to non-profit work.
- Transparency and accountability are essential to solving donor skepticism in the humanitarian sector.
- Use 'think big, break down, and execute' to achieve audacious goals like building 100 homes in 100 days.
- Leverage 3D printing technology to reduce construction costs, increase speed, and improve structural quality in remote areas.
- Prioritize local partnerships and community-led design to ensure long-term success and cultural relevance.
- Bold ideas attract bold people, including top-tier talent and visionary donors.

## Notable Claims

- 3D home printing can reduce the cost of a home from $6,500 to $3,000 over time. Evidence: Projected efficiency gains from scaling the technology and reducing waste.
- 3D printed homes are stronger than traditional cinderblock homes. Evidence: PSI compression strength tests on the first 3D printed house in Austin, Texas showed it was three times stronger.
- Housing stability directly impacts health and education outcomes. Evidence: Data showing increased sleep duration and improved school attendance for children in stable housing.
