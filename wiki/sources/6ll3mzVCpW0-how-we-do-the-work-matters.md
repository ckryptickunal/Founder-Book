# How we do the work matters!

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the importance of integrity and process-oriented execution in venture capital, arguing that long-term success is built on trustworthiness and the quality of one's actions rather than short-term gains.

## Key Ideas

- Trustworthiness is the primary trait founders look for in investors.
- Venture capital is an infinite game where bad actors suffer from adverse selection over time.
- Focusing on the process and quality of execution leads to better long-term outcomes than obsessing over winning.
- Venture capital structures are long-term commitments, often spanning over a decade.

## Notable Claims

- Being a bad actor in venture capital leads to adverse selection. Evidence: Because venture capital is an iterative, infinite game, bad behavior results in losing access to the best deals over the long run.
- Focusing on the process of improvement leads to winning. Evidence: Referencing Bill Walsh's coaching philosophy, where obsessing over the quality of execution and attitude naturally results in victory.
