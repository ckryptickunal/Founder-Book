## Summary

Elli Sharef, co-founder of HireArt, shares her experiences and advice for female founders at the 2014 Y Combinator Female Founders Conference. She emphasizes the importance of taking the leap to start a company, maintaining deep-rooted confidence, persisting through near-failure, and enjoying the unique journey of entrepreneurship.

## Key Ideas

- There is never a perfect time to start a company; one must simply take the first step.
- Confidence is a critical skill for founders, requiring both internal belief and the ability to externalize that vision to stakeholders.
- Startups often exist in a state of 'near-failure' where persistence and hustling are required to overcome obstacles.
- Female founders should embrace boldness and conviction, similar to the bravado often seen in male counterparts.
- Entrepreneurship is a challenging but rewarding journey that should be enjoyed, regardless of the outcome.

## Notable Claims

- There is never a 'perfect' time to start a company. Evidence: Sharef notes that waiting for the right amount of money, time, or lack of responsibilities is a fallacy.
- Confidence is a learnable and necessary skill for founders. Evidence: Sharef argues that founders must internalize their vision to effectively convince investors and hires.
