## Summary

Newsha Ghaeli, co-founder of Biobot Analytics, discusses the company's journey in building a human health intelligence platform using wastewater epidemiology. The company pivoted from early struggles selling to government agencies to becoming a critical player during the COVID-19 pandemic, eventually facing intense competition from big tech, which forced them to diversify into broader commercial healthcare applications.

## Key Ideas

- Wastewater contains valuable health data that can be used for public health surveillance and biosecurity.
- Wastewater data provides a more representative sample of community health than traditional clinical testing, especially for substance use disorders.
- Selling cutting-edge technology to government agencies is difficult due to slow adoption cycles and bureaucratic resistance.
- Creating a new market often invites competition from larger, well-funded entities that may use aggressive pricing strategies.
- Wastewater epidemiology is a dual-use technology with significant potential in both public health and private sector healthcare.

## Notable Claims

- Traditional opioid overdose data represents less than 1% of people suffering from substance use disorder. Evidence: Cited as a justification for why wastewater data is more representative of community health.
- A town in North Carolina decreased overdoses by 40% from 2018 to 2019 using Biobot data. Evidence: Used as a case study for the efficacy of wastewater-informed resource allocation.
- Verily undercut Biobot's price for a CDC contract by over 60%. Evidence: Based on public data from USAspending and SAM.gov.
