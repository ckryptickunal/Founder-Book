# Closing Remarks at Startup School NY 2014

## Summary

Alexis Ohanian delivers the closing remarks for the 2014 Y Combinator Startup School in New York, highlighting the inclusion of non-profits in YC, thanking organizers and speakers, and announcing future events including a London session and the first YC Hackathon.

## Key Ideas

- Y Combinator is actively accepting applications from non-profit organizations.
- Community feedback is essential for the improvement of future Startup School events.
- Startup School events are expanding to international locations like London.
- Y Combinator is launching its first-ever hackathon in August 2014.

## Entities

- [[entities/alexis-ohanian|Alexis Ohanian]] (person): Co-founder of Reddit and partner at Y Combinator.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.
- [[entities/watsi|Watsi]] (organization): A non-profit organization that funds medical treatments for people in need.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-ecosystem|Startup Ecosystem]]: The growth and support of both for-profit and non-profit startups through accelerators.
- [[topics/event-management|Event Management]]: The importance of community feedback and logistical coordination in hosting large-scale educational events.

## Notable Claims

- Non-profits are as welcome to apply to Y Combinator as for-profit startups. Evidence: Explicit statement by Alexis Ohanian regarding YC's updated application policy.

## Quotes

> If any of you are starting a non-profit, or have friends who are, you all are just as welcome to apply to YC, as you are-would be with your for profit start ups.
