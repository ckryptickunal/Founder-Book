# How to Get New Ideas

## Metadata

## Summary

Paul Graham explains that the most effective way to generate new ideas is by identifying anomalies—things that are strange, missing, or broken—particularly at the frontiers of knowledge where gaps in understanding become apparent.

## Key Ideas

- New ideas originate from noticing anomalies in everyday life or at the edges of knowledge.
- Knowledge grows in a fractal pattern, meaning that as you get closer to the frontiers, you discover more gaps.
- Gaps at the frontiers of knowledge often seem obvious once identified, leading to potential new discoveries or 'fractal buds'.
- The process of finding ideas is similar to the observational techniques used in standup comedy.
