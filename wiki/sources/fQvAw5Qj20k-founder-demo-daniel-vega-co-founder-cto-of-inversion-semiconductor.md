## Summary

Daniel Vega, co-founder and CTO of Inversion Semiconductor, discusses the critical need for domestic advanced semiconductor manufacturing in the United States. He highlights the industry's reliance on foreign lithography tools, specifically ASML, and introduces Inversion Semiconductor's mission to develop compact particle accelerator-based lithography machines to reclaim manufacturing capabilities.

## Key Ideas

- The US designs the world's most advanced semiconductors but currently manufactures 0% of them.
- The semiconductor supply chain is a 'wrapper' model where companies rely on layers of specialized upstream providers.
- Advanced lithography (EUV) is the primary bottleneck for domestic chip production.
- Inversion Semiconductor is developing compact particle accelerators to compete with traditional lithography methods.
- Industrial policy and collaboration with national labs are essential to rebuilding US manufacturing sovereignty.
