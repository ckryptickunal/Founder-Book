# What are some things you’ve had to unlearn?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `MDB_TbJLJuw`

## Summary

Y Combinator partners discuss the common challenges faced by former Big Tech employees transitioning to startup founders, specifically the need to unlearn corporate habits and over-engineering tendencies.

## Key Ideas

- Corporate infrastructure at companies like Google or Facebook often masks the lack of foundational skills required to build from scratch.
- Big Tech employees often mistake their proficiency with proprietary internal tools for general engineering expertise.
- The standard for product quality in large corporations is significantly higher than what is required for a startup's Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
- Founders coming from large tech companies often struggle to release products quickly because they are conditioned to over-engineer.

- [[topics/mvp-vs-corporate-standards|MVP vs. Corporate Standards]]: The conflict between the need for speed in startups and the high-quality, over-engineered standards of Big Tech.

## Notable Claims

- Nothing learned in a Big Tech job translates to a startup. Evidence: Founders report that the reliance on proprietary infrastructure and internal tools leaves them unprepared for building from scratch.
- Google engineers over-engineer their MVPs. Evidence: The standard for getting a product into beta at Google is 10x higher than what is necessary for a startup MVP.
