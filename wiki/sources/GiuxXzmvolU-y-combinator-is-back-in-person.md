## Summary

Y Combinator partners Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell discuss the return to in-person programming for the Summer 2022 batch, the strategic benefits of building during economic downturns, and the impact of their new standard investment deal on founder autonomy.

## Key Ideas

- Y Combinator is returning to in-person events, including retreats and meetups, while maintaining remote accessibility.
- Economic downturns are historically optimal times to build startups because they allow founders to focus on product-market fit without the distractions of hype cycles.
- The new YC standard deal provides $500,000 in funding, which helps founders remain 'default alive' and reduces dilution during seed rounds.
- Founders should avoid emotional, fear-based decision-making and instead perform sober analyses of their specific runway and burn rates.
- There is a notable trend of founders returning to major tech hubs like San Francisco and New York to be part of an active startup ecosystem.

## Notable Claims

- Some of the best companies were founded during economic downturns. Evidence: Cites Stripe, Airbnb, and Dropbox as examples of companies that emerged during difficult economic periods.
- The new YC standard deal reduces dilution for founders. Evidence: Founders are now able to raise seed rounds with 10% or less dilution compared to the historical 15-35%.
- Founders are returning to San Francisco because they want to be where the action is. Evidence: Internal YC survey data showed that previous remote-only batches expressed a strong desire to be in the Bay Area.
