# If you really believe in something, you've got to do it yourself!

## Metadata

- Video ID: `5-EYa31n3pE`
- Channel: Garry Tan

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the foundational process of starting a movement or business, emphasizing that true innovation begins by solving a personal problem that others have yet to recognize.

## Key Ideas

- Innovation often starts with solving a problem for yourself before scaling to others.
- The first stage of any movement is emergence or social ferment.
- Leadership and followership naturally follow when you create something that addresses a widespread, previously ignored need.

## Notable Claims

- The first stage of any movement is emergence or social ferment. Evidence: The speaker identifies this as the foundational phase where an individual identifies a problem nobody else cares about yet.
- Solving a personal problem is the best way to find a market for others. Evidence: The speaker suggests that if you solve a problem for yourself, you will likely find others who share the same need.

## Quotes

> Sometimes if you really believe in something you've got to do it yourself.
> What do you want that nobody else cares about yet? What problem do you see? First try to solve it for yourself.
