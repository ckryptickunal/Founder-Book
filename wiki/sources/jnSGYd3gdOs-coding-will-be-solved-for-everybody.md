# Coding Will Be Solved For Everybody

## Metadata

## Summary

The speaker argues that coding is becoming a universal skill, leading to the obsolescence of the 'software engineer' job title as roles shift toward generalist 'builders' who integrate coding with product management and user interaction.

## Key Ideas

- Coding is becoming a commoditized, universal skill rather than a specialized profession.
- The 'software engineer' job title will likely disappear or become vestigial.
- Future roles will prioritize generalist capabilities, combining coding with product management and user-centric design.
- Cross-functional teams where every member codes is becoming the new organizational standard.
