## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Sonya Kastner, founder and CEO of Pano, about how their AI-powered computer vision technology is revolutionizing wildfire detection and response by enabling early intervention before small fires escalate into mega-blazes.

## Key Ideas

- Current wildfire detection relies heavily on bystander 911 calls, which are often inaccurate and lead to delayed, inefficient responses.
- Pano uses a network of 360-degree mountaintop cameras combined with AI to detect smoke, verify fires, and provide real-time visual intelligence to emergency responders.
- Early detection allows agencies to deploy existing heavy equipment (planes, helicopters) to small fires, preventing them from becoming uncontrollable mega-fires.
- The firefighting sector is often hampered by outdated technology, creating a significant opportunity for modern IoT and AI solutions.
- Pano's solution is positioned as a cost-effective, scalable, and immediate 'low-hanging fruit' compared to long-term infrastructure projects like burying power lines.

## Notable Claims

- Only one out of every 20 911 calls reporting a wildfire is actually a real fire. Evidence: Stated by Sonya Kastner based on industry data regarding bystander reporting.
