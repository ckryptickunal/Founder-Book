# Airbnb and San Francisco

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-airbnb-and-san-francisco`

## Summary

Sam Altman defends Airbnb against San Francisco political criticism, arguing that the company is being used as a scapegoat for the city's broader housing crisis, which is primarily driven by supply shortages rather than home-sharing.

## Key Ideas

- Airbnb helps residents afford their rent and mortgages, preventing displacement.
- The impact of Airbnb on San Francisco housing prices is statistically negligible (less than 1%).
- The true cause of rising housing costs in San Francisco is a lack of new construction and supply-side constraints.
- Politicians are using Airbnb as a distraction from their failure to address the housing supply crisis.
- The sharing economy improves asset utilization, which should theoretically lower costs for everyone.

## Notable Claims

- Airbnb has affected the price of housing in San Francisco by less than 1%. Evidence: Research conducted by economist Tom Davidoff.
- 75% of Airbnb hosts in San Francisco use the income to stay in their homes. Evidence: Internal Airbnb host data.
- The housing cost increase in SF is due to a lack of new construction. Evidence: Comparison of housing demand versus the city's slow approval process for new units.
