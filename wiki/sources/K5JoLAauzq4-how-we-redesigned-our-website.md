# How We Redesigned Our Website

## Summary

Y Combinator's design team discusses the redesign of their website, shifting from a utilitarian, B2B-style layout to a storytelling-focused experience that emphasizes founder journeys, inspiration, and the 'formidable' nature of startups.

## Key Ideas

- The redesign prioritizes storytelling and inspiration over traditional conversion-focused B2B design.
- Emphasis is placed on the founders' journeys rather than just company logos or valuation metrics.
- The design process moved away from static Figma mockups toward live prototyping using AI tools like Opus 4.5 and Cursor.
- Interactive elements and animations were used to enhance communication rather than for aesthetic decoration.
- The site maintains core legacy copy from Paul Graham to preserve the organization's roots while updating the visual experience.

## Topics

- [[topics/web-design-philosophy|Web Design Philosophy]]: Moving from static, conversion-heavy B2B templates to interactive, narrative-driven experiences.
- [[topics/ai-assisted-development|AI-Assisted Development]]: Using AI agents to accelerate the prototyping and coding process, allowing designers to focus on high-level interactions.
- [[topics/founder-centric-branding|Founder-Centric Branding]]: Shifting brand focus from the organization to the individual founders to make success feel more attainable and relatable.

## Notable Claims

- It is never too early to apply to Y Combinator. Evidence: The team explicitly states they fund people who haven't quit their jobs, are still in school, or lack a fully formed idea.
- Live prototyping is more efficient than static design tools for complex interactions. Evidence: The team found that building directly in a repo allowed for better experimentation with animations and layout than Figma.
