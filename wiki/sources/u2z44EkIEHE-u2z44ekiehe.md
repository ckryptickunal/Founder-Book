## Summary

Aaron Harris interviews Charlie and Jackson, co-founders of Conductor, a Mac application that enables developers to run multiple AI coding agents simultaneously. The discussion covers their journey from Y Combinator to raising a $22 million Series A, the launch of Conductor Cloud, and their philosophy on building developer tools by dogfooding their own product.

## Key Ideas

- Conductor allows developers to manage multiple coding agents in one interface, moving beyond the limitations of a single IDE.
- The transition from local-only execution to Conductor Cloud allows agents to continue working even when the user's laptop is closed.
- The founders emphasize 'dogfooding'—building tools they personally need and using them to build the product itself.
- Engineering with AI is shifting from manual coding to an 'architect' role, where humans manage multiple agents like a CEO manages employees.
- The best engineers are those who can effectively manage AI agents, optimize workflows, and maintain 'slop-free' zones in their codebase.
- The founders believe current PR review processes are outdated and ripe for innovation.
