# The Engineer Crunch

## Summary

Sam Altman discusses the persistent difficulty startups face in hiring engineers, arguing that the 'engineer crunch' is a more significant barrier than capital access. He attributes the struggle to stingy equity practices, lack of a compelling mission, and poor founder-led recruiting efforts.

## Key Ideas

- Startups often fail to hire engineers because they are too stingy with equity grants.
- Mission-oriented companies have a significant competitive advantage in recruiting top engineering talent.
- Founders must lead the recruiting process themselves rather than relying on external recruiters in the early stages.
- If a company lacks a compelling mission, it should aim for a smaller, highly efficient engineering team.
- Immigration reform for technical talent is the most impactful government action for the startup ecosystem.

## Topics

- [[topics/equity-compensation|Equity Compensation]]: The importance of offering meaningful equity to early-stage employees to attract talent and align incentives.
- [[topics/recruiting-strategy|Recruiting Strategy]]: The necessity of founder-led hiring and the importance of mission-driven narratives.
- [[topics/immigration-policy|Immigration Policy]]: The argument that easing immigration for technical talent is critical for startup growth.

## Notable Claims

- Startups that are stingy with equity struggle to hire top engineering talent. Evidence: Altman notes that founders often complain about hiring difficulties while offering insufficient equity (0.1% to 0.2%).
- Founders must handle early-stage recruiting personally. Evidence: Altman asserts that outsourcing to recruiters before a company is established is ineffective.

## Quotes

> I have never seen a startup regret being generous with equity for their early employees.
> If the best part of your recruiting pitch is about how much money the company is going to make, you’ll have a harder time recruiting talent.
> The only thing you need to do is fix immigration for founders and engineers.
