## Summary

Siqi Chen, founder of Runway, discusses the evolution of business financial planning, the importance of design-forward software, and the role of storytelling in building successful startups. He explains how Runway acts as a 'tool for thought' for FP&A, moving beyond static spreadsheets to provide a dynamic, collaborative simulation of a business's future.

## Key Ideas

- Finance should be accessible to everyone in a company, not just the finance department.
- Modern business models need to evolve from static spreadsheets to dynamic, collaborative 'tools for thought'.
- Effective startup storytelling requires a believable villain—the status quo—to highlight the value of the new solution.
- AI should be integrated into workflows as a tool for thought rather than treated as an external, autonomous agent.
- The best way to build a career is to seek experiences that add new, diverse skills to one's repertoire.
- Leadership is about providing clarity and support, which paradoxically increases team autonomy.

## Topics

- [[topics/fp-a-software|FP&A Software]]: The transition from manual, error-prone spreadsheets to automated, collaborative financial modeling.
- [[topics/startup-storytelling|Startup Storytelling]]: The process of crafting a compelling narrative that identifies a clear villain in the existing market.
- [[topics/ai-in-saas|AI in SaaS]]: Using LLMs as 'if statements' to structure unstructured data rather than relying on unreliable autonomous agents.
- [[topics/leadership-philosophy|Leadership Philosophy]]: Moving from individual contributor mindsets to coaching and providing clarity for teams.

## Notable Claims

- Spreadsheets do not scale for companies as they grow in complexity. Evidence: As companies grow, they face increased data sources, higher dimensionality, and communication challenges that spreadsheets cannot handle.
- AI-powered features will soon become a baseline expectation rather than a differentiator. Evidence: Similar to how Ajax became standard in web development, AI will be embedded into all software, making 'AI-powered' a redundant label.
- Strong leadership increases team autonomy. Evidence: By providing clear mission and story, leaders empower teams to make independent decisions that align with company goals.
