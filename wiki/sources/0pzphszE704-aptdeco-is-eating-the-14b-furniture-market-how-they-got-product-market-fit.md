# AptDeco is eating the $14B furniture market: How they got product market fit

## Summary

AptDeco is a managed marketplace for used furniture that simplifies the buying and selling process by integrating logistics and delivery. Founded by Reham Fagiri and Kalam Dennis, the company addresses the $14 billion furniture market by focusing on high-quality brands, reducing landfill waste, and providing a seamless, white-glove experience for users.

## Key Ideas

- Managed marketplaces create value by handling logistics, payments, and trust, which are significant pain points in peer-to-peer transactions.
- Data-driven decision-making is essential for transitioning from unscalable manual processes to scalable software-based operations.
- Owning the logistics stack is a competitive advantage that improves customer experience and brand reputation.
- Sustainability is a byproduct of efficient marketplaces, as they prevent high-quality furniture from ending up in landfills.
- Intentional diversity in hiring leads to better product outcomes and organizational resilience.
- Founders should give themselves 'permission' to start businesses early rather than waiting for years of corporate experience.

## Notable Claims

- 80% of furniture ends up in landfills. Evidence: Cited by the founders as a statistic driving their sustainability mission.
- Owning logistics is superior to outsourcing for brand quality. Evidence: Founders noted that customers blame the platform, not the third-party mover, when deliveries fail.
- AptDeco has diverted 9 million pounds of CO2 from landfills. Evidence: Internal data tracking of furniture sales versus disposal.
