## Summary

The final day of Y Combinator's Startup Investor School focuses on the history of venture capital, the evolution of early-stage fundraising, and the importance of reputation and ethics for angel investors. Guest speakers Andy Bromberg and Aaron Harris, along with legendary investor Ron Conway, discuss the shift toward liquidity, the rise of token sales (ICOs), and the necessity of being a 'good' investor to secure deal flow.

## Key Ideas

- The venture capital industry is evolving toward faster liquidity, driven by new structures like tokens and secondary markets.
- The cost to start and invest in companies is decreasing, leading to a democratization of early-stage investing.
- Reputation is a critical asset for angel investors; being helpful and ethical directly correlates to better deal flow.
- Angel investing is a long-term game that requires a portfolio approach to mitigate high failure rates.
- Founders should be evaluated primarily on character, determination, and their ability to execute, rather than just the idea.
- The 'Ron Conway principle' emphasizes that being a 'good' investor is a deliberate strategy that yields better long-term returns.

## Topics

- [[topics/history-of-venture-capital|History of Venture Capital]]: An overview of the industry from the 1940s to the present, highlighting the shift from high-barrier principal investment to democratized access.
- [[topics/token-fundraising-icos|Token Fundraising (ICOs)]]: The emergence of token sales as a new fundraising mechanism, distinct from equity, with different valuation and liquidity characteristics.
- [[topics/investor-reputation|Investor Reputation]]: The concept that being helpful, honest, and ethical is a strategic advantage that attracts the best founders and deals.
- [[topics/portfolio-construction|Portfolio Construction]]: The recommendation to invest small amounts across many companies to manage risk and increase the likelihood of hitting a 'winner'.

## Notable Claims

- 40% to 60% of every company an angel invests in will go out of business. Evidence: Ron Conway's experience over 25 years of angel investing.
- Founders who send monthly updates outperform those who disappear. Evidence: Observation of Y Combinator portfolio company behavior.
- The arc of the market bends towards liquidity. Evidence: Historical trend analysis of venture capital and the emergence of token markets.
