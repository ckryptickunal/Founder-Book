## Summary

A retrospective on the 20-year history of Y Combinator, featuring co-founder Jessica Livingston discussing the organization's humble origins, the accidental discovery of the batch model, and the long-term vision for YC as a persistent, university-like institution.

## Key Ideas

- The best startups often begin as simple projects rather than grand attempts to change the world.
- The batch model of funding startups was an accidental discovery that proved to be highly effective.
- Y Combinator aims to function as a long-lasting institution similar to a university rather than a traditional product company.
- The core mission of YC is to encourage more startups to exist to improve the world.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-philosophy|Startup Philosophy]]: The approach of starting projects that interest you rather than forcing a startup concept.
- [[topics/batch-funding-model|Batch Funding Model]]: The methodology of funding multiple startups simultaneously to facilitate learning and community.
- [[topics/institutional-longevity|Institutional Longevity]]: The goal of building an organization that persists for centuries like a university.

## Notable Claims

- The batch funding model is significantly more powerful than traditional individual angel investing. Evidence: The founders observed the effectiveness of the model during their first summer funding eight companies.
-  Evidence: The structural similarity to a university allows for institutional persistence beyond that of a typical product company.

## Quotes

> It could in principle last for hundreds of years.
