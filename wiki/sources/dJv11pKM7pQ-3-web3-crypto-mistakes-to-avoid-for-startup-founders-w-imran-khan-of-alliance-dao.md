# 3 Web3/Crypto Mistakes to Avoid for Startup Founders (w/ Imran Khan of Alliance DAO)

## Summary

In this video, Garry Tan interviews Imran Khan, co-founder of Alliance DAO, to discuss the common pitfalls that cause 99% of crypto and Web3 startups to fail. The conversation emphasizes that successful projects prioritize building functional software and achieving product-market fit over premature fundraising, token speculation, or performative decentralization.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should raise small amounts of capital initially to focus on product-market fit rather than scaling prematurely.
- Tokens should be used as a tool for user engagement and rewarding long-term community members, not as a primary monetization strategy or product substitute.
- Governance should only be prioritized once a product has achieved enough utility and mindshare to make decentralization meaningful.
- Building a real product that solves actual problems is the most critical factor for success in both Web2 and Web3.
- Premature token launches often attract the wrong type of users who are speculators rather than customers.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The necessity of building a functional product that users want before seeking large-scale investment or decentralization.
- [[topics/token-economics|Token Economics]]: The strategic use of tokens to incentivize long-term engagement rather than using them as a shortcut for fundraising or product value.
- [[topics/decentralized-governance|Decentralized Governance]]: The challenges and timing of implementing governance models in crypto projects, emphasizing that it should follow, not precede, product success.

## Notable Claims

- Raising too much money too early constrains a startup's ability to pivot and find product-market fit. Evidence: Founders who raise large amounts on just an idea often work backwards and fail to build products that solve real problems.
- Tokens should be used for stickier engagement, not as a top-of-funnel acquisition strategy. Evidence: dYdX successfully used tokens to reward long-term users after years of established product utility.
