# Premature optimization

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-premature-optimization`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2013-06-06 23:41:38
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-premature-optimization

## Summary

Sam Altman argues that startups often focus on premature optimization—such as A/B testing and incremental growth hacks—before achieving product-market fit, which is a distraction from the primary goal of building a product that users love.

## Key Ideas

- Incremental improvements only provide value if the core business is already successful.
- Startups should prioritize achieving a 'step change' in growth rather than focusing solely on 10% gains.
- The most effective and sustainable growth strategy is word-of-mouth, which stems from building a great product.
- Founders often waste mental energy on optimization when they should be focused on product-market fit and market size.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Entrepreneur, investor, and author of the essay.

## Topics

- [[topics/premature-optimization|Premature Optimization]]: The act of focusing on incremental improvements before the core product has achieved sufficient user love or market fit.
- [[topics/startup-growth|Startup Growth]]: The transition from incremental gains to exponential growth through product quality rather than marketing tactics.

## Notable Claims

- Incremental optimizations are only useful if the business is already working. Evidence: If no users love the product, optimization efforts like A/B testing or email campaigns are a waste of time.
- Word-of-mouth is the most sustainable and cheapest form of growth. Evidence: Building a great product naturally leads to user-driven growth, which is more effective than paid acquisition.

## Quotes

> It’s dangerous to spend all your mental energy on incremental improvements when what you really need is a step change.
> Build a great product, and you will be able to make it grow.
