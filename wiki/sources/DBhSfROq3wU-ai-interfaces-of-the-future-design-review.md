## Summary

In this Y Combinator design review, host Aaron and guest Raphael Shad (creator of Notion Calendar) explore the evolution of AI user interfaces, moving beyond simple chat-based interactions toward specialized, verb-oriented, and multimodal experiences that keep humans in the loop.

## Key Ideas

- AI interfaces are shifting from static 2D 'noun-based' layouts to dynamic 'verb-based' workflows.
- Latency is a critical component of the user experience; showing latency (e.g., milliseconds) helps users build intuition and trust.
- Visual feedback (multimodal cues) is essential when voice AI is used to ensure the user knows the system is listening or processing.
- Canvas-based interfaces are emerging as a powerful way to visualize and control complex, multi-step AI agent workflows.
- Trading off high-fidelity output for immediate, low-resolution previews allows for faster iterative design.
- Adaptive UIs can dynamically change buttons and controls based on the specific context of the content being processed.
- The current era of AI interface design is comparable to the 'touch-first' revolution of 2010, requiring a complete reimagining of software components.

## Topics

- [[topics/human-in-the-loop-design|Human-in-the-loop Design]]: The necessity of keeping users involved in AI processes to validate, refine, and control outputs.
- [[topics/adaptive-interfaces|Adaptive Interfaces]]: Software UIs that dynamically change their controls based on the specific context of the data being handled.
- [[topics/latency-as-interface|Latency as Interface]]: The concept that the speed of an AI response is a core design element that dictates the perceived naturalness of the interaction.

## Notable Claims

- Canvas-based interfaces will become the standard for controlling and monitoring autonomous AI agents. Evidence: The complexity of multi-step agent workflows requires a visual, non-linear representation that traditional document-based UIs cannot provide.
- Blurring video during generation is a superior design pattern for AI video tools. Evidence: It provides immediate feedback to the user while the high-fidelity, computationally expensive generation happens in the background.
