## Summary

Andy and Heather from Y Combinator's Continuity team provide actionable advice for job seekers in a competitive market, emphasizing the importance of networking, due diligence on company viability, and effective self-marketing.

## Key Ideas

- The current job market has shifted from a candidate's market to a more competitive environment due to layoffs and slower growth.
- Early-stage startups are actively hiring and often offer greater ownership and growth potential than large tech companies.
- Candidates should perform due diligence by asking about product-market fit, runway, attrition, and company risks.
- Networking and direct outreach to leaders are more effective than relying solely on job applications.
- Candidates should be transparent about being part of a reduction in force (RIF) and focus on their impact and experience.
- Senior leaders should leverage relationships with VC Talent Partners to find roles.

## Notable Claims

- Startups are not necessarily more volatile than large companies. Evidence: Many early-stage companies are currently well-funded and hiring aggressively, while large companies are undergoing significant layoffs.
- Asking about work-life balance does not make a candidate look unmotivated. Evidence: It is a common question that helps set clear expectations and boundaries for both the candidate and the employer.
- Candidates should always negotiate their offers. Evidence: Companies often have flexibility in compensation packages, and 'don't ask, don't get' is a standard professional motto.
