## Summary

Kirsty Nathoo, CFO of Y Combinator, explains the mechanics of SAFEs (Simple Agreement for Future Equity), how they function in early-stage fundraising, and the critical importance of founders tracking their cap table dilution throughout the company lifecycle.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must actively manage their cap table and understand dilution at every stage.
- SAFEs are not debt; they are simple instruments for future equity conversion without interest or maturity dates.
- Post-money SAFEs provide a clearer, more predictable understanding of dilution compared to pre-money versions.
- The formula for investor ownership is: Amount Raised / Post-Money Valuation Cap.
- Over-optimizing for high valuation caps is often counterproductive and provides minimal long-term benefit to founders.
- Priced rounds involve three steps: SAFE conversion, option pool increase, and new money investment.

## Topics

- [[topics/dilution|Dilution]]: The reduction in ownership percentage for existing shareholders as new shares are issued to investors or employees.
- [[topics/cap-table-management|Cap Table Management]]: The responsibility of founders to track ownership stakes and understand the impact of various financing rounds.
- [[topics/valuation-caps|Valuation Caps]]: A mechanism in convertible instruments that sets a maximum valuation at which an investor's money converts into equity.
- [[topics/priced-equity-rounds|Priced Equity Rounds]]: A formal round of financing where a specific valuation is set and shares are issued to investors.

## Notable Claims

- Founders are solely responsible for understanding their cap table, not their lawyers. Evidence: Founders must know how much of the company they have sold to avoid being surprised by dilution at later stages.
- Post-money SAFEs make it easier for founders to understand dilution. Evidence: The formula (Amount Raised / Post-Money Valuation) clearly defines the percentage sold, unlike pre-money versions.
- Over-optimizing for valuation caps is rarely worth the effort. Evidence: Small changes in valuation caps result in negligible differences in final founder ownership percentages.
