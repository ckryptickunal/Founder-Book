## Summary

Dalton Caldwell, a partner at Y Combinator, explains that 'luck' in startups is not a random occurrence but a result of increasing one's 'surface area' for success by moving faster than competitors and iterating rapidly through ideas.

## Key Ideas

- Luck is created by increasing the frequency and speed of your actions.
- Moving faster allows startups to talk to more customers, write more code, and pivot more effectively.
- Successful pivots, such as those by Brex, Retool, and Magic, were only possible because the founders iterated through multiple ideas quickly.
- Pre-company luck is generated by networking, learning from others, and stepping outside of one's comfort zone.
- Building and shipping prototypes, even small ones, increases the likelihood of discovering a successful product.
- Founders should balance playing to their strengths with the need for technical competence when entering new domains.

- [[topics/surface-area-of-luck|Surface Area of Luck]]: The concept that by increasing the number of attempts and interactions, one increases the probability of a positive outcome.
- [[topics/founder-mindset|Founder Mindset]]: The importance of speed, discomfort, and proactive networking in early-stage ventures.

## Notable Claims

- Founders who move the fastest are the ones who get the luckiest. Evidence: Case studies of Brex, Retool, and Magic, which all pivoted from initial ideas to successful products through rapid iteration.
- You can increase your luck by building and shipping prototypes frequently. Evidence: The history of Facebook, where Mark Zuckerberg created numerous small projects and plugins before finding the core idea for Facebook.

## Quotes

> The way to create luck is to move much faster than everyone else and to give yourselves as many opportunity for that lucky thing to happen to you.
> The founders that get the luckiest are actually the founders that move the fastest to give them as much surface area as possible.
> You can dramatically increase the chance that you will get lucky if you just make things.
