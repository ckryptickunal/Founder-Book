## Summary

Eli Schwartz, former Director of SEO and Growth at SurveyMonkey, discusses the enduring importance of SEO in 2018, emphasizing a user-centric approach over technical gaming. He highlights the shift from paid acquisition to organic growth, the necessity of proper site architecture, and the importance of creating high-quality, relevant content that serves user intent.

## Key Ideas

- SEO is an untapped, high-return channel that becomes more critical as paid media costs rise.
- Site architecture is foundational; it must be crawlable and scalable from the start.
- Content should be written for users, not crawlers; raw text is essential for search engine understanding.
- Link building should be a byproduct of creating interesting, high-quality content rather than a spammy, transactional activity.
- Mobile optimization is important, but desktop remains critical for high-intent, complex purchasing decisions.
- International SEO is best handled via subdirectories rather than separate domains to leverage existing site authority.
- Avoid measuring success by keyword rankings; focus on traffic, conversions, and business impact.

## Notable Claims

- Buying links is a 'no-no' and can lead to penalties. Evidence: Google's guidelines explicitly advise against link schemes; reporting mechanisms exist for competitors.
- Meta keywords are no longer useful for SEO. Evidence: Modern search engines have advanced natural language processing and do not rely on meta tags to understand content.
- Mobile-first does not mean mobile-only. Evidence: Users still utilize desktop devices for high-value, complex purchasing decisions and research.
- GDPR is beneficial for SEO. Evidence: It forces better site architecture through mandatory privacy policy links and reduces reliance on paid tracking.
