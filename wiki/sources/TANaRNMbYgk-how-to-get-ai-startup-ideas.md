# How To Get AI Startup Ideas

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses strategies for identifying high-potential AI startup ideas, emphasizing that founders should move beyond 'hackathon' concepts and instead leverage deep domain expertise, internships, or direct observation of industry problems to build meaningful, scalable AI solutions.

## Key Ideas

- Avoid 'lazy' hackathon ideas; focus on solving hard, ambitious problems.
- Leverage existing domain expertise or past internships to identify unique gaps.
- Get out of the house: observe real-world industries, shadow workers, or take temporary jobs to uncover hidden inefficiencies.
- AI agents are significantly more valuable than traditional SaaS because they can replace expensive human labor.
- Don't be discouraged by competition; technical superiority can often overcome crowded markets.
- Founders should act like researchers, staying at the bleeding edge of technology to notice what is missing.
- Use personal connections (family, friends, former colleagues) to gain access to underserved industries.

## Notable Claims

- The best startup ideas are often hard to build. Evidence: Founders often gravitate toward easy hackathon ideas, but the most successful companies solve difficult, non-trivial problems.
- Internships are a primary source of billion-dollar startup ideas. Evidence: Many YC founders trace their successful ideas back to specific problems they encountered during internships at bleeding-edge companies.
- AI makes niche B2B problems more valuable. Evidence: Automating a specific task for a dentist or a car loan lender is now worth significantly more because AI can replace a human salary rather than just providing a tool.
