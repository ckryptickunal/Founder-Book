## Summary

A compilation of 60 Y Combinator startup founders sharing the diverse and often serendipitous ways they met their co-founders, ranging from childhood friendships and academic settings to professional networks and dedicated matching platforms.

## Key Ideas

- Co-founder relationships are often built on long-term trust, shared history, or complementary skill sets.
- Professional environments like previous workplaces or internships are common breeding grounds for startup partnerships.
- Shared values and passions, such as decarbonization, can serve as a catalyst for starting a company.
- Modern tools like the YC Co-founder Matching platform are becoming standard for finding partners outside of one's immediate social circle.
- The decision to co-found is often driven by mutual encouragement and the need for a partner to provide the confidence to take the leap into entrepreneurship.

## Notable Claims

- Having no overlapping skills can be a benefit for co-founders. Evidence: Founders noted that one person handling technical aspects while the other handles business/marketing creates a perfect match.
- The YC co-founder matching platform functions similarly to dating apps. Evidence: Founders described the process of swiping and finding someone who shares the same energy for building.
