## Summary

Sam Altman compiles a decade of media headlines from 2007 to 2015 that incorrectly predicted the bursting of a tech bubble, illustrating the persistent skepticism surrounding Silicon Valley valuations.

## Key Ideas

- Predictions of a tech bubble have been a constant theme in media for over a decade.
- Market skeptics have consistently underestimated the growth and valuation potential of major tech companies like Facebook.
- The narrative of a 'tech bubble' is often driven by disbelief in high valuations for companies that have not yet achieved traditional profitability.
- Historical data shows that bubble warnings are frequently premature or inaccurate.
