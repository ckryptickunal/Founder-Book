# Default Alive or Default Dead?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-default-alive-or-default-dead`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: October 2015
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-default-alive-or-default-dead

## Summary

Paul Graham explains the critical startup metric of being 'default alive' versus 'default dead'—whether a company will reach profitability based on current expenses and growth rates—and warns against the dangers of over-hiring and relying on external funding to mask poor product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Founders must regularly calculate if their startup is 'default alive' (profitable on current trajectory) or 'default dead' (will run out of money).
- The 'fatal pinch' occurs when a company is default dead, has slow growth, and lacks the time to pivot.
- Hiring too fast is the primary cause of death for startups that have raised capital.
- Investors are fickle; relying on future fundraising is a dangerous plan A.
- Large staff sizes are often an effect of growth, not a cause of it.
- Founders should focus on product evolution and growth through non-scalable methods rather than premature scaling.

## Notable Claims

- Hiring too fast is the biggest killer of startups that raise money. Evidence: Founders often over-hire to compensate for lack of product-market fit, which accelerates cash burn and makes the business harder to fix.
- There is little connection between how much a startup spends and how fast it grows. Evidence: Growth is primarily driven by the product hitting a significant user need, not by the size of the staff.
