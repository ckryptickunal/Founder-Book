## Summary

Brian Donohue, former General Manager of Instapaper and current Pinterest engineer, discusses the history of Instapaper, its acquisition by Pinterest, and his efforts to spin it back out into an independent, sustainable business.

## Key Ideas

- Instapaper was created by Marco Arment in 2008 to solve early mobile web pain points like slow networks and poor readability.
- The market for 'read-later' services shifted from a broad consumer need to a niche for voracious media consumers as mobile web and reading apps improved.
- Instapaper struggled to compete as a paid app against free alternatives like Pocket, necessitating a shift to a freemium model.
- Acquisitions by large companies can lead to 'maintenance mode' or shutdown if the product does not align with the parent company's top-line metrics.
- Donohue successfully negotiated the spin-out of Instapaper from Pinterest to ensure its long-term survival as a self-sustaining, small-team business.
- The business model for the independent Instapaper focuses on longevity and profitability rather than rapid growth or exit.

## Notable Claims

- The paid app download model was a major barrier to Instapaper's growth. Evidence: Data from a 'Free App of the Week' promotion showed more downloads in one week than in the previous two years.
- Instapaper can be self-sustaining with a small team. Evidence: The product was historically run by one person and can cover server costs through a modest subscription base.
