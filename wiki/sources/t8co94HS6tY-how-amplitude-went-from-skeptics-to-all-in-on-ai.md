# How Amplitude Went From Skeptics to “All In” on AI

## Metadata

## Summary

Spencer Skates, CEO and co-founder of Amplitude, discusses the company's strategic pivot to become an AI-native analytics platform. He details the challenges of reorienting a large, established organization, the importance of 'founder mode' leadership, and the necessity of training teams to understand AI capabilities rather than just following customer requests for 'faster horses.'

## Key Ideas

- Incumbent companies struggle with AI adoption due to internal skepticism and a reliance on traditional, slow-moving SAS delivery loops.
- Successful AI transformation requires a 'bottoms-up' approach where leadership trains the entire organization on model capabilities.
- AI-native product development requires a shift from asking customers what they want to understanding what is technologically possible.
- Founder-led transformation is essential in large companies to overcome inertia and cultural resistance.
- The 'AI visibility' market is becoming commoditized; real value lies in downstream business applications.
- Persistence through the 'rational quitting point' (1-2 years in) is the primary filter for successful founders.

## Notable Claims

- Google is the worst B2B company of all time. Evidence: Spencer Skates cites their institutional slowness and conservative nature as reasons for their vulnerability to disruption.
- AI visibility features are becoming commoditized. Evidence: Amplitude built similar functionality in a few months and gave it away for free, doubling signups.
- The best advice for founders is to be clear about what they are trying to learn. Evidence: Reflections on his own journey and the common failure of founders to define their 'why'.
