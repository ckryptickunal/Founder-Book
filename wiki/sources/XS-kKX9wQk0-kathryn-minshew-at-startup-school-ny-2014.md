## Summary

Kathryn Minshew, CEO and founder of The Muse, shares her startup journey at Y Combinator's Startup School 2014. She discusses the challenges of early-stage growth, the importance of persistence, the necessity of focusing on user data over investor hype, and strategies for building a team and product with limited resources.

## Key Ideas

- Startups are often a 'work in progress' and founders should focus on evolving from 'suck' to 'suck-less'.
- Validate ideas with real users rather than relying on friends and family for feedback.
- Persistence is crucial; many successful deals and partnerships require multiple attempts.
- Focus on building a team by understanding individual motivations beyond just salary.
- Don't believe the startup hype; compare your progress to your own data, not others' highlight reels.
- Prioritize integrity and shared values when choosing co-founders and team members.

## Notable Claims

- Most startups are a work in progress and founders should not be discouraged by early failures. Evidence: Minshew shares her own experience of having a previous startup with flat growth and a 'piece of crap' first version of The Muse.
- Content marketing is an effective way to build an audience for a marketplace startup. Evidence: The Muse used career advice articles to attract users before they had a large volume of job listings.
