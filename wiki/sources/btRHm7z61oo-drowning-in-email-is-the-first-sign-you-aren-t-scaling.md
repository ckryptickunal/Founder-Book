# Drowning in email is the first sign you aren't scaling.

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the critical transition founders must make from being creative, open-minded 'star players' to conscientious, delegating CEOs as their startup moves from pre-product market fit to scaling.

## Key Ideas

- Conscientiousness is the most critical trait for long-term startup survival.
- Founders must transition from an 'open/creative' mindset (pre-product market fit) to a 'conscientious/execution' mindset (post-product market fit).
- Being overwhelmed by email is a primary indicator that a founder is failing to scale and has become a bottleneck.
- Scaling requires shifting from being a 'star player' to a 'player-coach' who delegates and holds others accountable.
- Openness is positively correlated with early-stage success but can be anti-correlated with long-term survival.

- Conscientiousness is positively correlated with business survival over eight years. Evidence: A 2004 study from Bucknell University.
- Openness is anti-correlated with long-term business success. Evidence: A 2004 study from Bucknell University.
- Founders who cannot scale often become bottlenecks, leading to a plateau and eventual decline in company performance. Evidence: Observation of startup growth patterns and decision-making quality.
