# Keep Your Identity Small

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-keep-your-identity-small`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: February 2009
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-keep-your-identity-small

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that unproductive, heated debates—often labeled as 'religious wars'—occur when topics become tied to an individual's identity. He suggests that to think more clearly and engage in fruitful discussions, one should minimize the number of labels and beliefs they incorporate into their personal identity.

## Key Ideas

- Discussions degenerate when they become tied to personal identity rather than objective analysis.
- The 'religious war' phenomenon is not inherent to specific topics like politics or religion, but to the participants' emotional attachment to those topics.
- Identity-based thinking acts as a barrier to clear reasoning and objective truth.
- To improve intellectual clarity, one should keep their identity as small as possible by avoiding rigid labels.
- Scientific identity is an exception because it is defined by a commitment to evidence rather than a commitment to specific, unchangeable beliefs.

## Topics

- [[topics/identity-politics|Identity Politics]]: The tendency for individuals to adopt beliefs as core components of their self-image, which prevents objective discourse.
- [[topics/intellectual-honesty|Intellectual Honesty]]: The ability to evaluate information without the bias of pre-existing identity-based commitments.

## Notable Claims

- The more labels you have for yourself, the dumber they make you. Evidence: Labels create rigid identity boundaries that prevent individuals from considering evidence that contradicts their established beliefs.
- A scientist's identity is a 'placeholder' rather than a restrictive label. Evidence: Being a scientist commits one to following evidence wherever it leads, rather than committing to a specific set of unchangeable conclusions.

## Quotes

> The more labels you have for yourself, the dumber they make you.
> You can have a fruitful discussion about a topic only if it doesn't engage the identities of any of the participants.
> Considering yourself a scientist is equivalent to putting a sign in a cupboard saying 'this cupboard must be kept empty.'
