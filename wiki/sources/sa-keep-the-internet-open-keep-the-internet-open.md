# Keep the Internet Open

## Summary

Sam Altman argues for the preservation of net neutrality, framing the internet as a public good that requires common carrier regulation to ensure a level playing field for future innovation and startups.

## Key Ideas

- The internet should be treated as a public utility similar to electricity or telephone services.
- Net neutrality is essential for maintaining a meritocratic environment for startups.
- Incumbent tech companies have a responsibility to support the openness that enabled their own success.
- Charging different rates based on service relationships with ISPs is discriminatory and harmful to innovation.
- Historical precedents like the 1936 Electrical and Telephone Federal Act and the Carnegie library system support the concept of universal access.

## Topics

- [[topics/net-neutrality|Net Neutrality]]: The principle that ISPs should treat all data on the internet equally, without discriminating or charging differentially by user, content, or website.
- [[topics/common-carrier-regulation|Common Carrier Regulation]]: A regulatory framework that requires service providers to offer their services to the public on a non-discriminatory basis.
- [[topics/public-goods|Public Goods]]: The concept that certain services, like the internet, are essential for society and should be accessible to all as a basic right.

## Notable Claims

- Openness on the internet is the primary driver of the recent wave of software innovation. Evidence: Altman notes that startups succeed by merit rather than the regulatory weight of incumbency.
- Charging different services different rates based on ISP relationships is analogous to an electric company charging different rates based on appliance manufacturers. Evidence: Altman uses this comparison to illustrate the absurdity and unfairness of non-neutral internet pricing.

## Quotes

> The internet is a public good, and I believe access should be a basic right.
> You wouldn't accept your electric company charging you different rates depending on the manufacturer of each of your appliances.
> Openness is what made the recent wave of innovation happen.
