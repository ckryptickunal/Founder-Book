# See Randomness

## Summary

Paul Graham explores the human tendency to project purpose and self-centeredness onto the universe, arguing that intellectual progress requires discarding the assumption that humans are the center of existence.

## Key Ideas

- The history of ideas is a process of de-centering humans from the universe.
- Evolutionary biology, particularly the 'selfish gene' theory, challenges the notion of human agency.
- The 'blind spot' of human ego prevents us from seeing objective reality.
- Applying the principle of de-centering to everyday life helps avoid unnecessary conflict and misinterpretation.
- Randomness is often a more accurate explanation for events than intentional purpose.

## Notable Claims

- Humans are not the protagonists of the universe; we are vehicles for our genes. Evidence: Reference to Richard Dawkins' 'The Selfish Gene' theory.
- Preferring theories that do not center on yourself leads to more accurate conclusions. Evidence: The application of the principle to everyday scenarios like housemate conflicts.
