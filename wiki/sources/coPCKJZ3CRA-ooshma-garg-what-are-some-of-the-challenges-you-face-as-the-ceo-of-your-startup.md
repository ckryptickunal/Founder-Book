# Ooshma Garg: What are some of the challenges you face as the CEO of your startup?

## Metadata

## Summary

Ooshma Garg, CEO of Gobble, discusses the evolution of a founder's role from hands-on 'maker' to meeting-heavy management, highlighting the emotional highs and lows of the startup journey.

## Key Ideas

- The transition from 'maker time' to 'manager time' is a significant challenge for startup CEOs.
- Early-stage lows involve feelings of obscurity and isolation, requiring resilience and creative problem-solving.
- Maintaining a direct connection with customers is essential for personal satisfaction and long-term business success.
- Prioritizing strategic time is necessary to prevent being overwhelmed by administrative tasks.

## Entities

- [[entities/ooshma-garg|Ooshma Garg]] (person): Founder and CEO of Gobble.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): Startup accelerator and media channel.

## Topics

- [[topics/founder-psychology|Founder Psychology]]: The emotional toll and shifting priorities experienced by startup founders as their companies grow.
- [[topics/time-management|Time Management]]: The struggle to balance operational meetings with strategic 'maker' time.

## Notable Claims

- Direct customer interaction is a key driver of founder happiness. Evidence: Garg notes that she feels unhappy when she gets too far from the customer and feels gratified when meeting them in the wild.
- Long-term financial success is a byproduct of prioritizing customer experience. Evidence: Garg states that if you care about the customer and their experience, the money will follow.

## Quotes

> I have to fight for strategic time and maker time.
> If you care about the customer and that experience long term all the money will come.
