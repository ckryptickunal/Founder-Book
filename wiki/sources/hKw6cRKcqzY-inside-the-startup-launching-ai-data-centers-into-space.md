## Summary

StarCloud (formerly Lumen Orbit) is a startup aiming to build orbital data centers to solve terrestrial energy and water constraints. By leveraging declining launch costs, they seek to move AI compute to space, utilizing solar energy and deep-space infrared radiation for cooling, with the goal of eventually hosting massive, 40-megawatt data centers.

## Key Ideas

- Terrestrial data centers face severe limitations regarding energy grid capacity and fresh water consumption for cooling.
- Space-based data centers can utilize constant solar energy and dissipate heat via infrared radiation into the vacuum of space.
- Declining launch costs, driven by reusable rockets from companies like SpaceX and Stoke Space, make orbital infrastructure economically viable.
- The company's core IP involves large, low-mass, deployable radiators for thermal management.
- Adopting a 'hard' company strategy—tackling high technical risk—can simplify other business aspects like hiring and fundraising.
- The company successfully launched the first satellite carrying an Nvidia H100 GPU to test thermal and radiation shielding.

## Notable Claims

- Most new data centers will eventually be built in space. Evidence: Vision based on the scalability of space infrastructure versus terrestrial grid and water limitations.
- Space-based data centers require zero fresh water for cooling. Evidence: Heat is dissipated via infrared radiation into deep space rather than evaporation.
- It is easier to build a 'hard' company than an 'easy' one. Evidence: High technical difficulty attracts better talent and simplifies fundraising and market positioning.
