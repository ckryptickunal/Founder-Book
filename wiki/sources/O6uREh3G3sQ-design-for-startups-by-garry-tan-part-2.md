## Summary

Garry Tan provides practical, actionable advice for startup founders on when and how to hire designers, how to conduct effective design interviews, and how to leverage design as a core component of startup success.

## Key Ideas

- Design hiring should scale with the company: co-founders handle design early, consultants at seed, and dedicated teams by Series B.
- Designers are often intimidated by engineering-heavy teams; founders must create a supportive environment for them.
- Interviewing designers should focus on communication, empathy, and problem-solving rather than just portfolio aesthetics.
- Founders should avoid 'whiteboard-first' designers who don't ask about user needs or problems first.
- Startups compete against obscurity, not just competitors; being open and human-centric is a competitive advantage.
- Controversy can be a powerful marketing tool if it aligns with the brand identity.

## Notable Claims

- Startups compete against obscurity, not competitors. Evidence: The primary challenge for a new product is being discovered and understood by the target audience.
- Designers should not start drawing on a whiteboard immediately when asked a design question. Evidence: Doing so indicates a failure to prioritize user needs and problem definition over execution.
