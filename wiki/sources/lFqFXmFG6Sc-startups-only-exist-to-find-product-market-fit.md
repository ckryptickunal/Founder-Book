# "Startups only exist to find product-market fit."

## Metadata

## Summary

A Y Combinator perspective on the fundamental purpose of early-stage startups, arguing that they should focus exclusively on achieving product-market fit rather than mimicking the organizational structures of established enterprises.

## Key Ideas

- Startups are not miniature versions of big companies.
- The sole objective of a startup is to reach product-market fit.
- Prematurely adopting corporate functions (offices, unnecessary departments) creates distractions and hinders growth.
- Founders often build 'grown-up' structures to feel validated rather than to solve actual business needs.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The primary and singular goal for early-stage startups before scaling.
- [[topics/startup-operations|Startup Operations]]: The lean approach to resource allocation and team structure in early-stage ventures.

## Notable Claims

- Startups that mimic big company structures before achieving product-market fit perform worse. Evidence: The speaker notes that managing unnecessary roles creates distractions and consumes resources that should be dedicated to product-market fit.

## Quotes

> Startups only exist to reach product market fit like until then nothing else really matters.
> When I see people making mistakes, they're looking towards the bigger companies... and they're like, well, if I had this... I am now feeling more validated that my company's more grown up.
