## Summary

Domonique Fines, Director of Events at Y Combinator, discusses her non-linear career path from aspiring attorney to tech event planner. She shares her experiences in building community, her commitment to increasing diversity in the startup ecosystem through outreach, and her personal strategies for managing burnout and professional growth.

## Key Ideas

- Career paths do not need to be linear; it is acceptable to pivot and figure things out as you go.
- Proactive problem-solving is a core skill; if you see a gap, you should be the one to step up and address it.
- Diversity in tech requires intentional outreach to underrepresented communities and demystifying the startup world.
- Burnout management is essential for long-term success; taking intentional breaks and 'shutdown' time is necessary.
- The 'imposter syndrome' can be mitigated by being honest about what you don't know and seeking answers from others.

## Notable Claims

- Starting small is the most effective way to create larger change in community outreach. Evidence: Domonique's experience working with local Oakland schools and organizations to demystify tech.
- You do not need a traditional tech background to be successful in the tech industry. Evidence: Domonique's own career trajectory from law firm office manager to YC Director of Events.
