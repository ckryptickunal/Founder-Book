## Summary

Avni Patel Thompson, founder of Poppy, discusses her transition from a traditional corporate career at companies like P&G, Adidas, and Starbucks to the startup world. She emphasizes the importance of 'getting started' through small, actionable tests, the necessity of validating ideas with actual customer payments rather than just feedback, and the challenges of balancing dual-career parenting with entrepreneurship.

## Key Ideas

- The 'four-week test' is a powerful framework to validate a startup idea without needing significant capital or technical resources.
- True validation comes from customers paying for a product, not from friends or family offering polite encouragement.
- Growth is the ultimate metric that solves most startup problems, including fundraising and finding co-founders.
- Parenting and entrepreneurship share parallels in requiring resilience, clear boundaries, and the ability to 'do anything' to ensure the survival of the mission.
- Founders should focus on solving a specific, painful problem for a target user base before worrying about scaling to a billion-dollar vision.
- Building a startup requires persistence through rejection and the willingness to take big, calculated risks.
