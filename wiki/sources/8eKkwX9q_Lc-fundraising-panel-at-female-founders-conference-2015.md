## Summary

A panel discussion from the 2015 Female Founders Conference featuring Y Combinator alumni sharing candid, practical advice on the realities of fundraising. The discussion covers the difference between seed and Series A rounds, the importance of momentum, managing investor relationships, and the psychological challenges of startup life.

## Key Ideas

- Fundraising is a continuous, manufactured process that requires momentum and a high volume of meetings.
- The 'perfect' narrative seen in press releases often masks the messy, difficult reality of building a company.
- Seed fundraising is about personal persuasion and speed, while Series A is akin to enterprise sales, requiring deeper relationship building.
- Surviving as a company is more important than adhering to 'ideal' fundraising rules, such as avoiding party rounds.
- Transparency with the team about both good and bad news during the fundraising process builds trust.
- Product-market fit is often a result of luck, but hustle is required to capitalize on it.

## Notable Claims

- Fundraising is a numbers game. Evidence: Panelists suggest treating it like a sales funnel by maximizing meetings to iterate quickly and get to 'yes'.
- Seed rounds are about personal persuasion, while Series A is about enterprise sales. Evidence: Nancy Douyon notes that Series A investors need to defend their investment to partners, requiring a more formal, relationship-based approach.
- Party rounds are acceptable if they ensure survival. Evidence: Danielle Morrill argues that while party rounds are often criticized, surviving to the next stage is the priority.
