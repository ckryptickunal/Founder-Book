# Funding for COVID-19 Projects

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-funding-for-covid-19-projects`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2020-03-15 16:31:23
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-funding-for-covid-19-projects

## Summary

Sam Altman announces an initiative to provide funding for startups and projects addressing the COVID-19 pandemic, specifically focusing on supply chain manufacturing, drug screening, and novel vaccine or therapeutic development.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritizing engineering solutions for rapid production of medical supplies like ventilators and PPE.
- Focusing on high-risk, high-reward research areas that large pharmaceutical companies may overlook.
- Transitioning from public coordination methods to direct communication for project vetting.
- Seeking specialized contract research organizations for viral challenge studies.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Investor and former president of Y Combinator
- [[entities/sars-cov-2|SARS-CoV-2]] (other): The virus responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic

## Topics

- [[topics/pandemic-response|Pandemic Response]]: Mobilizing private capital to accelerate medical supply production and scientific research during a global health crisis.
- [[topics/venture-philanthropy|Venture Philanthropy]]: Using startup investment models to solve urgent, non-traditional engineering and scientific problems.

## Notable Claims

- The production of ventilators and PPE is primarily an engineering challenge rather than a scientific one. Evidence: Altman suggests that rapid repurposing and creative manufacturing processes are the primary requirements for scaling these supplies.

## Quotes

> I’m trying to fund startups/projects helping with COVID-19, because it’s basically the one thing I know how to do that can help.
> And of course, I think the best thing to do is still to get people to stay home.
