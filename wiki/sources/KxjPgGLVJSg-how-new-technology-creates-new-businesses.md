# How New Technology Creates New Businesses

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell and Michael Seibel from Y Combinator discuss how major technological shifts, such as the rise of AI, create unique opportunities for new founders to build businesses by leveraging new tools, often before these trends are recognized by traditional institutions.

## Key Ideas

- Technological shifts reduce the cost of starting a business by up to 10x, enabling new market opportunities.
- Early adopters who master new tools can create significant value and wealth before competition catches up.
- Traditional education systems lag behind technological innovation; learning happens best through communities of 'weirdos' on the internet.
- New technology empowers individuals to be self-employed and create value without needing to work for large corporations.
- Opportunities range from small-scale lifestyle businesses to massive, venture-backed companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/technological-leverage|Technological Leverage]]: How new tools allow individuals to perform tasks that previously required large teams or significant capital.
- [[topics/entrepreneurial-opportunity|Entrepreneurial Opportunity]]: The concept of 'Green Field' markets where early adopters face little competition when a new technology emerges.
- [[topics/self-employment|Self-Employment]]: The shift toward more people using technology to run independent businesses rather than seeking traditional employment.

## Notable Claims

- New technologies reduce the cost of starting a business by up to 10x. Evidence: Historical examples like eBay, Airbnb, and cloud computing lowering barriers to entry.
- Schools and universities are ineffective at teaching emerging technological trends. Evidence: The speakers note that institutions typically teach curricula from 10-20 years ago.
- New industries are created by 'weirdos on the internet' who experiment early. Evidence: Observation of early adopters on platforms like Reddit and niche internet communities.
