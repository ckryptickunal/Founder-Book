# The Most Important Decision is Getting Started – Laura Behrens Wu

## Summary

Laura Behrens Wu, founder and CEO of Shippo, shares her journey of building a shipping API company, emphasizing that the most critical step for any entrepreneur is simply getting started. She discusses the realities of fundraising, the importance of resilience, building a supportive team, and navigating the challenges of being a female founder in a male-dominated industry.

## Key Ideas

- The most important decision for a founder is to start, even if the initial idea is not perfect.
- Focus on solving 'painkillers' (real, urgent problems) rather than 'vitamins' (nice-to-have features).
- Fundraising is a process of persistence; expect many rejections and use them to refine your pitch.
- Company success is not equivalent to personal self-worth; maintain perspective during the highs and lows.
- Be authentic and unapologetic about your leadership style and company culture.
- Prioritize self-care and build a support network of mentors, co-founders, and peers.
- Systemic change in the tech industry requires female founders to lead by example and build inclusive cultures.
