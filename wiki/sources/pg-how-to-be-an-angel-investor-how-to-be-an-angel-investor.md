# How to Be an Angel Investor

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-how-to-be-an-angel-investor`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: March 2009
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-how-to-be-an-angel-investor

## Summary

Paul Graham provides a practical guide for aspiring angel investors, emphasizing that success comes from picking the right startups rather than mastering complex deal mechanics. He highlights the importance of empathy for founders, the value of syndicates, and the necessity of being a supportive, decisive, and ethical partner.

## Key Ideas

- Angel investing success is driven by picking the right startups, not by negotiating deal terms.
- The best way to start is by joining syndicates led by experienced investors.
- Valuation is often a reflection of bargaining power rather than a precise science.
- Investors should prioritize betting on 'relentlessly resourceful' people over specific market trends.
- Decisiveness is a critical trait; investors should avoid stringing founders along.
- Angel investing is not a zero-sum game; helping founders succeed benefits the investor.
- New investors should treat their first few investments as an educational expense by investing small, painless amounts.

## Notable Claims

- The median professional VC loses money. Evidence: Graham notes this as a surprising fact learned while working on Y Combinator.
- There is no rational way to value an early-stage startup. Evidence: Valuation is determined by bargaining power and the startup's need for capital.
- Angels are the limiting reagent in startup formation. Evidence: The scarcity of serious angel investors makes them essential to the Silicon Valley ecosystem.

## Quotes

> When you hear people talking about a successful angel investor, they're not saying 'He got a 4x liquidation preference.' They're saying 'He invested in Google.'
> What 'Make something people want' is for startups, 'Pick the right startups' is for investors.
> Good founders make things happen the way they want. Which is not to say they force things to happen in a predefined way. Good founders have a healthy respect for reality. But they are relentlessly resourceful.
> Angel investing is not a business where you make money by screwing people over. Startups create wealth, and creating wealth is not a zero sum game.
