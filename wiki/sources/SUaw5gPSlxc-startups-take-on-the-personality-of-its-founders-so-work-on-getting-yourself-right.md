# Startups take on the personality of its founders, so work on getting yourself right.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `SUaw5gPSlxc`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-11-27T23:18:09Z

## Summary

The speaker reflects on how personal psychological health directly impacts startup success, arguing that founders must address their internal issues through therapy and coaching to avoid projecting their 'unexamined demons' onto their companies.

## Key Ideas

- Startups inevitably mirror the personality and psychological state of their founders.
- Unresolved personal issues can lead to startup failure, as seen in the speaker's experience with Posterous.
- Founders should prioritize therapy and coaching early in their journey rather than waiting until they are managing teams.
- Investing in personal mental health is more valuable than spending money on superficial business expenses.

## Notable Claims

- Startups take on the personality of their founders. Evidence: The speaker cites this as a common observation and personal experience where their own unresolved issues negatively impacted their startup.
- Addressing personal psychological issues early is critical for startup leadership. Evidence: The speaker notes that waiting until they were running teams and making hard decisions was 'almost a little bit too late'.
