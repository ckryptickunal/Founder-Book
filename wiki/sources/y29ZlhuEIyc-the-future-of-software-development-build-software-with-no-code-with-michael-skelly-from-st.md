## Summary

Michael Skelly, founder of Stacker, discusses how his no-code platform enables non-technical users to transform spreadsheets into functional, multi-user business applications, effectively democratizing software development and operational innovation.

## Key Ideas

- No-code tools allow non-technical operators to build custom software without relying on engineering teams.
- The best software solutions often arise from 'scratching your own itch' by identifying manual, inefficient processes.
- Successful enterprise software should focus on solving specific human problems rather than just showcasing technical complexity.
- Iterative development is more effective when the people who understand the business process are the ones building the tools.
- Stacker acts as a 'translation layer' that turns static data (spreadsheets) into dynamic, interactive portals.

## Notable Claims

- Non-technical users can build complex, multi-user applications in under an hour using Stacker. Evidence: Michael Skelly notes that people who understand their own manual processes can design and deploy these apps themselves without needing a developer.
- The most effective software is built using the minimum amount of technology required. Evidence: Skelly argues that focusing on the problem space rather than technical complexity leads to more useful and sustainable products.
