# Building 10X more robots for American Factories: Outcomes as a service ($454 Billion Market)

## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Saman Farid, founder of Formic, a startup addressing the US manufacturing labor shortage by providing 'outcomes as a service.' Formic removes the technical and financial barriers to automation by financing, installing, and maintaining robotic systems for factories, allowing them to scale production without massive upfront capital expenditure.

## Key Ideas

- US manufacturing faces a massive labor shortage, leading to billions in lost economic output.
- Robotics adoption is hindered by high upfront costs, technical integration risks, and the need for ongoing maintenance.
- Formic operates as a 'FinTech' for robotics, using a $100 million debt facility to provide automation as a service.
- The 'outcomes as a service' model allows factories to pay for the productivity of a robot rather than the hardware itself.
- Automation enables human workers to transition from repetitive, rote tasks to more creative and higher-value roles.
- The smartphone revolution significantly reduced the cost of sensors and cameras, making modern robotics more accessible and capable.
