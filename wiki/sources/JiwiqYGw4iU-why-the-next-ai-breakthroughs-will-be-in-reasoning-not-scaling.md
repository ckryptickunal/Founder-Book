# Why The Next AI Breakthroughs Will Be In Reasoning, Not Scaling

## Metadata

- Video ID: `JiwiqYGw4iU`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-11-14T15:00:18Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JiwiqYGw4iU

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses the paradigm shift from scaling-based AI to reasoning-based AI, specifically focusing on OpenAI's o1 model. They highlight how o1's ability to perform complex reasoning, chain-of-thought, and multi-step problem solving is unlocking new capabilities in physical engineering, circuit design, and automated customer support, while emphasizing the critical importance of rigorous evaluation (evals) for building defensible AI startups.

## Key Ideas

- The next major AI breakthrough is shifting from raw scaling to advanced reasoning capabilities.
- o1's chain-of-thought reasoning enables AI to solve complex, multi-step engineering problems (e.g., PCB design, airfoil simulation) that were previously impossible for LLMs.
- Startups can build moats by focusing on proprietary data, rigorous evaluation (evals) pipelines, and deep vertical integration rather than just relying on base model performance.
- The 'worst' these models will ever be is right now; capabilities are improving on a weekly basis.
- Reinforcement learning, inspired by early OpenAI work on DOTA, is a key component of the o1 architecture.
- AI is moving from digital-only tasks to impacting the 'atom world' (physical engineering, energy, and scientific discovery).

## Topics

- [[topics/reasoning-vs-scaling|Reasoning vs. Scaling]]: The shift in AI research from increasing parameter counts to improving the model's ability to think and plan through steps.
- [[topics/ai-in-physical-engineering|AI in Physical Engineering]]: Applying AI to fields like mechanical, electrical, and chemical engineering to accelerate scientific progress.
- [[topics/startup-moats|Startup Moats]]: The strategy of using proprietary data and rigorous evaluation frameworks to build defensible businesses on top of foundation models.
- [[topics/reinforcement-learning|Reinforcement Learning]]: The use of reward functions and self-play, similar to AlphaGo and DOTA, to train models to reason correctly.

## Notable Claims

- o1 represents a step-function improvement over GPT-4 for complex reasoning tasks. Evidence: Diode Computer's PCB design tool failed with GPT-4 but succeeded with o1.
- Rigorous evaluation (evals) is the most important factor for building successful AI products. Evidence: Companies like the one mentioned by the hosts achieved 100% accuracy on specific tasks by focusing on 10,000+ test cases.
- AI will eventually accelerate scientific progress by outperforming humans in research. Evidence: Sam Altman's long-term vision for AGI and the current ability of o1 to solve partial differential equations.
