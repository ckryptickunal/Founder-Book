# Diana Hu on Augmented Reality and Building a Startup in a New Market

## Summary

Diana Hu, co-founder of the AR startup Escher Reality (acquired by Niantic), discusses the technical and economic challenges of building in the augmented reality space. She explains the 'installation' versus 'deployment' phases of technology cycles, the importance of hardware efficiency laws (Moore's, Koomey's, and Edholm's), and her personal journey as an immigrant founder.

## Key Ideas

- AR is currently in the 'installation' phase, requiring foundational infrastructure and tooling before mass-market 'deployment' can occur.
- The success of AR depends on hardware advancements in power efficiency and bandwidth, specifically following Koomey's Law and Edholm's Law.
- Founders in nascent markets should focus on building core technology and infrastructure rather than just applications.
- The immigrant experience fosters a 'positive determinism' and resilience that is highly beneficial for entrepreneurship.
- Acquisitions can be successful when the startup's original vision aligns with the parent company's long-term goals, allowing for faster scaling.

- [[topics/technology-cycles|Technology Cycles]]: The progression of new technologies through an installation phase (infrastructure building) and a deployment phase (application explosion).
- [[topics/startup-fundraising|Startup Fundraising]]: The process of finding investors who believe in a long-term vision, especially in unproven or nascent markets.
- [[topics/immigrant-entrepreneurship|Immigrant Entrepreneurship]]: The unique perspective and resilience gained by immigrants when navigating new cultural and economic environments to build companies.

## Notable Claims

- AR is currently in the installation phase of its technology cycle. Evidence: The need for specialized tooling and infrastructure, similar to how early web development required abstractions before mass adoption.
- Mobile hardware is becoming significantly more power-efficient. Evidence: Koomey's Law, which suggests that the power efficiency of computing doubles every 18 months.
- 5G will provide the necessary bandwidth for data-heavy AR applications. Evidence: The transition from LTE's 1 megabit speeds to 5G's potential 10 gigabit speeds.
