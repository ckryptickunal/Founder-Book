## Summary

Sachin from Skyvern explains how providing AI agents with deep, contextual data from internal communications and documentation is the key to avoiding 'slop' and scaling operations efficiently. He details how Skyvern uses autonomous agents for tasks like drafting product requirements (PRDs) and content marketing by grounding them in real customer data, while emphasizing the necessity of a remote-first, transparent documentation culture.

## Key Ideas

- AI agents produce poor results ('slop') primarily due to a lack of business context, not just model capability.
- Effective AI agents require access to a company's 'knowledge base,' including emails, Slack, Notion, call recordings, and databases.
- Remote-first companies have an inherent advantage in AI adoption because their communication is naturally recorded and searchable, unlike spoken in-person interactions.
- Adopting an 'adversarial review' process where sub-agents critique and refine the output of primary agents significantly improves quality.
- To leverage AI effectively, companies must restructure their communication habits, such as banning private DMs in favor of public channels and recording all meetings.

## Notable Claims

- Remote companies have an unfair advantage in AI adoption compared to in-person companies. Evidence: Remote companies naturally record communications (Slack, video calls), whereas in-person communication is often spoken and lost.
- AI agents can effectively draft complex PRDs (Product Requirements Documents). Evidence: Skyvern uses agents to search call recordings, Slack, and Notion to ground PRDs in evidence, followed by an adversarial review process.
