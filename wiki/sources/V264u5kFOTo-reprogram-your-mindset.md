## Summary

Garry Tan explores the concept of self-integration and mental reprogramming, using the metaphor of a 'Horse and Rider' to describe the relationship between our instinctual, subconscious parts and our conscious, rational selves. Drawing on Jungian psychology and Internal Family Systems (IFS), he argues that true personal growth and leadership come from acknowledging and integrating the 'Shadow' and hidden parts of the personality rather than suppressing them.

## Key Ideas

- The 'Horse and Rider' metaphor represents the interplay between instinctual drives and conscious decision-making.
- Self-work is akin to programming: identifying non-deterministic processes and applying strategic awareness to make intentional choices.
- The 'Shadow' consists of parts of ourselves we judge or deny; integrating these parts is essential for self-knowledge.
- Internal Family Systems (IFS) categorizes parts into Exiles, Managers, and Firefighters, all of which serve a purpose and deserve acceptance.
- Postel's Law (be lenient in what you accept from others, strict in what you put out) is a useful framework for interpersonal relationships and mental health.
- Unconscious parts will continue to direct one's life as 'fate' until they are made conscious.
