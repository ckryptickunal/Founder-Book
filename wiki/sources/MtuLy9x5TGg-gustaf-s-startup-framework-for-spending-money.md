# Gustaf’s Startup Framework For Spending Money

## Summary

Gustaf Alströmer outlines a framework for startup spending, arguing that startups should not mimic the operational structures of large enterprises until they have achieved product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Startups are not miniature versions of big companies.
- The sole purpose of a startup is to reach product-market fit.
- Spending money on non-essential functions or roles creates distractions that hinder progress.
- Founders often adopt corporate trappings to feel validated rather than to solve actual business problems.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The primary and singular goal of an early-stage startup.
- [[topics/startup-operations|Startup Operations]]: The lean approach to resource allocation and hiring in early-stage companies.

## Notable Claims

- Startups should not replicate the functional departments found in large enterprises. Evidence: Most functions in big companies have no equivalent or utility in a small startup seeking product-market fit.
- Hiring for unnecessary roles creates harmful distractions for founders. Evidence: Managing unnecessary staff takes time away from the core focus of achieving product-market fit.
