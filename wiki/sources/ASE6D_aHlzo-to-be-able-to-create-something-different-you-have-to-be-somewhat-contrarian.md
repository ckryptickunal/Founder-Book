# To be able to create something different, you have to be somewhat contrarian.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `ASE6D_aHlzo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-05-13T21:57:02Z

## Summary

A brief reflection on the personality traits of successful founders, arguing that the contrarian nature required to build something new often conflicts with traditional workplace social norms.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often possess a contrarian nature that makes them difficult to manage in traditional employment settings.
- The drive to fix broken systems is a core trait of a successful founder.
- Being 'likable' or conforming to workplace expectations can be antithetical to the disruptive mindset needed for innovation.
- The same traits that make for a 'shitty employee'—challenging authority and pointing out flaws—are essential for building a company.

## Topics

- [[topics/entrepreneurial-psychology|Entrepreneurial Psychology]]: The study of personality traits, such as contrarianism and problem-solving, that correlate with successful startup founding.

## Notable Claims

- Successful founders are often not the most likable people. Evidence: The speaker argues that the necessity of being contrarian to create something different prevents founders from conforming to social expectations.
- The urge to fix broken systems is a defining characteristic of a founder. Evidence: The speaker notes that founders are often 'so annoyed if something's broken they have to fix it,' even if it causes friction with others.

## Quotes

> To be able to create something different, you have to be somewhat contrarian.
> It can make a shitty employee but a really good founder.
