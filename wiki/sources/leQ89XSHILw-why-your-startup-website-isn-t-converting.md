# Why Your Startup Website Isn't Converting

## Summary

Yorn Van Djk, CEO of Framer, reviews several startup websites built on the Framer platform, providing expert design feedback focused on clarity, conversion optimization, and the effective use of animation.

## Key Ideas

- Websites should prioritize showing the actual product (screenshots or video walkthroughs) over abstract illustrations.
- Animations should be used sparingly to draw attention to key elements rather than as decorative clutter.
- Avoid high-friction CTAs like 'Book a Demo' for first-time visitors; offer lower-friction entry points like product demos or interactive trials.
- Attention to detail (typography, spacing, consistent UI patterns) is critical for building user trust.
- Avoid 'marketing speak' and generic claims; use specific, explanatory sub-headlines to clarify value propositions.
- Social proof is only effective if it includes specific, verifiable details like company names and real-world results.

## Notable Claims

- Generic 'Book a Demo' buttons often have poor conversion rates for new visitors. Evidence: Visitors need to understand the product value before committing to a 20-minute sales call.
- Inconsistent UI details (like spacing errors or random font choices) erode user trust. Evidence: Small errors signal a lack of attention to detail, making the product appear less professional.
- Animations should be used to guide the user's eye to the most important information. Evidence: Over-animation distracts from the core value proposition and makes the site feel cluttered.
