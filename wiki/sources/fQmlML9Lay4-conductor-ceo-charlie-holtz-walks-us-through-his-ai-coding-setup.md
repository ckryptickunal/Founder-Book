## Summary

Charlie Holtz, CEO of Conductor, demonstrates his AI-driven development workflow, emphasizing the use of multiple coding agents, voice-to-text interaction, and a 'conductor' metaphor for managing software projects. He discusses the importance of opinionated UI design, the shift from writing code to managing AI prompts, and the future of malleable, agentic software.

## Key Ideas

- Software development is shifting from manual coding to managing AI agents, where code is treated as 'sawdust' produced by high-level prompts.
- Effective AI-assisted development requires clear boundaries between AI-generated code and human-verified 'slot-free' zones.
- Visual, spatial GUIs are superior to terminal interfaces for managing complex agentic workflows.
- The 'Conductor' philosophy treats the developer as an orchestra leader who directs agents, reviews their output, and intervenes only when necessary.
- Malleable software should allow users to 'mod' their development environments similarly to how video games are modded.

## Notable Claims

- Code is becoming 'sawdust' in the age of AI. Evidence: Holtz argues that the value lies in the prompts and the intent, as code can be regenerated by future models.
- Terminal interfaces are inferior to GUIs for AI management. Evidence: Holtz claims humans are spatial creatures who benefit from visual organization of chats and review panels.
- AI can get stuck in a vicious cycle of bad code. Evidence: Holtz notes that if AI is allowed to write code without human oversight, it may replicate its own errors.
