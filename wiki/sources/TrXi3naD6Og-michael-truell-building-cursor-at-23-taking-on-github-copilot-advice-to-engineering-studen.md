# Michael Truell: Building Cursor At 23, Taking On GitHub Copilot & Advice To Engineering Students

## Summary

Michael Truell, co-founder of Cursor, discusses the company's evolution from a series of failed experiments in 2022 to becoming a leading AI-powered code editor. He emphasizes the importance of product-led growth, the shift toward AI-native software development, and the value of persistence and deep interest in one's work.

## Key Ideas

- The future of software development involves all coding flowing through AI models.
- Product-led growth in the AI space is driven by continuous iteration and making the product better based on user feedback.
- Building a successful startup often requires 'wandering the desert' and pivoting through multiple ideas before finding product-market fit.
- The transition from a general-purpose editor to an AI-native tool requires deep integration with the codebase rather than just simple autocomplete.
- Technical fundamentals (like CS and math) remain valuable as a general education, even as AI automates specific coding tasks.

## Notable Claims

- All software development will eventually flow through AI models. Evidence: Truell cites the rapid adoption of AI tools and the changing nature of coding workflows.
- Building a code editor from scratch is a massive undertaking that is best served by leveraging existing primitives like VS Code. Evidence: The team initially built their own editor but switched to a VS Code fork to focus on AI features.
- The best way to grow a product is to make it better, as users will naturally talk about it. Evidence: Cursor's growth from single-digit adoption in YC batches to 80% in one year.
