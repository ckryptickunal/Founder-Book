# Replit's CEO On The Only Two Jobs Left In The Company Of The Future

## Summary

Amjad Masad, CEO of Replit, discusses the evolution of his company from a browser-based IDE to a 'vibe coding' platform that empowers non-technical users to build real, scalable software using AI agents. The conversation covers the shift toward AI-native development, the importance of product-led growth, and the future of work where employees act as internal entrepreneurs using agents to solve business problems.

## Key Ideas

- Programming has become unnecessarily complex over time; Replit aims to make it accessible to anyone who can read and write.
- The primary users of Replit are 'tech-adjacent' creators like product managers, designers, and entrepreneurs, rather than traditional software engineers.
- Replit's 'vibe coding' allows users to build software through natural language and visual interfaces, abstracting away the underlying code.
- The company of the future will consist of generalist entrepreneurs who use AI agents to solve problems and improve business operations.
- AI agents are evolving toward multi-agent architectures that support parallel tasks, asynchronous design, and team collaboration.
- Sales in the AI era is shifting toward evangelism and education, helping companies transform their internal workflows.

## Topics

- [[topics/vibe-coding|Vibe Coding]]: A development paradigm where users build software using natural language and AI agents instead of writing manual code.
- [[topics/ai-native-development|AI-Native Development]]: The shift toward building software where AI agents handle the technical infrastructure, deployment, and scaling.
- [[topics/internal-entrepreneurship|Internal Entrepreneurship]]: The concept of employees acting as founders within a company to identify and solve operational bottlenecks using custom software.
- [[topics/product-led-growth-plg|Product-Led Growth (PLG)]]: A strategy where the product itself drives user acquisition and expansion within organizations.

## Notable Claims

- Programming has become more difficult and intimidating over the last few decades. Evidence: Comparison between early basic command line interpreters and modern complex web development stacks like React/Webpack.
- Non-technical users can build sophisticated, real-world software on Replit. Evidence: Examples of a physical therapist building a health-tech app and a founder building software for sports clubs.
- The company of the future will be composed of builders and salespeople. Evidence: The shift toward internal generalists who use agents to solve problems and salespeople who act as transformation educators.
