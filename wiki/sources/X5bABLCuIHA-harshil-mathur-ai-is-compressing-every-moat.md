# Harshil Mathur: AI Is Compressing Every Moat

## Summary

Harshil Mathur, co-founder of Razorpay, discusses the company's journey from a Y Combinator startup to India's largest payments platform. He emphasizes the importance of deep customer connection, the strategic value of navigating regulated industries as a 'moat,' and the necessity of maintaining 'founder mode' to drive product vision despite organizational scale.

## Key Ideas

- B2B is fundamentally a business of trust, which cannot be fully replaced by automation.
- Regulatory hurdles in fintech act as a competitive moat because they deter less committed entrants.
- Founders should avoid 'manager mode' and stay deeply involved in core product decisions.
- AI is compressing the time required to build, shifting the primary challenge from 'how to build' to 'what to build'.
- Capital efficiency is a strategic advantage, especially when it forces a company to focus on genuine value creation rather than burning cash.
- Early adoption of new infrastructure (like UPI) can provide a massive competitive wedge for small, agile startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/founder-mode|Founder Mode]]: The necessity for founders to remain hands-on with product vision and core decisions rather than delegating to managers.
- [[topics/regulatory-moats|Regulatory Moats]]: Using the difficulty of compliance and licensing as a barrier to entry for competitors.
- [[topics/customer-centricity|Customer-Centricity]]: Building conviction by staying in the weeds with customers and solving their actual pain points.
- [[topics/capital-efficiency|Capital Efficiency]]: The philosophy of growing sustainably by focusing on value-add rather than aggressive cash burn.

## Notable Claims

- AI will make the 'build' process easy, making 'what to build' the only remaining differentiator. Evidence: The rapid advancement of coding assistants and AI-driven development tools.
- Regulated industries provide a deeper moat than unregulated ones. Evidence: The high barrier to entry created by licensing and compliance requirements that discourage competition.
- Customer support is a trust-building channel, not just a cost center. Evidence: Razorpay's policy of having humans handle complex support issues to maintain trust.

## Quotes

> B2B is a business of trust. And the trust at the end of the day nothing replaces the human touch point of trust.
> If it is hard to sell to customers, it's a problem. If it's hard for other reasons, it's not really a problem. It's actually a moat.
> AI is going to bring down time to build so rapidly that the only differentiation is going to be how fast can you move?
> Nobody's going to care about your company as much as you do.
