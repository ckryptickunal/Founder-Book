## Summary

Ron Conway, a legendary angel investor and founder of SV Angel, discusses his philosophy on investing in founders, the importance of product focus, and the traits that define successful entrepreneurs during a 2014 Y Combinator Startup School session.

## Key Ideas

- Entrepreneurs are born with innate traits like work ethic, ambition, and toughness, but management and recruiting skills can be learned.
- The most successful founders are 'rifle-focused' on their product, often to the point of obsession.
- Great startups are usually born from a founder's personal need or 'aha' moment, rather than a desire to build a business for others.
- Founders should be willing to work 24/7; it is a vocation similar to a religious calling.
- The best co-founding teams are usually friends or colleagues who share the same level of passion and excitement for the idea.
- Investors should prioritize investing in people over the specific idea, as the idea often evolves.
- Admitting when a product is not getting traction is crucial; denial is a major pitfall for young founders.
- The 'portfolio approach' to angel investing helps mitigate the high failure rate of startups.

## Notable Claims

- 40% of SV Angel's investments fail. Evidence: Ron Conway states this as a statistic from their portfolio history.
- Single-founder startups are disproportionately unlikely to get accepted into Y Combinator. Evidence: Sam Altman notes this based on YC application data.
- The best founders are 'rifle-focused' on the product. Evidence: Ron Conway cites examples like Larry Page, Jack Dorsey, and Steve Jobs.
