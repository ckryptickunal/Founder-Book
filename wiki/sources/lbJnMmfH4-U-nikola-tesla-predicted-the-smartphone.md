## Summary

This video explores the concept of the 'global brain,' a term describing the interconnectedness of humanity through the internet and smartphones. It traces the evolution of global communication from the Pony Express to modern mobile technology, highlighting Nikola Tesla's early prediction of the smartphone and discussing how internet connectivity enables individuals to escape competition through unique, authentic self-expression.

## Key Ideas

- The smartphone has effectively connected humanity into a 'global brain,' enabling instant communication.
- Nikola Tesla predicted the invention of the smartphone in 1926, envisioning a device that would eliminate distance.
- The internet allows individuals to escape competition by leveraging their unique skills and interests to find a global audience.
- Authenticity is a competitive advantage; no one can compete with an individual on being themselves.
- The impact of the internet on culture is still in its early stages.
