# Writes and Write-Nots

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that the widespread adoption of AI will lead to a societal divide between 'writes' and 'write-nots,' warning that because writing is a fundamental form of thinking, the loss of writing skills will result in a decline in clear thinking capabilities.

## Key Ideas

- Writing is inherently difficult because it requires clear thinking.
- AI has removed the pressure to learn writing, which previously forced people to develop the skill.
- The middle class of 'okay' writers will vanish, leaving only those who write well and those who cannot write at all.
- Writing is not just a communication tool but a cognitive process; to stop writing is to stop thinking clearly.
- Writing will become a voluntary intellectual exercise, similar to how physical exercise replaced manual labor for maintaining strength.
