# Ask These Questions Before Starting An AI Startup

## Summary

Jordan Fisher, an alignment research lead at Anthropic and experienced startup founder, discusses the profound uncertainty and rapid changes brought by AI. He argues that founders must move beyond short-term planning and consider a two-year horizon that accounts for the arrival of AGI, emphasizing the need for trust, defensibility, and ethical considerations in an increasingly automated world.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should plan for a two-year horizon anticipating AGI rather than just the next six months.
- The 'buy side' (enterprises) will be armed with AGI, meaning incumbents will also benefit from AI, not just startups.
- Trust is the most critical challenge in an era of semi-automated teams and agentic behavior.
- Defensibility in a post-AGI world requires solving hard problems (e.g., infrastructure, energy, manufacturing) rather than just building software.
- The traditional startup mantra 'build something people want' should be expanded to 'build something society needs' and can trust.
- AI-powered auditing could provide a new mechanism for establishing trust in companies without human bias.
