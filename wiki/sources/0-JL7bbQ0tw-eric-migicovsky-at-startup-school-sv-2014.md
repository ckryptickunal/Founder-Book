## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, founder of Pebble, recounts the journey of building the first successful smartwatch, from his initial 2008 prototype as a student to a record-breaking Kickstarter campaign and the challenges of hardware manufacturing.

## Key Ideas

- Hardware startups benefit from launching early and iterating based on user feedback.
- The importance of focusing on a few core features (notifications, fitness, customization) to achieve product-market fit.
- Leveraging community and developer ecosystems (SDKs) can significantly extend the value of a hardware product.
- Using 'leaked' information and press engagement can be a strategic tool for early-stage startups.
- Hardware manufacturing requires hands-on involvement, often necessitating time spent at factories in Asia.

## Notable Claims

- The Pebble Kickstarter campaign raised $10 million from 70,000 people in 30 days. Evidence: Stated as the result of the campaign launch.
- A startup's product should do three things really well and be easily explainable. Evidence: Attributed to advice from Paul Buite.
- Leaking information to tech blogs can be a strategic way to generate early interest. Evidence: Described the process of emailing renders to blogs like CrackBerry.
