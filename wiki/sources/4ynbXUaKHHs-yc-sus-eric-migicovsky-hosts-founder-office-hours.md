## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, a Y Combinator partner, conducts live office hours for several Startup School founders. He provides tactical advice on balancing product development with sales, the importance of 'doing things that don't scale,' and the necessity of setting concrete, short-term goals to drive progress.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should prioritize getting paying customers before building complex processes or software.
- Use 'doing things that don't scale' (like manual phone calls) to gain real-time feedback and refine sales pitches.
- Set clear, two-week goals to maintain momentum and accountability.
- Be transparent with pilot customers about success metrics to facilitate conversion to paid contracts.
- Avoid overwhelming potential users with too much information; focus on clear value propositions and simple user flows.
- Segment your target market to identify who actually derives value from your product rather than trying to serve everyone.

## Notable Claims

- Doing things that don't scale is a secret advantage for early-stage startups. Evidence: Founders can personally call or onboard users to get high-quality feedback that large companies cannot replicate.
- Phone calls are superior to email for early sales outreach. Evidence: Phone calls provide real-time feedback on which messaging resonates, allowing for rapid iteration of the pitch.
