# See reality as it is first, before you try to bend it — The Stockdale Paradox

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the 'Stockdale Paradox' in the context of startup entrepreneurship, emphasizing the need for founders to balance unwavering faith in ultimate success with a brutal, honest confrontation of current reality.

## Key Ideas

- The Stockdale Paradox requires holding two opposing mindsets: absolute belief in prevailing in the end and the discipline to confront the brutal facts of the present.
- Startups are not 'book reports' but long-term callings that require 10+ years of commitment.
- Optimism without a grounding in reality is dangerous and can lead to failure, as seen in the experiences of POWs.
- Founders must distinguish between 'bending reality' for vision and 'denying reality' for operations; lying to oneself or one's team is a fatal management error.
- Founding a company is harder than being a CEO of an established firm because founders must simultaneously create and manage reality.

## Notable Claims

- Optimists who ignore current reality are more likely to fail. Evidence: Historical account of POWs in Vietnam who died of broken hearts after repeatedly setting and missing unrealistic release dates.
- Founding a company is more difficult than being a CEO of an existing company. Evidence: Existing CEOs only need to manage reality, whereas founders must both create and manage it.
