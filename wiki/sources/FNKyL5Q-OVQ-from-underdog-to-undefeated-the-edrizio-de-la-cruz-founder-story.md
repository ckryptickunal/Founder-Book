# From Underdog to Undefeated: The Edrizio De La Cruz founder story

## Summary

Edrizio De La Cruz, an immigrant from the Dominican Republic, shares his journey from growing up in Harlem to founding the fintech company Arcus (formerly Regali) and successfully selling it to MasterCard. The narrative highlights his resilience through extreme professional and personal challenges, including near-bankruptcy, being voted out of his own company, and personal tragedy.

## Key Ideas

- Immigrant experiences and personal adversity can provide unique insights into untapped market opportunities.
- The importance of persistence: 'sticking around long enough to get lucky' is a core tenet of startup success.
- YC's role in accelerating growth by forcing founders to focus on product development and customer feedback.
- The 'impediment to action' philosophy: using obstacles as fuel for progress rather than reasons to quit.
- The necessity of maintaining a sense of purpose larger than oneself to endure the darkest moments of entrepreneurship.

## Notable Claims

- The more unusual a market is, the bigger and more untapped the opportunity often is. Evidence: Edrizio's success in the remittance space, which was largely ignored by mainstream tech at the time.
- Success is the intersection of iteration, learning, and luck. Evidence: Edrizio's persistence through 144 failed meetings before securing a series A, and his eventual acquisition.
