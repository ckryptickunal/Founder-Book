## Summary

A brief reflection on the mindset shift required for founding engineers at early-stage startups, emphasizing the necessity of prioritizing customer needs over technical attachment to code.

## Key Ideas

- Founding engineers must detach their ego from their code.
- Early-stage code is frequently discarded or repurposed based on customer feedback.
- Avoiding the sunk cost fallacy is critical for startup agility.
- The primary goal is building the right product for the customer, not technical perfection.

## Entities

- [[entities/finley|Finley]] (company): A YC-backed startup featured in the video.

## Topics

- [[topics/engineering-mindset|Engineering Mindset]]: The transition from focusing on code quality to focusing on product-market fit.
- [[topics/startup-agility|Startup Agility]]: The ability to pivot and discard work quickly in response to customer needs.

## Notable Claims

- Founding engineers must be prepared to trash or repurpose code they spent months building. Evidence: The speaker notes that early-stage features are often discarded to ensure the product aligns with customer needs.

## Quotes

> The feature that you treasured and it was your baby for a couple months it's gonna get trashed.
> It's not about like this was super cool technically we want to keep it it's like a sunken cost fallacy.
