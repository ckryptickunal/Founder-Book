## Summary

Garry Tan explains Postel's Law—'be liberal in what you accept, and conservative in what you send'—and how this foundational internet principle serves as a superior framework for product design, interpersonal communication, and organizational management compared to the Golden Rule.

## Key Ideas

- Postel's Law is essential for the internet's decentralized functionality, allowing disparate systems to communicate despite errors.
- The principle requires individual responsibility: holding oneself to high standards while being forgiving of others' failures.
- In product design, being 'liberal in what you accept' means creating flexible user interfaces that accommodate various user inputs.
- Interpersonally, applying Postel's Law involves giving a 'charitable read' to digital communications to avoid unnecessary conflict.
- Management benefits from 'codified protocols' (like user manuals for teams) to reduce ambiguity and improve collaboration.

## Topics

- [[topics/postel-s-law|Postel's Law]]: A design principle for robust networks: be conservative in what you send, be liberal in what you accept.
- [[topics/product-design|Product Design]]: Applying the law to create user-friendly interfaces that handle diverse user input formats gracefully.
- [[topics/interpersonal-communication|Interpersonal Communication]]: Using the law to foster empathy and reduce conflict in digital communication by assuming positive intent.
- [[topics/management-protocols|Management Protocols]]: Using explicit communication manuals to define how team members work and interact.

## Notable Claims

- The internet would break without Postel's Law. Evidence: Web browsers and protocols like SMTP are designed to be tolerant of developer mistakes and malformed input, allowing systems to function despite errors.
- Postel's Law is more effective than the Golden Rule for creating great systems. Evidence: While the Golden Rule is for basic social functioning, Postel's Law encourages higher standards for oneself and greater tolerance for others, enabling complex, decentralized collaboration.
