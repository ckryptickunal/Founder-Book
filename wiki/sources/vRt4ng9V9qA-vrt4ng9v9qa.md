## Summary

An interview with the founders of Gryptile, a YC Winter '24 startup that uses AI to analyze large codebases, automate pull request reviews, and identify bugs, aiming to improve software resilience.

## Key Ideas

- AI is currently more effective at reading and understanding code than writing it.
- Code review serves a dual purpose: bug detection and human mentorship; AI should augment, not replace, the human element.
- Large codebases are a primary source of friction in software engineering, and AI can help bridge the knowledge gap for developers.
- There is significant variation in developer adoption of AI tools, ranging from skepticism to full embrace.
- Focusing on core product utility and customer needs is the most critical factor for early-stage startup success.
