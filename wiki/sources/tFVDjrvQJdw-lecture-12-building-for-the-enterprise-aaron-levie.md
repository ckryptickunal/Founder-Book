## Summary

Aaron Levie, CEO and co-founder of Box, discusses the evolution of enterprise software, arguing that it is currently the most opportune time to build in this sector due to shifts in cloud computing, mobile adoption, and user-led IT. He outlines strategies for startups to compete with incumbents by focusing on user experience, leveraging technology disruptions, and finding unique business models.

## Key Ideas

- Enterprise software is undergoing a massive shift from on-premise, IT-led models to cloud-based, user-led models.
- Startups should identify technology disruptions (e.g., cheaper storage, mobile ubiquity) to find gaps in incumbent offerings.
- The 'consumerization of IT' means enterprise products must now prioritize design and user experience to gain adoption.
- Startups should start with a small, specific 'wedge' problem and expand into the enterprise architecture over time.
- Building a platform with open APIs is superior to building custom, monolithic software.
- Sales teams should be consultative and domain-oriented, but they should never replace the need for a product that sells itself.

## Notable Claims

- The enterprise IT market is significantly larger than the consumer app market. Evidence: $3.7 trillion spent annually on enterprise IT vs. $170 billion in consumer app/advertising markets.
- Every industry is currently undergoing a technology-driven business model disruption. Evidence: Examples provided in retail (omni-channel), healthcare (personalized medicine), and media (on-demand distribution).
- Incumbents are often unable to compete with startups due to their existing business models. Evidence: Incumbents are often tied to legacy on-premise architectures or specific pricing structures that prevent them from adopting new, more efficient models.
