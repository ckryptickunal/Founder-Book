# How to learn WHILE you earn

## Metadata

- Video ID: `ThsDDp3_Oro`

- Published: 2024-04-17T16:00:32Z

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the financial and professional benefits of joining early-stage startups, highlighting the potential for significant wealth creation through equity for both founders and early employees.

## Key Ideas

- Founders start with 100% equity in a zero-value company, aiming for high-value exits.
- Early employees receive smaller equity stakes (0.25% to 2%) but face lower risk than founders.
- Joining a fast-growing company early allows individuals to 'learn while they earn' with significant upside potential.
- Equity in a successful startup can lead to life-changing financial outcomes even for non-founders.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): CEO and President of Y Combinator, investor, and content creator.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-equity|Startup Equity]]: The distribution of ownership stakes in early-stage companies and their potential long-term value.
- [[topics/career-strategy|Career Strategy]]: The trade-off between risk and reward when choosing to join a startup versus starting one.

## Notable Claims

- Early employees at successful startups can achieve life-changing financial outcomes. Evidence: Equity stakes of 0.25% to 2% in companies with valuations in the tens or hundreds of millions can result in significant wealth.
- Founders face higher risk but have higher potential equity upside compared to employees. Evidence: Founders start with 100% of a zero-value company, whereas employees join companies that are already established or growing.

## Quotes

> If you start a company or early enough you can learn while you earn.
> If you own 10 to 30% of that company by then you could be a millionaire to billionaire that's pretty crazy.
