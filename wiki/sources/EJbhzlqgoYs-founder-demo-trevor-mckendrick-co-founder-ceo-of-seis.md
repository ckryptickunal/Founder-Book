## Summary

Trevor Mckendrick, CEO of Seis, critiques the anti-competitive nature of the Zelle payment network, specifically focusing on the recent decision by its owner banks to restrict access for fintech companies and smaller financial institutions.

## Key Ideas

- Zelle is owned by the seven largest US banks, creating a de facto monopoly on instant peer-to-peer payments.
- Zelle's transaction volume exceeds that of Venmo, Cash App, RTP, and FedNow combined.
- The recent shutdown of the standalone Zelle app for fintech users effectively excludes non-bank institutions from the network.
- Zelle functions as critical financial infrastructure that should be treated as a public good rather than a closed system.
- The US lacks a truly open, universal instant payment network compared to successful models in Brazil and India.

- Zelle processes more transaction volume than Venmo, Cash App, RTP, and FedNow combined. Evidence: Speaker cites annual transaction volume exceeding $1 trillion for Zelle.
