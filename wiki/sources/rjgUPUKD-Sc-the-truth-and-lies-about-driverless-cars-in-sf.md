# The Truth and Lies About Driverless Cars in SF

## Summary

Garry Tan, a venture capitalist and tech advocate, argues that San Francisco's local government officials are intentionally spreading misinformation and manipulating data to obstruct the deployment of autonomous vehicles (AVs) by companies like Cruise and Waymo. Tan characterizes this as part of a broader ideological agenda by a 'hard left' faction within the Board of Supervisors to stifle private sector innovation, housing development, and merit-based systems, urging tech workers to engage in local politics to counter these policies.

## Key Ideas

- San Francisco officials are accused of using fraudulent statistics to justify banning autonomous vehicles.
- The California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) has criticized the city's data analysis as having an unacceptably high degree of error.
- Autonomous vehicle regulation is under state, not city, jurisdiction to prevent fragmented local rules.
- The opposition to AVs is framed as part of a larger ideological effort by specific supervisors to undermine the private sector and promote anti-growth policies.
- Tech industry professionals are encouraged to organize, donate, and participate in local elections to unseat officials who oppose technological progress.

## Notable Claims

- SF officials manipulated data to claim Cruise's accident rate was 6.3 times the national average. Evidence: The CPUC issued letters accusing the city of shoddy data analysis and statistical errors.
- The SF Board of Supervisors is intentionally blocking progress in housing and technology. Evidence: Tan cites the supervisors' opposition to private housing and their specific rhetoric against tech companies.
- Autonomous vehicles are safer than human drivers. Evidence: Tan asserts that AVs are tracking to be safer and that many reported incidents were caused by human drivers or environmental factors.
