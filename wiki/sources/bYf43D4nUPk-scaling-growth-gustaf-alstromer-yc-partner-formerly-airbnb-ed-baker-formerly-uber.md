## Summary

Gustaf Alstromer (Airbnb) and Ed Baker (Uber) discuss the evolution of growth teams, moving from early viral social media hacks to data-driven, cross-functional organizations within mature, real-world marketplaces.

## Key Ideas

- Growth teams should be cross-functional, integrating product, engineering, analytics, design, and marketing.
- A 'North Star' metric is essential to align the entire organization on a single goal.
- Growth strategies must evolve from user acquisition to retention and engagement as a company matures.
- Experimentation is the core of growth; intuition is often wrong, making rigorous A/B testing necessary.
- In two-sided marketplaces, growth accounting must be applied to both supply and demand sides.
- Real-world friction (e.g., driver onboarding) requires different growth tactics than purely digital products.
- Language and cultural nuances (e.g., 'invite' vs. 'announce') can significantly impact viral growth.

## Topics

- [[topics/north-star-metric|North Star Metric]]: The single most important metric that defines a company's success and aligns team efforts.
- [[topics/two-sided-marketplaces|Two-Sided Marketplaces]]: The complexity of growing both supply (drivers/hosts) and demand (riders/guests) simultaneously.
- [[topics/experimentation-culture|Experimentation Culture]]: Institutionalizing A/B testing to remove bias and validate product decisions.
- [[topics/internationalization|Internationalization]]: Adapting growth strategies for different cultural and technical environments, such as China.

## Notable Claims

- Friending was the number one driver of retention and engagement at Facebook. Evidence: Internal data analysis at Facebook during the speaker's tenure.
- Changing the copy from 'invite' to 'announce' significantly increased virality in Japan. Evidence: A/B test results showing a shift from the least viral to most viral country.
- The best growth teams eventually integrate product and growth functions rather than keeping them separate. Evidence: Observations of organizational evolution at Uber and Airbnb.
