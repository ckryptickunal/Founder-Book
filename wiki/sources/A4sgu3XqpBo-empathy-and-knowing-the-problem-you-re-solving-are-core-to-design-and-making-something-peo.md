# Empathy and knowing the problem you're solving are core to design (and making something people want)

## Metadata

- Video ID: `A4sgu3XqpBo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-11-04T00:21:42Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A4sgu3XqpBo

## Summary

The speaker argues that building successful technological products should be viewed through the lens of hosting a great party, emphasizing empathy, hospitality, and user experience over technical complexity.

## Key Ideas

- Product design should prioritize user comfort and welcoming experiences rather than just technical functionality.
- Founders and designers should adopt a mindset of hospitality to reduce friction for the user.
- The 'party' metaphor highlights the importance of human connection and thoughtful onboarding in software.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): Startup accelerator and host of the video content.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-design|Product Design]]: The philosophy of creating user-centric software by focusing on empathy and ease of use.
- [[topics/user-experience-ux|User Experience (UX)]]: The importance of removing friction and making users feel welcomed and valued.

## Notable Claims

- Building a technological product is best modeled as throwing a great party. Evidence: The speaker compares the onboarding process of software to the welcoming experience of a guest entering a party.

## Quotes

> It's actually about throwing the best possible party you possibly can.
> That sort of politeness that inviting welcome nature that thoughtfulness um that's something that you really have to keep in mind as you do uh your work as not just a designer but as a founder.
