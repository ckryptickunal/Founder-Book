## Summary

In this 2017 AMA, Sam Altman discusses his views on entrepreneurship, the future of Y Combinator, social issues like inequality and automation, and his personal habits, while offering advice to aspiring startup founders.

## Key Ideas

- Starting a startup is a learnable skill and a powerful tool for change.
- Inequality and the impact of automation on jobs are the most critical social problems to address.
- Persistence and personal relationships are the cornerstones of successful enterprise sales.
- Becoming a great engineer requires approximately 10,000 hours of practice and building.
- Rejection from accelerators like YC is common and should not deter determined founders.

## Notable Claims

- The most impactful companies usually have a new idea or execute an old idea in a fundamentally new way. Evidence: Altman's observation of successful companies within the YC ecosystem.
- Taking Omega-3 and B12 supplements can boost performance for vegetarians. Evidence: Altman's personal anecdotal experience.
- Becoming a great engineer requires at least 10,000 hours of practice. Evidence: Altman's endorsement of the 10,000-hour model for skill mastery.
