## Summary

David Molot, co-founder of hotglue, discusses the critical need for federal data portability regulations in the US to foster innovation and competition. He argues that current data monopolies held by big tech companies stifle startups, and advocates for an open API framework similar to the UK's Open Banking Initiative to democratize data access.

## Key Ideas

- Data access is the primary determinant of competitiveness in the AI era.
- Big tech companies maintain monopolies by hoarding user data, forcing smaller competitors into risky practices like web scraping.
- Data portability allows startups to compete on product quality rather than data accumulation.
- The UK's Open Banking Initiative serves as a successful model for mandated data interoperability.
- Federal data portability regulation could significantly boost GDP by enabling cross-domain innovation.
