# Building a startup shouldn't be a zero sum game.

## Metadata

## Summary

A brief reflection on the psychological and professional differences between playing positive-sum versus zero-sum games in the context of startup building, emphasizing the value of incremental learning over binary outcomes.

## Key Ideas

- Positive-sum games allow for continuous learning and growth regardless of the final outcome.
- Zero-sum games often result in binary outcomes where failure provides no transferable value.
- The danger of zero-sum games is the lack of incremental feedback, leading to sudden and catastrophic failure.
- Startup founders should prioritize environments and strategies that foster cumulative knowledge.

## Notable Claims

- Founders who engage in positive-sum games almost always gain valuable lessons even if the startup fails. Evidence: The speaker notes that these individuals take lessons with them that make them better off.
- Zero-sum games often result in a 'blow up' scenario where the only win is exiting the game. Evidence: The speaker contrasts this with positive-sum games, noting that zero-sum participants miss out on incremental lessons.

## Quotes

> Our friends who tried and failed to do positive some games they almost always learn something or advanced in some way.
> Whereas like the folks who are playing the zero-sum games if you don't cash out that was the only thing you could take out.
> Often the lesson just comes fast and hard at the end and then you're blown up.
