# Launch quickly, and iterate.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `fetC2EpDtN8`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-03-16T00:15:44Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fetC2EpDtN8

## Summary

A brief guide from Y Combinator emphasizing the importance of launching a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) quickly to facilitate real-world learning rather than delaying through excessive research or administrative tasks.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize launching a product over extensive market research.
- Real learning about user needs only occurs after a product is in their hands.
- Avoid common distractions like excessive surveys, interviews, or premature scaling.
- Iterative improvement based on user feedback is more effective than pre-launch perfection.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/mvp-minimum-viable-product|MVP (Minimum Viable Product)]]: The strategy of releasing a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and provide feedback for future development.
- [[topics/startup-methodology|Startup Methodology]]: The process of building and scaling a company, specifically focusing on avoiding 'busy work' that delays product-market fit.

## Notable Claims

- You only start learning about your user when you put a product in front of them. Evidence: The speaker contrasts this with ineffective methods like surveys and interviews which do not provide the same level of insight as actual product usage.

## Quotes

> The wrong answer is to do 100 surveys and 600 user interviews and contact every single one of the competitors and spend you know a year fundraising.
> You'd only really start learning about your user when you put a product in front of it.
