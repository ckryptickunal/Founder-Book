# What I've Learned from Hacker News

## Summary

Paul Graham reflects on the first two years of Hacker News, discussing the challenges of scaling a community, maintaining quality through moderation, and the psychological impact of online forums.

## Key Ideas

- Growth in online communities often leads to dilution of quality and character.
- The 'broken windows theory' applies to online forums: minor bad behavior encourages worse behavior.
- Community quality is driven more by the people attracted to the site than by the technology itself.
- The 'Fluff Principle' suggests that content easiest to judge (like rants or memes) will dominate unless actively moderated.
- Online forums can be dangerously addictive, potentially acting as a net drag on user productivity.
- Moderation should focus on behavior rather than people to maintain a healthy community culture.

## Notable Claims

- The quality of comments on a community site is strongly correlated with their length. Evidence: Graham observes that stupid comments tend to be short, while longer, more thoughtful arguments are rarer and higher quality.
- User behavior is malleable and influenced by the expected norms of the platform. Evidence: Users cross-posting between Reddit and Hacker News often adjust their tone to match the specific culture of each site.
- The 'broken windows theory' applies to online communities. Evidence: Minor bad behaviors, if left unchecked, lead to more severe forms of toxicity and community degradation.
