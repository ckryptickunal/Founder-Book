# How To Be The Next Elon Musk According To Elon Musk

## Summary

Elon Musk discusses his approach to career development and impact, emphasizing the importance of focusing on useful work rather than emulating specific individuals. He reflects on his early interests in sustainable energy, space exploration, and technology, explaining his decision to pivot from academic research to entrepreneurship to capitalize on technological inflection points.

## Key Ideas

- Focus on being useful rather than trying to emulate a specific person's career path.
- Identify technologies at a steep point in their inflection curve to maximize impact.
- Be willing to pivot from academic pursuits if real-world opportunities for impact arise.
- The importance of prioritizing practical application over theoretical research.

- [[entities/elon-musk|Elon Musk]] (person): Entrepreneur and engineer known for Tesla, SpaceX, and other ventures.

## Topics

- [[topics/career-strategy|Career Strategy]]: The philosophy of optimizing for utility and impact rather than status or following a predetermined path.
- [[topics/technological-inflection-points|Technological Inflection Points]]: The concept of identifying when a technology is ready for rapid adoption and scaling.
- [[topics/sustainable-energy|Sustainable Energy]]: Musk's early interest in energy storage and electric vehicle technology.

## Notable Claims

- One should prioritize working on technologies that are at a steep point in their inflection curve. Evidence: Musk cites his decision to leave Stanford to start an internet company in 1995 to avoid missing the growth of the internet.
- Academic research does not always lead to practical real-world applications. Evidence: Musk notes that one can earn a doctorate in fields that ultimately have no practical bearing on the world.
