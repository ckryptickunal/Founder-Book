# Coinbase’s Liftoff Moment: Why I love seed investing and being a CEO coach #shorts

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan and Brian Armstrong reflect on the early days of Coinbase, highlighting the pivotal moment they realized the company had achieved product-market fit through rapid growth and scaling challenges.

## Key Ideas

- The role of mentorship and CEO coaching in the early stages of a startup.
- The significance of product-market fit as the primary goal for seed-stage investors.
- Identifying product-market fit through rapid, recurring demand signals like repeated credit limit increases.

## Topics

- [[topics/seed-investing|Seed Investing]]: The process of investing in early-stage startups and the emotional reward of witnessing product-market fit.
- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The stage where a product satisfies strong market demand, often evidenced by rapid scaling and operational pressure.

## Notable Claims

- Coinbase achieved clear product-market fit when they had to increase their credit limits with SVB four times in a single week. Evidence: Brian Armstrong reported selling out of Bitcoin every single day during that period.

## Quotes

> Finding product-market fit is just magic.
> That was the day that it was clear you had product-market fit. And so that's what seed investing is all about.
