## Summary

Aaron Epstein from Y Combinator outlines the nine proven business models for billion-dollar companies and provides actionable pricing strategies for early-stage startups, emphasizing the importance of recurring revenue, high retention, and pricing based on value rather than cost.

## Key Ideas

- Focus on one of the nine proven business models (SaaS, Transactional, Marketplace, etc.) rather than reinventing the wheel.
- Marketplaces and transactional businesses are highly effective at creating 'winner-take-all' outcomes due to network effects and being in the flow of funds.
- Recurring revenue models (SaaS) provide predictability and compounding growth, provided retention is high.
- Pricing is a tool for learning; startups should charge from the beginning to validate value and customer segment interest.
- Avoid 'Cost-Plus' pricing; instead, price based on the value provided (e.g., money saved, time gained, risk reduced).
- Most startups undercharge; raising prices is often the easiest way to grow revenue without needing to acquire new customers.

## Notable Claims

- Marketplaces create 30% of the value in the YC top 100 despite being only 14% of the companies. Evidence: Analysis of the Y Combinator top 100 companies list.
- Transactional businesses outperform because they are in the flow of funds. Evidence: Comparison of business models like Stripe and Brex which act as critical infrastructure for payments.
- Raising prices is the easiest way to grow revenue. Evidence: Comparison between the difficulty of acquiring new customers versus adjusting pricing for existing value.
