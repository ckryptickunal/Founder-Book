## Summary

Chase Adam, founder of Watsi, discusses the origin and growth of his non-profit crowdfunding platform for healthcare. He details the challenges of building a mission-driven organization using startup methodologies, the importance of transparency, and the transition from a volunteer-led project to a Y Combinator-backed entity.

## Key Ideas

- Non-profits can benefit from the speed, scale, and efficiency of startup methodologies.
- Transparency and trust are the primary currencies for crowdfunding medical care.
- Starting as a volunteer-led organization allows for risk-taking and avoids the pressure of external funding early on.
- Focusing on a single, core metric is essential for early-stage growth.
- The 'house of cards' feeling is common in startups; success is often just a result of not quitting.
- Hiring people smarter than oneself is critical for long-term organizational health.

## Notable Claims

- Watsi was the first non-profit funded by Y Combinator. Evidence: Stated by the host in the introduction.
- Non-profits are essential for problems that markets and governments cannot solve. Evidence: Adam uses the example of a child in Somalia needing surgery that is not profitable for the private sector to provide.
- Every team member at Watsi raised more than 10 times their salary for patients. Evidence: Adam cites this as a key efficiency metric for the organization.
