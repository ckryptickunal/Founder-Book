## Summary

In this episode of Design Review, Y Combinator hosts and former Dribbble CEO Zach Aniso analyze startup websites to determine if they pass the 'first impression test' within five seconds. The discussion focuses on the importance of clarity, avoiding cognitive load, using motion intentionally, and optimizing for speed and mobile performance.

## Key Ideas

- The 'Don't Make Me Think' principle is critical for landing pages; users should immediately understand what the product does.
- Above-the-fold content should be distilled into one clear headline, one sub-headline, and one primary call to action.
- Animations should guide the user's eye toward key information rather than serving as distracting decoration.
- Avoid the 'Paradox of Choice' by limiting the number of call-to-action buttons above the fold.
- Technical jargon should be avoided unless the target audience is highly niche and specifically expects that language.
- Website performance and load speed are essential for both user retention and SEO rankings.
- Visual hierarchy is often broken by busy layouts or equal-weight columns that confuse the user's focus.

## Notable Claims

- Google deprioritizes websites that are heavy and load slowly. Evidence: Search engines prioritize mobile-first experiences, and slow load times negatively impact ranking criteria.
- Animations that are too fast or complex cause users to miss the core message. Evidence: Multiple examples showed users focusing on moving elements rather than the text or value proposition.
