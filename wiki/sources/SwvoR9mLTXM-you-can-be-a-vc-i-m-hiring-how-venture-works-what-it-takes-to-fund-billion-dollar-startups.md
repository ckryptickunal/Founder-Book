# You can be a VC (I’m hiring): How venture works & what it takes to fund billion dollar startups

## Summary

Garry Tan, founder of Initialized Capital, explains the mechanics of venture capital, the importance of contrarian thinking, and the firm's philosophy of 'benevolent' investing. He announces that Initialized is hiring for Principal and Partner roles, emphasizing a search for builders with deep technical or product experience rather than traditional finance backgrounds.

## Key Ideas

- Venture capital is a 'winner-take-all' industry where the quality of the investor significantly impacts a startup's success.
- The primary goal of a VC is to generate returns for limited partners (LPs) by backing founders who solve consequential problems.
- Successful VCs often act as contrarians, identifying value in ideas that seem 'crazy' or non-obvious to the general public.
- Benevolent investing—being fair, direct, and honest—is a competitive advantage that attracts the best founders.
- Technical and design-oriented founders are preferred because they possess the ability to build, not just sell.
- Venture capital is an 'infinite game' where long-term reputation and integrity are more valuable than short-term gains.

## Notable Claims

- Venture capital is a winner-take-all industry. Evidence: Top-tier VC backing helps startups hire better talent, raise more capital, and build superior products.
- Micromanaging founders during downturns leads to startup failure. Evidence: Forcing founders to focus on board reports instead of product/market issues causes them to lose focus and fail.
- The best founders choose investors based on trustworthiness. Evidence: High-quality founders have options and prioritize partners who are fair and direct.
