## Summary

Grace Garey, co-founder of Watsi, shares the origin story of the global crowdfunding platform for healthcare. She explains how traditional nonprofit models often fail patients by prioritizing donor interests over actual needs, and how Watsi uses technology to create direct, transparent connections between donors and patients to fund life-changing medical treatments.

## Key Ideas

- Traditional humanitarian aid often prioritizes donor interests over the actual needs of the people being served.
- Technology can scale individual human connections to solve global problems.
- Transparency and direct impact (100% of donations going to healthcare) are essential for building trust.
- Surrounding yourself with people who are passionate about their work is crucial for success.
- The world needs more people dedicated to solving specific, meaningful problems rather than creating solutions in search of problems.

## Notable Claims

- Up to 94% of money raised for the Haiti earthquake relief did not reach the intended beneficiaries. Evidence: Cited as a commonly reported statistic regarding the inefficiency of large-scale humanitarian aid.
- Watsi's model allows for more effective healthcare delivery than traditional restricted funding. Evidence: Example of a doctor in Guatemala who could not treat patients with heart defects due to donor restrictions until Watsi provided a direct funding channel.
