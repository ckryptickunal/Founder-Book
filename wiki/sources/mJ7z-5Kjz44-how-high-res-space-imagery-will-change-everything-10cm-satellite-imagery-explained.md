## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Topher, the founder of Albedo Space, a startup building low-orbit satellites that provide 10cm resolution visible imagery and high-resolution thermal imagery. By leveraging advancements in 'New Space' technology, electric propulsion, and cloud-based ground stations, Albedo aims to provide high-fidelity data at a fraction of the cost of traditional, billion-dollar satellite programs.

## Key Ideas

- 10cm satellite imagery enables new applications in property insurance, autonomous vehicle mapping, and infrastructure monitoring.
- Thermal imagery at high resolution provides a new data set for carbon emissions tracking and agricultural efficiency.
- Advances in electric propulsion and on-orbit refueling allow satellites to operate in lower orbits despite atmospheric drag.
- Cloud providers like AWS and Microsoft are democratizing access to space data by providing global ground station networks.
- The 'New Space' ecosystem, including reduced launch costs and commodity satellite components, allows for smaller, more efficient satellite architectures.
