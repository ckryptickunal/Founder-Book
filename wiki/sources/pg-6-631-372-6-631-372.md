## Summary

Paul Graham recounts the discovery of a 2003 patent he unknowingly received for 'Revenue Loop,' an algorithm developed at Viaweb in 1998 that ranks search results by multiplying advertiser bids by transaction volume to improve relevance and revenue.

## Key Ideas

- Search result ranking can be optimized by combining bid amounts with actual user transaction data.
- The 'Revenue Loop' algorithm automatically identifies shopping-related searches and improves relevance by prioritizing sites where users actually make purchases.
- In 1998, Yahoo was uninterested in this technology because their business model relied on high-priced banner ads rather than performance-based search revenue.
- Patent applications can continue through the legal pipeline long after the original inventors have moved on, often resulting in obscured, overly-formalized language.

## Notable Claims

- Ranking search results by bid multiplied by transactions is a better measure of relevance than bid-only or text-only ranking. Evidence: It uses actual user behavior (purchasing) as a signal of satisfaction with the search result.
- In 1998, Yahoo's revenues would have decreased if they had implemented performance-based search ranking. Evidence: Advertisers were overpaying for banner ads, and performance-based models would have lowered the cost per click/conversion.
