# What Would Elon Musk Work On If He Were 22?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `vVnDE8wSrVo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2017-01-10T00:32:22Z

## Summary

In this interview, Elon Musk discusses the criteria for choosing impactful work and identifies three critical areas for future development: Artificial Intelligence, genetics, and brain-computer interfaces.

## Key Ideas

- Work does not need to change the world to be valuable; providing small benefits to many people is sufficient.
- Artificial Intelligence is the most pressing issue for the future of humanity, requiring careful development to ensure positive outcomes.
- Genetics offers the potential to cure diseases like Alzheimer's and dementia through reprogramming.
- Humanity is currently limited by the low-bandwidth interface between the biological brain and digital tools, necessitating a high-bandwidth brain-computer interface.

## Entities

- [[entities/elon-musk|Elon Musk]] (person): Entrepreneur and CEO of companies like Tesla and SpaceX.

## Topics

- [[topics/artificial-intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]]: The critical need to ensure AI development aligns with positive human outcomes.
- [[topics/genetics|Genetics]]: The potential for genetic reprogramming to eliminate diseases and improve human health.
- [[topics/brain-computer-interface|Brain-Computer Interface]]: Overcoming the bandwidth constraint between the human cortex and digital devices.

## Notable Claims

- AI is the single biggest item in the near term that is likely to affect humanity. Evidence: Musk cites the potential for AI to go wrong and the necessity of ensuring it goes right.
- Humans are effectively superhuman due to digital tools but are limited by the interface between the brain and these tools. Evidence: Musk describes computers, phones, and email as a 'digital tertiary self' constrained by low-bandwidth communication.

## Quotes

> Stuff doesn't need to be changed the world just to be good.
> It's very important that we have the advent of AI in a good way that is something that if you could look into the crystal ball and see the future you would like that outcome.
> We're effectively superhuman. But we're extremely bandwidth constrained in that interface between the cortex and your sort of that tertiary digital form of yourself.
