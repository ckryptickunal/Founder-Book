# Software to avoid the software people

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-software-to-avoid-the-software-people`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2013-03-18 17:52:03
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-software-to-avoid-the-software-people

## Summary

Sam Altman observes a strategic shift in B2B startups from building tools for internal IT departments to creating software that empowers non-technical employees to bypass IT bottlenecks.

## Key Ideas

- B2B startups are increasingly targeting non-technical end-users rather than IT departments.
- Internal IT departments often act as bottlenecks for business units needing software solutions.
- Startups can achieve faster adoption by selling directly to the person waiting in the internal development queue.
- There is a growing trend of 'shadow IT' where business units procure their own software to avoid slow internal processes.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Author of the essay and former president of Y Combinator.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding for startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/b2b-saas-strategy|B2B SaaS Strategy]]: The evolution of sales strategies for business-to-business software companies.
- [[topics/shadow-it|Shadow IT]]: The practice of employees using software or hardware without explicit organizational IT department approval.

## Notable Claims

- B2B startups are shifting focus from selling to IT departments to selling to non-technical business users. Evidence: Observation of Y Combinator startups changing their target customer base to avoid internal IT blocks.
- Bypassing internal IT departments is faster and easier for business units. Evidence: The existence of a 'development queue' that creates friction for non-technical employees.

## Quotes

> So now the startups are trying to avoid the developers at the other company (so they don't get blocked) and sell to the person who is waiting in the internal development queue.
