## Summary

John Glasgow, CEO and founder of Campfire, discusses building an AI-native ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) system designed to automate manual accounting and financial reporting for high-growth tech companies. The conversation covers Campfire's journey through Y Combinator, their strategy for displacing legacy incumbents like NetSuite, and the importance of founder-led sales and rapid product iteration.

## Key Ideas

- Campfire positions itself as an AI-native ERP, focusing on automating manual tasks for finance and accounting teams.
- The 'why now' for a new ERP is driven by the modernization of the rest of the finance tech stack (payroll, spend management) leaving the core general ledger as the last outdated piece.
- Founder-market fit was critical; Glasgow's 10-year background in corporate finance provided the credibility needed to tackle a mission-critical, established category.
- The go-to-market strategy involved a narrow focus on specific pain points (e.g., approval workflows, multi-entity accounting) that legacy systems handled poorly for tech companies.
- Rapid product velocity and the ability to ship features quickly gave customers confidence to switch from established incumbents like NetSuite.
- Founder-led sales remain essential even in the AI era to maintain proximity to customer feedback and drive product direction.
