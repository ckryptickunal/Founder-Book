# High Resolution Fundraising

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-high-resolution-fundraising`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: September 2010
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-high-resolution-fundraising

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that 'high-resolution fundraising'—using flexible instruments like convertible notes to offer different terms to different investors—is superior to traditional fixed-size equity rounds, as it eliminates investor deadlocks and allows startups to adjust fundraising based on real-time market feedback.

## Key Ideas

- Convertible notes allow startups to break fundraising deadlocks by rewarding early investors with lower valuations.
- Investor behavior is heavily influenced by social proof; investors often wait for others to commit before acting.
- Fixed-size equity rounds are inefficient for angel investing because they force a 'one-size-fits-all' price on investors with different risk profiles and value-adds.
- Startups should not fix a specific fundraising amount in advance, as the optimal amount depends on investor interest.
- High-resolution fundraising rewards bold investors with better terms and access, while penalizing 'follower' investors.

## Topics

- [[topics/convertible-notes|Convertible Notes]]: Debt instruments that convert into equity, providing flexibility in valuation and speed in closing deals.
- [[topics/fundraising-deadlocks|Fundraising Deadlocks]]: The phenomenon where investors delay commitments to see if others will invest first, stalling the fundraising process.
- [[topics/high-resolution-fundraising|High-Resolution Fundraising]]: The practice of offering different terms or valuations to different investors based on their timing, risk, or strategic value.

## Notable Claims

- Fixed-size equity rounds are an inefficient imitation of VC Series A rounds. Evidence: Series A rounds involve a single lead investor, whereas angel rounds involve multiple investors who should not necessarily receive the same price.
- Markets naturally evolve toward higher resolution. Evidence: The shift from rigid equity rounds to flexible convertible notes demonstrates a move toward more granular, efficient pricing.

## Quotes

> By far the biggest influence on investors' opinions of a startup is the opinion of other investors.
> Markets always evolve toward higher resolution.
> It's just not reasonable to expect startups to pick an optimal round size in advance, because that depends on the reactions of investors, and those are impossible to predict.
