# How do you structure your schedule to be most productive?

## Summary

This video discusses the fundamental conflict between 'Manager' and 'Maker' schedules, emphasizing the necessity of long, uninterrupted blocks of time for deep cognitive work like programming.

## Key Ideas

- Manager schedules consist of fragmented, short meetings suitable for administrative tasks.
- Maker schedules require long, uninterrupted blocks of time to maintain cognitive state.
- Programming requires significant 'warm-up' time to load complex logic into working memory.
- Frequent interruptions destroy productivity for deep work tasks.
