# Bitcoin Price Pressure

## Summary

Sam Altman analyzes the structural downward price pressure on Bitcoin, identifying merchant liquidation, miner electricity costs, and tax obligations as primary drivers, while arguing that long-term success depends on genuine transaction utility rather than speculation.

## Key Ideas

- Bitcoin faces inherent downward price pressure unlike traditional financial assets.
- Merchants selling Bitcoin for fiat currency to cover operational costs creates constant sell pressure.
- Miners are forced to sell Bitcoin to cover electricity costs, especially as mining difficulty increases.
- Government tax policies and the lack of tax-payment utility for Bitcoin contribute to selling.
- The blockchain technology underlying Bitcoin is likely to have a significant long-term impact regardless of Bitcoin's specific market success.

## Notable Claims

- The default price pressure of the Bitcoin ecosystem is downward. Evidence: Merchants sell for fiat, miners sell for electricity, and tax obligations require fiat conversion.
- Mining is currently unprofitable with reasonable electricity costs. Evidence: As difficulty rises and price falls, miners must sell a larger percentage of their holdings to cover fixed costs.
- Blockchain technology will be a key innovation regardless of Bitcoin's success. Evidence: Increasing number of blockchain-based companies appearing in Y Combinator interviews.
