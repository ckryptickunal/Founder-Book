# How Surbhi Sarna Built A $275M Biotech Company From Nothing

## Summary

Surbhi Sarna recounts her journey from a high school health scare involving ovarian cysts to founding InVision Medical, a biotech company that developed a device for early ovarian cancer detection. Despite facing significant bias as a young woman of color without advanced degrees, she successfully navigated fundraising, clinical trials, and FDA clearance, eventually selling her company to Boston Scientific for $275 million. She now serves as a group partner at Y Combinator, mentoring founders in the healthcare and biotech sectors.

## Key Ideas

- Personal experience can be a powerful driver for innovation and entrepreneurship.
- Founders should prioritize talking to customers and industry experts early to validate concepts.
- Bias against underrepresented founders and 'women's health' markets is a significant hurdle that requires persistence to overcome.
- A lack of traditional credentials (MD/PhD) should not deter founders if they possess deep domain knowledge and a clear problem-solving mission.
- Leveraging personal networks for introductions is more effective than relying on a large, pre-existing professional Rolodex.
