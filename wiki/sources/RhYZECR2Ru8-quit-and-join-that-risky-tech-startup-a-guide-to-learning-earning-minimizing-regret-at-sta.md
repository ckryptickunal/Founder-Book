# Quit and join that risky tech startup? A guide to learning, earning & minimizing regret at startups

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the importance of taking career risks for software engineers, designers, and builders. He outlines three paths for increasing risk—intrapreneurship, joining a startup, and founding a startup—and provides a framework for evaluating opportunities based on learning potential and financial compensation (salary vs. equity). He concludes by advocating for Jeff Bezos' 'regret minimization framework' to help professionals make long-term career decisions.

## Key Ideas

- The biggest risk for skilled builders is taking no risk at all.
- Career risk can be categorized into three levels: intrapreneurship, joining a startup, and starting a company.
- Evaluate job offers using two axes: learning (skills, network, product experience) and earning (salary vs. equity).
- When comparing equity, consider percentage ownership, company valuation, and vesting schedules.
- Use the 'regret minimization framework' to project yourself to age 80 to determine if a decision will lead to long-term regret.
- Understand the 'commandos, army, and police' stages of business growth to identify where you fit in an organization.
