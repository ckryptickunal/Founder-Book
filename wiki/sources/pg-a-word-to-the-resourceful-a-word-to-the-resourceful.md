# A Word to the Resourceful

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-a-word-to-the-resourceful`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: January 2012
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-a-word-to-the-resourceful

## Summary

Paul Graham explores the correlation between startup success and 'resourcefulness,' identifying a specific trait he calls 'conversational resourcefulness.' He argues that the most successful founders are those who can take a single piece of advice or a lead and execute on it independently, whereas unsuccessful founders often exhibit a form of denial or weakness that makes them difficult to communicate with.

## Key Ideas

- Successful founders are 'fire-and-forget': they require minimal guidance to execute tasks.
- Conversational resourcefulness is the ability to hear a single piece of advice and immediately understand and act upon all its implications.
- Unsuccessful founders often struggle with 'denial,' filtering advice to fit their pre-existing plans rather than engaging with the reality of the feedback.
- Being 'hard to talk to' is a symptom of a deeper lack of resourcefulness, not the cause of startup failure.
- The adage 'a word to the wise is sufficient' serves as the core metaphor for high-performing, resourceful founders.

## Notable Claims

- The most successful startups are led by founders who can take care of themselves. Evidence: Graham notes that successful founders do not require babysitting during processes like fundraising; they take a lead and close it.
- Unsuccessful founders are not necessarily less intelligent, but they lack the eagerness to follow implications. Evidence: Graham observes that they are intellectually capable but exhibit a 'glazed over' look when receiving advice, indicating an internal process of rationalization or dismissal.

## Quotes
