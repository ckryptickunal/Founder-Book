## Summary

Garry Tan explains the psychological concept of the 'golden shadow,' suggesting that feelings of intense awe or envy toward successful founders are often projections of one's own latent, unrealized potential.

## Key Ideas

- The 'golden shadow' is a psychological projection where we see our own hidden strengths in others.
- Feelings of envy or painful awe toward successful individuals are indicators of personal untapped potential.
- Admiration for specific traits in others, such as boldness or creativity, suggests those traits are dormant within oneself.
- Recognizing these projections allows individuals to reclaim and develop their own capabilities.
