## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, founder of Pebble, discusses the strategic importance of selecting and compensating startup advisors, emphasizing the value of mentors who are only a few years ahead in their entrepreneurial journey.

## Key Ideas

- Seek advisors who are 3-5 years ahead of your current stage for tactical, relevant advice.
- Engage more experienced, later-stage mentors for high-level strategic thinking.
- CEOs must synthesize advice from multiple sources rather than relying on a single mentor.
- Standard advisor equity grants typically range from 0.25% to 0.75% vesting over two years.
- Establish a regular cadence, such as weekly calls, to ensure accountability and consistent value from advisors.

## Notable Claims

- Advisors 3-5 years ahead are more useful for tactical advice than later-stage mentors. Evidence: Their experiences are fresher, and they can provide specific, actionable examples of what worked and what didn't.
- Standard advisor equity grants are typically between 0.25% and 0.75%. Evidence: Common industry practice for long-term advisory relationships.
