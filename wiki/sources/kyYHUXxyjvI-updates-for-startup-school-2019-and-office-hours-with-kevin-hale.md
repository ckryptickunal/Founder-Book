## Summary

Kevin Hale, a Y Combinator partner, discusses the 2019 updates to Startup School, a free online program designed to help founders build startups. The program focuses on practical, tactical advice, community building, and providing tools for early-stage founders, including single founders looking for co-founders and international participants.

## Key Ideas

- Startup School is the largest source of companies accepted into YC batches.
- The 2019 curriculum is shifted to focus on practical, tactical, and investor-focused evaluation of startup ideas.
- A new co-founder matching directory and group session structure helps solo founders find partners.
- Startup School is expanding to 18 international cities for meetups to foster community beyond San Francisco.
- Lectures are shortened to 20 minutes to improve engagement and focus on actionable content.
- The program emphasizes the importance of 'first principles' and honest self-evaluation through weekly updates.
- Early-stage success often comes from a customer-driven mindset, extreme responsiveness, and simple, intuitive product design.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The concept of achieving a state where the market demands the product, often characterized by uncontrollable growth.
- [[topics/customer-experience|Customer Experience]]: The strategy of providing fast, human, and helpful support to build trust and retention in early-stage startups.
- [[topics/co-founder-matching|Co-founder Matching]]: The process of finding and vetting a business partner, compared to dating in terms of intensity and required compatibility.
- [[topics/market-sizing|Market Sizing]]: The methodology of using bottom-up calculations to determine if a business model can reach a $100M+ revenue scale.

## Notable Claims

- Startup School is the single largest source of companies accepted into YC batches. Evidence: Data from the 2018 class showed over 60 companies accepted into YC came from Startup School.
- Giving equity to co-founders is the only appropriate use of equity for finding a partner. Evidence: YC discourages paying third-party recruiters or investors with equity for co-founder matching.
- The most important part of Startup School is the accountability and group sessions, not the lectures. Evidence: Lectures are available for free online, but the structure and community drive actual progress.
