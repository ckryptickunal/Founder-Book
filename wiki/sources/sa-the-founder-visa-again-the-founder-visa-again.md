# The Founder Visa (again)

## Summary

Sam Altman proposes a pilot program for a 'founder visa' that would allow Y Combinator to sponsor 100 visas annually for international startup founders, arguing that this small-scale experiment could drive significant job creation and economic growth in the U.S.

## Key Ideas

- The U.S. should implement a small-scale pilot program for founder visas to bypass the gridlock of comprehensive immigration reform.
- Y Combinator is willing to act as a beta tester for allocating 100 visas per year to international founders.
- The O1 visa process could be modified to better accommodate startup founders.
- Attracting international talent is essential for maintaining U.S. competitiveness in startup creation.
- Small, incremental policy changes are more effective than waiting for broad-based immigration reform.

## Notable Claims

- Less than 5% of the world's best founders are born in the U.S. Evidence: Based on the premise that intelligence and determination are evenly distributed globally.
- A pilot program of 100 visas could have a measurably large effect on U.S. job creation. Evidence: The potential for one of the 50 startups created annually to become a major corporation like Google or Facebook.
