# The secret to better product demos

## Summary

A brief guide on improving product demos by shifting the focus from product features to user-centric storytelling.

## Key Ideas

- Bad demos function like boring tours of software features.
- Great demos function like stories with a clear protagonist.
- The user should be the main character, and the product should be a prop used to solve their problem.
- Emotional connection with the user's pain is essential for an effective demo.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-demos|Product Demos]]: The practice of demonstrating software or product functionality to potential users or investors.
- [[topics/storytelling|Storytelling]]: Using narrative structures to make technical presentations more engaging and persuasive.

## Notable Claims

- Focusing on the user rather than the product makes a demo ten times better. Evidence: The speaker asserts this based on their experience observing numerous product demos.

## Quotes

> Great demos focus on the user not the product.
> Ultimately your product is just a prop she uses to solve a problem.
> Don't show me your product tell me a story about your user.
