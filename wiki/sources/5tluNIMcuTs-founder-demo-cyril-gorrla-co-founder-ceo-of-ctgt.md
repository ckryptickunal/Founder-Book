## Summary

Cyril Gorrla, CEO of CTGT, presents a solution to the 'black box' problem in AI, focusing on how modern LLMs use opacity, bias, and censorship to manipulate information. CTGT provides an 'AI accountability layer' that identifies and adjusts specific features within neural networks to remove censorship and bias in real-time, without altering core model weights, aiming to secure democratic infrastructure.

## Key Ideas

- Modern AI models function as opaque black boxes that can silently filter or distort information.
- Censorship and bias in AI pose significant risks to national security and democratic decision-making.
- CTGT's technology identifies specific neural features responsible for censorship and allows for real-time adjustment of these features.
- Unlike fine-tuning, CTGT's approach does not alter core model weights or knowledge, preserving the model's original capabilities.
- AI transparency and auditability are essential infrastructure requirements for governments and Fortune 500 companies.
- The goal is to move away from the status quo of 'throwing money at models' toward first-principles research in AI safety.

- [[topics/national-security|National Security]]: The risk posed by foreign-developed AI models that may enforce authoritarian agendas or propaganda.

## Notable Claims

- DeepSeek's R1 model shows 90% alignment with the official Chinese government perspective on sensitive topics. Evidence: Analysis of 125 sensitive questions where 114 favored the official Chinese viewpoint.
- CTGT can remove censorship from models without altering core weights or knowledge. Evidence: Demonstrated by increasing features associated with Tiananmen Square, allowing the model to provide a full, non-refusal answer.
