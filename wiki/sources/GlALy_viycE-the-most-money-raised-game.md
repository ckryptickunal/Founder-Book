# The "Most Money Raised" game

## Summary

A critique of the 'fundraising game' where founders prioritize raising capital over building a sustainable business, leading to loss of control, misaligned incentives, and inability to pivot.

## Key Ideas

- Raising excessive capital can lead to losing control of the company to the board.
- Investors often pressure founders to burn cash unnecessarily.
- Large funding rounds can attract employees who believe the company has already 'made it' rather than those focused on early-stage execution.
- High capital and momentum make it significantly harder to pivot when the business model is failing.
- Winning the fundraising game often results in a company that is harder to manage and fix.

## Notable Claims

- Raising as much money as possible often leads to losing control of the company. Evidence: The speaker notes that board members can fire founders when challenges arise after significant fundraising.
- Excessive funding makes pivoting nearly impossible. Evidence: The momentum and expectations created by large capital inflows create a path that is difficult to deviate from, even when it leads to failure.
