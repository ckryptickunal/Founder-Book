# The End of the Designer–Engineer Divide

## Summary

Rio Lou, Head of Design at Cursor, discusses the transformative impact of AI on design and software development. He outlines his mission to empower designers to code using AI agents, advocating for a 'systems-first' design philosophy that prioritizes core primitives over feature bloat. Lou details Cursor's evolution, including unifying its AI features and shifting to an agent-centric UI, and introduces his 'Baby Cursor' prototyping method for rapid, live code iteration. He envisions a future where design and engineering roles blur, code becomes a shared language, and interfaces adapt dynamically to individual user preferences, emphasizing the need for designers to cultivate deep craft, systems thinking, and domain knowledge.

## Key Ideas

- AI agents enable designers to code and build directly, blurring traditional design and engineering roles with code as a shared language.
- A 'systems-first' design approach, focusing on simple, flexible core primitives, is crucial for building scalable and coherent products.
- Designers should adopt a 'sculpting' workflow, where AI generates initial code, and designers refine and mold it to perfection.
- Live code prototyping tools, like 'Baby Cursor,' significantly accelerate iteration and testing of complex interactions compared to traditional mockups.
- Future user interfaces will be adaptive and recomposed by AI to fit individual user preferences and contexts, while maintaining fundamental system principles.
- Essential skills for designers in the age of AI include deep craft, systems thinking, and comprehensive domain knowledge to effectively leverage AI tools.

## Notable Claims

- Rio Lou's personal KPI at Cursor is to turn all designers into coders. Evidence: Rio states: 'My personal KPI at cursor this year is to turn all the designers into coders.'
- AI agents, like those in Cursor, make the coding process significantly quicker and less intimidating for designers. Evidence: Rio explains: 'But now it's like with the agent with cursor you make this process so quick... you can actually start coding without knowing how to code.'
- Cursor 2.0's agent-centric UI, which starts with a prompt box, is more approachable for non-coders than its previous file-centric view. Evidence: Rio contrasts: 'When you open up cursor when you have nothing, you still have like a blank editor, a blank file tree. It's really hard for like a person who isn't used to coding to do anything in that state versus now we flipped it when you start uncursor in this new world you can just type in your prompt boom see what happens.'
- Cursor's new composer model is four times faster and twice as cheap as Sonnet models. Evidence: Rio states: 'And with our new composer model, like it's four times faster than sonnetss and it's like twice as cheap.'
- AI currently struggles with making outputs 'pretty' or perfectly aligned with human aesthetic desires, leaving that as a key role for designers. Evidence: Rio notes: 'If you get something bad or like ugly, it's actually your job to make it pretty the way you want it. And that's the part that the AIS can't really do right now.'
