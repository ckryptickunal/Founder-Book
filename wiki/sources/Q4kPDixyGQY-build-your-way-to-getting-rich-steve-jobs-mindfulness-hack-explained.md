## Summary

Garry Tan reflects on his early career struggles, specifically his 2002 mindset of complaining and angst, and how shifting toward mindfulness—inspired by Steve Jobs—helped him move from a scarcity-driven perspective to one that recognizes opportunities, such as the one he missed by turning down an early job offer at Google.

## Key Ideas

- Complaining and wallowing in self-pity creates a negative feedback loop that hinders personal and professional growth.
- Mindfulness allows for a calmer, more observant state of mind, which is essential for recognizing subtle opportunities.
- Conventional wisdom among peers can often be wrong; individual intuition and observation are more reliable guides.
- An anxious mind interprets rejection as a personal failure, whereas a mindful mind views it as information or a redirection.
- Building and creating value is a more effective path to wealth than simply focusing on 'getting rich'.

- Complaining with peers creates a harmful shared worldview. Evidence: Tan notes that 90% of his early IM conversations were complaints, which reinforced his own negative outlook.
