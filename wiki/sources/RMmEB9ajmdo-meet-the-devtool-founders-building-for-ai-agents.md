# Meet The DevTool Founders Building For AI Agents

## Summary

This transcript explores the profound impact of AI agents on software development, shifting the focus from writing code to understanding context, solving problems, and building customer relationships. Speakers from various companies (Infracloud, Resend, Recall.ai, Griptape, Firecrawl, Porter) highlight how AI agents accelerate development, improve software quality, and make engineers more valuable. The discussion emphasizes the need to design products and documentation with 'agent experience' as a primary consideration, noting that agents are increasingly becoming users and even driving customer acquisition. The future envisions agents making autonomous decisions, assisting in strategic planning, and enabling non-engineers to contribute to codebases, leading to a diverse market of specialized coding agents and an unpredictable, rapidly evolving technological landscape.

## Key Ideas

- AI agents are fundamentally transforming software development, shifting focus from code to context, problem-solving, and customer relationships.
- AI agents significantly increase developer productivity, accelerate feature delivery, and improve software quality.
- The role of software engineers is becoming more valuable, not less, as agents handle toil and allow humans to focus on higher-level tasks.
- Products and services must prioritize 'agent experience' in their design, including CLI-first interfaces and context-window-optimized documentation.
- AI agents can provide valuable strategic critique and feedback, helping to reveal gaps in human plans.
- Open models are rapidly catching up to closed-source alternatives in capability and efficiency.
- The market for AI coding agents is unlikely to be winner-take-all, with a long tail of specialized agents emerging for diverse user personas.
- AI agents enable non-engineering roles (e.g., finance, customer support) to contribute directly to codebases.
- The pace of change in AI is extremely rapid and unpredictable, requiring constant adaptation and a willingness to deprecate outdated features.
- Agents are increasingly becoming a primary distribution channel and user base for developer tools.
- The future of programming involves 'idea guy programming,' where developers express intent and agents generate industrial-grade code.
- AI is making open-source tooling much better, closing the gap with closed-source solutions.

## Notable Claims

- Code doesn't matter anymore; what matters is context, understanding problems deeply, and building customer relationships. Evidence: Repeated statement at the beginning and end of the transcript, emphasizing a core shift in focus for software development.
- AI agents make engineers more valuable, not less, by taking out toil and enabling faster, higher-quality software development. Evidence: Multiple speakers state, 'The AI agents have taken so much toil out of building software that now we can get a lot better stuff out a lot faster, a lot higher quality. It's going to make uh engineers more valuable, not less valuable.'
- The capabilities of current AI models make the impossible possible and set a new 'table stakes' for development. Evidence: A speaker states, 'The capabilities that models give you today make the impossible possible. And not only that, they make it table stakes.'
- AI coding models unexpectedly drive customer acquisition by recommending products like Recall.ai. Evidence: A Recall.ai representative notes, 'We looked at the ways customers find out about Recall, and we discovered that all the coding models recommend Recall all the time, and this was super surprising to us.'
- Given sufficient context, AI agents possess remarkably good judgment for strategic planning and product direction. Evidence: A speaker describes agents critiquing their plans for the next few weeks based on thousands of support tickets and team shipping data, stating, 'it was remarkable that they had very clear judgment.'
- Open models are rapidly catching up to and becoming more efficient than closed models. Evidence: A speaker claims, 'It's still much of a secret that open models are quickly catching up and are far more efficient to run.'
- The market for coding agents will not be winner-take-all, but rather a long tail of specialized products. Evidence: A speaker predicts, 'coding agents are not actually a winner-take-all market. There's going to be a very long tail of various different products that are very customized for a certain type of person.'
- Non-engineering roles like finance and customer support are beginning to contribute directly to codebases using AI agents. Evidence: A speaker shares observations: 'finance people contributing to the pricing or the the billing logic of the of the product. Or you saw folks from sort of the customer support org that are spinning up agents to fix bugs.'
- AI technology has increased, not decreased, the need for software engineers. Evidence: A speaker from Recurly states, 'AI technology they think it's going to replace software engineers but what we've actually seen at Recurly is it's increased the need for software engineers. We're actually hiring more people now than we planned to hire before.'
- By late 2025/early 2026, the relationship flipped, with agents doing 90% of the coding. Evidence: A speaker reflects, 'since, you know, the late 2025, early 2026, we flipped to the agents actually can do 90% of the coding and the the power relationship or the the relationship flipped.'
