## Summary

Visiting partner Aaron Epstein and YC's Eric Migicovsky provide rapid-fire, actionable feedback on several startup websites, focusing on improving clarity, messaging, and user trust.

## Key Ideas

- Websites must immediately answer 'What is this?' and 'Who is this for?'
- Avoid jargon and overly broad 'visionary' statements; focus on tangible benefits.
- Use social proof (logos, user counts) to build trust, especially for new or unknown brands.
- Don't sell features; sell the superpowers or benefits the product provides.
- Use standard design patterns and mental models rather than reinventing navigation.
- If a product is in beta or early stages, be honest and clear about it rather than hiding behind complex landing pages.
- Test your website by watching real users navigate it (e.g., using FullStory or user testing services).

## Notable Claims

- Overloading a landing page with too many features reduces conversion. Evidence: Multiple examples showed that users get confused when presented with too many disparate value props.
- People do not read long blocks of text on websites. Evidence: The reviewers consistently skipped body text and focused only on headers and images during their analysis.
- Using standard design patterns improves usability. Evidence: Users have mental models for how e-commerce and SaaS sites should work; deviating from these creates unnecessary friction.

## Quotes
