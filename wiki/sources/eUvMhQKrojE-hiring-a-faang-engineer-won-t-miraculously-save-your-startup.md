# Hiring a FAANG engineer won’t miraculously save your startup

## Metadata

## Summary

The video discusses the common mistake early-stage startup founders make by over-hiring expensive engineers from major tech companies, a phenomenon dubbed 'sebastianism'.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often believe that hiring a high-profile engineer from a FAANG company will solve all their startup's problems.
- The cost of hiring top-tier talent from big tech is often unsustainable for early-stage startups.
- Sebastianism is the psychological tendency to wait for a 'savior' figure to fix systemic issues.
- High compensation packages at big tech companies create unrealistic salary expectations for early-stage startups.
