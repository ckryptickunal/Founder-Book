## Summary

Diode is an AI-enabled hardware design firm that automates the creation of printed circuit boards (PCBs). By reframing PCB design as a software engineering problem, the founders use LLMs to generate designs, significantly increasing speed and efficiency while maintaining high-quality verification standards for clients ranging from startups to Fortune 100 companies.

## Key Ideas

- Reframing hardware design as a software problem allows for the application of modern compiler and verification techniques to PCB development.
- AI models possess latent knowledge of electrical engineering but require a code-based representation of designs to be effective.
- The '80/20' rule in AI allows for 'services-as-a-product' models where AI handles the bulk of the work while humans perform final verification.
- Customer discovery is critical; the founders pivoted from a verification-only tool to a full-service design generation platform after realizing users wanted solutions, not just tools.
- Hardware is the next frontier for AI-driven productivity, moving beyond software to impact physical world infrastructure.

## Notable Claims

- Diode can design over 100 boards in a few months, a task that would take traditional engineers significantly longer. Evidence: The founders attribute this to internal software tools that automate the design pipeline.
- Traditional PCB design tooling has not evolved significantly since the 1980s. Evidence: The founders note that current tools are heavily visual and graphical, lacking the automation benefits found in modern software development.
