## Summary

Adora Cheung, CEO and co-founder of Homejoy, shares her experiences and strategies for scaling a startup, covering product growth, organizational development, and personal management for founders.

## Key Ideas

- Scaling should only begin after achieving product-market fit, identifying a massive opportunity, and ensuring the timing is right.
- Growth strategies should prioritize sticky growth and viral growth over paid acquisition, which can be dangerous if not managed carefully.
- Founders must transition from doing everything themselves to hiring A+ players and treating the recruiting process with the same rigor as product development.
- Communication mechanisms must evolve as the company grows; transparency and shared mission/values are essential for alignment.
- Founders need to manage themselves by building a support network of peers, maintaining emotional stability, and protecting time for deep thinking.
- Scaling a startup is like driving a junk car at 200 mph while simultaneously replacing its parts to turn it into a high-performance vehicle.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The essential prerequisite for scaling, defined by whether customers actually want and need the product.
- [[topics/growth-strategies|Growth Strategies]]: The distinction between sticky, viral, and paid growth, and the risks associated with each.
- [[topics/organizational-scaling|Organizational Scaling]]: The process of hiring A+ talent, establishing core values, and evolving communication structures as a team grows.
- [[topics/founder-self-management|Founder Self-Management]]: Strategies for maintaining sanity, managing stress, and protecting time for strategic thinking.

## Notable Claims

- Scaling a startup is 10 times harder than reaching product-market fit. Evidence: Anecdotal experience from the speaker's journey at Homejoy.
- Founders should not build internal tools if third-party solutions are available. Evidence: Homejoy's negative experience building their own phone system versus using specialized third-party software.
- Core values should serve as a hiring framework. Evidence: Values help others in the organization make hiring decisions that align with the founder's vision.
