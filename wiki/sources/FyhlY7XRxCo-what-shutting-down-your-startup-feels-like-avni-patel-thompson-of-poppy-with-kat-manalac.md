# What Shutting Down Your Startup Feels Like - Avni Patel Thompson of Poppy with Kat Manalac

## Summary

Avni Patel Thompson, founder of the startup Poppy, discusses the difficult process of shutting down her company after three years. She details the economic challenges of the childcare industry, the decision-making process behind closing the business rather than pivoting, and the importance of transparency with investors and employees throughout the wind-down.

## Key Ideas

- Startup failure is often a result of challenging unit economics rather than a lack of effort or vision.
- Transparency with investors and employees, including sharing financial realities, is crucial for maintaining trust during a shutdown.
- Founders should view their startup journey as a series of experiments and learning opportunities rather than a single binary outcome of success or failure.
- The decision to shut down should be based on whether the business can achieve the founder's original mission at scale, rather than just keeping the company alive as a smaller, less impactful entity.
- Grieving the loss of a startup is a normal and necessary part of the founder's journey.

## Notable Claims

- Childcare is an inherently margin-challenged category. Evidence: The tension between what parents can afford to pay and what skilled caregivers need to earn makes it difficult to build a scalable, high-margin business.
- Transparency with investors throughout the company's life makes the shutdown process easier. Evidence: By providing monthly updates and being honest about financial status, the final decision to shut down was not a surprise to investors.
