## Summary

Stoke Space is a aerospace startup founded by former Blue Origin engineers Andy and Tom, aiming to revolutionize the space industry by developing fully and rapidly reusable rockets. By focusing on a reusable second-stage capsule capable of surviving atmospheric re-entry, the company seeks to drastically lower costs and increase launch frequency, moving beyond the industry standard of discarding upper stages.

## Key Ideas

- Full and rapid reusability is the next frontier for lowering the cost and increasing the reliability of space access.
- The second-stage capsule is the primary barrier to full reusability due to the extreme heat and speeds encountered during re-entry.
- Vertical integration—building parts in-house—is essential for rapid iteration and development cycles.
- Software and custom internal tools like 'Bolt Line' are critical for managing the complex maintenance and operational data required for reusable vehicles.
- Hardware startups require a different fundraising and operational approach compared to traditional SaaS companies.

## Notable Claims

- Full reusability of rockets will act as an 'iPhone App Store moment' for the space industry. Evidence: The founders argue that lowering costs and increasing frequency will enable new, currently unimagined applications.
- In-house manufacturing reduces iteration cycles from months to days. Evidence: By controlling the machine shop and testing facilities, the team can modify and re-test hardware immediately after failure.
- The Andromeda capsule can survive 2700° F re-entry temperatures. Evidence: The design utilizes a custom heat shield with cold liquid hydrogen flowing through a heat exchanger.
