## Summary

Pete Koomen, YC Group Partner and Optimizely co-founder, provides a tactical guide for early-stage startup founders on navigating the enterprise sales funnel, emphasizing that founders themselves must lead the sales process before achieving product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Founders are the best salespeople for their own products because they possess the necessary expertise and conviction.
- Sales pre-product-market fit is about experimentation and learning, not just revenue.
- Avoid the 'easy' trap: don't waste time selling to people who are easy to talk to but don't actually need the product.
- The sales funnel is not adversarial; it is about helping customers solve problems.
- Implementation is the founder's responsibility; a deal is not finished until the customer is successfully using the product.
- Pricing should be treated as an experiment; don't be afraid to quote higher prices to test demand and seriousness.

## Notable Claims

- If you cannot sell your product yourself, you will not be able to hire someone else to do it successfully. Evidence: Founder-led sales are required to build credibility and maintain the tight feedback loop necessary before product-market fit.
- High prices can actually help you sell more effectively. Evidence: Higher prices signal value and filter for customers who are genuinely desperate for a solution, as seen with Stripe.

> In your first few sales, you're optimizing for learning, not unit economics.
