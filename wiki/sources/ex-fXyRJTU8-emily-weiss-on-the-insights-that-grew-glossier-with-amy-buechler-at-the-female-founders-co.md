# Emily Weiss on the Insights That Grew Glossier - With Amy Buechler at the Female Founders Conference

## Summary

Emily Weiss, founder of Into The Gloss and Glossier, discusses the evolution of her direct-to-consumer beauty brand, emphasizing the importance of community, customer feedback, and the democratization of the beauty industry.

## Key Ideas

- Beauty serves as a conduit for women to connect, share, and develop their personal voices.
- Technology has upended the traditional brand-to-customer paradigm, allowing for more direct, two-way relationships.
- Product development at Glossier is an art form that balances customer feedback with editorial expertise rather than pure crowdsourcing.
- Direct-to-consumer models allow for deeper customer data insights and personalized experiences that traditional retail cannot match.
- Hiring for potential and cultural fit is often more valuable than hiring for traditional experience, especially in rapidly evolving fields like marketing.

## Topics

- [[topics/direct-to-consumer-dtc-retail|Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) Retail]]: The shift from traditional retail channels like Sephora to online, brand-owned platforms.
- [[topics/community-driven-product-development|Community-Driven Product Development]]: Using social media and blog feedback to inform product iterations and new launches.
- [[topics/beauty-industry-evolution|Beauty Industry Evolution]]: The move away from 'beauty shaming' and rigid standards toward individual curation and self-expression.
- [[topics/management-and-hiring|Management and Hiring]]: The philosophy of hiring for curiosity and potential over established playbooks.

## Notable Claims

- 79% of Glossier's growth is driven by peer-to-peer recommendations. Evidence: Emily Weiss cites this as the primary driver of their 600% year-over-year growth.
- Traditional beauty brands lack a direct relationship with their customers. Evidence: Weiss notes that brands sold through third-party retailers do not own customer data or direct communication channels.
