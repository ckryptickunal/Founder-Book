# Secrets You Can Learn From Your Customers

## Summary

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell discuss the critical importance of early-stage founders personally engaging with their customers to gain insights. They argue that genuine empathy and direct, one-on-one interaction—rather than relying on data reports or surveys—are the fastest ways to understand customer problems and build successful solutions.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often start with overconfidence, but must quickly pivot to a mindset of learning from users.
- Caring about customers personally creates trust and unlocks insights that cannot be gathered through mass communication.
- The best product insights often come from simple, direct questions asked during one-on-one conversations.
- Founders should avoid building layers (like PMs or data teams) between themselves and their customers in the early stages.
- Solving small, obvious problems for users can lead to massive breakthroughs in product-market fit.

## Notable Claims

- The fastest way to learn about a problem is to care about your customers. Evidence: Examples from Airbnb, Brex, and Twitch show that personal interaction led to faster product improvements than data analysis.
- Hiring intermediaries like PMs too early prevents founders from learning. Evidence: The speakers note that layers between founders and customers obscure the raw, actionable feedback needed for early-stage growth.
