## Summary

Rujul Zaparde, co-founder of Zip, explains the company's early strategy of securing its first 10 enterprise customers through cold outreach to validate product-market fit, emphasizing the importance of building an outbound sales muscle early on.

## Key Ideas

- Validating product-market fit by selling to strangers rather than relying on referrals or personal networks.
- Using cold LinkedIn outreach as a primary, cost-effective channel for early customer acquisition.
- Leveraging 'advice-seeking' as a non-threatening entry point to learn about industry problems.
- Building an outbound-driven sales culture from day one.
- The necessity of proving a business idea is worth the founder's time investment.

## Notable Claims

- Selling to 10 strangers is a better indicator of product-market fit than selling to friends or referrals. Evidence: Strangers have no obligation to the founder, so a purchase indicates genuine value.
- Asking for advice is an effective way to initiate conversations with potential enterprise customers. Evidence: It allows founders to learn about industry problems while building relationships without immediate pressure to buy.

## Quotes

> If you can get 10 different people and 10 different companies in the world to take out their proverbial credit cards and buy your thing when they don't owe you anything in the world that means like you're more likely to have Market fit.
> We would just reach out and we would ask truthfully initially for advice because we wanted to learn more about the space and what their problems were.
