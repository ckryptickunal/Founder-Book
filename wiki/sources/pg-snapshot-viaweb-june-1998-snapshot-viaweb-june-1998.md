## Summary

Paul Graham reflects on the state of his startup, Viaweb, in June 1998, just before its acquisition by Yahoo. He discusses the technical and operational constraints of the early web, including small screen sizes, the lack of modern SEO, the necessity of hosting servers in-office, and the creative workarounds used to manage growth and competition.

## Key Ideas

- Early web design was constrained by small screen resolutions and limited font rendering, often requiring text to be rendered as images.
- Startups in the 90s relied heavily on traditional PR firms for visibility rather than modern digital marketing.
- Infrastructure was primitive; Viaweb hosted servers in-office, leading to significant heat and bandwidth management challenges.
- Viaweb used clever tactics like 'ringer' employees and obfuscated URLs to deter competitors.
- The company utilized a custom template language, RTML, which was essentially Common Lisp disguised with a syntax-heavy interface.

## Notable Claims

- Google was not considered a major search engine in June 1998. Evidence: Graham lists seven search engines that mattered at the time, excluding Google, which incorporated in September 1998.
- Viaweb's software was used to build the Viaweb site itself. Evidence: Graham notes they wanted to experience the product exactly as their users did.
