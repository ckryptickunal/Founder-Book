## Summary

Kimberly Bryant, founder of Black Girls Code, shares her journey as an 'accidental social entrepreneur' at the 2015 Female Founders Conference. She discusses the mission of her organization to empower girls of color in technology, the importance of mentorship, and key lessons for startup founders, drawing inspiration from both Jim Collins' 'Good to Great' and the wisdom of Oprah Winfrey.

## Key Ideas

- Technology education is a matter of social equity and justice, not just skill acquisition.
- Founders should define core values and maintain a 'reckless disregard for the impossible' to achieve growth.
- Building a successful organization requires hiring people who are smarter and more capable than the founder.
- The 'Big Hairy Audacious Goal' (BHAG) for Black Girls Code is to train 1 million girls of color to code by 2040.
- Diversity in technology is a business and social imperative to ensure broader innovation.
- Founders must focus on their passion, which serves as a guiding force during difficult times.

## Notable Claims

- The number of women receiving computer science degrees has plummeted since the late 1980s. Evidence: Data showing a drop from 36% in 1989 to 12-14% at the time of the talk, with even lower percentages for women of color.
- Technology is currently designed mostly by males, limiting innovation. Evidence: The speaker argues that missing out on the perspectives of half the population limits the creativity and solutions available to the industry.
