# Reprogram your mind with exec coaching

## Summary

Garry Tan and Cameron Yarbrough, founder of Torch.io, discuss the critical role of executive coaching, therapy, and inner work in helping startup founders overcome personal trauma, improve leadership, and navigate business challenges.

## Key Ideas

- Many startup founders are driven by early-life trauma, which can lead to both high performance and dysfunctional leadership patterns.
- The 'horse and rider' metaphor illustrates the need to align emotional impulses (the horse) with conscious, rational decision-making (the rider).
- Executive coaching and therapy provide a safe, professional space to unpack personal history and develop self-awareness that friends or family cannot provide.
- High-empathy, high-accountability leadership is essential for building resilient organizations, especially during crises like COVID-19.
- Group coaching (T-group style) is a powerful, advanced tool for founders to realize they are not alone and to gain diverse perspectives on leadership.
- Behavioral change is physically difficult because it requires rewiring neurobiology; consistent practice and external support are necessary to overcome old patterns.

## Notable Claims

- Trauma is a common origin for many successful startup founders. Evidence: Cameron Yarbrough's clinical experience observing founders from tumultuous family systems.
- Executive coaching and therapy are essential for making unpopular but necessary business decisions. Evidence: The guest's experience in making the difficult decision to acquire Everwise despite contrary advice.
- Transparency with employees during a crisis builds trust. Evidence: The guest's practice of being open about churn and layoffs at Torch.io.
