# Emergent: How Six Months of Tinkering Led To A $100M ARR Company

## Summary

Mukund, co-founder of Emergent, discusses his journey from building the logistics giant Dunzo to creating Emergent, an AI-native platform that enables users to build and ship functional software without programming knowledge. The company achieved $100M ARR in just nine months by focusing on autonomous agents that handle the entire software development lifecycle, including backend and database management.

## Key Ideas

- Democratizing software development by allowing non-programmers to build and ship functional applications via chat.
- The importance of 'living at the edge'—building for capabilities that are not yet fully possible but are clearly emerging.
- Transitioning from building local, operations-heavy businesses to global, AI-native software companies.
- Using benchmarks like SweetBench to focus engineering efforts and drive innovation in agentic systems.
- The necessity of 'doing things that don't scale' early on to deeply understand customer pain points.
- The shift from building simple co-pilots to creating autonomous multi-agent systems that handle complex tasks like testing and deployment.

- Emergent reached $100M ARR in nine months. Evidence: Stated by the founder during the interview.
- Building a global company from India is as difficult as building a local one. Evidence: Founder's personal experience with Dunzo and Emergent.
- Living at the edge of technology allows for early discovery of critical infrastructure problems. Evidence: Emergent's need to invent container and snapshotting technology before it was standard.
