# This AI Startup Is Taking Over Phone Sales

## Metadata

## Summary

Simple AI, founded by former Y Combinator software engineers Cat and Zach, develops advanced AI voice agents that handle sales phone calls for businesses. After initially exploring consumer-focused AI products like a 'better Siri' and an AI that made calls on behalf of users (which gained viral attention), they pivoted to a B2B model due to inbound interest from companies. Their current product helps businesses like Omaha Steaks manage complex sales, significantly improve upsell rates (30% better than trained human reps), and provide a highly personalized customer experience. Simple AI focuses on deep integration with legacy systems, invests heavily in R&D to minimize latency and enhance agent capabilities, and aims to increase customer revenue and satisfaction rather than just cutting costs. They recently raised $14 million and are actively hiring to scale their team and product.

## Key Ideas

- Simple AI provides AI voice agents that handle end-to-end sales phone calls for businesses, sounding natural and requiring no human intervention.
- The company's journey involved a significant pivot from consumer AI products (a 'better Siri' and an AI for making personal calls) to a B2B enterprise solution.
- The pivot was driven by direct inbound interest from businesses, notably Omaha Steaks, who recognized the potential of the voice AI for their call centers.
- Simple AI's agents are highly effective at sales, outperforming trained human representatives in upselling by 30% and offering personalized interactions based on customer history and demographics.
- The technology addresses critical business problems like seasonal workforce scaling (e.g., Omaha Steaks' holiday rush) and integration with complex, often legacy, IT systems.
- Simple AI prioritizes enhancing customer experience and increasing revenue for its clients, rather than solely focusing on cost reduction.
- Significant engineering challenges include achieving ultra-low latency (under 600ms), fine-tuning custom AI models for specific customer needs, improving transcription accuracy for details like addresses, and robust agent orchestration to prevent AI 'lying' or errors.
- The founders emphasize the importance of listening to users, adapting to market needs, and building 'something people want' over having a perfectly defined idea from the start, drawing on their experience at Y Combinator.
