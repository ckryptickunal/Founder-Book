# Write Like You Talk

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that writing should mirror spoken language to improve readability and clarity, suggesting that formal, complex writing often obscures meaning and creates distance between the writer and the reader.

## Key Ideas

- Writing in spoken language makes content more accessible and engaging.
- Formal writing often uses complex sentence structures and vocabulary that would sound unnatural in conversation.
- Complex language can give writers a false sense of depth, masking a lack of substance.
- Experts in difficult fields often use simple, informal language to communicate complex ideas effectively.
- A practical editing strategy is to read drafts aloud or explain them to a friend to identify unnatural phrasing.
