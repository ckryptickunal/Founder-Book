# Startups That Sell to the Biggest Companies in the World

## Metadata

## Summary

Y Combinator highlights a shift in the startup landscape where early-stage companies are increasingly securing multi-million dollar deals with Fortune 10 enterprises, driven primarily by the rapid development capabilities enabled by AI.

## Key Ideas

- AI has lowered the barriers for startups to sell to large enterprises by facilitating faster product development and solving critical internal problems.
- The traditional 'stealth mode' approach of building for years to achieve feature parity is becoming obsolete.
- Large enterprise leaders are actively seeking AI solutions and are more willing to engage with small, agile teams.
- Coding agents allow small teams to ship complex, nuanced products in months rather than years.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/enterprise-sales|Enterprise Sales]]: The process of startups selling products and services to large, established corporations.
- [[topics/ai-driven-development|AI-Driven Development]]: The use of AI tools and coding agents to accelerate the product development lifecycle for small teams.

## Notable Claims

- AI has removed the historical barriers that prevented startups from selling to large companies. Evidence: Enterprise leaders are actively seeking AI solutions, and coding agents allow small teams to build enterprise-grade products in months.
- The strategy of operating in stealth for years to reach feature parity is dead. Evidence: Small teams can now ship thoughtful, nuanced products quickly enough to be useful to Fortune 10 companies.

## Quotes

> For the first time, a two-to-three person team can ship something a Fortune 10 company can find useful.
> The meme where some startups want to operate in stealth for three years until they reach feature parity with an incumbent before launching is dead.
