# The Truth About The AI Bubble

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses the stabilization of the AI economy in 2025, noting a shift in model preference toward Anthropic, the commoditization of base models, and the emergence of an orchestration layer for startups. They explore the 'AI bubble' narrative, comparing current infrastructure buildouts to the internet's telecom era, and highlight the ongoing necessity of human teams despite AI-driven efficiency gains.

## Key Ideas

- The AI economy has matured and stabilized, moving from a period of chaotic, rapid shifts to a more predictable playbook for AI-native startups.
- Anthropic has overtaken OpenAI as the preferred LLM provider for YC startups, driven by strong performance in coding and reasoning tasks.
- Startups are increasingly using an 'orchestration layer' to arbitrage between different models, selecting the best tool for specific tasks rather than relying on a single provider.
- The 'AI bubble' is viewed as a necessary infrastructure installation phase, similar to the 1990s telecom boom, which will eventually enable a massive deployment phase for consumer applications.
- Despite AI productivity gains, startups are still hiring significant teams because customer expectations for software quality and complexity have risen proportionally.
- Domain-specific models, fine-tuned with RL on proprietary datasets, are proving competitive against larger, general-purpose foundation models.

## Notable Claims

- Anthropic has replaced OpenAI as the most popular model choice for YC startups. Evidence: Data from the Winter 2026 YC selection cycle shows Anthropic usage surpassing OpenAI.
- The current AI infrastructure buildout is analogous to the 1990s telecom bubble. Evidence: Excess capacity in telecom enabled YouTube; similarly, current GPU/compute gluts will enable future AI applications.
- Startups are not yet able to run trillion-dollar companies with one person. Evidence: Despite AI efficiency, companies still require human teams to meet rising customer expectations and execute complex tasks.
