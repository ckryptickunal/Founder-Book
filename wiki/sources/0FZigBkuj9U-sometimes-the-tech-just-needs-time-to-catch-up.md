# Sometimes, the tech just needs time to catch up.

## Metadata

## Summary

A brief overview of how the company Replicate persisted through early struggles and a period of obscurity before finding massive success following the emergence of image diffusion models.

## Key Ideas

- Technology adoption often requires patience and timing.
- Startups may face periods of dormancy or pivot-like pauses before finding product-market fit.
- External technological shifts, such as the rise of image diffusion models, can act as a catalyst for rapid growth.

## Entities

- [[entities/replicate|Replicate]] (company): A platform for running machine learning models in the cloud.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-resilience|Startup Resilience]]: The necessity of persistence and the ability to weather periods of low traction.
- [[topics/technological-timing|Technological Timing]]: The impact of external market shifts on the viability of existing software products.

## Notable Claims

- Replicate paused operations for several months during the pandemic due to poor performance. Evidence: The speaker notes that the company was going so poorly they stopped working on it for several months.
- Replicate's growth exploded overnight following the release of image diffusion models. Evidence: The speaker describes a transition from two years of obscurity to sudden rapid growth once the new technology emerged.

## Quotes

> Sometimes it will take a bit of time for technology to catch up.
> They were just building this thing in obscurity for two years until the image diffusion models came out and then it just like exploded like overnight.
