## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Ivan Lee, founder of Datasore, to discuss the ethics of AI, the importance of training data, and the role of data labeling in machine learning. They explore why AI models can exhibit biases, how these systems act as mirrors to human society, and the necessity of high-quality tooling for data scientists.

## Key Ideas

- AI models are not inherently biased but act as mirrors reflecting the data and societal biases they are trained on.
- Data labeling is a critical, time-consuming bottleneck in the machine learning pipeline that requires better, professional-grade tooling.
- Removing sensitive attributes (like names) from training data is often insufficient to prevent bias, as models can find secondary proxies (like hobbies) to replicate those biases.
- The quality of an AI model is fundamentally limited by the quality and integrity of the data used to train it.
- Curiosity is the most important trait for success in the rapidly evolving field of technology.

- [[topics/natural-language-processing-nlp|Natural Language Processing (NLP)]]: A branch of AI focused on the interaction between computers and human language.

## Notable Claims

- AI models are not inherently racist; they are mirrors of the data provided to them. Evidence: Models learn from human-generated content (newspapers, messages) which inherently contains human biases.
- Removing specific identifiers from training data does not eliminate bias. Evidence: Models can identify secondary proxies, such as hobbies or interests, to maintain discriminatory patterns.
- Data scientists spend more than half of their time cleaning and labeling data. Evidence: Industry surveys indicate that data preparation is the primary bottleneck in the machine learning cycle.
