# Tracy Young on the importance of representation and recognizing unconscious bias.

## Summary

Tracy Young discusses her personal struggle with imposter syndrome and the systemic unconscious biases that shaped her early perceptions of what a founder should look like.

## Key Ideas

- Imposter syndrome is often fueled by systemic societal expectations rather than individual inadequacy.
- Early childhood education and media representation play a critical role in shaping perceptions of leadership.
- Unconscious bias creates narrow archetypes for success that exclude diverse groups.
- Recognizing and challenging internal narratives is essential for personal and professional growth.

## Topics

- [[topics/imposter-syndrome|Imposter Syndrome]]: The internal feeling of being a fraud despite evidence of success, often exacerbated by a lack of representation.
- [[topics/unconscious-bias|Unconscious Bias]]: Social stereotypes about certain groups that individuals form outside their own conscious awareness.
- [[topics/representation|Representation]]: The visibility of diverse groups in leadership roles and media, which influences self-perception and career aspirations.

## Notable Claims

- Systemic societal factors influence an individual's perception of their own capability to be a founder. Evidence: The speaker notes that her belief that she didn't fit the founder mold was reinforced by school environments and media representation.
