# The idea for DoorDash didn’t come from surveys—it came from living a day in the customer’s shoes.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `KOgCiznK8ug`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-04-01T22:43:40Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KOgCiznK8ug

## Summary

DoorDash co-founders discovered their business model not through surveys, but by shadowing a macaroon store owner, where they identified a significant unmet demand for delivery services.

## Key Ideas

- Direct observation of customer pain points is more effective than survey-based research.
- Identifying a specific, recurring problem for a single merchant can reveal a scalable market opportunity.
- The transition from a niche solution to a platform requires validating consumer demand and supply-side logistics (driver workforce).

## Entities

- [[entities/doordash|DoorDash]] (company): A food delivery platform.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator.

## Topics

- [[topics/customer-discovery|Customer Discovery]]: The process of understanding customer needs through immersive observation rather than traditional surveys.
- [[topics/market-validation|Market Validation]]: Testing whether a identified merchant need translates into consumer demand and a viable labor supply.

## Notable Claims

- Customers often struggle to articulate their actual problems when asked directly in surveys. Evidence: The founders found that shadowing business owners revealed insights that surveys failed to capture.
- A thick booklet of rejected delivery orders indicated a massive, unserved market for local delivery. Evidence: The macaroon store owner was turning away significant business because she lacked a delivery infrastructure.

## Quotes

> We wanted to actually feel what it was like their lived experience versus just asking a bunch of survey questions.
> I think sometimes it's very very hard for any customer to tell you exactly what is inside their brain when you ask them what problems they have.
