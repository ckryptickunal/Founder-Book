# US DoD needs great software | Palantir eng & special forces vet founded Adyton and Mustr

## Summary

James Boyd, a former Special Forces soldier and early Palantir employee, discusses his startup Adyton and its flagship product, Muster. The company aims to modernize Department of Defense (DoD) software by providing high-quality, mobile-first communication and accountability tools, addressing the critical gap between consumer-grade technology and the outdated, paper-based systems currently used by the US military.

## Key Ideas

- The US military suffers from a significant 'software gap' compared to the private sector, leading to lost productivity and safety risks.
- Muster is a mobile-first platform designed to digitize personnel accountability and communication within the DoD.
- Defense tech startups can succeed by adopting a bottom-up adoption strategy, proving value to end-users before seeking top-down enterprise procurement.
- Building software for the DoD requires navigating complex security, compliance, and connectivity challenges that are not present in consumer app development.
- There is a massive opportunity to catalyze a new generation of defense innovation by combining Silicon Valley engineering talent with military mission-driven purpose.

## Notable Claims

- The US military's reliance on outdated software costs billions in productivity and can cost lives. Evidence: Anecdotal evidence from the founder regarding his brother's inability to coordinate effectively during a firefight due to restricted device capabilities.
- Muster can reduce personnel accountability time from hours (using phone trees) to 30 minutes. Evidence: Case study of an Army Brigade using the app during the 2020 Nashville bombing event.
