## Summary

Dr. Elizabeth Iorns, founder and CEO of Science Exchange, shares nine foundational lessons for startup success based on her experience transitioning from academic research to building a marketplace for scientific experiments.

## Key Ideas

- Solve problems you personally experience to ensure deep understanding and passion.
- Validate that a problem is real by talking to users and launching a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) early.
- Build a team that shares your vision and values, not just one with technical skills.
- Establish a strong company culture early to aid in team retention and decision-making.
- Choose investors who share your values, as they are partners you cannot fire.
- Maintain momentum through agile frameworks and action-oriented planning.
- Leverage existing tools and services to focus on core business value rather than building everything from scratch.
- Perform 'unscalable' tasks early on to build deep relationships and trust with initial users.
- Persistence is critical; startups only truly fail if they run out of money or give up.
