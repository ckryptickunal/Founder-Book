## Summary

Katie Dill, Head of Design at Stripe, discusses the design philosophy and iterative process behind the company's new homepage. The conversation highlights the transition from a static, text-heavy site to a more dynamic, visual experience that better reflects Stripe's expanded product suite, while emphasizing the role of AI in prototyping and the importance of maintaining high design standards.

## Key Ideas

- A website serves as a company's manifesto, reflecting its values, sophistication, and identity.
- Design should prioritize 'showing' over 'telling' to effectively communicate complex product offerings.
- AI is a powerful tool for rapid prototyping and experimentation, but it does not replace human taste, craft, or attention to detail.
- The 'gravitational pull to mediocrity' is a constant threat that requires intentional effort to fight.
- Design systems are essential for scaling, and AI can help manage these systems to ensure consistency.
- The 'walk the store' practice—where team members regularly use their own products—is critical for identifying friction and maintaining a cohesive user experience.
