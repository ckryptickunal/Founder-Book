## Summary

Garry Tan provides strategic guidance for startup founders who have recently raised capital, emphasizing that fundraising is merely the starting gun. He outlines a framework for financial stewardship, the importance of building a 24-month operating plan, prioritizing retention over growth, and managing through potential business downturns.

## Key Ideas

- Raising capital is not a celebration of success, but the beginning of a high-stakes phase requiring fiduciary responsibility.
- Every dollar spent must be evaluated for its potential return on investment.
- Founders should work backwards from the next funding milestone to create a realistic 24-month operating plan.
- Retention must be solved before aggressive growth; otherwise, the business suffers from a 'leaky bucket' effect.
- If growth stalls, founders should aim to become 'default alive' (profitable), reduce burn, or consider a pivot.
- The 'triple, triple, double, double, double' growth formula is a benchmark for SaaS companies aiming for an IPO.

## Topics

- [[topics/fiduciary-duty|Fiduciary Duty]]: The obligation of founders to treat investor capital as a tool to generate more value rather than personal wealth.
- [[topics/operating-plans|Operating Plans]]: A living financial document tracking revenue, costs, and hiring to ensure the company reaches its next milestone.
- [[topics/retention-vs-growth|Retention vs. Growth]]: The principle that acquiring new customers is ineffective if existing customers do not stay (churn).
- [[topics/default-alive|Default Alive]]: The state where a company generates enough revenue to survive without needing further external funding.

## Notable Claims

- Focusing on growth before retention creates a 'leaky bucket' that prevents a sustainable business. Evidence: If churn is high, new user acquisition is wasted effort as the customer base does not compound.
- SaaS companies should aim for 3x year-over-year growth to reach $100M in revenue in 5 years. Evidence: Historical data from companies like Salesforce, Zendesk, and Marketo.

## Quotes

> Raising money is the starting gun. Don't celebrate the raise, celebrate when you finish a stage.
> A startup is a promise that you'll be good stewards of it and put it into things that will generate more money.
> Focus on retention before growth. Otherwise, you've got a leaky bucket.
> Nobody will bridge you to nowhere.
