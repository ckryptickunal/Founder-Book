# Solving the $160 billion food waste problem: Meet Stefan Kalb of Shelf Engine

## Summary

Stefan Kalb, founder of Shelf Engine, discusses his company's mission to reduce the massive $160 billion annual food waste problem in the U.S. by using AI-driven demand forecasting and a unique business model where they guarantee sales and take on inventory risk for major grocers.

## Key Ideas

- Approximately 30% of perishable food in grocery stores is wasted, often due to manual, inaccurate ordering processes.
- Shelf Engine differentiates itself from traditional SaaS by guaranteeing sales and managing inventory risk, aligning their incentives with the grocers' profitability.
- The company evolved from a traditional SaaS model to a risk-sharing model after realizing that manual data entry and store-level workload were barriers to adoption.
- The business model aligns mission (reducing food waste) with capitalism (increasing grocery store profits), making it a compelling value proposition for enterprise clients.
- Founders should be willing to pivot ('be like water') when initial product-market fit is elusive, as demonstrated by Shelf Engine's transition to a risk-sharing model.
- The company is currently scaling rapidly, hiring aggressively, and working with major retailers like Kroger, Target, and Walmart.

- 30% of all perishable food in the U.S. goes to waste. Evidence: Cited as a general industry statistic for highly perishable categories.
- Grocery stores often under-report their actual food waste numbers. Evidence: Based on Shelf Engine's experience auditing new stores and finding higher-than-reported spoilage rates.
