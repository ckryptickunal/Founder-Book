# The Hacker's Guide to Investors

## Metadata

## Summary

Paul Graham provides a candid, insider perspective on the venture capital and angel investment landscape, explaining how investors think, why they operate the way they do, and how startup founders should navigate these relationships to maintain their company's momentum.

## Key Ideas

- Investors are the limiting factor in creating startup hubs because they are less mobile than founders.
- Angel investors are more critical than VCs for early-stage startups because they fund riskier projects and provide better advice.
- Most VCs are 'momentum investors' who rely on social proof and traffic rather than deep technical understanding.
- Valuations are largely arbitrary and often used as a tool to control deal terms rather than reflect actual company value.
- Raising money is a significant time-sink that can kill a startup's momentum; founders should prioritize product development over funding.
- Investors are often emotional, timid, and prone to herd behavior, which explains why they prefer to co-invest and avoid 'looking bad'.
- Founders should aim to be 'cockroaches'—small, ugly, and indestructible—to avoid being at the mercy of investors.

## Notable Claims

- Investors are the limiting factor in creating a startup hub. Evidence: Investors are wealthy and less willing to relocate compared to founders.
- VCs are momentum investors who don't understand the technology. Evidence: VCs win by noticing trends early rather than predicting them, relying on traffic and social proof.
- Raising money is a major threat to startup momentum. Evidence: The process can take months, during which the focus shifts from product development to fundraising.
