# Investors don’t validate your startup — users do.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `mzBrYp3d0y0`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-04-03T21:52:16Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mzBrYp3d0y0

## Summary

Y Combinator highlights that startup success is driven by user validation rather than universal investor approval, using the story of Envision to illustrate that persistence and personal commitment can overcome repeated rejection.

## Key Ideas

- Investor rejection is a normal part of the startup journey, even for successful companies.
- Founders only need a few believers, not universal consensus, to secure funding.
- Personal commitment, such as forgoing a salary, can demonstrate conviction to potential investors.
- Persistence in fundraising is essential, as evidenced by the 50+ rejections faced by Envision's founder.
