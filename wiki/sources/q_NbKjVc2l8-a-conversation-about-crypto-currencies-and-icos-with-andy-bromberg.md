# A Conversation About Crypto-currencies and ICOs with Andy Bromberg

## Summary

Andy Bromberg, co-founder and president of CoinList, discusses his entrepreneurial journey, the failure of his previous venture SideWire, and the evolving landscape of cryptocurrency, token sales, and blockchain technology.

## Key Ideas

- Entrepreneurship is often about solving problems or finding alternatives to traditional jobs rather than just seeking profit.
- The importance of having aligned, supportive investors who provide guidance during both good and bad times.
- Cryptocurrency and tokens are fundamentally about incentive alignment and creating trustless systems that remove intermediaries.
- Token sales (ICOs) are currently suitable for a very small number of projects where a token is essential to the network's function.
- The crypto industry is in its infancy, and current high levels of capital inflow are likely due to speculative demand rather than mature market dynamics.
- Future trends in tokenization will likely involve asset-backed tokens (security tokens) and a move toward more traditional venture capital norms like vesting and lockups.

## Notable Claims

- Tokens are fundamentally about incentive alignment. Evidence: The Filecoin example shows how tokens incentivize storage providers, verifiers, and users to act honestly without a central authority.
- Most ICOs are not good candidates for tokenization. Evidence: CoinList rejected over 2,495 out of 2,500 inbound requests for token sales.
- The current ICO market is concerningly large. Evidence: In 2018, more money was raised via ICOs than traditional venture capital, despite the industry being only a few years old.
