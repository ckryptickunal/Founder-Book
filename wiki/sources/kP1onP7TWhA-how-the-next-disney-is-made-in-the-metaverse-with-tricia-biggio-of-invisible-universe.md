## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Tricia Biggio, CEO of Invisible Universe, to discuss how the next generation of entertainment giants—akin to Disney or Pixar—will be built through community-driven, decentralized storytelling on the internet, utilizing NFTs and social media to foster fan participation and ownership.

## Key Ideas

- The future of media involves community-driven storytelling where fans contribute to character development and plotlines.
- Invisible Universe is pioneering a model where NFT holders gain collaborative rights to influence animated series.
- Traditional top-down studio models are being challenged by decentralized, internet-native distribution.
- Building in public and listening to community feedback significantly improves content performance.
- The acquisition of Moonbug Entertainment (Cocomelon) for $3 billion demonstrates the massive value of internet-native IP.
- Fans want to move beyond passive consumption to active participation and potential value-sharing in the IP they support.

## Notable Claims

- The next Disney will be built on the internet through community-driven IP. Evidence: The success of Moonbug Entertainment and the rise of decentralized, fan-participatory models like Invisible Universe.
- Listening to the community and responding quickly to feedback improves content performance. Evidence: Data from web2 projects showing that rapid, community-responsive content production yields better results.
- NFTs can serve as a 'ticket to ride' for collaborative storytelling. Evidence: Invisible Universe's model where NFT holders gain access to writing rooms and character development.
