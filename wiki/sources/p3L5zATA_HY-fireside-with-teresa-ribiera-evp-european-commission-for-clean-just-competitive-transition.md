## Summary

Teresa Ribera, the Executive Vice President of the European Commission for Clean, Just & Competitive Transition, discusses her approach to enforcing the Digital Markets Act (DMA). She emphasizes that regulation is essential for fostering innovation, ensuring a level playing field, and protecting consumer rights against dominant incumbents. Ribera highlights the importance of interoperability, transparency, and international cooperation, while expressing a commitment to learning and adjusting regulatory strategies as the digital landscape evolves.

## Key Ideas

- The DMA is a tool to create space for innovation and challenge market incumbents.
- Good regulation acts as a foundation for innovation rather than a barrier.
- Interoperability is a critical requirement for preventing closed digital ecosystems.
- The European Commission is committed to an iterative, learning-based approach to digital enforcement.
- There is a shared interest between the US and Europe in protecting consumers and ensuring fair competition.

## Notable Claims

- The DMA helps small digital companies compete against traditional incumbents. Evidence: Ribera notes that smaller companies are gaining market share through the culture of compliance and transparency fostered by the DMA.
- Regulation is not inherently anti-innovation. Evidence: Ribera argues that chaotic, 'wild west' markets hinder innovation, whereas clear rules provide a stable environment for new players.
