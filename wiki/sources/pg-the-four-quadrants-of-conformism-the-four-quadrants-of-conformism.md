# The Four Quadrants of Conformism

## Summary

Paul Graham proposes a framework for classifying human personality based on two axes: conventional vs. independent-mindedness and passive vs. aggressive behavior. He argues that these four quadrants determine how individuals interact with societal rules and that the aggressively conventional-minded pose a significant threat to free inquiry and innovation.

## Key Ideas

- Human conformism can be mapped on a 2x2 grid: aggressively conventional, passively conventional, passively independent, and aggressively independent.
- Personality type is more predictive of behavior than the specific rules of a society.
- The aggressively conventional-minded are the primary drivers of censorship and moral policing.
- Societal prosperity depends on protecting independent-minded individuals, who are the source of new ideas and innovation.
- Modern institutions, particularly universities and social media platforms, are currently failing to protect free inquiry, leading to a potential exodus of independent thinkers.
- The independent-minded are resilient and will likely create new institutions if existing ones become compromised.

- [[topics/free-inquiry|Free Inquiry]]: The necessity of debating 'bad' ideas to prevent the degradation of intellectual progress and innovation.
- [[topics/institutional-decline|Institutional Decline]]: The observation that universities and other institutions are losing their capacity to protect independent thought.

## Notable Claims

- Aggressively conventional-minded people are responsible for a disproportionate amount of the world's trouble. Evidence: Their tendency to enforce orthodoxy and punish dissenters creates a 'race to the bottom' in moral purity.
- Banning 'bad' ideas is inherently dangerous. Evidence: The process is prone to error, and ideas are interconnected, meaning censorship of one topic inevitably restricts intellectual exploration in others.
- The independent-minded are the 'canaries in the coalmine' for free speech. Evidence: Because they explore the edges of thought, they are the first to notice when the space for inquiry is being restricted.

## Quotes

> The call of the aggressively conventional-minded is 'Crush <outgroup>!'
> Societies prosper only to the extent that they have customs for keeping the conventional-minded at bay.
> Having ideas in a world where some ideas are banned is like playing soccer on a pitch that has a minefield in one corner.
> The independent-minded are good at protecting themselves. If existing institutions are compromised, they'll create new ones.
