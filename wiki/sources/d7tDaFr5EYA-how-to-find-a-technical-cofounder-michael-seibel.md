## Summary

Michael Seibel, CEO of Y Combinator, outlines a practical, step-by-step strategy for non-technical founders to find a technical co-founder, emphasizing the importance of leveraging existing networks and making formal, concrete offers rather than informal requests.

## Key Ideas

- Start by listing and approaching friends and coworkers who are skilled engineers.
- Make formal, concrete offers (equity/salary) rather than asking for 'side help' or weekend work.
- If your immediate network is insufficient, join a small startup to build a network of engineers.
- Avoid large tech companies if you are non-technical, as they often isolate non-engineers from the engineering team.
- Consider learning to code yourself as a viable alternative.
- College is a prime environment for building long-term co-founder relationships.

## Notable Claims

- Asking friends to work on a project on the weekends is an ineffective way to recruit a co-founder. Evidence: Engineers are frequently asked for side help, which does not demonstrate the commitment of a true co-founder.
- Working at a small startup is better for networking than working at a large tech company. Evidence: Small companies foster closer interaction between non-technical and technical staff compared to the silos of large corporations.
