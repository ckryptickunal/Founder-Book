# How to Present to Investors

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-how-to-present-to-investors`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: September 2010
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-how-to-present-to-investors

## Summary

Paul Graham provides a practical guide for early-stage startup founders on how to effectively present their companies to investors, emphasizing clarity, brevity, and authentic communication over polished corporate sales tactics.

## Key Ideas

- Startups should explain what they do and why users want it within the first sentence.
- Avoid 'talking and driving' by having one person speak while another operates the computer.
- Focus on the problem being solved rather than secondary issues like business models or competitors.
- Confidence should be demonstrated through the product's quality rather than explicit claims of passion.
- Use specific, limited data points to anchor the presentation in reality.
- Create a memorable 'soundbite' to help investors categorize and remember the startup.

## Notable Claims

- Business models for very early-stage startups are usually wrong and should not be the focus of a pitch. Evidence: Investors expect the model to change; focusing on the problem being solved is a better use of limited time.
- Haste does not necessarily make waste for startups. Evidence: Many successful startups began with rapid, intense prototype development.
- Investors are better at detecting 'bullshit' than founders are at producing it. Evidence: Founders should be candid about what they haven't figured out yet rather than trying to appear more corporate.

## Quotes

> If you feel you're speaking too slowly, you're speaking at about the right speed.
> Your primary goal is not to describe everything your system might one day become, but simply to convince investors you're worth talking to further.
> If you're solving an important problem, you're going to sound a lot smarter talking about that than the business model.
