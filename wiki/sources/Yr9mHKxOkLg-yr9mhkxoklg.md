## Summary

Senator Scott Wiener, alongside Y Combinator CEO Garry Tan and Economic Security California Action, announced the 'BASE Act' (SB 1074), a California legislative proposal aimed at curbing anti-competitive 'self-preferencing' behaviors by trillion-dollar digital platforms. The bill seeks to protect startups and mid-sized companies from being marginalized by dominant tech giants that manipulate search results, restrict interoperability, and use non-public data to clone competitor products.

## Key Ideas

- Large tech platforms use their control over infrastructure to 'self-preference' their own products, stifling innovation and harming startups.
- The BASE Act targets companies with over $1 trillion in market capitalization and 100 million+ monthly US users.
- Current federal antitrust laws are deemed inadequate for the digital era, necessitating state-level action.
- The bill is modeled after California's Cartwright Act but specifically addresses modern digital practices like search manipulation, data harvesting, and blocking interoperability.
- Founders shared personal experiences of being blocked by Apple, Meta, and Google, highlighting how these barriers prevent market competition.
- The legislation is presented as 'anti-corruption' work rather than an attack on vertical integration or successful business models.
