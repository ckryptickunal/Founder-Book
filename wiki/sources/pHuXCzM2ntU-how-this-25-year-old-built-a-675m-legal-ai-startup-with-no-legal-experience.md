# How This 25-Year-Old Built A $675M Legal AI Startup (With No Legal Experience)

## Summary

Max Junior Strand, CEO and co-founder of Legora, discusses the rapid growth of his legal AI startup, which recently raised $80 million in Series B funding. Legora provides an AI-powered workspace for lawyers, automating tasks like document review, drafting, and research. The company emphasizes a 'human-in-the-loop' approach, deep integration with Microsoft Word, and a strategy of partnering with law firms to navigate the industry's digital transformation.

## Key Ideas

- Legal tech has shifted from fragmented point solutions to integrated AI-powered workspaces.
- The 'aha' moment for legal AI is the ability to perform complex, multi-document due diligence in minutes rather than days.
- Selling to law firms requires a 'we win if you win' partnership model rather than a traditional vendor-client relationship.
- Hiring former founders and high-agency individuals is a key strategy for maintaining high velocity and scaling culture.
- The line between software and service is blurring as AI agents take on more complex, autonomous workflows.
- Building a successful vertical AI company requires avoiding direct competition with AI labs and focusing on deep, domain-specific workflows.

## Notable Claims

- AI can reduce due diligence time from days to minutes. Evidence: Legora's tabular review feature allows lawyers to query hundreds of documents in parallel with automated citations.
- Law firms are now more open to adopting AI than they were two years ago. Evidence: Increased price pressure and the need for efficiency have broken the previous equilibrium of legal services, forcing firms to innovate to remain competitive.
- Hiring former founders is a superior strategy for startup growth. Evidence: Former founders bring high agency, ownership, and an entrepreneurial mindset that helps the company scale faster.
