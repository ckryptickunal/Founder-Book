# Founder burnout happens a lot. A good co-founder can help shoulder the load.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `cwmydEeoMJ4`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-02-02T20:15:59Z

## Summary

A Y Combinator partner discusses the reality of founder burnout, emphasizing that while co-founders can help balance energy levels, persistent burnout usually stems from deeper, underlying issues that must be identified and addressed.

## Key Ideas

- Founder energy levels naturally fluctuate over time.
- A primary benefit of having a co-founder is the ability to share the workload during periods of low energy.
- Persistent burnout is often a symptom of a deeper, specific problem rather than just fatigue.
- Blanket advice is ineffective for burnout; founders must investigate the root cause of their specific situation.

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and advice to startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/founder-burnout|Founder Burnout]]: The psychological and physical exhaustion experienced by startup founders during the long-term process of building a company.
- [[topics/co-founder-dynamics|Co-founder Dynamics]]: The collaborative relationship between founders and how they support each other through the highs and lows of entrepreneurship.

## Notable Claims

- Persistent burnout is almost always caused by a deeper, underlying issue. Evidence: The speaker notes that based on their experience, burnout is rarely just surface-level fatigue and requires identifying the specific root cause.

## Quotes

> That's a big part of the value of having a great co-founder is that if you're low on energy on a given day maybe they can step it up and carry the load that day.
> In my experience there's almost always some deeper issue that's causing these like longer term feelings of burnout.
> Without solving that underlying problem that feeling is not going to go away.
