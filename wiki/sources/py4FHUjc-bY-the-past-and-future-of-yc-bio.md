## Summary

This presentation by YC partners Jared Friedman and Surbhi Sarna outlines the evolution of YC Bio, Y Combinator's healthcare and biotechnology program. Since its inception in 2014, the program has grown to become the world's largest healthcare investor, challenging the misconception that YC focuses solely on software. The speakers discuss the program's history, the shift toward scientist-led startups, the importance of community, and future initiatives to support bio-entrepreneurs.

## Key Ideas

- YC Bio was founded on the belief that scientists, like software engineers, should commercialize their own research.
- The program has invested in over 500 healthcare and bio companies, with a combined valuation of $30 billion.
- Combining biotech founders with software founders in the YC ecosystem is a strategic advantage that fosters speed and innovation.
- YC provides specialized resources for bio companies, including regulatory guidance, clinical study support, and co-founder matching.
- The program is shifting toward a more proactive approach, including a national tour to recruit scientists and increased engagement with industry strategics.

## Notable Claims

- YC is the largest healthcare investor in the world by volume of companies funded. Evidence: YC has invested in over 500 healthcare and bio companies since 2014.
- Scientists are better suited to lead biotech companies than business executives. Evidence: The program's success is attributed to the shift toward scientist-founders commercializing their own research.
- Mixing bio and software founders is a feature, not a bug. Evidence: Bio founders benefit from the speed, agility, and networking of the broader YC tech community.
