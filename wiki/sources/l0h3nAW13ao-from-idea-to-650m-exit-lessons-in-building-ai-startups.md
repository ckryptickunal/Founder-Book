# From Idea to $650M Exit: Lessons in Building AI Startups

## Summary

Jake Heller, founder of CaseText and creator of the AI assistant CoCounsel, shares his journey from building a legal tech startup to a $650 million acquisition by Thomson Reuters. He outlines a framework for building successful AI startups by focusing on solving specific professional tasks, rigorously evaluating AI performance, and prioritizing product quality over traditional sales tactics.

## Key Ideas

- Identify AI opportunities by looking at tasks people are currently paying others to perform.
- Categorize AI applications into three types: assisting professionals, replacing tasks, or enabling previously unthinkable workflows.
- The total addressable market for AI is the combined salaries of those currently performing the task, which is significantly larger than traditional SaaS pricing.
- Reliability is achieved through rigorous, iterative evaluation (evals) rather than just building a flashy demo.
- Product quality is the most effective form of marketing; word-of-mouth and organic growth outperform traditional sales strategies.
- Defensibility in AI comes from the complexity of the implementation, data integrations, and the depth of domain-specific prompt engineering, not just the underlying model.

## Notable Claims

- The total addressable market for AI is 10x to 1000x larger than traditional SaaS because it targets the cost of human labor. Evidence: Comparison between $20/month SaaS seats and $20,000/month professional salaries.
- Most AI startups fail because they build demos that are 60-70% accurate and never invest in rigorous evaluation. Evidence: Observation of industry trends and the 'pilot' trap where projects fail to convert to real revenue.
- Defensibility is built through the grind of implementation, not by hiding the underlying model. Evidence: The complexity of data integrations, prompt tuning, and workflow orchestration makes a product hard to replicate.
