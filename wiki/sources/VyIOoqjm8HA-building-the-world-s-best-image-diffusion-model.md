# Building The World's Best Image Diffusion Model

## Summary

Suhail Doshi, founder and CEO of Playground, discusses the development of their state-of-the-art (SOTA) image diffusion model, emphasizing a product-first approach that prioritizes user experience, prompt adherence, and text generation accuracy over raw model access.

## Key Ideas

- Moving from raw model access to a visual-first, template-based user experience.
- The importance of 'maniacal' attention to detail in model training (e.g., kerning, skin texture, film grain).
- Prioritizing prompt understanding and text generation accuracy as the primary differentiators.
- The 'entanglement' problem where high prompt adherence can sometimes conflict with traditional aesthetic evaluation metrics.
- The necessity of aligning research goals with real-world user utility rather than just academic benchmarks.
- The strategic advantage of identifying and pivoting toward large, underserved commercial markets (e.g., graphic design vs. niche AI toys).

## Notable Claims

- Playground's model achieves SOTA performance in text adherence and spatial reasoning. Evidence: Demonstrated ability to generate accurate text and follow complex spatial instructions in design templates.
- Current aesthetic evaluation benchmarks are flawed. Evidence: Users often prefer less accurate images because they are 'prettier,' creating an entanglement between adherence and aesthetics.
- The biggest market for image generation is graphic design. Evidence: Comparison to Canva's revenue scale versus niche AI art tools.
