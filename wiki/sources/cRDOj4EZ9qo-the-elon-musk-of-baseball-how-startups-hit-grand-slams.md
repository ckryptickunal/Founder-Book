# The Elon Musk of Baseball: How Startups Hit Grand Slams

## Summary

Garry Tan uses the legend of Babe Ruth's 'called shot' as a metaphor for startup success, arguing that founders must set audacious goals, master execution through self-awareness, actively craft their company's narrative, and view their career as an infinite game where successes compound.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should set highly ambitious goals rather than playing it safe, as ambitious goals often face less competition.
- Vision without execution is hallucination; founders must know their strengths and weaknesses to build complementary teams.
- Myth-making and storytelling are essential for capturing imagination and building a company's legacy.
- Startup success is an infinite game where each venture provides the capital and social leverage to tackle larger, more impactful problems.
- Founders should invest in skill acquisition before launching a startup to better evaluate and lead talent.

## Notable Claims

- Ambitious goals face less competition than small, incremental ones. Evidence: Larry Page's observation that fewer people attempt difficult, ambitious projects.
- Myth-making is a necessary component of startup success. Evidence: The evolution of the Babe Ruth 'called shot' story and the growth of PayPal's mission.
- Founders should not start companies until they have honed specific skills. Evidence: The speaker's own experience working at Microsoft and Palantir before founding his own company.
