# Jessica Livingston on Cofounder Disputes and Making Something People Want

## Summary

Jessica Livingston discusses the two primary causes of startup failure: co-founder disputes and the inability to build a product that people actually want. She emphasizes the importance of vetting co-founders and the necessity of iterating on ideas based on user feedback.

## Key Ideas

- Co-founder relationships are critical; choosing the wrong partner is a major cause of startup failure.
- The most common reason startups fail is the inability to make something people want.
- Successful startups often pivot or significantly refine their initial ideas based on user interaction.
- Execution and attention to detail are as important as the initial idea.
- Founders should not ignore red flags regarding co-founder trust, work ethic, or competence.
