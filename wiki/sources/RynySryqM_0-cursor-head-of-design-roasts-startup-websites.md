## Summary

Rio Lu, Head of Design at Cursor, reviews several YC startup websites built using Cursor. The session focuses on identifying common 'vibe-coded' design pitfalls, such as excessive use of purple gradients, distracting animations, and unclear value propositions, while providing actionable advice on improving user experience, messaging, and design consistency.

## Key Ideas

- Avoid 'vibe-coded' design patterns like generic purple gradients, massive shadows, and sloppy typography.
- Prioritize clarity: users should immediately understand what the product is, who it is for, and why it is credible.
- Use clear, literal sub-headlines to explain the product if the main marketing headline is abstract.
- Avoid distracting animations or auto-playing elements that steal user attention without purpose.
- Design for the target audience, not for the AI's default aesthetic preferences.
- When building AI tools, show the user what the AI is thinking or doing to manage expectations during wait times.
- Avoid 'sign-up walls' early in the user journey; let users experience the product first.

## Notable Claims

- AI-generated websites often suffer from 'vibe-coding' which results in disjointed and generic aesthetics. Evidence: Rio points out specific examples of inconsistent shadows, typography, and layout across multiple reviewed sites.
- Users need to understand the product within 5 seconds of landing on the page. Evidence: Rio emphasizes that if a user has to work too hard to figure out what a product does, they will leave.
- Hijacking the scroll is a negative user experience. Evidence: Rio deducts points from Freya's site for overriding standard browser scrolling behavior.
