# How to Find the Right Co-founder

## Summary

Y Combinator partner Hager explains the critical importance of finding a co-founder for a startup, detailing the benefits of productivity, moral support, and pattern matching to success. The video provides a practical guide on how to identify, test, and formalize a co-founder relationship, including advice on equity splits and defining leadership roles.

## Key Ideas

- Co-founders provide essential emotional support and productivity boosts that employees cannot replicate.
- While single founders can succeed, they usually require high conviction and technical ability to build an initial product.
- The best way to find a co-founder is to work on low-stakes projects with friends or colleagues to test compatibility under stress.
- Prioritize trust and shared values over a perfect skill set match; skills can be hired or learned.
- Equity should generally be split 50/50 to ensure long-term motivation and commitment.
- The CEO title should be decided based on who is best suited for external investor relations, not ego.

## Notable Claims

- The most successful startups in history almost always had co-founders. Evidence: Cites Apple, Facebook, Google, and Microsoft as examples.
- The single most important thing to know about a potential co-founder is how they handle stress. Evidence: Startups are inherently taxing, and a co-founder must be able to provide support during emotional lows.
- Default equity splits should be 50/50. Evidence: Ensures both founders are equally motivated for the long-term journey.

## Quotes

> The best people to start companies with are always people that you've got some set of personal experiences where you kind of know their character.
> Don't rule someone out who's potentially a great co-founder because they don't sort of have this checklist of specific skills you're looking for.
