## Summary

One Kronos is a modern stock exchange that utilizes combinatorial auction theory to allow institutional traders to execute complex, multi-asset 'atomic' trades. Founded by Kelly Little Page and Steven Johnson, the company spent six years in development to overcome significant technical, regulatory, and liquidity challenges, eventually capturing 0.3% of daily US equity volume.

## Key Ideas

- Combinatorial auctions allow traders to bundle multiple assets into a single atomic trade, solving the 'exposure problem' where traders cannot hedge positions simultaneously.
- Current market structures often rely on outdated 'price-time priority' matching, which creates latency race conditions and inefficiencies.
- Building a new exchange is a 'hard tech' problem requiring deep systems engineering, regulatory navigation, and solving the 'cold start' liquidity problem.
- Small, high-performing teams can achieve massive scale by focusing on first-principles engineering and efficient infrastructure.
- The same combinatorial auction principles used in finance could potentially optimize other fragmented markets, such as GPU compute capacity.

## Topics
