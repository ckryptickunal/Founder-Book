## Summary

Harge Tagger from Y Combinator explains the critical importance of having a co-founder for startup success, offering practical advice on how to find, evaluate, and maintain a healthy co-founding relationship.

## Key Ideas

- Co-founders are essential for handling the extreme workload and emotional volatility of building a startup.
- The most successful startups are almost always built by teams, even if one founder becomes the public face.
- The primary cause of startup failure is the breakdown of the co-founder relationship.
- Ideal co-founders should be vetted for their ability to handle stress, alignment on long-term goals, and complementary skill sets.
- Equity should generally be split equally to ensure both founders are fully invested.
- Regular, scheduled check-ins are necessary to address friction before it becomes irreparable.

## Notable Claims

- Failed co-founder relationships are the number one reason startups fail. Evidence: Observed data from Y Combinator's history of funding hundreds of startups.
- You should not overly fixate on a co-founder's current specific technical skills. Evidence: The 'meta-skill' of being smart and willing to learn is more important for long-term startup growth.
- You should split equity equally between co-founders. Evidence: Equal splits ensure both parties feel equally invested over the 10+ year lifespan of a successful company.
