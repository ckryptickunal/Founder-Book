# It only really matters if you can find a handful of users that use your product habitually.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `viRkOHNG4Mo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-06-17T20:19:11Z

## Summary

A brief guide for early-stage startup founders emphasizing that habitual user retention is the only metric that truly matters for initial product validation.

## Key Ideas

- Vanity metrics like signups and waitlist numbers are secondary to actual product usage.
- The primary goal for a new startup is to find a small group of users who integrate the product into their daily workflow.
- Without habitual usage, other growth metrics are irrelevant.

## Notable Claims

- The number of users who use a product habitually is the most important metric for a new startup. Evidence: The speaker argues that without habitual usage, all other metrics like signups or waitlist numbers are meaningless.

## Quotes

> It only really matters if you can find a handful of users who will use your product and then build it into their daily workflow.
