# How Not to Die

## Summary

Paul Graham discusses the primary causes of startup failure, arguing that most startups die not from lack of potential, but from founder demoralization and distraction. He emphasizes that persistence, maintaining accountability through communication, and avoiding 'side projects' are the critical factors in surviving long enough to achieve success.

## Key Ideas

- Startup success is often a matter of simply not dying.
- Communication with investors and peers acts as a forcing function for progress.
- Demoralization is the root cause of most startup deaths, rather than external factors like running out of money.
- Startups rarely succeed on the first attempt; iteration based on a core group of ecstatic users is essential.
- Distraction, such as graduate school or consulting, is a primary predictor of failure.
- Public commitment and the fear of humiliation are powerful motivators that prevent founders from quitting.

## Notable Claims

- Regular communication with investors is a 100% accurate predictor of startup survival. Evidence: Observation of Y Combinator startups over five cycles.
- Distraction is fatal to startups. Evidence: Founders who pursue graduate school or side projects are significantly more likely to fail.
- Startups are more motivated by the fear of public failure than the hope of wealth. Evidence: The case of Octopart founders who became 'grimly determined' after public exposure.
