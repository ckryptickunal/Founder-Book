## Summary

Avni Patel Thompson, founder of Poppy, discusses her journey from a corporate career to building a childcare startup. She outlines a two-phase framework for founders: 'Get Going' (validating the idea through low-cost, manual testing) and 'Get Growing' (scaling, hiring, and achieving product-market fit).

## Key Ideas

- Validate ideas by solving the immediate problem in front of you using off-the-shelf tools before building custom technology.
- Growth is the best validator; it helps secure co-founders, investors, and confidence.
- A technical co-founder is essential for a tech company to maintain long-term vision and ambition.
- Hiring should prioritize mission alignment and resourcefulness over pedigree or traditional experience.
- Transparency with early users is critical when making difficult business model pivots.
- Founders must prioritize self-care to avoid burnout, as the startup journey is a long-term commitment.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-validation|Startup Validation]]: Using low-cost, time-bound experiments to prove market demand before investing in full-scale development.
- [[topics/founder-dynamics|Founder Dynamics]]: The importance of finding a technical co-founder and managing the emotional highs and lows of entrepreneurship.
- [[topics/scaling-operations|Scaling Operations]]: Transitioning from a survival-mode 'firefighting' phase to a sustainable, growth-oriented business model.

## Notable Claims

- If you can show growth, you can overcome skepticism from investors and potential co-founders. Evidence: Avni notes that her growth metrics were the primary factor in securing her CTO and successful YC application.
- Early users feel ownership of the product and should be treated with transparency during business pivots. Evidence: The backlash Poppy faced when changing their pricing model taught them that users need to be brought into the decision-making process.
