# Don't Just Check Off Boxes

## Metadata

## Summary

A brief piece of advice from a Y Combinator session emphasizing the importance of intrinsic motivation and genuine collaboration over the academic tendency to prioritize resume-building and box-checking.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize projects based on genuine personal interest rather than external validation.
- Choose collaborators based on mutual respect and enjoyment of their company.
- Avoid the 'box-checking' mentality common in academic environments.
- Commit to long-term building rather than short-term task completion.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/career-development|Career Development]]: Focusing on meaningful work and long-term growth over superficial achievements.
- [[topics/collaborative-dynamics|Collaborative Dynamics]]: The importance of working with people you respect and enjoy.

## Notable Claims

- Academic environments often encourage students to prioritize checking boxes over building things. Evidence: The speaker notes that students are frequently pulled toward task-oriented behaviors rather than sustained, interest-driven projects.

## Quotes

> I think just working on things that you're interested in, doing it with people both that you enjoy being around, but that you respect a ton, and taking that really seriously.
> There's so many things that pulls you toward more checking boxes and less focusing on building something up over time.
