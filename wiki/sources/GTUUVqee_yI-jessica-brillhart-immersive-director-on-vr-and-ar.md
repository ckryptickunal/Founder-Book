## Summary

Jessica Brillhart, a former Google principal filmmaker for VR, discusses her transition to founding her own company, Vrai Pictures. She explores the convergence of VR, AR, and machine learning, emphasizing the importance of 'story crafting' over traditional 'storytelling' in immersive media. She advocates for using technology to create meaningful, functional experiences rather than just entertainment, while acknowledging the ethical responsibilities of creators in immersive spaces.

## Key Ideas

- The shift from 'storytelling' (active direction) to 'story crafting' (creating worlds for users to decode).
- The convergence of VR, AR, and machine learning into a unified immersive environment.
- The importance of limitations in creative work to foster innovation.
- The ethical responsibility of creators when building immersive experiences that feel like reality.
- The potential for immersive media to serve functional purposes beyond entertainment, such as in education or architecture.
- The significance of audio fidelity in creating presence within VR.

## Notable Claims

- Traditional storytelling does not work in VR because the medium allows users to be present in the space. Evidence: Brillhart argues that 'telling' is a means to an end, and in VR, the user can decode the story themselves through world-building.
- Audio is more important than visual fidelity for creating a sense of presence in VR. Evidence: She notes that audio can be 'good enough' even with low-end hardware, whereas visual fidelity is constantly changing and harder to maintain.
- The distinction between VR and AR will eventually disappear. Evidence: She suggests that technologies are converging and will eventually be referred to as a single, unified immersive environment.
