## Summary

Gustaf Alstromer, a partner at Y Combinator and former growth team member at Airbnb, explains the principles of user growth for startups. He emphasizes that growth should only be pursued after achieving product-market fit, outlines the distinction between product growth, performance marketing, and brand marketing, and advocates for data-driven decision-making through A/B testing.

## Key Ideas

- Growth is only beneficial after a startup has achieved product-market fit; applying growth tactics prematurely can be detrimental.
- Product-market fit can be measured by identifying a core value metric and tracking its repeat usage frequency over time.
- Growth teams should consist of a cross-functional group including engineers, data scientists, designers, and product managers.
- A/B testing is the most reliable way to make product decisions, as gut feelings are often wrong.
- Startups should focus on product growth (funnel optimization) and performance marketing before investing in brand marketing.
- Growth channels like SEO, referrals, and paid acquisition should be approached strategically based on user behavior and product type.

## Notable Claims

- Applying growth tactics before finding product-market fit is dangerous and often leads to a temporary spike followed by a crash. Evidence: Common growth graphs show a sharp decline when the 'fuel' of growth tactics is not supported by actual product value.
- A/B testing is essential because intuition is often wrong even among experienced teams. Evidence: Multiple examples from Airbnb showed that teams were split on which features would perform better, and data often contradicted the majority opinion.
- Paid acquisition should only be pursued if the company has revenue and a clear understanding of LTV (Lifetime Value) vs. CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost). Evidence: Buying ads without revenue or a path to profitability is a common mistake that leads to unsustainable burn.
