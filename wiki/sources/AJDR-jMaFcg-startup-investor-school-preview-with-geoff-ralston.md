## Summary

Geoff Ralston, a partner at Y Combinator, introduces 'Investor School,' a four-day course designed to teach the fundamentals of angel investing. The program aims to help new investors avoid common pitfalls, understand the mechanics of startup deals, and foster a more thoughtful, effective, and diverse community of angel investors.

## Key Ideas

- Angel investing is a high-risk endeavor that requires being prepared for total loss of capital.
- Successful angel investing is built on conviction, deal flow, and maintaining strong relationships with founders.
- Investors should avoid 'spray-and-pray' tactics and instead focus on being helpful, thoughtful partners to founders.
- The landscape of early-stage investing is evolving rapidly with new models like equity crowdfunding, ICOs, and online platforms like AngelList.
- Investors should prioritize investing in areas where they have some familiarity or a strong belief in the technology's future impact.
- Being a 'good' investor involves being honest, providing clear feedback, and not being a burden to founders during difficult times.

## Topics

- [[topics/angel-investing|Angel Investing]]: The practice of providing capital to startups in exchange for equity, often in the early stages.
- [[topics/startup-due-diligence|Startup Due Diligence]]: The process of evaluating a company's potential, founder passion, and market opportunity before investing.
- [[topics/investment-mechanics|Investment Mechanics]]: Understanding financial instruments like convertible notes, cap tables, and dilution.
- [[topics/investor-psychology|Investor Psychology]]: Managing emotions, maintaining conviction, and avoiding common behavioral biases in high-risk investing.
- [[topics/evolution-of-venture-capital|Evolution of Venture Capital]]: The shift from traditional VC models to new methods like accelerators, crowdfunding, and crypto-based fundraising.

## Notable Claims

- Angel investing is a high-risk paradigm where investors should be prepared for the total loss of their investment. Evidence: Ralston notes that the default position for an investment should be that it is worth zero until proven otherwise.
- Being a 'good' investor is essential for long-term success and deal flow. Evidence: Founders refer good investors to other founders, creating a network effect for high-quality deal flow.
- The landscape of startup investing is changing rapidly due to new technologies and platforms. Evidence: The rise of ICOs and online platforms like AngelList has fundamentally altered how capital is raised compared to the 20th century.
