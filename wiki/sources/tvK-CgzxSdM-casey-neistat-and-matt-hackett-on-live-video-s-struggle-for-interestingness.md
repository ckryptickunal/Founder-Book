# Casey Neistat and Matt Hackett on Live Video's Struggle for Interestingness

## Summary

Casey Neistat and Matt Hackett discuss the challenges of building live video products, the importance of solving for 'interestingness,' and their experiences navigating fundraising and acquisition as a startup team.

## Key Ideas

- The 'struggle for interestingness' is the primary hurdle for live video platforms; raw, unedited life is often boring.
- Successful products (like Snapchat lenses or video games) provide a framework or 'box' that makes content inherently interesting.
- Founders should be transparent with their team only when information is actionable; otherwise, it creates unnecessary paralysis.
- Fundraising and acquisition are sales processes that require empathy for the other party, persistence, and a 'jockey over the horse' mentality.
- Brute force and relentless execution are often more critical to success than intellectualizing or over-analyzing.
- Respecting others' time by being direct and specific in communication is essential for professional success.

## Topics

## Notable Claims

- Live video platforms struggle because they are too wide open and lack a 'box' to make content interesting. Evidence: Comparison between the failure of raw live-streaming and the success of video games or Snapchat lenses.
- Investors bet on the jockey, not the horse. Evidence: Anecdote about Gary Vaynerchuk investing in Beme despite not understanding the product.
- Anything other than a signed term sheet is effectively a 'no' from an investor. Evidence: Advice based on the founders' experience navigating various forms of investor rejection.
