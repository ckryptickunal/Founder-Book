## Summary

Selina Tobaccowala, former President and CTO of SurveyMonkey and co-founder of Evite, shares insights on scaling companies, building engineering cultures, and the importance of aligning with co-founders on values and financial outcomes early in the startup journey.

## Key Ideas

- Align with co-founders on long-term goals, exit strategies, and company values before starting.
- Design for global and international markets (language, currency, mobile ecosystems) from the beginning.
- Empower engineers by connecting their work to business metrics and clear mission impact.
- Build a culture of A/B testing and data-driven decision-making into the product and organization.
- Prioritize hiring people who believe in the mission and promote from within to maintain culture during growth.
- Maintain a 'family-friendly' culture through trust, clear goal-setting, and leading by example regarding work-life balance.
- Avoid over-raising capital; focus on positive gross margins and sustainable business fundamentals.

## Topics

- [[topics/scaling|Scaling]]: Strategies for growing a company from a small team to hundreds of employees while maintaining culture and efficiency.
- [[topics/engineering-leadership|Engineering Leadership]]: Managing technical teams by providing context, business metrics, and clear goals.
- [[topics/board-governance|Board Governance]]: The importance of assembling diverse, independent boards early to provide mentorship and oversight.
- [[topics/work-life-balance|Work-Life Balance]]: Creating flexible, trust-based work environments that accommodate family needs without sacrificing performance.

## Notable Claims

- Engineers are the most innovative people in an organization. Evidence: Tobaccowala's experience leading technical teams at SurveyMonkey.
- Over-raising capital can be detrimental to a startup. Evidence: Evite's experience raising $37 million for online invitations, which led to inefficient spending.
- A/B testing is crucial for product growth. Evidence: The viral growth of Evite was driven by constant A/B testing of user registration flows.
