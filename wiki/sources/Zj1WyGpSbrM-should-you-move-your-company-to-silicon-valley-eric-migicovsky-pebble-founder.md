# Should You Move Your Company to Silicon Valley? - Eric Migicovsky, Pebble Founder

## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, founder of Pebble, discusses the strategic decision of whether to relocate a startup to Silicon Valley. He highlights the benefits of proximity to customers, the high-density professional network, and the competitive 'forcing function' of the environment, while balancing these against the high costs of living and hiring.

## Key Ideas

- Proximity to customers is the primary driver for relocation.
- Silicon Valley offers a unique, high-density network that provides mentorship and serendipitous connections.
- The competitive environment acts as a forcing function, pushing founders to iterate faster and perform better.
- High costs of living and talent acquisition are significant barriers for early-stage startups.
- Founders should stay in their current location if they have a specific unfair advantage, such as local research ties or a unique talent pool.
- Short-term immersion (like the Y Combinator program) can provide network benefits without requiring a permanent move.

## Notable Claims

- Being close to your customers allows for faster iteration and better product-market fit. Evidence: Direct feedback loops enable quicker solutions and validation.
- Silicon Valley acts as a forcing function that increases startup velocity. Evidence: The author noted that the first three years of his startup could have been achieved in six months in the Valley due to the competitive pressure.
- High costs in Silicon Valley can be mitigated by working at a big tech company first. Evidence: Working at established tech firms provides higher income and builds a professional network before starting a venture.
