## Summary

Sarah Nahm, CEO of Lever, discusses her journey from a design-focused background to founding a recruiting software company, emphasizing the importance of proactive hiring, inclusive culture, and treating 'hiring as a product'.

## Key Ideas

- Founding is a non-linear process that requires building relationships over time rather than waiting for lightning-strike moments.
- Hiring is the most critical task for a founder; it should be treated with the same strategic rigor as product development.
- Proactive recruiting involves outbound sourcing and nurturing long-term relationships with potential candidates.
- Impact descriptions (outcomes-based) are more effective and inclusive than traditional, skill-heavy job descriptions.
- Diversity and inclusion should be integrated into the company culture from the earliest stages, rather than treated as a later-stage add-on.
- Culture is defined by the 'surface area' of decisions, such as office chores, holiday schedules, and how teams communicate about differences.

- [[topics/inclusive-culture|Inclusive Culture]]: Building environments that welcome diverse backgrounds by focusing on shared values and equitable policies.
- [[topics/founder-development|Founder Development]]: The transition from individual contributor to CEO and the necessity of learning to manage through others.

## Notable Claims

- Hiring is the most important job for a startup founder. Evidence: Founders who do not prioritize hiring hold their companies back regardless of their other skills.
- Impact descriptions are more effective than traditional job descriptions. Evidence: They attract higher-quality talent and are more inclusive to non-traditional candidates.
- Diversity and inclusion should be addressed before a company scales. Evidence: It is harder to retrofit inclusive culture into a large organization than to build it from the start.
