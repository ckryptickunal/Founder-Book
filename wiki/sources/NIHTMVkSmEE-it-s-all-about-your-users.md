# It’s all about your users.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `NIHTMVkSmEE`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-01-26T18:20:21Z

## Summary

A brief lecture from Y Combinator emphasizing the critical importance of prioritizing user feedback over internal 'vision' and the tendency of founders to avoid the discomfort of early sales and product iteration.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often prioritize their own internal vision over actual user needs.
- Building in isolation is a common trap to avoid the discomfort of sales and potential rejection.
- The most effective path is identifying a specific problem someone is willing to pay to fix.
- Founders often delay shipping due to embarrassment or fear of negative feedback.
- Direct contact with the real world and users is essential for product success.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/customer-development|Customer Development]]: The process of validating a product idea by engaging directly with potential users rather than building in isolation.
- [[topics/startup-psychology|Startup Psychology]]: The common emotional barriers founders face, such as fear of rejection and perfectionism.

## Notable Claims

- Founders often fail because they do not pay enough attention to their users. Evidence: The speaker notes that founders frequently spend time building elaborate products in isolation rather than engaging with the market.
- The best product ideas often stem from problems the founder personally experiences. Evidence: The speaker identifies personal experience with a problem as the 'best case' scenario for identifying a viable business solution.

## Quotes

> You will make up some idea in your own head that you will call your vision and then you will spend a lot of time thinking about your vision in a cafe by yourself.
> You will not ship fast enough because you don't want to face the likely feedback.
> You'd be way better off finding someone anyone who has a problem that they will pay you to fix and fixing it.
