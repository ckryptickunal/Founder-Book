# Stop selling your time!

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the transition from trading time for money to building scalable products, emphasizing the importance of leverage to achieve wealth.

## Key Ideas

- Trading time for money is a finite game with a hard ceiling on potential earnings.
- Wealth creation requires moving away from hourly labor toward leverage.
- Building products that generate income while sleeping is the key to escaping the time-for-money trap.
- There is a physical limit to how many hours one can work in a week.

## Topics

- [[topics/leverage|Leverage]]: The use of tools, code, or capital to multiply output without a proportional increase in time input.
- [[topics/wealth-creation|Wealth Creation]]: The shift from linear income (hourly wages) to exponential income (scalable products).

## Notable Claims

- Selling your time is a finite game that limits wealth. Evidence: There are a finite number of hours in a day and a human life, creating a hard cap on productivity.
- Products can generate money while you sleep. Evidence: Digital products and automated systems function independently of the creator's active labor hours.

## Quotes

> If you make a product that can make money for you while you sleep that's Leverage.
