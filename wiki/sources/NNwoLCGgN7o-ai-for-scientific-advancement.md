## Summary

Y Combinator highlights the stagnation in traditional scientific software tools and calls for startups to leverage AI and test-time compute to revolutionize fields like chemistry, biology, and material science.

## Key Ideas

- Scientific software tools in chemistry, biology, and material science have remained largely unchanged for decades.
- Traditional scientific problem-solving relies heavily on manual methods performed by PhDs.
- Test-time compute is a key technological enabler for new scientific startups.
- There is a significant opportunity for AI to accelerate the physical manufacturing and discovery processes.
