## Summary

Brandon, co-founder of Vori, discusses the company's journey from a Y Combinator startup to a $22 million Series B company. Vori provides an all-in-one operating system for independent supermarkets, replacing antiquated paper-based processes with digital inventory management, point-of-sale systems, and AI-driven automation to improve profitability and operational efficiency.

## Key Ideas

- Grocery retail is a massive, largely undigitized industry that relies on outdated paper, pencil, and fax-based processes.
- Vori started with a 'wedge' product—a simple mobile app for reordering inventory—before expanding into a full-stack operating system.
- The company focuses on three core P&L levers for grocers: increasing net sales, improving gross margins, and optimizing labor.
- AI agents are used to automate inventory replenishment, dynamic pricing, and personalized marketing, effectively creating a 'self-driving' grocery store.
- Success in the grocery industry requires deep relationship-building, persistence, and 'superhero' levels of customer service to earn trust.
- Vori aims to become the clearinghouse for trade in the global food supply chain by digitizing the entire transaction layer.
