## Summary

A panel of Y Combinator partners answers common questions from aspiring founders at Startup School SV 2016, covering topics such as when to start a company, how to find ideas, the challenges of being a solo founder, and the realities of the YC application process.

## Key Ideas

- Start your startup now rather than waiting to become a domain expert; you can gain expertise while building.
- Focus on solving problems you are deeply passionate about rather than trying to impress others with buzzwords.
- Being a solo founder is exceptionally difficult; co-founders provide essential emotional and operational support.
- Avoid 'cargo culting' startup culture; focus on building your own path rather than following arbitrary rules or mimicking others.
- The YC application process is open, free, and does not require warm intros; it is a useful framework for refining your business even if you aren't accepted.
- Prioritize product-market fit and retention before scaling or focusing on PR.
- Don't be overly secretive about your idea; feedback is more valuable than protecting a 'secret' that likely has no copycats.
