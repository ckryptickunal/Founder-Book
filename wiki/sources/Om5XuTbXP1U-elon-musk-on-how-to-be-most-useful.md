# Elon Musk On How To Be Most Useful

## Summary

Elon Musk discusses his framework for determining how to be most useful, emphasizing the 'area under the curve' concept where the value of an endeavor is determined by the magnitude of the impact multiplied by the number of people affected.

## Key Ideas

- Utility is defined as the product of the 'utility delta' (improvement over the status quo) and the number of people impacted.
- High-impact, low-reach projects are equivalent in value to low-impact, high-reach projects.
- Starting a company is not a rational choice if the goal is a risk-adjusted financial return.
- Formal education like a PhD is not a prerequisite for being useful.

## Notable Claims

- Starting a company is an irrational decision if the goal is the best risk-adjusted financial return. Evidence: Musk notes that the high failure rate and difficulty make it a poor choice for those seeking safety or guaranteed returns.

## Quotes

> Whatever the thing is that you're trying to create, what would be the utility delta compared to the current state of the art times how many people it would affect.
> If the objective was to achieve the best risk-adjusted return, starting a company is insane.
