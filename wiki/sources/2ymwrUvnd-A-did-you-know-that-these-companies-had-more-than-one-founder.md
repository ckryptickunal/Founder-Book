# Did you know that these companies had more than one founder?

## Metadata

## Summary

This video highlights that many iconic technology companies, often associated with a single famous founder, were actually established by multiple co-founders.

## Key Ideas

- Successful startups frequently rely on the collaborative efforts of multiple founders.
- Public perception often focuses on a single 'face' of a company, overshadowing other essential co-founders.
- Early-stage co-founders may leave shortly after inception, yet still play a role in the company's origin.

## Notable Claims

- Most successful companies are started by more than one founder. Evidence: The video provides examples of Microsoft, Apple, and Facebook having multiple co-founders.
- Apple had three co-founders, not just two. Evidence: The video identifies Ronald Wayne as the third co-founder who left early.
- Facebook had four co-founders in addition to Mark Zuckerberg. Evidence: The video references the history depicted in 'The Social Network' to support this claim.
