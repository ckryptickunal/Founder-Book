## Summary

Tom Brown, co-founder of Anthropic, discusses his career trajectory from early YC startups to OpenAI and the founding of Anthropic. He highlights the importance of scaling laws, the shift toward developer-focused AI tools like Claude Code, and the massive infrastructure challenges facing the industry, particularly regarding power and compute capacity.

## Key Ideas

- The 'wolf' mindset—taking ownership and operating without explicit instructions—is superior to the 'dog' mindset of waiting for tasks in big tech.
- Scaling laws (the relationship between compute, data, and model performance) are the primary driver of AI progress.
- Anthropic's success in coding tasks is attributed to prioritizing internal developer experience and 'dogfooding' rather than optimizing for public benchmarks.
- The AI industry is undergoing the largest infrastructure buildout in history, with power availability being the primary bottleneck.
- Treating the AI model itself as a user (empowering it with tools and context) is a key strategy for building effective agentic products.
- Anthropic maintains a multi-vendor hardware strategy (GPUs, TPUs, Trainium) to maximize capacity and match specific chips to specific workloads.

## Topics

- [[topics/scaling-laws|Scaling Laws]]: The empirical observation that increasing compute and data leads to predictable, exponential gains in model intelligence.
- [[topics/ai-infrastructure|AI Infrastructure]]: The massive global effort to build data centers, secure power, and manufacture specialized hardware for AGI.
- [[topics/agentic-ai|Agentic AI]]: The shift from chat-based models to autonomous agents capable of using tools and executing complex workflows like coding.
- [[topics/interpretability|Interpretability]]: The long-term research effort to understand the internal mechanisms of neural networks to ensure safety.

## Notable Claims

- Humanity is on track for the largest infrastructure buildout of all time, exceeding the Apollo and Manhattan projects. Evidence: Projected 3x per year increase in spending on AGI compute.
- Public benchmarks are often gamed by AI labs. Evidence: Anthropic avoids dedicated teams for benchmark optimization to prevent perverse incentives.
- Power is the biggest bottleneck for AI infrastructure in the US. Evidence: Difficulty in permitting and building data centers, with some companies resorting to extreme measures like using jet engines for power.
