# Critiquing AI Startup Websites with YC President Garry Tan

## Summary

Garry Tan, President of Y Combinator, joins a design review session to critique the websites of several AI startups. The discussion focuses on the importance of clear value propositions, avoiding 'clever' but confusing taglines, the necessity of demonstrating a 'feat of strength' (product demo), and the strategic use of social proof and visual hierarchy to convert visitors.

## Key Ideas

- AI startups must clearly explain their 'why now' and define the new category they are creating.
- Avoid being 'clever' at the expense of being clear; users need to immediately understand what the product is and if it is for them.
- Prioritize a single, clear call-to-action (CTA) rather than overwhelming users with multiple competing options.
- Use 'feats of strength'—compelling, interactive demos—to showcase the unique capabilities of AI products.
- Avoid 'friction' like forced sign-ups before allowing users to experience the product's value.
- Design should be opinionated, using contrast and spacing to guide the user's eye toward the most important information.
- Social proof, such as logos of known companies and testimonials from real users, significantly increases trust.

## Notable Claims

- Being too general with a website's messaging is problematic; you must write for a specific ideal customer profile (ICP). Evidence: The critique of Reality Defender, which failed to identify its specific user base, leading to confusion.
- Forcing users to sign up before they can interact with the product causes high drop-off rates. Evidence: The critique of Magic Flow, where the 'try it' button led to a login wall, causing frustration.
- Animation and motion should be used sparingly to guide user attention, not to overwhelm. Evidence: The comparison between the overwhelming visuals of Pump and the balanced, chill design of Voiceflow.
