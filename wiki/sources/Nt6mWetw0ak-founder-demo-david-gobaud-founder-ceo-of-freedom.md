## Summary

David Gobaud, founder of Freedom, advocates for open-source AI as a critical safeguard for individual autonomy, democratic values, and American technological leadership. He argues that decentralized, locally-run AI prevents the risks of 'black box' closed-source systems controlled by big tech or authoritarian regimes.

## Key Ideas

- Open-source AI democratizes access, allowing startups and researchers to innovate without needing billions in training capital.
- Closed-source AI poses risks regarding censorship, bias, and lack of transparency, effectively acting as a 'black box'.
- Local, decentralized AI prevents dependency on centralized cloud providers, protecting individual privacy and autonomy.
- American leadership in AI depends on fostering open-source ecosystems to compete with foreign models like DeepSeek.
- Freedom (the company) focuses on building local-first, end-to-end encrypted AI solutions.

## Topics

- [[topics/open-source-ai|Open-Source AI]]: AI models with accessible weights and architecture that allow for local execution, modification, and community-driven innovation.
- [[topics/ai-sovereignty|AI Sovereignty]]: The concept of maintaining control over AI systems to ensure they align with democratic values and prevent monopolistic control by big tech.
- [[topics/local-ai-execution|Local AI Execution]]: Running AI models on personal hardware to ensure privacy, reduce costs, and eliminate reliance on cloud-based 'black box' services.

## Notable Claims

- Open-source AI prevents AI monopoly pricing. Evidence: Startups like Andy Search would not exist without the lower costs associated with open-source models compared to big tech providers.
- Closed-source AI lacks accountability and transparency. Evidence: Examples include Google's AI generating historically inaccurate images and DeepSeek's inability to discuss sensitive political topics like Tiananmen Square.

> Supporting open-source AI really supports freedom and it's democratic tech leadership that America has done before very successfully.
> You don't want everyone to end up plugged into some central closed source AI.
