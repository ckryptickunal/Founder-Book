## Summary

Sherwood, a two-time YC founder, discusses his new company, Sasabi, an AI-native observability platform designed for fast-moving engineering teams. He explains Sasabi's core principle, "logs are all you need," which leverages AI to process unstructured log data, challenging the traditional three pillars of observability (logs, metrics, traces). Sherwood contrasts Sasabi with his first YC company, Opkit (a healthcare AI venture), emphasizing the critical importance of founder-market fit and building a company aligned with personal experience and passion. He shares insights from his career, including building observability at Brex, and highlights the value of YC for accelerating growth, establishing a strong company culture, and providing unique go-to-market opportunities. The discussion also covers lessons learned about the long-term nature of startups and the ideal qualities for engineers joining Sasabi.

## Key Ideas

- Sasabi is an AI-native observability platform that simplifies finding and fixing production issues by allowing natural language queries.
- Sasabi's controversial 'logs are all you need' principle for observability, enabled by AI's ability to process unstructured log data, challenges the traditional 'three pillars' (logs, metrics, traces) approach.
- The importance of founder-market fit and building a company aligned with personal experience, passion, and strengths, contrasting Sasabi with his previous venture, Opkit.
- Observability is crucial for responding to unpredictable production issues, as 'nothing really prepares you for production.'
- The maintenance of existing software represents a larger and less automated opportunity than the creation of new software, which Sasabi aims to address.
- Y Combinator provides acceleration, establishes a fast-shipping culture, and offers unique go-to-market opportunities (selling to batchmates).
- Startups are a long-term game where relationships and lessons compound over time.
