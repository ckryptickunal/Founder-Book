# How to Design Hardware Products with Hosain Rahman (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 17)

## Summary

Hosain Rahman, CEO of Jawbone, discusses the company's philosophy and process for building hardware products at the intersection of engineering, beauty, and data. He emphasizes the 'system' approach—integrating hardware, software, and data—to create wearable devices that act as context engines for the user's life.

## Key Ideas

- Products should be viewed as systems (hardware, software, and data) rather than discrete objects.
- Wearables serve as 'context engines' that understand the user's state to improve interactions with the surrounding environment.
- The 'Why' framework: Defining the specific user problem to solve before building.
- Balancing the rapid iteration of software with the deliberate, long-cycle nature of hardware development.
- The importance of 'hero experiences'—magical details that create emotional connections with users.
- Cross-functional pods are essential for managing trade-offs across different engineering disciplines.

## Topics

- [[topics/internet-of-things|Internet of Things]]: The ecosystem of smart, connected devices and the need for an organizing principle to make them user-friendly.
- [[topics/product-development-process|Product Development Process]]: A multi-stage framework involving exploration, validation, concept, planning, and development.
- [[topics/wearable-computing|Wearable Computing]]: The role of body-worn sensors in tracking health and providing context for other smart devices.
- [[topics/system-thinking|System Thinking]]: The methodology of managing trade-offs across hardware, software, and data teams to ensure a cohesive user experience.

## Notable Claims

- Wearables will become the central hub for the Internet of Things. Evidence: Because they are worn 24/7, they provide unique context (heart rate, activity, stress) that can inform other smart devices like thermostats or cars.
- The wireless speaker market was created by the Jambox. Evidence: In 2010, the market for wireless speakers was 0%; by 2013, it had grown to 78% of the total speaker market.
- Hardware companies can successfully adopt software-like iteration speeds. Evidence: By forcing cross-functional communication and using 'fast-track' programs, Jawbone manages to iterate on hardware despite long tooling cycles.
