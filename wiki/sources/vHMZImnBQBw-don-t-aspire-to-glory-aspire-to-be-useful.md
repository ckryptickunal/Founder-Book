# Don't aspire to glory. Aspire to be useful.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `vHMZImnBQBw`
- Channel: Garry Tan
- Published: 2025-06-22T00:17:43Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vHMZImnBQBw

## Summary

Garry Tan and Elon Musk discuss the core philosophy of career success, emphasizing that individuals should prioritize being useful to others over seeking personal glory or status.

## Key Ideas

- Success is defined by the total utility provided to the maximum number of people.
- True work is defined by the tangible impact a product or service has on society.
- Ego management is a critical requirement for anyone working in a startup environment.
- Aspiring to be useful increases the probability of professional success.

## Entities

- [[entities/elon-musk|Elon Musk]] (person): CEO of Tesla and SpaceX
- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): CEO of Y Combinator

## Topics

- [[topics/career-philosophy|Career Philosophy]]: The shift from seeking external validation to focusing on practical utility.
- [[topics/startup-management|Startup Management]]: The necessity of doing whatever is required to ensure a product's success, regardless of role.

## Notable Claims

- The probability of success is higher if you aspire to do 'true work' rather than seeking glory. Evidence: Musk defines true work as the total utility provided to fellow human beings multiplied by the number of people impacted.

## Quotes

> Don't aspire to glory, aspire to work.
> Try to be as useful as possible.
> Always be smashing your ego.
