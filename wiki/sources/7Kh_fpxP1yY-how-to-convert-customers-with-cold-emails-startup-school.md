## Summary

Aaron Epstein, a YC Group Partner, provides a comprehensive guide on crafting effective cold emails for startups, emphasizing that manual, highly personalized outreach is superior to automation in the early stages. He outlines a seven-principle framework for email copy and stresses the importance of funnel tracking, targeting, and persistence to convert leads into customers.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize warm intros over cold emails whenever possible.
- Map out your sales funnel and track conversion rates at every stage to identify bottlenecks.
- Start with manual, personalized outreach to learn what works before attempting to automate.
- Targeting is the highest-leverage activity for improving open rates.
- Write emails like a human talking to a friend, avoiding overly formal or 'salesy' language.
- Focus on the reader's problems rather than your own goals.
- Use uncommon commonalities to build genuine rapport.
- Follow up multiple times, as one email is rarely sufficient.

## Notable Claims

- A warm intro results in a conversion rate two to three times higher than a cold email. Evidence: General industry observation cited by the speaker based on YC experience.
- Conversion rates decrease as you scale. Evidence: The speaker notes that as you grow, you inevitably target less ideal prospects.
- Founders sending emails personally is more effective than using account executives or automation. Evidence: Recipients take direct outreach from leadership more seriously.
