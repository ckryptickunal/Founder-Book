# Micromanagement is toxic: Delegation is the cure (6 simple steps)

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the critical importance of delegation for managers and founders, explaining that micromanagement is a toxic, non-scalable behavior. He provides a six-step tactical framework for effective delegation and emphasizes the necessity of value alignment between leaders and their teams.

## Key Ideas

- Managers should focus only on tasks that nobody else on their team can perform.
- Delegation requires clear communication of objectives, context, success metrics, and priorities.
- Micromanagement often stems from a lack of alignment on values and goals rather than just poor performance.
- Effective leaders act as conductors, hiring people who are better at their specific roles than the leader is.
- If you are constantly frustrated with output or feel the need to be CC'd on everything, you are likely micromanaging.

## Topics

- [[topics/delegation|Delegation]]: The process of assigning tasks to team members while maintaining clarity and authority.
- [[topics/micromanagement|Micromanagement]]: A counter-productive management style characterized by excessive oversight and lack of trust.
- [[topics/management-alignment|Management Alignment]]: Ensuring that the manager and the direct report share the same values, goals, and understanding of 'why' a task is being done.
- [[topics/time-management|Time Management]]: The shift from selling time as a consultant to building systems and teams that generate equity.

## Notable Claims

- Micromanagement does not scale and is universally disliked by employees. Evidence: Personal anecdote of a direct report feeling disrespected and general management theory.
- If you are micromanaging, it is often because you are not aligned on values with your team. Evidence: Analysis of why managers feel the need to oversee every detail.
