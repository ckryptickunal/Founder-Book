# Don't Read The Comments

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-don-t-read-the-comments`

## Summary

Sam Altman advises startup founders to ignore negative online commentary and press, emphasizing that criticism is often a byproduct of innovation and that the most successful companies frequently face early skepticism.

## Key Ideas

- Negative feedback is a common experience for successful founders.
- Founders should develop a thick skin to focus on building rather than reacting to critics.
- Most startups fail, making cynicism an easy but unproductive stance.
- True innovation takes time, and many iconic companies were initially dismissed.
- The most valuable companies of the future have not yet been created.

## Notable Claims

- The most valuable company the world will ever see has not yet been started. Evidence: Historical trends show that innovation is continuous and skeptics have been wrong every time they claimed nothing new could be built.
- Overnight success usually takes a decade of uphill work. Evidence: Reference to the early struggles of companies like Google, Facebook, Airbnb, and Uber.
