# Michael Seibel: How do you decide what to build next?

## Summary

Michael Seibel explains that the key to deciding what to build next is not predictive genius, but rather building a fast, iterative development process that allows teams to test MVPs quickly and discard what doesn't work.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize speed of execution over trying to predict the perfect product feature.
- Build a team capable of shipping MVPs quickly without frustration.
- Maintain a long-term 10-year vision while remaining tactical and agile in the short term.
- Avoid the 'home run' trap where teams spend months perfecting a single idea that may fail.

## Notable Claims

- Teams that build MVPs quickly and iterate based on data outperform those that try to predict the future. Evidence: The speaker contrasts the 'super genius' approach with the 'tactically talented' team approach.
- Spending months perfecting a single feature often leads to failure. Evidence: The speaker cites the 'spiral of death' experienced at Justin.tv when they only swung for home runs.

## Quotes

> It's far more important to have a tactically talented team that can build MVPs quickly... than it is to be a super genius who can imagine what's going to happen in the future.
> You have to have that imagination for your vision for where it's gonna be ten years now... [but] it's really hard to nail those if you have a process that can rip out things quickly.
