## Summary

The founders of Letter AI, Alli and Arman, announce their $40 million Series B funding round. Letter AI is an AI-native enablement platform designed to help revenue teams accelerate deal cycles by providing personalized training, coaching, and content. They discuss their customer success stories with large enterprises like Lenovo and Adobe, and fast-growing startups like Plaid, highlighting use cases such as rapid onboarding and AI-powered sales simulations. The founders also share their journey, including a pivot from their initial Y Combinator project, Tractatus, after realizing the limitations of legacy enablement tools. They introduce a new product, Letter Compass, which personalizes content for individual sellers, and outline their vision for Letter AI to become the most essential tool in a seller's daily workflow.

## Key Ideas

- Letter AI, an AI-native enablement platform, has secured $40 million in Series B funding.
- The platform helps revenue teams with faster onboarding, personalized content delivery, and AI-powered sales simulations.
- Letter AI serves a diverse customer base, including large enterprises (e.g., Lenovo, Adobe) and fast-growing startups (e.g., Plaid).
- The company successfully pivoted from an earlier venture (Tractatus, developer tools for generative AI) during Y Combinator, driven by insights into the inefficiencies of legacy sales enablement tools.
- Letter AI boasts near 100% customer adoption and is considered an essential tool by its users, significantly reducing onboarding times for new sales teams.
- A new product, Letter Compass, personalizes enablement content based on a seller's specific book of business and CRM data.
- Letter AI is developing advanced AI technologies, including MCP servers and agent-to-agent protocols, to integrate deeply with customers' internal and customer-facing AI investments.
- The future vision for Letter AI is to be the single most important, daily-used tool for every customer-facing team member, redefining the future of CRM.

## Notable Claims

- Letter AI helps revenue teams ramp up more quickly. Evidence: Customers are 'productive in about half the time as they were prior to letter' and a Fortune 100 customer onboarded hundreds of new sellers over a weekend, an exercise that would have taken 'at least a month' pre-Letter.
- Letter AI enables personalized content delivery for sales prospects. Evidence: The platform leverages AI to 'curate personalized content to be able to share with the prospect at just the right time.'
- Letter AI offers AI roleplay and simulation capabilities for sales professionals. Evidence: Users 'can practice before you go into a call with an actual buyer' to avoid 'fail[ing] in a live conversation, especially with a high stakes prospect.'
- Letter AI's previous venture, Tractatus, lacked stickiness. Evidence: Developers 'would very quickly do prototyping in our platform and then they would just go build it themselves and so it wasn't very sticky.'
- Legacy enablement tools are expensive and suffer from low adoption. Evidence: Alli's experience showed 'a really expensive tool... that it's getting very low adoption' with sellers saying 'I can't find anything in there. I barely log in.'
- Letter AI achieves close to 100% customer adoption. Evidence: Founders state, 'We see customers with close to 100% adoption. Sellers are going in there every single day, every single week.'
- Letter AI is considered an essential tool by its users. Evidence: An anecdote from a customer suggests that 'if Letter disappeared, there would be a line of folks outside the door of the enablement team banging and asking for it to come back.'
