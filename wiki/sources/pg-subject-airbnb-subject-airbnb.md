# Subject: Airbnb

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-subject-airbnb`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: March 2011
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-subject-airbnb

## Summary

Paul Graham shares a 2009 email exchange with venture capitalist Fred Wilson, documenting his efforts to convince Wilson to invest in the early-stage startup Airbnb. The correspondence provides a rare, transparent look at the behind-the-scenes negotiations and skepticism involved in early-stage venture capital decision-making.

## Key Ideas

- Investors often struggle to see the potential of disruptive, early-stage startups.
- Founders' ability to be on the ground and understand their users is a strong indicator of future success.
- Startup ideas often evolve significantly from their original concept.
- The 'behind-the-scenes' advocacy by mentors and incubators is a critical, often invisible part of the startup ecosystem.
- Marketplace businesses face skepticism regarding their ability to scale into larger, established industries like hotels.

## Notable Claims

- Airbnb could eventually become as large as eBay. Evidence: Paul Graham argued that Airbnb's model of facilitating commerce between two parties has the potential to dominate the accommodation market.
- Meeting the founders is more important than debating the idea beforehand. Evidence: Graham noted that YC had doubts about the Airbnb concept that vanished once they met the founders.
