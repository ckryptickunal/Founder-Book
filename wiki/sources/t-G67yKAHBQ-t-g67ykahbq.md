## Summary

The speaker argues that traditional hierarchical company structures, modeled after Roman legions, are becoming obsolete due to AI. Instead, companies should be reimagined as recursive, self-improving AI loops where domain knowledge is made legible, and AI agents handle operational tasks, coordination, and continuous improvement, allowing humans to focus on high-stakes, high-emotion, and novel interactions.

## Key Ideas

- Companies should transition from hierarchical structures to self-improving AI loops.
- AI should not be a bolt-on productivity tool but the core engine of company operations.
- Making company knowledge (emails, Slack, docs) 'legible' to AI is the prerequisite for automation.
- Middle management is becoming redundant as AI takes over coordination tasks.
- Internal software should be treated as ephemeral and generated on-demand, while business context and data are the permanent, valuable assets.
- Humans should focus on the 'edges' of the company—high-stakes, high-emotion, and novel real-world interactions.
