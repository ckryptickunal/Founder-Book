# How To Use AI In Your Startup

## Metadata

## Summary

Y Combinator partners discuss how founders should approach building startups in the age of AI, emphasizing that while leveraging LLMs is essential, success still depends on solving real customer problems, deep domain expertise, and physical proximity to the innovation hub of San Francisco.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should not pivot to AI just for the sake of it; they must solve genuine, high-value problems.
- AI is a foundational technology shift similar to the transition to the cloud or mobile; it enables the replacement of legacy software.
- The best way to find startup ideas is to observe professionals in specific industries performing repetitive tasks and automate them.
- Physical presence in the Bay Area provides a significant competitive advantage through networking, knowledge sharing, and proximity to experts.
- Healthcare and administrative workflows are ripe for disruption by AI agents that can bridge legacy systems.

## Notable Claims

- Founders should not fear the speed of big companies in the AI space. Evidence: Despite ChatGPT being over two years old, many legacy industries remain largely untouched by AI, leaving significant room for startups.
- Healthcare administrative tasks are highly automatable. Evidence: The US healthcare system spends over $1 trillion on administration, much of which involves humans manually moving data between legacy software systems.
