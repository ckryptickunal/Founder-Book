## Summary

Emmett Shear, co-founder of Twitch, recounts his journey from his first startup Kiko Calendar to the eventual success of Twitch. He emphasizes that startup success is rarely immediate, often requiring years of failure, skill acquisition, and persistence. He highlights the importance of learning to talk to users, staying focused, and the necessity of passion to endure the 'long period of pain' inherent in building a company.

## Key Ideas

- Persistence is the most critical factor in startup success; don't give up.
- Startup founders often lack necessary skills (hiring, management, product design) at the start and must learn them through trial and error.
- Product-market fit is often stumbled upon rather than perfectly planned.
- Talking to users effectively is a specific, difficult skill that is essential for finding the right product direction.
- Avoid premature business development deals; focus on building the product first.
- Founders must love what they do to survive the inevitable long, difficult periods of growth stagnation.

## Notable Claims

- Business development deals are a distraction for early-stage startups. Evidence: Shear notes that every time they attempted such deals at Justin.tv before having a working product, it was a waste of time.
- Most founders do not actually know how to talk to users initially. Evidence: Shear admits he thought he was talking to users correctly for years before realizing he was actually failing to understand their needs.
