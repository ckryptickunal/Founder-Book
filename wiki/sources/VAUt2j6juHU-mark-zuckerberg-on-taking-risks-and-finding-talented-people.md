# Mark Zuckerberg on Taking Risks and Finding Talented People

## Summary

Mark Zuckerberg discusses the early days of Facebook, the influence of Peter Thiel, his philosophy on risk-taking, and his approach to hiring and talent development.

## Key Ideas

- The biggest risk in a rapidly changing world is not taking any risks at all.
- Founders should prioritize raw talent over specific prior experience when hiring.
- Internal growth and providing opportunities for employees to advance are key to retaining top talent.
- Facebook's early growth was organic, with founders initially planning to return to Harvard before the company's momentum made dropping out inevitable.

## Topics

- [[topics/risk-management|Risk Management]]: The necessity of taking risks in a stagnant environment to avoid failure.
- [[topics/talent-acquisition|Talent Acquisition]]: Strategies for identifying raw talent and hiring individuals who can grow with the company.
- [[topics/organizational-growth|Organizational Growth]]: The importance of promoting from within and creating opportunities for employees to lead new product groups.

## Notable Claims

- In a world that is changing quickly, the biggest risk is not taking any risks. Evidence: Zuckerberg argues that while individual decisions have downside risks, stagnation in aggregate guarantees failure.
- Prior experience is not the most important factor when hiring. Evidence: Zuckerberg points to his own success starting at 19 and the fact that most of his current product group leaders did not start in leadership roles.

## Quotes
