# Don’t be thirsty

## Summary

Garry Tan, CEO of Y Combinator, discusses the psychological and strategic pitfalls of 'neediness' in entrepreneurship. He argues that desperation repels investors and opportunities, while detachment, resilience, and a focus on building undeniable value attract success. By reframing rejection as fuel and focusing on internal growth rather than external validation, founders can shift the power dynamic in their favor.

## Key Ideas

- Desperation and neediness act as deal killers in business and personal relationships.
- Rejection should be treated as fuel for growth rather than a reflection of personal identity.
- Building an 'undeniable' product is more effective for networking than traditional, forced networking.
- True confidence is not 100% certainty, but the belief that one has a real chance at success.
- Shifting focus from external validation to internal execution (building, coding, designing) creates long-term value.
- Presence and detachment allow opportunities to find the founder rather than the founder chasing them.
