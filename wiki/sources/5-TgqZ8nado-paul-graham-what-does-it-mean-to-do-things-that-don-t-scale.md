# Paul Graham: What does it mean to do things that don't scale?

## Summary

Paul Graham explains the startup strategy of 'doing things that don't scale,' emphasizing the necessity of manual, artisanal efforts in the early stages to acquire customers and gain critical product insights.

## Key Ideas

- Early-stage startups should prioritize manual, labor-intensive tasks to acquire initial customers.
- Manual processes provide deep, firsthand learning that cannot be achieved through automated or scalable systems.
- The 'unscalable' phase is a temporary but essential foundation for future growth.
- Founders often mistakenly avoid manual work because it doesn't look like a 'giant' company's operations.

## Notable Claims

- Doing things that don't scale is necessary to eventually become a large company. Evidence: Founders learn critical lessons from manual interactions that inform future scalable systems.

## Quotes

> What doing things that don't scale means is do those things early on anyway because you know if you don't do them you'll never be big and you've got nothing to lose.
