## Summary

The founders of Giger, Varun and Esher, discuss their AI agent platform for enterprise customer support. They differentiate themselves from competitors by building a general-purpose product that avoids the 'forward-deployed engineer' consulting model, instead using an AI-native approach where Python and natural language instructions allow for rapid deployment and complex, multi-party call handling at scale.

## Key Ideas

- Giger avoids the traditional 'consultative' model by building a core product that handles complex use cases out-of-the-box.
- The platform treats Python as a first-party primitive, allowing AI to write code based on business logic provided in natural language.
- AI agents can handle multi-party, real-time communication, such as coordinating between a customer and a delivery driver simultaneously.
- The company prioritizes hiring high-agency individuals who are mission-aligned, often targeting former founders.
- Customer support is an ideal vertical for current LLMs due to the manageable scope of context required compared to broader enterprise operations.
- Giger aims to expand from customer support into broader operations automation for Fortune 500 companies.

## Notable Claims

- Giger can go live with complex enterprise use cases in one week compared to months for competitors. Evidence: The founders cite their experience deploying at DoorDash scale.
- AI agents can handle multi-party communication better than human agents. Evidence: The ability to manage parallel calls between customers and dashers without hold times.
- The 'forward-deployed engineer' model is a result of products not being general enough. Evidence: Giger's use of Python primitives allows their product to generalize across different enterprise needs without custom code.
