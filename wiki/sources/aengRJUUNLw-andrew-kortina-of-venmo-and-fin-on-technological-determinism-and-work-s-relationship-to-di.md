# Andrew Kortina of Venmo and Fin on Technological Determinism and Work's Relationship to Dignity

## Summary

Andrew Kortina, co-founder of Venmo and Fin, discusses the intersection of work, human dignity, and technological determinism. He explores why society links personal value to productivity, the potential for AI to eventually replace human labor, and the importance of intentionality in avoiding 'automatic' life paths.

## Key Ideas

- The historical link between work and human dignity was a survival mechanism that may become obsolete as technology creates abundance.
- Technological determinism suggests that efficient, useful technologies will inevitably be created, regardless of who builds them.
- Many modern technology companies 'oversell' the idea that working for them provides unique meaning or impact.
- The 'automatic' mode of living—following pre-set career and life tracks without reflection—is a significant risk to personal authenticity.
- Fin is an AI-assisted service that uses human labor to provide high-quality personal assistance, aiming to bridge the gap until fully autonomous AI exists.
- Measurement and managing complexity are the primary challenges in building a service that aims to perform any task a human assistant would.

## Notable Claims

- Money is the algorithm that already controls human behavior. Evidence: Kortina argues that financial incentives have historically dictated human activity more effectively than any modern AI.
- The 'automatic' mode of living leads to existential regret. Evidence: Kortina references Charlie Kaufman's observations on repetitive, unthinking social interactions as a metaphor for a life lived without reflection.
- Fin's business model is a bet on the persistence of human labor. Evidence: Kortina explains that while they aim for AI-driven tasks, they are building a system to optimize human work until full automation is possible.
