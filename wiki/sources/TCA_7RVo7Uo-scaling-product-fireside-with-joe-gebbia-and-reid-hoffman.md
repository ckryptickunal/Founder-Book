## Summary

Joe Gebbia, co-founder of Airbnb, discusses the company's early growth strategies with Reid Hoffman, emphasizing the importance of doing things that don't scale to achieve product-market fit, the necessity of design-led thinking, and the challenge of maintaining culture while scaling.

## Key Ideas

- Doing things that don't scale is essential for early-stage startups to understand user needs and fix product flaws.
- Design is not just about aesthetics; it is a holistic approach to every touchpoint of the user experience.
- Ethnographic research (observing users in their natural environment) is critical for identifying the gap between product and market.
- Trust is a design problem; companies must engineer conditions that make users feel comfortable with new, unconventional behaviors.
- Culture is built by the people you hire; it is easier to set the foundation early than to fix it later.
- Scaling requires transitioning from manual, founder-led operations to systems that preserve the original mission and quality.

## Topics

- [[topics/scaling|Scaling]]: The transition from manual, non-scalable early operations to automated, high-growth systems.
- [[topics/design-thinking|Design Thinking]]: A holistic approach to product development that considers the entire end-to-end user journey.
- [[topics/trust-engineering|Trust Engineering]]: Designing product features and interfaces to overcome user biases and build confidence in peer-to-peer transactions.
- [[topics/company-culture|Company Culture]]: The importance of hiring for mission alignment and maintaining core values during rapid growth.

## Notable Claims

- Doing things that don't scale is a valid and necessary strategy for early-stage startups. Evidence: Airbnb founders personally visited hosts in New York to take professional photos and gather feedback, which led to a doubling of revenue.
- Culture is driven by the people you hire, not the other way around. Evidence: Airbnb implemented 'core values interviewers' to ensure new hires shared the company's mission, rather than just technical skill.
- Design can be used to build trust between strangers. Evidence: Airbnb found that specific levels of disclosure in user messaging increased reservation acceptance rates.
