## Summary

The video features an interview with Quinn and Hassan, co-founders of Tavis, an AI research lab that recently raised a $40 million Series B. They discuss Tavis's mission to build "AI humans" capable of seeing, hearing, responding, acting, and looking like humans in real-time. The founders demonstrate an AI assistant named Charlie and detail the evolution of their company from personalized video to an SDK for custom AI employees, serving clients like Amazon and Alibaba. They highlight a pivotal moment where they transitioned from an AI sales company to an AI research lab focused on foundational models and perception. Tavis is now launching "Tavis Pals," a consumer-facing product aiming to make AI interaction as natural as talking to a friend, addressing the "human convenient problem." They also touch on the ethical implications of AI, emphasizing its role in improving experiences where human alternatives are scarce or unaffordable, rather than solely replacing jobs. The interview concludes with advice for founders on conviction and momentum.

## Key Ideas

- Tavis develops 'AI humans' designed to mimic human interaction in real-time, including sight, sound, response, and appearance.
- The company's product evolution moved from personalized video to an SDK for custom AI employees, and is now launching 'Tavis Pals' for consumers and prosumers.
- Tavis serves diverse sectors including learning & development, healthcare (patient intake, nutrition coaches, elderly companionship), and go-to-market (SDR, solutions engineer, customer support).
- A significant strategic pivot occurred from being an 'AI sales company' to an 'AI research lab' focused on building foundational models for human behavioral analysis and simulation.
- The goal is to solve the 'human convenient problem,' making computer interaction feel intuitive and natural, like talking to a friend or co-worker.
- Tavis addresses concerns about AI job displacement by focusing on replacing 'bad machines' and providing access to services (e.g., therapy, telehealth) where human alternatives are inaccessible or unaffordable.
- Building empathetic AI involves collecting nuanced perception data (expressions, gestures) and modeling human conversations and reactions to create human simulation models.
- Key advice for founders includes having deep conviction in their vision and maintaining consistent momentum in their work.
- Tavis's core motto is 'faster faster until the thrill of speed overcomes the fear of death,' reflecting their commitment to rapid innovation.
