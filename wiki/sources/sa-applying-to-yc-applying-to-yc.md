## Summary

Sam Altman addresses common misconceptions about applying to Y Combinator, emphasizing that the accelerator is open to startups at any stage of development and does not require prior introductions or connections.

## Key Ideas

- YC accepts startups ranging from early-stage ideas to those with significant revenue and funding.
- The value of the YC network and resources remains relevant even for later-stage companies.
- Re-applying to YC is encouraged, as many successful founders were accepted only after multiple attempts.
- YC intentionally operates differently from traditional VCs by reviewing applications without requiring warm introductions.

## Notable Claims

- YC can provide value to companies up to Series B stage and potentially beyond. Evidence: The speaker notes that YC continues to add features for alumni and that the network is beneficial at any stage.
- Founders do not need a pre-existing relationship or introduction to get into YC. Evidence: The speaker explicitly states that YC's model involves reviewing thousands of applications from unknown founders without introductions.
