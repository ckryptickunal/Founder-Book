# Parker Conrad (Rippling CEO) built an $11 BILLION customer-obsessed business

## Summary

Parker Conrad, CEO of Rippling, discusses the company's evolution into a 'compound startup' that integrates HR, IT, and finance systems through a unified employee data graph. The conversation highlights the shift from fragmented point solutions to bundled, interoperable platforms, and details Conrad's data-driven approach to customer support, including the radical transparency of publishing real-time support metrics.

## Key Ideas

- The 'compound startup' model: Building a suite of interoperable products on a single underlying data primitive (the employee graph) is more effective than building narrow, fragmented point solutions.
- Employee Graph vs. Directory: A true employee graph includes roles, permissions, and relationships, enabling automated workflows across IT and HR, unlike static directories like Active Directory.
- The power of 'anecdata': Looking at individual customer support interactions (streams of data) provides more context and actionable insights than relying solely on aggregate metrics.
- Radical transparency in support: Publishing real-time support performance data builds trust and forces internal accountability.
- Focusing on the 90th percentile: Improving the worst 10% of support interactions is more effective for scaling quality than focusing on median response times.
- The 'rebundling' wave: The market is shifting away from fragmented SaaS point solutions back toward integrated, all-in-one platforms.

## Notable Claims

- Focusing on 90th-percentile support response times improves overall support quality faster than focusing on medians. Evidence: Rippling saw dramatic improvements in response times within two weeks of switching their internal reporting to 90th-percentile metrics.
- The 'compound startup' model is superior to the 'focused startup' model in the current market. Evidence: Market saturation of point solutions makes it harder to build large businesses on single features; integrated platforms offer better value and stickiness.
