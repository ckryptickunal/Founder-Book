## Summary

Rio Lou, Head of Design at Cursor, discusses the evolving role of designers in an AI-driven world. He advocates for designers to become 'coders' by leveraging AI agents to build functional software, moving away from static design artifacts toward a 'sculpting' process where code is the shared language.

## Key Ideas

- Designers should embrace coding by using AI agents to bridge the gap between vision and implementation.
- The future of design involves 'sculpting' software—starting with a rough AI-generated output and refining it into a polished product.
- Product design should be 'systems-first,' focusing on simple, flexible primitives that can be reconfigured rather than adding endless features.
- AI agents allow for rapid iteration, enabling designers to test interactions and motion in real-time rather than relying on static mockups.
- The boundary between design and engineering is blurring; code is becoming the universal language for product development.
- Adaptive UIs should focus on reconfiguring familiar primitives based on user context rather than generating unpredictable interfaces.
