# Are Software Patents Evil?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-are-software-patents-evil`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: March 2006
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-are-software-patents-evil

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that while the patent system is flawed and often misunderstood, software patents are not inherently 'evil' but rather a standard, albeit imperfect, tool in the 'ritualized warfare' of business. He suggests that for startups, patents are primarily useful as bargaining chips for acquisitions rather than defensive weapons, and that the real threat to innovation is not the patent system itself, but the potential for increased corporate secrecy if patents were abolished.

## Key Ideas

- Software patents are functionally identical to hardware patents; opposing them is effectively opposing the patent system as a whole.
- The USPTO struggles with software patents because they lack the technical expertise to distinguish between 'new' and 'non-obvious' inventions in a rapidly evolving field.
- Startups should generally ignore patent infringement risks and focus on building great products, as they are rarely targets for litigation until they become successful.
- Patents serve as a 'mating dance' for startups, providing legitimacy and an excuse for larger companies to acquire them rather than attempting to build competing products.
- Patent trolls are an obsolete business model, but they do not significantly hamper innovation because they only target companies that have already succeeded.
- The alternative to patents is not a free-for-all, but a return to extreme corporate secrecy, which would be more damaging to innovation.

## Notable Claims

- Software patents are no different from hardware patents. Evidence: Modern machines are increasingly defined by software (loops and closures) rather than physical components (levers and gears).
- Startups should not worry about patent infringement. Evidence: Startups are too poor to be worth suing for money and are rarely targeted by competitors until they become significant threats.
- Patent trolls are an obsolete business model. Evidence: They extract value through legal extortion rather than wealth creation, similar to pre-industrial courtiers extracting taxes.
