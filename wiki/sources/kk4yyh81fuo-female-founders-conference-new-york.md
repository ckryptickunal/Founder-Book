## Summary

The Female Founders Conference in New York (2018) features Y Combinator co-founder Jessica Livingston and several successful female founders. The event focuses on the unique challenges and strategies for women in the startup ecosystem, emphasizing the importance of authenticity, mission-driven hiring, and ignoring traditional 'founder' molds.

## Key Ideas

- Successful startups often grow organically from the founders' own lives and unique, sometimes 'weird' combinations of skills.
- Hiring should be based on values and mission alignment rather than just 'culture fit,' which can be a proxy for bias.
- Founders should ignore mainstream opinions and focus entirely on what their users want.
- Raising money is a tool, but maintaining frugality and 'scrappiness' after funding is critical for long-term success.
- Founders should map their mission to specific metrics to maintain focus while scaling.
- There is no single 'mold' for a successful founder; diversity of background and approach is a competitive advantage.

## Notable Claims

- The most successful startups grow organically out of the founders' lives. Evidence: Livingston's experience with YC and the founders' stories of solving personal frustrations.
- Culture fit interviewing is often a proxy for bias. Evidence: The Muse founders explain how it leads to hiring people who look and act like the existing team.
- Raising large amounts of money can be dangerous if it leads to organizational bloat. Evidence: Reshma Saujani's advice on maintaining frugality and 'scrappiness' post-funding.
