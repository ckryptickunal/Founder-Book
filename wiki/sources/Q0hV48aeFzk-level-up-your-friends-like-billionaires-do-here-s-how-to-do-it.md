# Level up your friends like billionaires do. Here's how to do it.

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan critiques the advice of 'constantly upgrading' one's peer group for professional gain, arguing that such a strategy attracts 'sociopaths' rather than genuine creators. He advocates for building communities based on shared values, creativity, and mutual support rather than transactional networking.

## Key Ideas

- Sociopathic networking is a local maximum that ultimately repels true creators.
- A 'scene' is a community of creators who riff off each other to produce new, innovative things.
- The 'average of your five closest friends' principle is best applied by choosing people based on character and creativity, not professional status.
- Mainstream success often attracts people who only care about money, power, and fame.
- True success involves staying grounded and keeping old, authentic friends who knew you before you were successful.
