## Summary

David Lee, partner at SVAngel, shares insights on startup evaluation, investor selection, and the importance of founder reputation, drawing from his experience with over 500 founders and his father's entrepreneurial journey.

## Key Ideas

- Investors prioritize founders over ideas because ideas evolve while people remain constant.
- The ability to articulate a vision is the most critical leadership skill for a founder.
- Great founders are strong-willed but flexible, capable of synthesizing multiple inputs to make informed decisions.
- Founders should prioritize 'value-add' investors over valuation or dilution concerns.
- A founder's reputation is their most valuable asset; controlling small details and demonstrating work ethic builds long-term success.

## Notable Claims

- Ideas morph, but people do not. Evidence: SVAngel's historical data and experience with over 500 founders.
- The best founders are great listeners. Evidence: The success of founders like Mark Zuckerberg and Rick Morrison in synthesizing feedback to improve their business models.
- Reputation is a founder's biggest asset. Evidence: Anecdotes about founders like Larry Page and Sergey Brin attending all seminars and Joseph Lawler's preparation for networking events.
