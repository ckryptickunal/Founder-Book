# What I Wish Someone Had Told Me

## Summary

Sam Altman outlines fundamental principles for building successful companies and achieving long-term goals, emphasizing the importance of audacious ideas, high-conviction bets, and the power of compounding.

## Key Ideas

- Audacious goals are more motivating and easier to pursue than trivial ones.
- Incentives are the primary drivers of organizational behavior.
- Execution should be measured in weeks, while planning should be measured in decades.
- Bureaucracy and organizational charts should never hinder productivity.
- Recruiting high-potential individuals with a fast rate of improvement is critical.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Entrepreneur, investor, and CEO of OpenAI.

## Topics

- [[topics/organizational-management|Organizational Management]]: Strategies for building teams, fighting bureaucracy, and maintaining focus on outcomes.
- [[topics/strategic-planning|Strategic Planning]]: The balance between long-term vision and rapid, iterative execution.
- [[topics/recruitment|Recruitment]]: The philosophy of hiring for net impact and high-potential growth.

## Notable Claims

- It is easier to do a hard thing that matters than an easy thing that does not. Evidence: Audacious ideas provide greater motivation for teams.
- Fast iteration compensates for being wrong. Evidence: Rapid feedback loops allow for course correction before failure becomes terminal.

## Quotes

> Plans should be measured in decades, execution should be measured in weeks.
> Incentives are superpowers; set them carefully.
> Fight bullshit and bureaucracy every time you see it.
> Inspiration is perishable and life goes by fast.
