# Microsoft is Dead

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-microsoft-is-dead`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: April 2007
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-microsoft-is-dead

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that Microsoft lost its dominance and relevance in the technology industry by 2005, shifting from a feared monopoly to an irrelevant legacy player due to the rise of Google, web-based software, broadband, and Apple's resurgence.

## Key Ideas

- Microsoft's influence waned as the industry shifted from desktop-centric software to web-based applications.
- Google replaced Microsoft as the most significant and dangerous force in technology.
- The rise of Ajax and broadband internet made the desktop environment less critical.
- Apple's OS X resurgence captured the loyalty of developers and tech-savvy users, relegating Windows to a legacy status.
- Microsoft's internal culture and inability to innovate in the web era prevent them from regaining their former status.

## Notable Claims

- Microsoft was effectively dead by 2005. Evidence: The shift in developer preference toward Macs, the rise of web-based apps, and Microsoft's lack of relevance in the startup ecosystem.
- Microsoft unintentionally enabled the technology that killed them. Evidence: Microsoft created the XMLHttpRequest object for Outlook, which became the foundation of Ajax.
