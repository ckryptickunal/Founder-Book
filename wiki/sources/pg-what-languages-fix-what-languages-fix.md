## Summary

Paul Graham explores the evolution of programming languages by framing each language as a solution to specific limitations or frustrations found in its predecessors.

## Key Ideas

- Programming languages are often created to address the perceived shortcomings of existing tools.
- The history of programming languages can be mapped as a series of reactions to complexity, syntax, or functional limitations.
- Languages are rarely created in a vacuum; they are defined by what they seek to improve or replace.
