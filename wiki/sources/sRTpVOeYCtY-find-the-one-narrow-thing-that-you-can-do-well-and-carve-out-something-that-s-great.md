# Find the one narrow thing that you can do well, and carve out something that's great.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sRTpVOeYCtY`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-05-01T19:50:06Z

## Summary

A brief strategic guide from Y Combinator on startup success, emphasizing the importance of hyper-specialization in vertical markets to achieve sustainable revenue and autonomy.

## Key Ideas

- Focus on a narrow vertical market rather than broad, general-purpose solutions.
- Small teams can dominate niche markets by solving specific problems better than competitors.
- Reaching $10-20 million in annual revenue provides the option to bootstrap and maintain ownership.
- Success in a niche provides a foundation for future expansion or long-term organic growth.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/vertical-saas|Vertical SaaS]]: The strategy of building software tailored to the specific needs of a narrow industry or user group.
- [[topics/bootstrapping|Bootstrapping]]: Growing a company using its own revenue rather than relying on external venture capital.

## Notable Claims

- Narrow vertical focus allows small teams to compete effectively against larger incumbents. Evidence: By solving all problems for a specific subset of users (e.g., appellant lawyers), a small team can outperform generalist competitors.
- Reaching $10-20 million in annual revenue enables a company to operate without external funding. Evidence: At this revenue scale, companies can often sustain growth through organic cash flow, preserving founder equity.

## Quotes

> Find the one narrow thing that you can do well, and carve out something that's great.
> Don't try to make the thing for all lawyers; you can make it just for say appellant lawyers.
> Once you get there then you have options... you could just start growing it organically out of cash flow.
