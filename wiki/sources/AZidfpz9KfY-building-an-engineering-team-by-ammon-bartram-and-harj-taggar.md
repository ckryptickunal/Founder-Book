## Summary

Ammon Bartram and Harj Taggar of Triplebyte provide a comprehensive guide for early-stage startup founders on building an engineering team, covering sourcing strategies, structured interview techniques to reduce bias, and best practices for closing candidates.

## Key Ideas

- Hiring is a high-stakes, repetitive sales process that requires a structured funnel approach.
- Personal networks are the most effective source for early hires; use 'sourcing parties' to leverage team connections.
- Interviews are inherently noisy; use structured interviews with defined criteria to improve reliability.
- Avoid 'leap of insight' questions; use multi-step problems that allow candidates to demonstrate their process.
- Hide credentials (school/previous company) during technical screens to reduce bias and focus on actual skill.
- Speed is a competitive advantage for startups; be responsive and transparent with offers.
- Compete with tech giants by emphasizing learning, ownership, and career growth rather than just compensation.

## Notable Claims

- Interviewer agreement on candidate quality is very low (0.1 inter-rater reliability). Evidence: Data collected from Triplebyte's platform comparing multiple interviews for the same candidates.
- Structured interviews are more predictive of on-the-job success than unstructured ones. Evidence: General industry data and research on hiring efficacy.
- Most engineers prefer standard technical interviews over trial periods. Evidence: Triplebyte research showing only 20% of engineers are willing to do trial periods.
