# RFS: AI to build enterprise software

## Metadata

- Video ID: `fx3mqS_faOQ`

## Summary

Y Combinator's Request for Startups (RFS) seeking founders to build AI-driven enterprise software that automates customization, potentially disrupting traditional sales-heavy software models.

## Key Ideas

- Enterprise software is traditionally viewed as boring due to the heavy reliance on sales and custom feature requests.
- AI can enable a new model where a single base software product is automatically customized for each client.
- Traditional enterprise software companies rely on large sales teams to promise custom features, which are then manually built by engineers.
- Building AI that writes software for enterprise clients is a highly lucrative and disruptive business opportunity.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/enterprise-software|Enterprise Software]]: Software designed for business use, traditionally characterized by high customization and sales-driven development.
- [[topics/ai-driven-development|AI-Driven Development]]: The use of artificial intelligence to automate the writing and customization of software code.

## Notable Claims

- AI can disrupt the enterprise software market by automating the customization process. Evidence: By providing a starter piece of software and using AI to tailor it to specific customer needs, companies can bypass the traditional, inefficient sales-to-engineering pipeline.

## Quotes

> What if AI could completely change how enterprise software gets built and sold?
> What enterprise software companies are good at is building big sales teams that go out and promise everything to everyone and then get their Engineers to build it.
