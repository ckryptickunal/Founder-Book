# Should you be the CEO?

## Summary

Garry Tan reflects on his experience pitching to Andreessen Horowitz in 2010, where he and his co-founder failed to designate a single CEO. He explains why the 'co-CEO' model is generally ineffective for startups, causing decision-making delays and organizational friction, and provides guidance on how founders should determine if they truly need the authority and responsibilities of the CEO role.

## Key Ideas

- The co-CEO model is rarely effective and often leads to frustration, chaos, and slow decision-making for the rest of the organization.
- A startup needs a single point of authority to make life-or-death decisions quickly.
- Choosing to be CEO should be based on a need for agency and the desire to fix broken systems, rather than ego.
- The CEO role requires a specific skillset—fundraising, hiring, managing, and driving vision—that is distinct from being an individual contributor or product builder.
- Founders must be honest with themselves about whether they are willing to sacrifice hands-on technical work to focus on the singular responsibilities of the CEO.
