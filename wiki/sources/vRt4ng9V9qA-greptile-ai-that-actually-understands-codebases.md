## Summary

Grapile is an AI-powered platform from the YC Winter 24 batch that helps software teams review large codebases and pull requests. It aims to reduce the time senior engineers spend on code review, identify significantly more bugs than human-only processes, and allow human reviewers to focus on higher-level architectural and mentorship aspects. Grapile leverages large language models' strength in understanding code, having evolved from a codebase Q&A tool to a bug detection system, recognizing the increasing importance of software resilience. The company notes varying developer adoption of AI tools but emphasizes focusing on customer needs for startup success.

## Key Ideas

- Grapile uses AI to understand large codebases for automated code review and pull request analysis.
- The platform significantly reduces code review time and uncovers more bugs than traditional human-only processes.
- Human involvement in code review remains crucial for mentorship, strategic architectural insights, and future-proofing, while AI handles tedious bug detection.
- The rise of large language models (LLMs) that excel at understanding code, rather than just generating it, created the opportune moment for Grapile.
- Grapile's origin stems from the challenge of navigating complex and unfamiliar codebases, leading to the realization that persistent bug detection is a critical problem.
- There is a wide spectrum of developer willingness to adopt AI tools, from strong embrace to complete resistance.
- Startups should prioritize building products that customers genuinely love and need, avoiding distractions.

## Notable Claims

- Grapile reduces the amount of time it takes for code to get reviewed and reveals many more bugs than a purely human-driven process would. Evidence: Grapile reviews 5 to 8 million lines of code weekly and revealed 20,000 bugs across pull requests in one past week.
- Humans should always be involved in the code review process. Evidence: Code reviews serve purposes greater than just detecting bugs, such as mentorship and senior engineers' clairvoyance on future codebase direction, which AI cannot replicate.
- Large language models are better at reading code than they are at writing it. Evidence: Grapile's co-founders observed this when experimenting with Da Vinci 2 and continue to leverage this capability.
- Making software more resilient is increasingly important every year. Evidence: Our grid and supply chains are controlled by software, and the world's resilience is tied to its most resilient software.
- The job of engineering is hard primarily because codebases are big and complicated. Evidence: Personal experience of Grapile's founders struggling with a departed team member's frontend codebase and similar experiences at companies.
- There is significant variation in developers' propensity to adopt AI developer tools. Evidence: Some very smart developers are completely against AI adoption, while others strongly embrace it.
