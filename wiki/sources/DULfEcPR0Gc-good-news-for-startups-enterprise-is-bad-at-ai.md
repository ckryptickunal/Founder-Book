# Good News For Startups: Enterprise Is Bad At AI

## Metadata

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses why large enterprises struggle to implement AI, arguing that internal IT departments and traditional consulting firms lack the technical expertise and product-centric mindset to build effective AI-native solutions. This failure creates a significant market opportunity for specialized startups that can integrate deeply into enterprise workflows, despite the high difficulty of implementation.

## Key Ideas

- Enterprise AI failure is often due to internal engineering teams who are skeptical of AI and refuse to use modern tools.
- Large companies rely on consultants (like Deloitte or EY) who lack the technical depth to build functional software, leading to 'camel' products designed by committee.
- Startups have a unique advantage because they can build AI-native products that solve specific, deep-seated business problems that incumbents cannot.
- The 'moat' for AI startups lies in the high switching costs once an enterprise integrates a solution into their core business processes.
- Successful founders in this space are polymaths who combine technical excellence with deep empathy for the human and political processes within large organizations.
- Misleading interpretations of MIT studies regarding AI failure rates are being used by 'AI influencers' to push a narrative of skepticism, ignoring the actual opportunity for builders.

## Notable Claims

- Enterprises are failing at AI because their internal engineers do not believe in the technology. Evidence: Observations from YC partners regarding the skepticism of internal engineering teams toward codegen tools.
- The switching costs for AI solutions in enterprise are a significant moat. Evidence: A quote from a CIO of a $5 billion financial services firm stating that training a system creates prohibitive switching costs.
- Startups have a higher success rate in enterprise AI than internal teams or consultants. Evidence: Data from an MIT study showing that projects using outside vendors like Greenlight or Tactile had higher success rates than internal builds.
