# Startup Ideas You Can Now Build With AI

## Metadata

## Summary

The Y Combinator partners discuss how the rapid advancement of AI, particularly LLMs and large context windows, has fundamentally shifted the 'idea maze' for startups. They argue that many previously impossible or inefficient business models—such as full-stack services and complex marketplaces—are now viable due to AI's ability to automate evaluation, knowledge work, and operations. The hosts emphasize that the current era favors builders who follow their curiosity and experiment with new technology over those strictly adhering to traditional 'lean startup' customer discovery methods.

## Key Ideas

- AI has shifted the 'idea maze,' making previously impossible or inefficient startup ideas viable.
- The cost of intelligence is dropping, enabling a return to premium-model consumer AI products.
- Full-stack startups are now more feasible because AI agents can handle operational tasks, improving gross margins.
- The 'lean startup' advice of 'sell before you build' is becoming outdated; 'build and explore' is now more effective.
- Many established companies are failing to integrate AI, leaving a massive opportunity for new entrants.
- Infrastructure and tooling for AI agents remain a high-growth area for new startups.

## Notable Claims

- AI allows for the creation of 'full-stack' companies with software-like gross margins. Evidence: AI agents can replace large human operations teams that previously eroded margins in tech-enabled services.
- The 'lean startup' methodology is less relevant in the current AI era. Evidence: The rapid pace of technological change makes building and exploring more effective than traditional long-term customer discovery.
- Google's organizational structure hinders its AI product success. Evidence: Internal competition between DeepMind and GCP orgs leads to fragmented API offerings and 'shipping the org'.
