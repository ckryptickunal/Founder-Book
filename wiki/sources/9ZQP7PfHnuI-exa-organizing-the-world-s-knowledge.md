## Summary

Will Brick, CEO of Exa.ai, discusses the company's mission to build a search engine specifically designed for AI agents rather than humans. Exa provides search infrastructure via API, allowing AI systems to access high-quality, real-time data, and recently secured $85 million in Series B funding to scale their proprietary search technology and GPU infrastructure.

## Key Ideas

- Traditional search engines are optimized for human behavior, whereas AI requires high-quality, complex, and large-scale data retrieval.
- Exa functions as 'search infrastructure' for AI agents, operating under the hood of enterprise applications.
- The company maintains its own full-stack search engine and proprietary models to ensure customization, data privacy, and performance that off-the-shelf solutions cannot provide.
- AI agents require a spectrum of latency profiles, ranging from fast synchronous search to slow, high-quality deep research.
- The ultimate goal is to solve the 'coordination problem' by providing perfect, real-time access to the world's knowledge.

## Notable Claims

- LLMs cannot memorize the entire web, making search engines essential for AI. Evidence: Information theory constraints; the web is petabytes in size, while LLM weights are terabytes.
- Building a custom search engine is necessary for enterprise-grade AI. Evidence: Requirements for zero data retention, domain filtering, and high-volume result retrieval that Google/Bing do not support.
