# RFS: New enterprise resource planning (ERP) software

## Summary

Y Combinator expresses interest in funding startups that are building modern, user-friendly Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) software to replace legacy systems.

## Key Ideas

- ERP systems serve as the operating system for businesses.
- Legacy ERPs are often clunky, expensive, and disliked by employees.
- There is a significant market opportunity for modern, industry-specific ERP solutions.
- Modern ERPs should leverage AI, mobile-first design, and intuitive user interfaces.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding for startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/enterprise-resource-planning-erp|Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)]]: Software systems used by organizations to manage day-to-day business activities and operations.
- [[topics/b2b-saas-innovation|B2B SaaS Innovation]]: The modernization of mission-critical business software through better UX, AI, and mobile integration.

## Notable Claims

- Legacy ERP systems are generally difficult to use and expensive. Evidence: Common user experience complaints and high cost structures associated with traditional enterprise software.
- There is a significant opportunity for new startups to disrupt the ERP market. Evidence: The combination of modern UI, mobile capabilities, and AI can solve industry-specific problems better than legacy incumbents.
