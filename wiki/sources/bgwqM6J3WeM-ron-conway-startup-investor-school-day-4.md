## Summary

Ron Conway, a legendary angel investor and founder of SV Angel, discusses his investment philosophy, the importance of founder character, and the mechanics of building a successful startup portfolio during a guest lecture at Y Combinator's Startup Investor School.

## Key Ideas

- Invest in the founder first, the idea second; look for determination and integrity.
- Angel investing is a high-risk game where 40-60% of companies will likely fail; winners must win big to cover losses.
- Adopt a portfolio approach by investing smaller amounts (e.g., 25k) across 5-10 companies in a sector where you have domain expertise.
- The primary value an investor adds is helping founders build their management team and providing access to distribution networks.
- Failure is a natural part of the business model; don't quit after the first loss.
- Civic engagement and integrity are essential components of being a 'good' investor in the modern ecosystem.

## Notable Claims

- 40% to 60% of startups in an angel portfolio will go out of business. Evidence: Conway cites this as a standard failure rate based on his 25 years of experience.
- The most important value an investor adds is helping build the management team. Evidence: Conway notes that after initial mentoring, hiring and distribution are the biggest hurdles for startups.
- Blockchain and crypto represent the next wave of the internet (Web 3.0). Evidence: Conway identifies this as a sector for ambitious investors to study.
