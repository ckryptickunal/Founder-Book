# The best consumer companies incorporate both virality and network effect to grow organically.

- Video ID: `nty_szMaNTs`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-10-08T19:21:24Z

## Summary

This video explains the synergy between virality and network effects as core drivers for the organic growth of successful consumer companies, using WhatsApp as a primary case study.

## Key Ideas

- Metcalfe's Law defines the value of a network as the square of the number of its nodes.
- Network effects occur when a product becomes more valuable as more people use it.
- Virality and network effects are distinct but complementary mechanisms for organic growth.
- WhatsApp serves as a prime example of a product that gains utility through user expansion.

## Entities

- [[entities/whatsapp|WhatsApp]] (product): A messaging application that demonstrates strong network effects.
- [[entities/metcalfe-s-law|Metcalfe's Law]] (other): A principle stating that the value of a network is proportional to the square of the number of connected users.

## Topics

- [[topics/network-effects|Network Effects]]: The phenomenon where a service increases in value as the user base grows.
- [[topics/organic-growth|Organic Growth]]: The process of expanding a user base through inherent product utility and viral loops rather than paid acquisition.

## Notable Claims

- The best consumer companies incorporate both virality and network effects to grow organically. Evidence: The speaker identifies these as the two primary mechanisms for sustainable organic user acquisition.

## Quotes

> The more people who use this thing the more valuable it gets.
> The best consumer companies incorporate both virality and network effect which are different concepts but very closely related.
