# Tim Brady - How do you calculate burn rate, runway and growth rate?

## Summary

Tim Brady explains the essential financial metrics for early-stage startups: burn rate, runway, and growth rate, emphasizing their importance for investor communication and operational management.

## Key Ideas

- Burn rate is a cash flow metric, not a profit and loss metric; it measures net cash outflow.
- Runway is calculated by dividing total cash on hand by the monthly burn rate.
- Growth rate should be calculated as a Compounded Monthly Growth Rate (CMGR) to avoid overstating performance.
- Investors value recurring revenue (MRR) significantly higher than non-recurring revenue due to predictability.
- Founders should review these metrics weekly to manage startup health and prioritize effectively.

## Topics

- [[topics/burn-rate|Burn Rate]]: The net amount of cash a company spends each month.
- [[topics/runway|Runway]]: The number of months a startup can operate before running out of cash.
- [[topics/growth-rate|Growth Rate]]: A measure of how fast sales are increasing, ideally calculated as a compound rate.
- [[topics/revenue-classification|Revenue Classification]]: The distinction between recurring revenue (subscriptions) and non-recurring revenue (one-off purchases).

## Notable Claims

- Calculating growth rate as a simple average rather than a compound rate makes founders look unsophisticated or dishonest to investors. Evidence: The example of 100% growth over six months vs. the actual 35% CMGR.
- Recurring revenue is valued more highly by venture investors than non-recurring revenue. Evidence: Predictability of cash flow makes recurring revenue models more attractive.
