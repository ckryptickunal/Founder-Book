## Summary

Chad Rigetti, founder of Rigetti Computing, discusses the challenges and motivations behind building a 'hard tech' startup in the field of quantum computing. He explains the transition from classical microchip-based computing to quantum systems, the necessity of building an entire supply chain from scratch, and the importance of pursuing high-impact, long-term technological goals that stir the heart.

## Key Ideas

- Hard tech startups face fundamental questions of technical possibility, unlike traditional software startups.
- Quantum computing leverages quantum mechanics to solve problems in chemistry, material science, and AI that are intractable for classical supercomputers.
- Building a hard tech company requires vertical integration, including developing proprietary fabrication, control electronics, and software.
- The primary challenge for founders is balancing product development with building the organizational capabilities and infrastructure required to support that product.
- Hiring should focus on individuals who 'pump entropy out of the system' by creating order, clarity, and systems.

## Topics

- [[topics/quantum-computing|Quantum Computing]]: The use of quantum-mechanical phenomena to perform computation, specifically using superconducting qubits.
- [[topics/hard-tech|Hard Tech]]: Startups that tackle fundamental scientific or engineering challenges where the path to success is not clearly defined.
- [[topics/moore-s-law|Moore's Law]]: The observation that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles about every two years, which Rigetti notes is leveling off.
- [[topics/amdahl-s-law|Amdahl's Law]]: A formula used to find the maximum improvement of a system when only part of it is improved, highlighting limits to parallelization.

## Notable Claims

- Quantum computers will provide a faster and cheaper path to exascale computing power than classical supercomputers. Evidence: Comparison of cooling systems and chip efficiency versus the massive power and size requirements of classical supercomputers like Tianhe-2.
- Hard tech companies create greater defensibility and leverage than traditional software startups. Evidence: The necessity of building proprietary supply chains and infrastructure creates high barriers to entry.

## Quotes

> I have a particular love for the startups where they're trying to do something it's not clear if it's technically possible but if they do, it changes the world.
> A hard tech company has to deal with, in addition to all of the tech execution, Market risk, all the standard things... fundamental questions of possibility.
> Some people create order and clarity in their wake... other people generate entropy. Know what you're looking for, hire people who pump entropy out of your vision.
