## Summary

Paige from Y Combinator provides a recap of an AMA event focused on navigating job searches at startups during an economic downturn, highlighting key strategies for standing out and evaluating company stability.

## Key Ideas

- Personalized outreach is essential to avoid being ignored by recruiters.
- Job interviews are a two-way street; candidates should actively vet company stability and runway.
- There is no 'better' startup based solely on age; fit depends on individual risk tolerance and career goals.
- Candidates should research the specific needs of a company to tailor their applications effectively.

## Notable Claims

- Personalizing outreach messages is the most effective way to stand out in a competitive job market. Evidence: Recruiters can easily identify generic, copy-pasted applications, which are often ignored.
- Candidates should interview the founders to gauge company stability. Evidence: Interviewing is a two-way street; candidates need to ensure the company is a good fit for their own risk profile.
