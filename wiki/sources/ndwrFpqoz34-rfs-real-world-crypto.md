# RFS: Real-world crypto

## Summary

Y Combinator is seeking startup applications for projects that bridge the gap between blockchain technology and real-world assets, specifically focusing on tokens that represent enforceable ownership of tangible value.

## Key Ideas

- Crypto's reputation suffers from a lack of tangible underlying value in many existing tokens.
- Stablecoins serve as a successful model for tokens representing real-world value (US dollars).
- There is significant potential in tokenizing real estate, bank deposits, and business equity.
- Legal and regulatory compliance is the primary barrier to entry for real-world asset (RWA) projects.
- Y Combinator is prioritizing technical founders who possess or partner with deep legal expertise.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/real-world-assets-rwa|Real-World Assets (RWA)]]: The process of tokenizing physical or financial assets on a blockchain to provide verifiable ownership.
- [[topics/regulatory-compliance|Regulatory Compliance]]: The legal challenges associated with ensuring crypto tokens provide enforceable rights under existing property and securities law.

## Notable Claims

- Crypto tokens that represent ownership of real-world assets are more valuable than those without. Evidence: The success and utility of stablecoins representing US dollars.

## Quotes

> We're really interested in hearing from teams who are taking this idea further and building crypto projects where a token gives you a real ownership right in something that's real and valuable.
