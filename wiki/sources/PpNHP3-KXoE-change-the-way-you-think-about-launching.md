# Change the way you think about launching.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `PpNHP3-KXoE`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-04-19T19:17:56Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PpNHP3-KXoE

## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator emphasizing that startups should view launching as a continuous, iterative process rather than a single, high-stakes event.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often overthink their first launch, fearing it is their only chance for success.
- Spending months preparing for a single launch is a high-risk strategy that can lead to startup failure.
- Launching should be treated as a continuous activity rather than a one-time milestone.
- Early feedback is critical, and delaying a launch to achieve perfection is counterproductive.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-strategy|Startup Strategy]]: The methodology of building and releasing products to maximize market feedback and minimize failure risk.
- [[topics/iterative-development|Iterative Development]]: The practice of shipping early and often to refine products based on real-world usage.

## Notable Claims

- Most startups will find that no one cares about their initial launch. Evidence: The speaker notes that founders who spend months preparing often face indifference from the market.
- Spending six months on a first launch can lead to startup failure. Evidence: The speaker argues that the delay prevents necessary feedback and iteration, potentially killing the company before it gets a second chance.

## Quotes

> I want you to think about launching as something that you do continually.
> If it takes you six months to get the first version of your product in front of anyone your startup may be dead before you get another chance to launch.
