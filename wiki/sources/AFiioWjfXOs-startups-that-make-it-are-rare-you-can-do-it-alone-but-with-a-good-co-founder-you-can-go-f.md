# Startups that make it are rare. You can do it alone, but with a good co-founder you can go farther.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `AFiioWjfXOs`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-04-03T16:48:29Z

## Summary

A discussion on the strategic importance of co-founders in startups, arguing that while solo founding is possible, a strong co-founder partnership provides the necessary support and competitive advantage to build exceptional, market-dominant companies.

## Key Ideas

- A bad co-founder is worse than being solo, but a great co-founder is the optimal path for long-term success.
- Startups are rare and difficult, requiring every possible advantage to succeed.
- The goal of a startup should be to 'play to win' by attracting resources and dominating the market.
- Avoiding interpersonal conflict by working alone limits potential and is not a viable strategy for building something significant.
- All complex problems are ultimately people problems; learning to navigate them is essential for achievement.

## Notable Claims

- Exceptional, breakout companies almost always have a healthy co-founder relationship. Evidence: The speakers argue that the rarity of startup success necessitates every advantage, including the support system provided by a co-founder.
- If you do not play to win, a competitor will. Evidence: The competitive nature of markets ensures that someone else will aggressively pursue dominance if you do not.
