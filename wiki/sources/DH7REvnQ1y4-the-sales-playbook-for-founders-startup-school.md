## Summary

A guide for early-stage B2B founders on navigating the sales process, moving from inefficient, long-term design partnerships to rapid, recurring revenue contracts through structured pilots and clear value propositions.

## Key Ideas

- Avoid long, unpaid design partnerships that lack clear scope and commitment.
- Focus on building a 'narrow wedge' product that solves a specific, burning problem rather than a broad platform.
- Use pilots or proof-of-concepts to prove value, but keep them short and tied to specific success metrics.
- Transition from free trials to paid pilots to ensure customer commitment.
- The 'pro move' is using recurring revenue contracts with an opt-out period to bypass secondary sales negotiations.
- Identify and empower an internal champion within the customer organization to navigate procurement and budget hurdles.
- Prioritize speed to first value over complex technical integrations during the pilot phase.

- 90% of founders get stuck in the early stages of long, unpaid design partnerships. Evidence: Observation from YC partners working with early-stage B2B startups.
- Getting a financial commitment, even a small one, makes customers take a pilot more seriously. Evidence: Founders report higher engagement when customers have 'skin in the game'.
