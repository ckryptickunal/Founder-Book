## Summary

Suhail Doshi, co-founder of Mixpanel, provides a framework for early-stage founders to measure product growth by focusing on simplicity, user behavior, and retention rather than vanity metrics.

## Key Ideas

- Focus on a simple growth formula: visits, signups, valuable actions, retention, and referral.
- Avoid over-complicating analytics; pick 3-5 'North Star' metrics to guide the team.
- The 'back button' is the primary enemy of landing pages; keep messaging clear and value-oriented.
- Retention is the most critical metric; avoid the 'shark fin' effect where viral growth masks poor long-term engagement.
- Talk to users directly when you have few customers; qualitative feedback is often more valuable than data points at the start.
- Be 'harsh' with metrics; define success by meaningful actions rather than simple logins or registrations.

## Notable Claims

- Monthly active users (MAU) is often a vanity metric. Evidence: It can be easily inflated by one-time users who never return.
- Revenue churn is mathematically non-intuitive and dangerous. Evidence: A 7% monthly churn rate results in a 58% annual revenue loss, which is difficult to overcome.
- The 'next feature' fallacy distracts founders. Evidence: Founders often believe a new feature will solve growth issues when they should be focusing on fixing retention or core product value.
