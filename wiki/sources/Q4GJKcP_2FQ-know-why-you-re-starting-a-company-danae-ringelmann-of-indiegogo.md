## Summary

Danae Ringelmann, co-founder of Indiegogo, explains why identifying a core, authentic 'why' is the most critical factor for startup success, resilience, and strategic decision-making.

## Key Ideas

- A founder's 'why' must be authentic to their core to survive the inevitable hardships of entrepreneurship.
- Clarity of purpose helps founders pivot effectively by focusing on the problem rather than a specific product solution.
- A strong mission acts as a filter for hiring talent and attracting customers who share those values.
- The '5 Whys' exercise is a practical tool to move from superficial goals to deep, irrational, and core beliefs.
- Purpose-driven strategy provides 'bumper lanes' for execution, such as Indiegogo's decision to remain an open, global platform.

## Topics

- [[topics/entrepreneurial-resilience|Entrepreneurial Resilience]]: The necessity of having a deep-seated mission to persist through long, difficult periods of startup growth.
- [[topics/strategic-decision-making|Strategic Decision Making]]: How a core mission informs business strategy, such as choosing an open platform model over a gated one.
- [[topics/the-5-whys-exercise|The 5 Whys Exercise]]: A method to drill down into the core, irrational beliefs that drive a founder's motivation.

## Notable Claims

- If your reason for starting a company is not authentic to your core, your chances of failure increase significantly. Evidence: Ringelmann's personal experience and observation of the difficulty of the entrepreneurial journey.
- Being obsessed with a 'why' rather than a specific product solution allows for better pivoting. Evidence: Indiegogo pivoted from an offline fund model to an online perks-based platform because the goal was democratizing capital, not a specific product.
