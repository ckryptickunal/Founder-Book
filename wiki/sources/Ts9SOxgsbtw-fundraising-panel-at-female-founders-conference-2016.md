## Summary

A panel discussion at the 2016 Female Founders Conference featuring four successful Y Combinator alumni who share their experiences, strategies, and challenges in fundraising for their respective startups.

## Key Ideas

- Fundraising is a sales process that requires resilience, preparation, and the ability to handle rejection without taking it personally.
- Founders should focus on metrics, traction, and clear storytelling to demonstrate the potential for a billion-dollar outcome.
- Not every business needs to raise venture capital; bootstrapping to profitability is a valid and often superior path for many.
- Investors often use 'soft' rejections; founders should recognize these and move on rather than waiting for a definitive 'no'.
- Maintaining professional grace during difficult interactions with investors is crucial for long-term reputation.

## Notable Claims

- A long, drawn-out fundraising process can kill a company. Evidence: Caitlin Gleason notes that she optimized for speed to avoid the distraction and drain of a prolonged search.
- Investors often use non-committal language instead of saying 'no'. Evidence: Liz Wessel explains that phrases like 'come back when you have a lead' are often soft rejections.
- You don't need to raise money just because it's a trend. Evidence: Marty Ramamoorthy emphasizes that many successful businesses are built without venture capital.
