# Motion: Redefining How Work Gets Done

## Summary

The founders of Motion discuss their journey from high-frequency trading to building a multi-million dollar B2B work management platform. After achieving success with a consumer-focused AI calendaring tool, they pivoted to a B2B model to better serve SMBs and are now integrating AI agents to automate business workflows.

## Key Ideas

- Founders pivoted from a successful $20M ARR consumer product to a B2B platform because the former lacked the scale to become a billion-dollar business.
- Motion differentiates itself by combining traditional project management with AI agents that perform actual work, rather than just managing it.
- The platform's competitive advantage lies in having deep, proprietary context about a business's operations, which is essential for effective AI agent performance.
- The founders' motivation is driven by a 'chip on their shoulder' and a desire to prove themselves, rooted in their experiences as immigrants and early-career traders.
- Successful B2B products require deep feature parity and a unique value proposition that makes them significantly better than incumbents like Jira or Asana.

- [[topics/ai-agents|AI Agents]]: The transition from software that manages work to software that executes tasks autonomously.
- [[topics/founder-psychology|Founder Psychology]]: The role of personal ambition, immigrant experience, and the 'fear of losing' in startup success.

## Notable Claims

- Consumer-focused productivity tools often struggle to reach billion-dollar scale because they are viewed as 'nice-to-haves' rather than 'must-haves'. Evidence: The founders observed lower retention and lower dollar-based retention compared to B2B products.
- AI agents perform better when they have access to the underlying platform and data of the business. Evidence: Motion's agents leverage the existing project management and knowledge base data to provide superior context compared to independent agents.
