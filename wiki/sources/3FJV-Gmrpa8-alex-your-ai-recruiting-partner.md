## Summary

Alex, founded by Eron Wong and John Wright, is an AI recruiting partner that automates and enhances the hiring process for companies and staffing firms. It conducts autonomous phone screens, video interviews, scheduling, and ATS updates, aiming to interview every applicant and match the best candidates to opportunities. The company recently secured a $17 million Series A funding round and rebranded from its previous name to 'Alex' to align with its AI interviewer's identity. Alex addresses the inefficiencies of a tripling applicant volume and rising time-to-hire by leveraging AI to improve candidate experience, identify overlooked talent, and free up human recruiters for more strategic tasks.

## Key Ideas

- Alex is an AI recruiting partner that automates the initial stages of the hiring process, including phone screens, video interviews, scheduling, and ATS updates.
- The platform addresses critical inefficiencies in the modern hiring market, such as overwhelming applicant volume, long time-to-hire, and recruiter bandwidth limitations.
- Alex aims to democratize job opportunities by allowing every candidate to receive a first interview, regardless of background, and helps identify 'diamonds in the rough' lost in large applicant databases.
- The AI learns from existing company data (ATS, job descriptions) and can be fine-tuned for niche roles, assessing both hard and soft skills through video and audio analysis.
- Alex is not designed to replace human recruiters but to 'supercharge' them by automating administrative tasks, allowing them to focus on strategic relationship-building and candidate closing.
- The company recently raised a $17 million Series A round to further develop its product and expand its go-to-market efforts, emphasizing the importance of brand and relationships in enterprise sales.
- Alex has developed advanced features like cheat detection (for AI-assisted candidates and deepfakes) to maintain interview integrity in an evolving, adversarial job market.
- The company rebranded to 'Alex' from its previous name to reduce confusion, as customers were already referring to the AI interviewer by that name, making the AI more approachable and grounded.

## Notable Claims

- Applicant volume has tripled in the past 3 years. Evidence: Stated by John Wright, highlighting the inefficiency of the current hiring market.
- Time to hire is at an all-time high, about 60 days. Evidence: Stated by John Wright, comparing it to closing a house in San Francisco faster than hiring an engineer.
- Alex has helped hire thousands of people and is filling tens of thousands of active job roles since launching last year. Evidence: Stated by Eron Wong, demonstrating the scale of Alex's operations.
- Alex can identify and place overlooked candidates who were 'lost in the database'. Evidence: Example given of a staffing firm hiring for Cobalt developers, where Alex found 11 relevant candidates in their ATS who were then immediately placed.
- Alex's stack rank of candidates is often similar or better than a traditional recruiter's. Evidence: Measured in pilots, with Alex providing video evidence and specific quotes to justify skill scores (e.g., Python knowledge).
- Hired candidates through Alex show increased retention rates. Evidence: Tracked as a success metric, indicating better matching and qualification.
- The launch of GPT-4 Turbo at the end of 2023 made low-latency, conversational voice agents possible. Evidence: Stated by Eron Wong as the catalyst for Alex's development and launch in early 2024.
- Alex can detect candidates using AI to cheat during interviews, including deepfakes. Evidence: The founders describe building 'cheat detection' models due to the 'adversarial effects' of the job market.
