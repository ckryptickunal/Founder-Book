# How I got into Y Combinator

## Summary

Garry Tan explains that the secret to finding mentors and career success is not to ask for mentorship, but to provide value to people you admire. He illustrates this with his own experience of using his photography skills to document Y Combinator's Startup School, which led to his acceptance into the program and eventually a career as a YC partner.

## Key Ideas

- Don't ask people to be your mentor; instead, find people you believe in and offer them your unique skills.
- Being a 'great joiner' early in your career is the best preparation for becoming a great leader or founder.
- Providing value first creates a 'magnet effect' that attracts opportunities and smart people to you.
- Diversify your skills; hobbies like photography can unexpectedly open doors in professional settings.
- The 'give first' mindset creates long-term professional dividends.
