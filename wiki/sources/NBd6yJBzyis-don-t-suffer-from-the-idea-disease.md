# Don’t suffer from the “idea disease”

## Metadata

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the 'idea disease'—the misconception that a great idea constitutes the majority of the work—and introduces the 'Disney Method' as a framework for product development. This method balances three distinct mindsets: the Dreamer (what to build), the Realist (how to build it), and the Critic (why to build it).

## Key Ideas

- The 'idea disease' is the false belief that having a great idea is 90% of the work.
- Execution requires craftsmanship and the ability to navigate constraints.
- The Disney Method provides a structured approach to innovation through three distinct roles.
- Successful founders must either embody or build teams that balance dreaming, realism, and critical thinking.
- The 'Dreamer' stage focuses on ideal outcomes without constraints.
- The 'Realist' stage focuses on implementation, resource management, and trade-offs.
- The 'Critic' stage evaluates the 'why,' potential negative impacts, and overall viability.
