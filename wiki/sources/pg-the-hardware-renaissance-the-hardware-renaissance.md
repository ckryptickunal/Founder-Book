# The Hardware Renaissance

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-the-hardware-renaissance`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: October 2012
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-the-hardware-renaissance

## Summary

Paul Graham discusses the resurgence of hardware startups in 2012, noting that despite investor bias, hardware companies are performing well due to advancements in prototyping, manufacturing, and crowdfunding.

## Key Ideas

- Hardware startups are performing better than their software counterparts in recent Y Combinator batches.
- Investor bias against hardware is a trailing indicator; founders are better at predicting the future by building it.
- Technological advancements like 3D printing, Arduinos, and improved electric motors have lowered the barrier to entry for hardware.
- The historical dominance of software as a business model may be a temporary phenomenon rather than a permanent rule.
- Crowdfunding and online retail have reduced traditional bottlenecks for hardware distribution.

## Topics

- [[topics/hardware-renaissance|Hardware Renaissance]]: The shift in the startup ecosystem where physical products are becoming as viable and scalable as software.
- [[topics/prototyping-technology|Prototyping Technology]]: The role of 3D printing, CNC milling, and Arduinos in making hardware development more accessible.
- [[topics/investor-bias|Investor Bias]]: The historical reluctance of venture capitalists to fund hardware due to perceived risks and capital intensity.

## Notable Claims

- Hardware startups are currently outperforming non-hardware startups in Y Combinator batches. Evidence: Data from the most recent batch of 84 companies where 7 were hardware-focused.
- The dominance of software as the primary model for rapid business growth is a relatively recent development, dating back to roughly 1990. Evidence: Historical observation of business model evolution.
