# You don’t need a billion dollar idea to get started.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `DVibT82PSsk`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2022-12-16T19:31:25Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DVibT82PSsk

## Summary

Y Combinator emphasizes that early-stage startup success depends more on founder attributes—such as determination, execution ability, and open-mindedness—than on the initial business idea, which is often subject to change.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should prioritize starting and building something over waiting for a perfect, billion-dollar idea.
- Investors at the early stage fund the founders rather than the specific business concept.
- Successful founders often pivot or refine their initial ideas based on market feedback.
- Key founder traits include determination, a track record of shipping products, and domain expertise.

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Notable Claims

- Early-stage funding is primarily based on the attributes of the founders rather than the initial idea. Evidence: Y Combinator's experience funding companies where founders frequently pivot or change their initial concepts.
