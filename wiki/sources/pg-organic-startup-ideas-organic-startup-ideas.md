# Organic Startup Ideas

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-organic-startup-ideas`

- Published: April 2010

## Summary

Paul Graham explores the most effective way to generate startup ideas, arguing that 'organic' ideas—those born from solving personal problems—are superior to 'made-up' ideas intended for others. He emphasizes that successful startups often begin as simple projects rather than grand business plans and encourages founders to focus on fixing broken things in their daily lives.

## Key Ideas

- The best startup ideas come from asking what you personally wish someone would build.
- Organic ideas (solving your own problems) are generally more successful than made-up ideas (guessing what others need).
- Many successful companies, like Apple and Facebook, started as personal projects rather than intentional business ventures.
- Founders should focus on the idea itself rather than the 'startup' aspect, as early-stage projects are often dismissed as toys.
- Young founders are uniquely positioned to find organic ideas because they are at the forefront of new technology adoption.

## Notable Claims

- Organic startup ideas are more likely to succeed than made-up ones. Evidence: Successful companies like Apple, Google, and Facebook began as solutions to the founders' personal needs.
- Ideas that look like 'toys' are often the most promising. Evidence: History shows that microcomputers, planes, and cars were initially dismissed as toys before becoming transformative technologies.
- Young founders are better at finding organic ideas. Evidence: They are at the forefront of technology and are the first to encounter new, fixable problems.

## Quotes

> Organic startup ideas usually don't seem like startup ideas at first.
> Don't be discouraged if what you produce initially is something other people dismiss as a toy. In fact, that's a good sign.
