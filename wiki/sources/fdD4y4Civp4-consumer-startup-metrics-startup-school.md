## Summary

This video provides a comprehensive guide to essential metrics for consumer startups, emphasizing the importance of organic growth, unit economics, retention, and customer satisfaction. It highlights the necessity of viral loops and network effects for sustainable scaling, while warning against over-reliance on paid acquisition channels.

## Key Ideas

- Growth rates of 15% month-over-month are ideal, while 5% or lower is unlikely to lead to breakout success.
- Organic growth (virality and network effects) is superior to paid growth because it pays back indefinitely.
- Paid referral schemes should be treated as paid acquisition and monitored for cannibalization and fraud.
- Unit economics must be calculated on a per-customer basis, subtracting variable costs from revenue.
- Identifying a 'magic moment' in the user journey is critical for predicting and improving long-term retention.
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) of +50 is a minimum baseline for successful consumer startups.
- Over-reliance on paid ad platforms like Google and Meta can lead to margin erosion and vulnerability to platform changes.
