# Should You Quit Your Job At A Unicorn?

## Summary

Dalton Caldwell and Michael Seibel from Y Combinator discuss the critical evaluation of late-stage 'unicorn' startups, advising employees on how to determine if they should stay or leave based on internal metrics rather than external hype.

## Key Ideas

- Employees often have better information about a company's health than investors or the public.
- A 'unicorn' valuation does not guarantee success; many will fail to IPO or be acquired successfully.
- High strike prices on stock options at late-stage companies can leave employees with underwater equity.
- Revenue growth and product engagement are the most reliable indicators of company health.
- External fundraising announcements are often lagging indicators and can be misleading.
- Moving to an earlier-stage company can offer better equity upside and more responsibility compared to a stagnant late-stage unicorn.
