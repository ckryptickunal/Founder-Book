## Summary

Amber Atherton (Zyper) and Iba Masood (TARA) discuss their experiences raising Series A funding, emphasizing the importance of founder-investor fit, maintaining a structured fundraising process, and the shift in operational focus post-funding.

## Key Ideas

- Fundraising is a long-term partnership; prioritize the individual partner over the VC firm's brand or valuation.
- Maintain a structured, data-driven process (e.g., tracking spreadsheets, investment memos) to manage investor pipelines efficiently.
- Avoid 'optimizing for valuation' at the expense of long-term alignment.
- Post-Series A, the focus must shift from pure revenue growth to building culture, hiring the right team, and establishing operational frameworks like OKRs.
- Founders should be wary of 'hot deal' optics and focus on genuine product-market fit and traction.
- Back-channeling with other founders in a VC's portfolio is essential to understand how a partner behaves when things go wrong.

## Notable Claims

- Optimizing for valuation can lead to misaligned incentives. Evidence: Founders suggest that high valuations require unrealistic growth, which can create pressure and misalignment.
- A term sheet is not a guarantee of funding. Evidence: Term sheets are essentially letters of intent; deals can and do fall through after they are signed.
- Hiring early-stage employees is different from hiring late-stage executives. Evidence: Early-stage hires possess a 'hustle' mentality necessary for scrappy, pre-scale environments.
