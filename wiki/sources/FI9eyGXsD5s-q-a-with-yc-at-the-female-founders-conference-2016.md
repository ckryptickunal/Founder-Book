## Summary

In this 2016 Q&A session at the Female Founders Conference, Y Combinator partners Jessica Livingston and others address common concerns from prospective applicants. They emphasize that YC is open to founders of all backgrounds, ages, and stages of development, focusing primarily on team determination, domain expertise, and the potential for a large-scale impact rather than rigid criteria.

## Key Ideas

- There is no 'too early' or 'too late' to apply to YC; the application process itself is a valuable exercise for refining business ideas.
- YC prioritizes teams that demonstrate determination, the ability to execute, and deep domain expertise.
- The 'trough of sorrow' is a normal part of the startup journey; YC views its role as helping founders navigate and overcome these difficult periods.
- Solo founders are accepted, but the difficulty of managing a startup alone makes having a co-founder advantageous, provided the partnership is organic and tested.
- Founders should be able to explain their business clearly and succinctly in a way that is understandable to anyone.
- The goal for the next decade is to see a significant increase in female-founded unicorns.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-selection-criteria|Startup Selection Criteria]]: YC looks for determined teams, domain experts, and ideas that have the potential to become very large.
- [[topics/co-founder-dynamics|Co-founder Dynamics]]: The importance of organic relationships and the dangers of 'tacking on' a co-founder just to meet application requirements.
- [[topics/communication-skills|Communication Skills]]: The necessity for founders to distill their business model into a simple, clear, and understandable message.

## Notable Claims

- The YC application process is beneficial even if the applicant does not submit it. Evidence: Founders report that answering the application questions helps them distill their thoughts and think about their business in a new way.
- Successful startups are often more 'broken' than they appear from the outside. Evidence: Jessica Livingston notes that even highly successful companies have significant internal challenges.

## Quotes

> We don't care if someone's in the trough of sorrow... that is our job to help you get out of the trough of sorrow.
> The worst thing to do is to go out and seek out a co-founder just because you don't think you can do it alone.
> I want there to be like tons of female founded unicorns.
