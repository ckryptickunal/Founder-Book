# The U.S. Digital Service

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-the-u-s-digital-service`

- Published: 2015-08-14 15:59:37

## Summary

Sam Altman highlights the U.S. Digital Service (USDS) as a successful initiative that applies startup product development methodologies to modernize critical government software and services.

## Key Ideas

- Government technology can be significantly improved by adopting startup-style product development.
- Small, agile teams deployed within government agencies can achieve massive cost savings and efficiency gains.
- The USDS serves as a model for tech professionals to perform a 'tour of duty' in public service.
- Successful government tech projects, like the HealthCare.gov rescue, demonstrate the viability of this approach.

## Entities

- [[entities/u-s-digital-service|U.S. Digital Service]] (organization): A federal agency that brings tech industry best practices into government.
- [[entities/healthcare-gov|HealthCare.gov]] (product): A federal health insurance marketplace website.
- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Entrepreneur, investor, and author of the essay.

## Topics

- [[topics/civic-tech|Civic Tech]]: The application of modern software engineering and design practices to government services.
- [[topics/public-service|Public Service]]: The concept of tech professionals dedicating time to work within government agencies.

## Notable Claims

- The USDS replaced a $200 million login system with one that cost $4 million to build. Evidence: Comparison of the original HealthCare.gov login system costs versus the USDS-led replacement.
- The USDS attracts talent comparable to top Silicon Valley startups. Evidence: Recruitment of engineers and product managers from companies like Amazon, Google, and Facebook.

## Quotes

> I think it’d be great if it became a new tradition that people from the tech world do a tour of duty serving our country at some point in their careers.
