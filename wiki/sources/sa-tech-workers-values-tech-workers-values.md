# Tech Workers' Values

## Summary

Sam Altman, Debra Cleaver, and Matt Krisiloff propose a collective effort for tech employees to influence corporate values, focusing on privacy, civil rights, economic distribution, and reducing societal polarization.

## Key Ideas

- Tech companies possess significant power to influence fundamental rights and societal well-being.
- Employees have the leverage to shape corporate policies and values.
- Tech companies should decentralize high-paying jobs to address economic inequality.
- The industry has a responsibility to mitigate the polarization it has helped create.
- Organized employee advocacy, similar to a union, can drive corporate social responsibility.

- Tech companies have an obligation to reduce the polarization they have helped create. Evidence: The authors argue that the influence of tech platforms on public discourse necessitates a proactive role in healing societal divides.
- Tech companies are highly receptive to employee influence. Evidence: The authors suggest that collective employee action can effectively shape the values and policies of their employers.
