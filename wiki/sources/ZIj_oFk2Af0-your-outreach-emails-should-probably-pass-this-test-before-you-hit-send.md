# Your outreach emails should probably pass this test before you hit send.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `ZIj_oFk2Af0`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-04-17T20:56:10Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZIj_oFk2Af0

## Summary

A brief Y Combinator segment advising startup founders to evaluate their cold outreach emails by asking if they would personally respond to them, highlighting the common pitfall of sending templated, low-effort spam.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often send outreach emails they would personally ignore or delete.
- The 'would I respond?' test is a critical filter for cold email quality.
- Templated emails with minimal customization are perceived as spam.
- Founders should not underestimate the intelligence or standards of their potential customers.

## Notable Claims

- Many founders send emails that they themselves would not respond to. Evidence: The speaker notes that when asked directly, founders admit they would archive emails identical to the ones they are currently sending.

## Quotes

> You should always say to yourself if I received this email that I am sending would I respond?
> Don't think your customers are smart or something like this you really don't care.
