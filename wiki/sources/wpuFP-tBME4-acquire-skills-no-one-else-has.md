# Acquire skills no one else has!

## Metadata

- Video ID: `wpuFP-tBME4`

- Published: 2024-03-15T16:00:52Z

## Summary

Garry Tan explains that career and startup success depends on acquiring rare, unique skill combinations rather than relying on traditional credentials.

## Key Ideas

- Value is created by possessing rare combinations of skills that others lack.
- Investors prioritize founders with tangible building skills over those with mere credentials.
- Founders must develop core competencies before attempting to raise capital.
- Competitive advantage in business is analogous to sports, where unique execution leads to elite performance.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): Investor and President/CEO of Y Combinator

## Topics

- [[topics/skill-acquisition|Skill Acquisition]]: The strategic importance of developing rare, non-obvious skill sets to gain a competitive edge.
- [[topics/startup-fundraising|Startup Fundraising]]: The criteria investors use to evaluate founders, focusing on capability over credentials.

## Notable Claims

- Investors prefer founders with specific building skills over those with only credentials. Evidence: Investors avoid funding founders who lack the skills to compete against more capable rivals.

## Quotes

> What is most useful is actually acquiring skills that nobody else has, especially in combinations that are rare.
> Good investors look at the skills of the founders and the teams they fund not just the credentials.
