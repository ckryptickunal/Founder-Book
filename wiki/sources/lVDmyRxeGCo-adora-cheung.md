## Summary

Adora Cheung, founder and CEO of Homejoy, shares the story of her three-year struggle to find a viable startup idea, emphasizing the importance of persistence, operational grit, and the reality of building a business from scratch.

## Key Ideas

- Finding a startup idea is a process of trial and error that requires extreme patience.
- Founders should solve problems they actually encounter in their daily lives.
- Operational experience, such as performing the service yourself, is crucial for understanding the business and identifying inefficiencies.
- Startups are not about luck; they are about working hard and refusing to quit.
- Maintaining a calm, analytical mindset when facing crises prevents unnecessary stress and allows for better problem-solving.
