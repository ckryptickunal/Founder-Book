# Sales pre-PMF should be done by the founders.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `aXmmS9QKRDg`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-08-23T18:54:18Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aXmmS9QKRDg

## Summary

This Y Combinator video argues that early-stage startup founders must personally handle sales before achieving product-market fit (PMF), as the role requires entrepreneurial vision, rapid iteration, and direct feedback loops that hired salespeople cannot replicate.

## Key Ideas

- Founders are uniquely positioned to sell their product before PMF because they possess the necessary vision and credibility.
- Hiring a salesperson too early is ineffective because the sales process is still experimental and requires deep product knowledge.
- Pre-PMF sales is an entrepreneurial activity, not a repeatable process that can be delegated.
- A tight feedback loop between the person selling and the person building the product is essential for early-stage success.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit-pmf|Product-Market Fit (PMF)]]: The stage at which a startup has identified a target market and satisfies that market with its product.
- [[topics/startup-sales-strategy|Startup Sales Strategy]]: The methodology of acquiring initial customers and validating business models before scaling.

## Notable Claims

- Founders are the only people capable of selling their product before achieving product-market fit. Evidence: The process requires vision, credibility, and constant experimentation that hired staff cannot perform without established playbooks.

## Quotes

> Sales pre-pmf is fundamentally entrepreneurial; it requires vision and credibility with customers and lots of experimentation and a tight feedback loop with the people building the product.
