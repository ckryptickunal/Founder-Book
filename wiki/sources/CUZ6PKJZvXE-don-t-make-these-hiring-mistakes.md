# Don't Make These Hiring Mistakes

## Summary

In this episode of Inside the Group Partner Lounge, Y Combinator partners discuss the common pitfall of premature hiring in early-stage startups. They argue that most hiring advice is tailored for post-product-market-fit companies, and applying it to pre-product-market-fit startups can accelerate failure by increasing burn rate and complexity.

## Key Ideas

- Most startup hiring advice is intended for companies that have already achieved product-market fit.
- Hiring is often used as a false metric of progress or a way to avoid doing difficult founder-level work.
- Premature hiring increases burn rate and reduces runway, which are the primary causes of startup failure.
- Founders should focus on solving core problems themselves before attempting to delegate to specialists.
- Hiring too early creates management overhead and potential cultural toxicity, which can lead to founder burnout.
- Successful companies like Airbnb and Stripe hired very slowly in their early stages.

## Notable Claims

- Hiring more people does not solve the problem of needing more features or reaching profitability faster. Evidence: Founders often mistake hiring for a business model, but employees collect salaries rather than generating revenue.
- Premature hiring can accelerate a startup's death. Evidence: High salary burn rates deplete runway, and managing a larger team distracts from the critical work of finding product-market fit.
- The best founders innately understand that a small team of high-quality people is better than a large team of mediocre ones. Evidence: Successful founders often report that their most productive times were when they were in a room with a very small, elite group.
