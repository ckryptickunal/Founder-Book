## Summary

Sam Altman argues that net neutrality is essential because consumers lack choice in 'last-mile' internet providers, necessitating government protection to prevent traffic discrimination and ensure the internet remains an open platform for innovation.

## Key Ideas

- The 'last-mile' internet provider monopoly prevents free-market competition.
- Internet access should be treated as a public service with fair access as a basic right.
- Traffic discrimination by ISPs threatens the ability of new, fragile startups to compete.
- Mandating that infrastructure owners lease lines to competing ISPs would solve the competition problem.
- Net neutrality regulations are necessary as long as consumers lack provider choice.

## Topics

- [[topics/net-neutrality|Net Neutrality]]: The principle that internet service providers should treat all data on the internet equally.
- [[topics/last-mile-monopoly|Last-Mile Monopoly]]: The lack of competition among local internet service providers, which limits consumer choice.
- [[topics/internet-innovation|Internet Innovation]]: The role of an open internet in allowing new, small companies to grow and succeed.

## Notable Claims

- Last-mile traffic discrimination should be per se illegal. Evidence: Consumers lack freedom of choice in providers, making them vulnerable to abuse by monopolistic ISPs.
- The internet is a public service and fair access should be a basic right. Evidence: Municipalities often granted monopolies and used tax dollars to fund the development of last-mile infrastructure.

## Quotes
