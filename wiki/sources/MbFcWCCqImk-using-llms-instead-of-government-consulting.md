# Using LLMs Instead of Government Consulting

## Metadata

- Video ID: `MbFcWCCqImk`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-08-03T15:54:46Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MbFcWCCqImk

## Summary

Y Combinator discusses the potential for LLM-based startups to disrupt the inefficient and expensive government consulting industry by automating tasks currently performed by large firms like Deloitte and Accenture.

## Key Ideas

- The US government spends hundreds of billions annually on inefficient consulting services.
- Political pressure is mounting to reduce wasteful government spending.
- Existing government software, often built by traditional consulting firms, is outdated and ripe for replacement.
- LLMs are now capable of performing many tasks currently handled by human consultants.
- Y Combinator is actively funding startups that help companies navigate government procurement (FedRAMP) and assist agencies in streamlining regulations.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding for startups.
- [[entities/deloitte|Deloitte]] (company): A major multinational professional services network.
- [[entities/accenture|Accenture]] (company): A global professional services company.
- [[entities/fedramp|FedRAMP]] (project): A US government-wide program that provides a standardized approach to security assessment, authorization, and continuous monitoring for cloud products and services.

## Topics

- [[topics/government-procurement|Government Procurement]]: The process by which the government acquires goods and services, often hindered by complex regulations like FedRAMP.
- [[topics/ai-disruption|AI Disruption]]: The application of LLMs to automate professional services and software development tasks traditionally held by large consulting firms.

## Notable Claims

- LLMs are currently capable of performing many of the jobs that government consulting firms do. Evidence: The speaker notes that LLMs are now advanced enough to handle tasks like regulatory analysis and policy compliance.
- There is significant political pressure to cut wasteful government consulting spending. Evidence: The speaker identifies this as a primary driver for the shift away from traditional consulting models.

## Quotes
