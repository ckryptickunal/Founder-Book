# We fund smart founders, irrespective of what they want to work on.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sjRtaX35TJM`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-02-28T22:27:52Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sjRtaX35TJM

## Summary

Y Combinator partners clarify that their high investment rate in AI companies is driven by founder interest rather than an internal institutional thesis or bias toward AI.

## Key Ideas

- Y Combinator's investment strategy is founder-centric rather than sector-centric.
- The high percentage of AI companies in recent batches reflects broader market trends and founder priorities.
- External perceptions of YC's investment strategy often misattribute founder-led trends to institutional bias.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-investment-strategy|Startup Investment Strategy]]: The process and philosophy behind how venture capital firms select companies to fund.
- [[topics/artificial-intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]]: The current surge in founder interest and development of large language model-based startups.

## Notable Claims

- Approximately 50% of the Summer 2023 Y Combinator batch consisted of companies working on large language models. Evidence: Stated by Y Combinator partners during the discussion.
- Y Combinator does not have a specific institutional thesis that favors AI over other sectors. Evidence: Partners clarify that they fund smart founders regardless of their chosen field.

## Quotes

> We fund the smart Founders like irrespective of what they want to work on.
> The fact that half the batch is working on AI says something much more interesting than just the YC Partners think AI is cool.
