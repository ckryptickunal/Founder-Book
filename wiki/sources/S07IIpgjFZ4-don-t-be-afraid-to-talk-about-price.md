# Don’t Be Afraid to Talk About Price

## Metadata

## Summary

A brief advisory for startup founders on overcoming the fear of discussing pricing early in the sales process to validate customer willingness to pay and qualify leads.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often avoid pricing conversations due to fear of losing potential customers.
- Discussing price is essential for validating the value proposition.
- Pricing conversations help disqualify leads that are not ready, able, or willing to purchase.
- Value-based pricing questions help determine how much a solution is worth to a customer.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/sales-strategy|Sales Strategy]]: Techniques for engaging potential customers in pricing discussions to improve sales efficiency.
- [[topics/customer-qualification|Customer Qualification]]: The process of identifying which prospects are serious buyers versus those who are not.

## Notable Claims

- Bringing up pricing early in a conversation rarely scares away a serious customer. Evidence: The speaker asserts this is 'almost always not true' based on their experience with founders.

## Quotes

> If I can solve this problem for you and deliver these metrics we've talked about, how much would that be worth to you?
> You really need to disqualify customers who just aren't ready, able, or willing to buy your product.
