# Request for Startups: Government 2.0 - Michael Seibel

## Metadata

- Video ID: `MFqMaVn5r-Y`

## Summary

Michael Seibel, CEO of Y Combinator, introduces a 'Request for Startups' (RFS) focused on 'Government 2.0,' encouraging founders to build for-profit tech companies that address societal issues typically handled by government entities.

## Key Ideas

- Silicon Valley investors are highly interested in supporting founders with strong social missions.
- Startups must be for-profit, scalable businesses that leverage software to solve public sector problems.
- There is a significant opportunity for technology to fill gaps where government services are failing or inefficient.
- YC is actively seeking applicants who prioritize community and societal improvement over simple wealth accumulation.

## Entities

- [[entities/michael-seibel|Michael Seibel]] (person): CEO of Y Combinator's accelerator.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator and venture capital firm.

## Topics

- [[topics/government-2-0|Government 2.0]]: The application of technology and software to improve or replace inefficient government services and societal infrastructure.
- [[topics/social-entrepreneurship|Social Entrepreneurship]]: Building for-profit businesses that have a primary goal of creating positive social impact.

## Notable Claims

- Silicon Valley investors are eager to support founders with a social mission. Evidence: Seibel notes that YC has successfully funded numerous startups addressing food stamps, healthcare access, and mass incarceration.
- Government 2.0 startups must be for-profit businesses. Evidence: Seibel explicitly states that YC invests in tech companies and the business must be a real, for-profit entity.

## Quotes

> We're looking for startups that are helping address problems in society that you would expect government to solve but they clearly are not.
> If your sole goal is not to get rich or to build a big company but to actually make the world a better place or your community a better place we are interested in funding you.
