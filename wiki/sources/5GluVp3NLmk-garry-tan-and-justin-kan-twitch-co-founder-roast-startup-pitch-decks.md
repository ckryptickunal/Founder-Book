## Summary

Garry Tan and Justin Kan review and critique startup pitch decks, offering candid feedback on business models, market sizing, and presentation clarity for early-stage companies.

## Key Ideas

- A pitch deck must clearly communicate the problem, the market size, and why the founders are the right team to solve it.
- For consumer apps, long-term retention (D30/D90) is the most critical metric for investors.
- B2B software businesses in fragmented, offline industries (like pharmacy supply chains) often have high potential for 'roll-up' and data monetization.
- Founders should avoid 'vanity metrics' and focus on clear, bottom-up market analysis.
- The 'why now' factor is essential to explain why a business is viable today when it might not have been previously.

## Notable Claims

- A 20% or higher D30/D90 retention rate is a strong indicator of an investable consumer app. Evidence: Garry Tan notes that this metric is the primary filter for top-tier consumer app investments.
- Data monetization is a viable long-term business model for B2B supply chain platforms. Evidence: Comparison to Instacart and Vetco, which leverage transaction data for advertising and financial services.
