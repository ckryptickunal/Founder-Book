## Summary

Michael Seibel of Y Combinator explains why relying on outsourced development shops to build an initial MVP is often a trap for early-stage tech startups, emphasizing the importance of finding a technical co-founder for speed, cost-efficiency, and iteration capability.

## Key Ideas

- Outsourced dev shops often lead to slower development cycles and higher costs than anticipated.
- Investors generally prefer startups with internal technical capability over those that outsource, as it demonstrates the ability to iterate.
- The 'trap' occurs because founders feel they are making progress initially, only to run out of funds when the product requires inevitable iteration.
- Technical co-founders are more motivated and aligned with the long-term success of the startup compared to external contractors.
- Most early-stage investors prioritize the team's ability to build and pivot over the initial state of the product.

## Notable Claims

- Outsourcing development makes it harder to raise venture capital. Evidence: Many investors avoid companies that outsource because it signals a lack of internal technical capability and agility.
- Working with a dev shop is often more expensive and slower than having a technical co-founder. Evidence: Dev shops have conflicting priorities and lack the equity-driven motivation of a founder, leading to inefficiencies.
