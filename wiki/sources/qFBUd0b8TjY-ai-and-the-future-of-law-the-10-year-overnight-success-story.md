# AI and the Future of Law: The 10 Year "Overnight" Success Story

## Summary

Jake Heller, co-founder of Casetext, discusses the company's 10-year journey from a crowdsourced legal research platform to a $650 million acquisition, driven by the integration of large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4 to automate complex legal tasks.

## Key Ideas

- The '10-year overnight success' phenomenon requires persistence, iteration, and being prepared when technology breakthroughs occur.
- A 'golden demo' that showcases immediate, high-value time savings is critical for B2B SaaS adoption.
- LLMs act as a new base layer of capability, similar to cloud computing, enabling massive efficiency gains in professional services.
- The 'last mile' of AI implementation—handling scale, accuracy, and hallucinations—is where significant engineering challenges and business value lie.
- AI has the potential to solve critical societal issues, such as reducing backlogs for the Innocence Project.

## Notable Claims

- GPT technology is currently underhyped. Evidence: The ability of models to perform human-level cognitive tasks at scale is a transformative shift that is only beginning to be realized.
- A 'golden demo' is the most effective way to secure enterprise contracts. Evidence: Casetext used real-world case data (e.g., Enron) to show lawyers they could perform days of work in minutes, leading to immediate sales.
