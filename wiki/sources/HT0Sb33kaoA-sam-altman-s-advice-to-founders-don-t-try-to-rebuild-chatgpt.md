# Sam Altman's advice to founders: Don't try to rebuild ChatGPT

## Metadata

- Video ID: `HT0Sb33kaoA`

## Summary

Sam Altman advises startup founders against competing directly with ChatGPT, encouraging them instead to build on top of OpenAI's platform by leveraging new features like traffic integration and authentication services.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should avoid building direct clones of ChatGPT as OpenAI has a significant head start.
- OpenAI aims to position itself as a platform for other developers rather than a competitor.
- Future integrations like 'Sign in with OpenAI' and traffic referrals will create new opportunities for startups.
- The broader AI ecosystem offers vast opportunities beyond the core functionality of ChatGPT.

## Notable Claims

- OpenAI intends to drive traffic to new startups via ChatGPT. Evidence: Altman mentions the potential for an app or agent store within ChatGPT to refer users to external startups.
- Building a direct competitor to ChatGPT is a poor strategic choice for new startups. Evidence: Altman cites OpenAI's significant head start and commitment to improving their own product as reasons to avoid direct replication.
