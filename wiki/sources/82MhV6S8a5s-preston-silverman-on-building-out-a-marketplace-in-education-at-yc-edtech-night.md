## Summary

Preston Silverman, founder and CEO of RaiseMe, discusses building a marketplace platform that helps students accrue college scholarships and grants throughout high school, rather than waiting until the application process. The company aims to make higher education more accessible and transparent by providing predictable financial aid based on student progress.

## Key Ideas

- Financial aid is often awarded too late in the college application process to influence student decisions.
- RaiseMe allows students to earn 'micro-scholarships' throughout high school based on their academic and extracurricular progress.
- Building a marketplace requires solving the 'chicken and egg' problem by focusing on network density and securing supply (funds) before scaling demand (students).
- Early-stage startup success relies on tactical execution, user research, and resilience rather than immediate 'rocket ship' growth.
- The biggest barriers to college completion include a lack of personalized guidance and mentorship, and a disconnect between education and career skills.

## Notable Claims

- Over 80% of jobs created in the US since the recession require a college degree. Evidence: Cited by Preston Silverman as a core reason for the importance of expanding access to higher education.
- Product-market fit is not a binary state but a gradual process of iteration. Evidence: Silverman's experience of iterating on the platform and seeing student sign-ups grow over time.
