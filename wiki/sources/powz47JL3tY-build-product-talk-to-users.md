# Build product. Talk to users.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `powz47JL3tY`
- Channel: Y Combinator

## Summary

A concise advisory from Y Combinator emphasizing that early-stage startup founders should exclusively focus on product development and user acquisition, warning against common 'productive-feeling' distractions like excessive networking and consuming startup advice.

## Key Ideas

- Startup success at early stages is binary: build the product or get users.
- Activities that feel productive but don't directly result in product growth or user adoption are dangerous distractions.
- Networking events and reading generic startup advice are often wastes of time for founders.
- Validation only comes from having a product that users want and are willing to pay for.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to startups.

## Topics

## Notable Claims

- Almost everything that isn't building a product or getting people to use it is a waste of time for early-stage startups. Evidence: The speaker argues that activities like networking and reading advice provide a false sense of productivity without contributing to core business viability.

## Quotes
