# Virality and network effects drive organic growth.

## Summary

This video explains the distinction and synergy between virality and network effects as core drivers for organic growth in consumer companies, using Facebook and WhatsApp as primary case studies.

## Key Ideas

- Virality is the process of existing users introducing the product to new users.
- Network effects occur when a product's value increases as the number of users grows.
- Virality and network effects are distinct but complementary growth mechanisms.
- Metcalfe's Law states that the value of a network is proportional to the square of the number of its nodes.

## Entities

- [[entities/facebook|Facebook]] (company): A social media platform used as a case study for viral mechanics.
- [[entities/whatsapp|WhatsApp]] (product): A messaging application used as an example of network effects.
- [[entities/metcalfe-s-law|Metcalfe's Law]] (other): A principle stating the value of a network is the square of its nodes.

## Topics

- [[topics/virality|Virality]]: The mechanism by which current users acquire new users through product usage.
- [[topics/network-effects|Network Effects]]: The phenomenon where a product becomes more valuable as the user base expands.

## Notable Claims

- The best consumer companies incorporate both virality and network effects. Evidence: The speaker highlights how these mechanisms work together to drive organic growth.
- Tagging friends in photos on Facebook was a viral mechanic. Evidence: The process prompted non-users to sign up to view photos they were tagged in.

## Quotes

> Virality is the idea that one user using your product introduces it to other users somehow in the use of your product.
> Network effect is the idea that the product gets better the more nodes in the network exist.
