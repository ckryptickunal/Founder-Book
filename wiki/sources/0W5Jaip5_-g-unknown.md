## Summary

Anne, co-founder and CEO of Poppy, shares her journey of transitioning from a failed first startup to building a successful childcare platform. She emphasizes the importance of solving real problems, focusing on growth metrics, and the necessity of persistence for founders.

## Key Ideas

- Build what people need rather than following personal passion blindly.
- Focus on a single growth metric and aim for consistent weekly growth (10-20%).
- Use 'concierge' MVPs (manual processes) to test ideas before building complex software.
- Funding should be viewed as a tool to buy time for experimentation, not a budget to spend.
- Founders must find their own 'village' of support to sustain the emotional toll of entrepreneurship.

- [[topics/founder-resilience|Founder Resilience]]: The psychological challenge of dealing with failure and the necessity of choosing to continue despite setbacks.

## Notable Claims

- The most effective way to build a startup is to solve a problem people actually have, rather than following passion. Evidence: Anne's first startup failed because it was based on passion but lacked market need; Poppy succeeded by addressing the specific pain point of childcare scheduling.
- You can validate a business idea with very little capital. Evidence: Anne launched the initial version of Poppy using free or trial-based tools (Squarespace, Typeform, Excel) with only $200.
