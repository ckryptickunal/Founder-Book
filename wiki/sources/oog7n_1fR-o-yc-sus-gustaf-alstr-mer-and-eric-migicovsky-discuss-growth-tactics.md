# YC SUS: Gustaf Alströmer and Eric Migicovsky discuss growth tactics

## Summary

Gustaf Alströmer, a Y Combinator partner and former growth lead at Airbnb, joins Eric Migicovsky to discuss practical growth strategies for startups. The conversation covers the shift from ad-driven growth to monetization-first models, the importance of early user feedback, and tactical approaches to building marketplaces and viral loops in both B2C and B2B contexts.

## Key Ideas

- Startups should focus on charging users early rather than relying on advertising, as competing with Google and Facebook for ad revenue is extremely difficult.
- Paid growth is only sustainable if you have a clear understanding of your customer acquisition cost (CAC) and lifetime value (LTV).
- Founders should perform 'things that don't scale' in the early days, such as manual outreach and direct customer support, to build deep product understanding.
- For marketplaces, build supply artificially first (e.g., using friends) to ensure a high-quality experience before acquiring demand.
- Referral programs work best for products that are inherently social, have high-frequency usage, and offer a meaningful incentive.
- B2B growth can be accelerated by building viral loops directly into the product, such as collaborative document sharing or easy onboarding for team members.

## Notable Claims

- Most startups should not rely on advertising as their primary business model. Evidence: It is extremely difficult to compete with the advertising dominance of Google and Facebook.
- Ten users are sufficient for initial user testing. Evidence: A small sample size is enough to identify major friction points if the testing is open-ended and unguided.
- Referral programs are a form of paid marketing. Evidence: They require a clear ROI calculation to ensure the cost of the incentive is covered by the value of the acquired customer.
