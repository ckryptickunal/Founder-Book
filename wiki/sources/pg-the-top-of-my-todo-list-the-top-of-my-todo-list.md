# The Top of My Todo List

## Summary

Paul Graham reflects on Bronnie Ware's 'Top Five Regrets of the Dying,' identifying them as dangerous errors of omission that occur by default. He proposes a strategy to counteract these tendencies by inverting the regrets into a set of active commands placed at the top of his daily todo list.

## Key Ideas

- The most dangerous mistakes in life are errors of omission, which occur by default.
- Modern life often forces individuals to act like 'cogs' in a machine, suppressing personal desires and connections.
- To avoid default mistakes, one must actively transform their life or create external reminders.
- Inverting negative regrets into positive commands serves as a practical tool for behavioral change.

## Notable Claims

- Errors of omission are more dangerous than errors of commission. Evidence: They occur by default, making them harder to notice and avoid without conscious effort.
- The five regrets of the dying represent a portrait of post-industrial man. Evidence: The tendency to shrink oneself to fit circumstances and act like a cog in a machine.
