# Groww: If Your Customers Don't Love It or Hate It, You've Already Lost

## Summary

Lalit Keshre, co-founder of the Indian fintech company Groww, discusses the company's journey from a failed robo-advisor startup to a public, market-leading investment platform. He emphasizes the importance of customer obsession, iterative product development, transparency, and maintaining a strong, value-aligned co-founder relationship.

## Key Ideas

- Build products by reading between the lines of customer feedback rather than just following direct requests.
- Focus on 'customer love' and organic growth through word-of-mouth rather than paid acquisition.
- Reduce startup risk by identifying and narrowing down the core 'question marks' (uncertainties) in the business model.
- Maintain a strong, value-aligned co-founder relationship by documenting core values and clearly defining ownership.
- The barrier to entry for building consumer products has significantly lowered due to AI and automation tools.
- Founders should remain 'power users' of their own products and stay in direct contact with customers regardless of company scale.

## Notable Claims

- If your customers don't love or hate your product, you have already lost. Evidence: Indifference indicates that the product does not solve a meaningful problem for the user.
- The barrier to building a startup has significantly decreased. Evidence: Modern AI tools and automation allow a single person to handle tasks that previously required a team of 10-15 people.
