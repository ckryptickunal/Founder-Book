# Helping African Businesses Get Paid, Shola Akinlade of Paystack

## Summary

Shola Akinlade, co-founder of Paystack, discusses the company's journey from a Y Combinator-backed startup to a leading payments infrastructure provider in Africa. He highlights the challenges of building in emerging markets, the importance of local context, and the company's mission to simplify digital payments for African businesses.

## Key Ideas

- Paystack simplifies payment acceptance for African merchants by reducing complex, multi-step processes into a streamlined two-step experience.
- The company focuses on 'first principles' design, acknowledging that for many users, Paystack is their first experience with online digital payments.
- Building a successful startup in Africa requires navigating unique constraints, such as infrastructure gaps and diverse cultural/linguistic landscapes, rather than just replicating Western models.
- YC's value lies not just in funding, but in the network, the 'Bookface' community, and the long-term perspective on building sustainable companies.
- Akinlade emphasizes the importance of hiring senior talent to help scale infrastructure and set engineering standards, even if it means recruiting globally to upskill local teams.

## Notable Claims

- Less than 2% of consumer spending in Nigeria is done with cards. Evidence: Akinlade cites this as evidence of the massive opportunity for digital payment adoption.
- It takes about seven years to build a solid startup. Evidence: Akinlade references this advice from YC as a way to manage expectations and maintain a long-term view.
- Paystack is the first company in Nigeria to offer an automated dispute process. Evidence: Akinlade notes this as a critical feature for building trust in digital payments.
