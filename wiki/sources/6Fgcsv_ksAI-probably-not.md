## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator warning early-stage founders against using paid acquisition to grow free consumer apps, arguing that it leads to unsustainable growth and suggesting that organic, non-obvious distribution channels are the key to success.

## Key Ideas

- Paid acquisition for free apps is generally ineffective and unsustainable.
- Successful consumer apps grow through non-obvious, low-cost distribution methods.
- Paid marketing should only be used as a controlled experiment to learn specific metrics, not as a primary growth strategy.
- Spending money on user acquisition for free apps often results in temporary spikes followed by a return to baseline.

## Notable Claims

- Spending money to acquire users for a free app is ineffective. Evidence: The speaker notes that metrics often spike temporarily but inevitably return to the starting point.
- Successful consumer apps grow through non-obvious, cheap distribution. Evidence: The speaker observes that most large-scale consumer apps achieved their size by finding unique, low-cost ways to reach users.
