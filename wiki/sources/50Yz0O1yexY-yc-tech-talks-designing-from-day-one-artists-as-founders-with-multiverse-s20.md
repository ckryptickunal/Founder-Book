## Summary

The founders of Multiverse, a startup building a next-generation tabletop RPG platform, discuss the strategic value of including artists as co-founders. They argue that artists possess inherent entrepreneurial skills, audience-building experience, and the ability to create compelling visual narratives that are essential for entertainment-focused tech products.

## Key Ideas

- Artists are often already entrepreneurs, managing their own businesses through platforms like Patreon, Twitch, and Kickstarter.
- For entertainment-based startups, having a strong creative vision is as critical as technical execution.
- Illustrators can help startups test market interest early by producing high-quality visual assets and trailers before full product development.
- Recruiting artists from 'scrappy' backgrounds (social media, independent work) is often more effective than hiring from large, established AAA studios.
- Artists provide immediate access to wider networks of creative talent.

## Notable Claims

- Artists are inherently entrepreneurial. Evidence: Many illustrators already manage their own businesses, handle marketing, and generate revenue through platforms like Patreon and Kickstarter.
- High-quality visual assets can secure investor interest. Evidence: Investors were willing to commit to a seed round based on the quality of the company's trailer rather than just the business pitch.
