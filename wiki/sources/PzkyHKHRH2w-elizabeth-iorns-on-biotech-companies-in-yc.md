# Elizabeth Iorns on Biotech Companies in YC

## Summary

Elizabeth Iorns, founder of Science Exchange and Y Combinator expert partner, discusses the role of YC in supporting biotech startups. She explains how the program helps scientists de-risk their technology, navigate the path from academia to industry, and secure funding, while debunking myths about the necessity of business co-founders for scientific founders.

## Key Ideas

- Biotech founders often lack exposure to entrepreneurship and harbor misconceptions about venture capital, such as believing it must be repaid.
- Y Combinator provides biotech companies with focus, market strategy, and access to capital, helping them reach proof-of-concept stages.
- De-risking technology is the primary goal for early-stage biotech startups, rather than the market-focused growth strategies seen in software.
- Intellectual property (IP) is the cornerstone of biotech; scientists must navigate university tech transfer offices to license their discoveries.
- There is a significant gap in 'translational research' funding between academic discovery and clinical application.
- CRISPR is a game-changing technology for high-throughput screening and gene editing, though regulatory hurdles remain.
- Longevity research currently lacks robust clinical endpoints, making it difficult to distinguish between legitimate science and hype.
