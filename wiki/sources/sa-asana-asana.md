## Summary

Sam Altman explains his investment in Asana, highlighting its role in improving organizational productivity through clear task management, goal tracking, and measurement.

## Key Ideas

- Success in companies relies on clear tasks, clear communication, and frequent measurement.
- Most companies struggle with task visibility, leading to poor collaboration and decision-making.
- Product love is a critical indicator of a company's potential for long-term success.
- Asana has the potential to significantly increase global productivity by digitizing work tracking.
- Strong team culture and mission alignment are essential components of a valuable company.

## Entities

- [[entities/asana|Asana]] (company): A work management platform designed to help teams organize, track, and manage their work.
- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Investor and former President of Y Combinator.

## Topics

- [[topics/organizational-productivity|Organizational Productivity]]: The impact of clear goal setting and task measurement on company performance.
- [[topics/startup-investment-criteria|Startup Investment Criteria]]: The factors investors look for, including product love, team culture, and market potential.

## Notable Claims

- Most companies fail to implement clear tasks, communication, and measurement. Evidence: Altman's observation from talking to startup employees who lack a clear sense of company priorities.
- Asana's recurring revenue is highly sticky and doubles annually. Evidence: Cited as a metric of 'product love' and business health.

## Quotes

> You make what you measure.
> Better work tracking leads to better collaboration and better decision-making.
> Asana is the kind of lever that could someday massively increase the productivity of hundreds of millions of people around the world.
