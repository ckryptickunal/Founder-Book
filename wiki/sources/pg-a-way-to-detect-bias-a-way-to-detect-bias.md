# A Way to Detect Bias

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-a-way-to-detect-bias`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: October 2015
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-a-way-to-detect-bias

## Summary

Paul Graham outlines a methodology for detecting bias in selection processes by analyzing the performance of successful applicants, arguing that if a group faces higher barriers to entry, those who succeed will statistically outperform their peers.

## Key Ideas

- Bias in selection processes can be detected by comparing the performance of successful candidates from different groups.
- If a selection process is biased against a specific group, the successful members of that group must be of higher quality than their counterparts to overcome the bias.
- This method requires a random sample of selected applicants, measurable performance data, and an assumption of equal ability distribution across groups.
- Publicly available data on portfolio companies and performance metrics allows third parties to audit organizations for bias without needing access to internal applicant pools.

- [[topics/venture-capital|Venture Capital]]: The industry used as the primary example for applying the bias detection technique.

## Notable Claims

- Bias against a group in a selection process manifests as higher performance among the successful members of that group. Evidence: Logic dictates that if the barrier to entry is higher for a specific group, only the most capable individuals from that group will pass, resulting in higher average performance.
- First Round Capital's study inadvertently revealed their own selection bias. Evidence: The firm's data showed that startups with female founders outperformed those without by 63%.

## Quotes
