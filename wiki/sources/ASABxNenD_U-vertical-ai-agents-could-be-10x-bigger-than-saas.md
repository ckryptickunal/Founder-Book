# Vertical AI Agents Could Be 10X Bigger Than SaaS

## Metadata

- Video ID: `ASABxNenD_U`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-11-22T15:00:20Z

## Summary

The Y Combinator team discusses the emergence of vertical AI agents, arguing they represent a new computing paradigm that could be 10 times larger than the SaaS industry. By automating entire business functions rather than just providing software tools, these agents can replace human teams, significantly reducing operational costs and headcount.

## Key Ideas

- Vertical AI agents will likely create $300 billion+ companies by automating entire business functions.
- The shift to AI agents is analogous to the SaaS boom enabled by XML HTTP requests (AJAX) in 2004.
- Unlike general-purpose AI, vertical AI agents succeed by solving deep, domain-specific problems.
- The most successful AI startups are replacing human teams (e.g., QA, recruiting, customer support) rather than just making them more efficient.
- Founders should look for 'boring, repetitive, administrative' tasks within industries they have personal access to.
- The 'Coase's theory of the firm' suggests that AI will allow companies to remain smaller while achieving massive scale, potentially extending the 'Dunbar number' for organizational management.

## Notable Claims

- Vertical AI agents will be 10 times bigger than the SaaS industry. Evidence: SaaS only improved software efficiency, whereas AI agents replace the human labor cost, which is a much larger portion of enterprise spending.
- Incumbents rarely win in non-obvious, risky categories. Evidence: Google and Facebook dominated obvious consumer products, but startups won in categories like Uber and Airbnb because they were risky and non-obvious.
- AI agents can replace entire teams, not just assist them. Evidence: Companies like Metic and Salient are successfully replacing QA and debt collection teams respectively, rather than just providing tools for them.
