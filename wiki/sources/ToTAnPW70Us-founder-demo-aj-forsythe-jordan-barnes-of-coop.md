## Summary

Coop is a technology company aiming to build a decentralized food production network by integrating AI-powered smart home technology into backyard agriculture. Founded by AJ Forsythe and Jordan Barnes, the company provides smart chicken coops equipped with cameras and AI to monitor flock health, security, and egg production, with plans to expand into other produce and livestock verticals.

## Key Ideas

- Decentralizing food production by enabling backyard agriculture through smart technology.
- Using AI-driven computer vision to monitor animal health, security, and resource management (e.g., water levels).
- Disrupting the industrial 'Big Poultry' supply chain by providing a more nutritious, hyper-local alternative.
- Transitioning from a consumer product company to a platform for hyper-local food distribution.
- Long-term vision includes scaling food production systems for future space colonies.

## Notable Claims

- Backyard eggs on a non-monoculture diet are 200% more nutritious than industrial eggs. Evidence: Cited by the founders as a comparative nutritional advantage over industrial poultry.
- The average egg in a grocery store is 45 to 60 days old and travels 1,000 miles. Evidence: Presented as a critique of the current industrial food supply chain efficiency.
- The AI model can distinguish between specific individual hens and detect health issues like lack of water. Evidence: Demonstrated through the company's proprietary 'Esteein AI' software.
