# Don't get caught up in the current fads and "doing what's cool."

## Metadata

- Video ID: `v8Y5xzalOVk`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-06-05T17:18:14Z

## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator warning founders against chasing market trends and 'fads' that have already reached peak popularity, using the post-COVID remote startup boom as a cautionary example.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should build for long-term value rather than reacting to immediate, temporary market shifts.
- Success often comes from being positioned in a space before it becomes a popular trend.
- Entering a market at the peak of its 'coolness' is usually a sign that the opportunity has already passed.
- Successful companies like Zoom succeeded because they spent years building infrastructure before the market demand spiked.

## Notable Claims

- Founders who start companies in response to a fad are usually too late to capture the market. Evidence: The speaker notes that by the time a trend is 'cool,' the opportunity has often already peaked.
- Zoom's success during COVID-19 was the result of years of prior development, not a reactive pivot. Evidence: The speaker highlights that Zoom had a significant head start in technology and infrastructure before the pandemic began.

## Quotes

> You want to get in before everyone thinks it's cool not at the peak of everyone thinking it's cool.
> The peak of everyone thinking it's cool is usually right before everything is the end.
