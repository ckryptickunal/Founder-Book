## Summary

Blake Robbins, a 26-year-old venture capitalist at Ludlow Ventures, discusses his career trajectory from a college student cold-emailing startups to helping build the major e-sports brand 100 Thieves. The conversation explores the power of 'engineering serendipity,' the evolving relationship between creators and venture capital, and the strategic value of influencer-led distribution in building consumer brands.

## Key Ideas

- Persistence and thoughtfulness in cold outreach are key to breaking into competitive industries like venture capital.
- Investing time and effort into building a resume through internships at high-growth companies can be as valuable as financial capital.
- The 'creator economy' offers a unique distribution advantage for consumer brands, allowing them to bypass traditional, expensive ad-spend models.
- True alignment between creators and startups often requires 'skin in the game,' such as equity-for-distribution deals or pooling personal brands into the business.
- The role of a VC is increasingly about connecting smart people and helping founders navigate the intersection of tech and content.

## Notable Claims

- Influencer-led distribution is the most repeatable strategy for building consumer brands in 2020. Evidence: Traditional consumer brand building is hindered by high customer acquisition costs on platforms like Facebook and Google.
- Equity-for-distribution deals can be more synergistic than cash-for-impression deals. Evidence: Aligns the creator's long-term incentives with the company's success, similar to early employees.
