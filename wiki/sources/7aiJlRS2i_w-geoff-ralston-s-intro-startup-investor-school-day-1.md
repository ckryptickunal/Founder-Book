## Summary

Geoff Ralston introduces the inaugural 'Startup Investor School' by Y Combinator, a MOOC designed to educate both new and experienced angel and seed investors on the fundamentals of startup investing, the ecosystem's mechanics, and the future of venture capital.

## Key Ideas

- Angel and seed investors are critical to the startup ecosystem as they provide the initial capital that allows companies to scale.
- The course aims to create a permanent, accessible repository of knowledge for future startup investors.
- Startup investing offers a unique window into future technological and societal trends.
- The curriculum covers the 'why, how, and which' of investing, mechanics, founder interactions, and future industry trends.
- Y Combinator views the course as a way to improve the quality of the investor pool, which ultimately benefits YC companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/angel-investing|Angel Investing]]: The practice of providing initial capital to startups in exchange for equity.
- [[topics/startup-ecosystem|Startup Ecosystem]]: The interconnected network of founders, investors, and resources that drive innovation.
- [[topics/venture-capital-history|Venture Capital History]]: The evolution of startup funding from early firms like ARDC to modern practices.

## Notable Claims

- Seed investing is a critical component for companies to take flight and scale. Evidence: Historical success of early investments in companies like Digital Equipment Corporation.
- Startup investing has changed radically over the last decade and will continue to evolve. Evidence: Observation of industry trends and the shift in investment mechanics.
