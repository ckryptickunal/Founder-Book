# Founder Control

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-founder-control`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: December 2010
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-founder-control

## Summary

Paul Graham discusses the shifting power dynamics in startup board structures, arguing that founders retaining board control after a Series A round is becoming increasingly common and should be the new industry norm.

## Key Ideas

- Founders retaining board control after Series A is no longer an anomaly but a growing trend.
- VCs often discourage founders from seeking board control by framing it as unreasonable or inexperienced behavior.
- Board control is not absolute power but provides significant influence in decision-making processes.
- The shift toward founder control will likely accelerate as top-tier startups demand it, forcing VCs to adapt.
- VCs benefit from backing founders they trust rather than relying on the ability to fire them through board control.

## Notable Claims

- Founders retaining board control after a Series A round is becoming the new norm. Evidence: Surveys of Y Combinator-funded companies showed a dozen instances where founders maintained a majority of board seats.
- VCs often resist founder control to avoid appearing weak to their own partners. Evidence: VCs are motivated by the ability to brag about favorable terms in their term sheets.
