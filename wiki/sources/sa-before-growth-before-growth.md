# Before Growth

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sa-before-growth`
- Channel: Sam Altman Essays
- Published: 2016-01-15 18:23:06
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sa-before-growth

## Summary

Sam Altman argues that Silicon Valley's obsession with premature growth often leads to failure, advising founders to prioritize product-market fit and user love before scaling.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should prioritize building a product users love over hitting arbitrary weekly growth targets.
- Premature growth often masks a lack of product-market fit through 'growth hacking'.
- The ultimate test for a startup is whether users spontaneously recommend the product to others.
- A strong sense of mission and a potential monopoly are critical components that should be identified early.
- Investors will eventually discover a lack of product-market fit through poor retention metrics.

## Entities

- [[entities/sam-altman|Sam Altman]] (person): Author of the essay and former president of Y Combinator.
- [[entities/silicon-valley|Silicon Valley]] (other): The global hub for technology startups and venture capital.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The stage where a product satisfies a strong market demand and users love it enough to recommend it.
- [[topics/growth-hacking|Growth Hacking]]: Techniques used to artificially inflate growth metrics without a solid underlying product foundation.

## Notable Claims

- Prematurely targeting growth leads to nebulous products that fail when retention numbers are scrutinized. Evidence: Investors eventually dig into retention numbers, revealing the lack of genuine user love.
- The best technology companies establish a monopoly early in their lifecycle. Evidence: Altman notes that successful companies usually figure out their path to a monopoly alongside their product-market fit.

## Quotes

> The right initial metric is 'do any users love our product so much they spontaneously tell other people to use it?'
> If you don’t figure it out, no amount of growth hacking will make you into a great company.
