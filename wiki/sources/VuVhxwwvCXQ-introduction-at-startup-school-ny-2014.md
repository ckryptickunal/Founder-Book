## Summary

Alexis Ohanian delivers the opening remarks at the first Y Combinator Startup School held in New York City in 2014, emphasizing the growth of the startup ecosystem, the importance of peer networking, and the accessibility of entrepreneurship in the internet age.

## Key Ideas

- Startup School provides a platform for founders to learn from peers and experienced practitioners.
- The barrier to entry for entrepreneurship has significantly lowered; one only needs a laptop to start a company.
- Networking with fellow attendees is as valuable as the scheduled stage presentations.
- The New York City startup scene is growing, with many YC alumni returning to the city after their time in Silicon Valley.
- Entrepreneurship among students and young people has surged compared to a decade prior.

## Notable Claims

- In the internet age, you don't need to open a factory to change the world, you just need to open a laptop. Evidence: The speaker contrasts the low capital requirements of modern software-based startups with traditional industrial business models.
- There are 28 Y Combinator companies based in New York City as of 2014. Evidence: Stated by the speaker as a testament to the growth of the NYC tech ecosystem.
