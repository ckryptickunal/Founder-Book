# Find a Pain Point Worth Solving

## Metadata

- Video ID: `VtDBvmxiXnA`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-11-24T20:08:29Z

## Summary

A brief guide from Y Combinator on identifying high-value business opportunities by focusing on acute, painful problems that can be solved with software and AI.

## Key Ideas

- High-value business opportunities often exist in plain sight as unsolved pain points.
- Effective startup ideas should focus on specific individuals experiencing significant, non-trivial pain.
- The severity of the pain is a key indicator of a potential billion-dollar market opportunity.
- AI and software are primary tools for alleviating professional or operational pain.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-ideation|Startup Ideation]]: The process of identifying market gaps and problems worth solving to build a scalable business.
- [[topics/pain-point-analysis|Pain Point Analysis]]: Evaluating the severity of a problem to determine if it warrants a software-based solution.

## Notable Claims

- Solving acute pain points can lead to businesses with market caps in the billions. Evidence: The speaker asserts that such opportunities are lying in plain sight and can be addressed with software and AI.
- A valid startup idea must address a problem that is 'pretty painful' rather than just a 'nice to have'. Evidence: The speaker distinguishes between minor inconveniences and career-threatening issues like the risk of being fired.

## Quotes

> You can go almost anywhere and find some pain points, some problem that could be solved with software and especially with AI that frankly just isn't being solved.
> You should start with do I have a specific person who has some sort of pain point and it's pretty painful.
