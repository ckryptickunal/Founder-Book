# Why Design Matters: Lessons from Stripe, Lyft and Airbnb

## Summary

Katie Dill, Head of Design at Stripe, discusses the critical role of design in building successful startups, emphasizing that high-quality design is a foundational element of trust, functionality, and long-term business success rather than just a cosmetic layer.

## Key Ideas

- Design is instrumental in building user trust, especially for products involving sensitive actions like financial transactions or sharing personal spaces.
- The 'gravitational pull' of any organization is toward mediocrity; maintaining high design standards requires constant, intentional effort and the courage to delay shipping to get details right.
- Design should be treated as a core competency, not a service-based department; designers should be embedded within product teams to maintain context and shared goals.
- Founders play a vital role in setting a culture of design excellence by sweating the details and prioritizing user needs over short-term metrics.
- A 'walk the store' approach—where employees regularly use their own products and log friction—is an effective way to maintain product quality and identify bugs.
- Design quality can be evaluated using a hierarchy: functionality, usability, and finally, craft/beauty.

## Notable Claims

- Design quality directly impacts business conversion rates. Evidence: Redesigning a single email with better hierarchy and clearer calls to action increased conversion by 20%.
- Design is essential for building trust in B2B and high-stakes consumer products. Evidence: Users subconsciously equate poor UI/UX details with a lack of reliability in handling money or personal safety.
- Taste can be honed through observation and practice. Evidence: Founders can improve their design sense by analyzing products they like/dislike and hiring people who exemplify high standards.
