# Over-complicated pricing could kill a sales process.

## Metadata

## Summary

A brief advisory from Y Combinator emphasizing the importance of simple pricing models and the preference for recurring revenue over usage-based models to ensure financial stability.

## Key Ideas

- Over-complicated pricing structures negatively impact sales processes.
- Monthly or Annual Recurring Revenue (MRR/ARR) provides better protection during economic downturns compared to usage-based pricing.
- Usage-based revenue carries the risk of sudden, significant drops in income.
- Investors prefer the predictability of recurring revenue models.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and guidance to startups.

## Topics

- [[topics/pricing-strategy|Pricing Strategy]]: The methodology of setting prices to maximize sales and ensure business sustainability.
- [[topics/recurring-revenue|Recurring Revenue]]: A business model based on predictable, repeated payments from customers over time.

## Notable Claims

- Over-complicating pricing kills the sales process. Evidence: General business observation regarding friction in customer acquisition.
- Recurring revenue is safer than usage-based revenue during economic downturns. Evidence: Contracts protect revenue until renewal, whereas usage-based revenue can drop immediately if customer activity declines.

## Quotes

> Over complicating pricing will kill a sales process.
> If it's pure usage based Revenue there's a real risk that your Revenue just falls off a cliff in a in a bad month.
