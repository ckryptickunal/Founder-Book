# Parahelp: The End-to-End AI Support Agent

## Summary

Parahelp, an agentic AI company founded by Anker and Mass from Denmark, recently secured a Series A funding round. Their journey involved significant pivots, starting from an NFT investment app before evolving into a sophisticated AI support agent for customer service. Parahelp specializes in providing end-to-end resolution for support tickets, particularly for fast-moving software companies, including prominent AI-native firms like Perplexity, Framer, and 11Labs. Their core innovation lies in their AI agents' ability to not only respond to queries but also take direct actions, such as processing refunds through integrations with billing systems like Stripe, often with human oversight for sensitive tasks. A key development is their 'Parahelp Assistant,' an AI-powered 'forward-deployed engineer' that automates the generation and testing of evaluation sets, enabling rapid customer onboarding and continuous optimization of the AI agent's policies. This multi-agent architecture, leveraging advanced LLMs like Opus 4, allows for real-time quality assurance, proactive policy improvements, and even revenue-generating activities like upselling and churn prevention. The founders emphasize a product-first philosophy, a willingness to pivot, and a culture of embracing failure and playing offense, contrasting with traditional Danish entrepreneurial norms.

## Key Ideas

- Parahelp's origin story involves multiple pivots, from an NFT investment app to a leading agentic AI customer support solution.
- Parahelp's core offering is an agentic AI system that provides end-to-end resolution for customer support tickets, including taking direct actions like processing refunds.
- The company strategically focuses on fast-moving software companies, especially AI-native ones, due to their unique knowledge management and integration needs.
- The 'Parahelp Assistant' functions as an AI-powered 'forward-deployed engineer,' automating the creation and testing of evaluation sets and policy optimization, significantly scaling customer onboarding and maintenance.
- Parahelp employs a sophisticated multi-agent architecture (Ask, Configure, Test, Deep Research modes) for continuous improvement and adaptation of its AI agents.
- The system creates a 'flywheel' effect where a research agent identifies patterns in human resolutions, which are then configured, tested, and published as new AI policies.
- Parahelp's value proposition extends beyond cost reduction to include revenue generation and churn prevention by identifying upsell opportunities and proactively engaging customers.
- The Parahelp platform often goes 'viral' within customer organizations, becoming a central knowledge hub for various departments beyond just customer support.
- The founders advocate for a culture of embracing pivots, playing offense, and learning from failure, drawing inspiration from Silicon Valley's entrepreneurial spirit.
- Parahelp's agents can handle sensitive actions like processing refunds, often with a Slack-based human approval flow for critical transactions, demonstrating high reliability.

- Startups are not really a thing in Denmark; there isn't even a Danish word for 'startup'. Evidence: Stated by Anker, noting that 'people just say how's your project going?'
- Parahelp was the first company to partner with Stripe in Europe for their NFT investment app. Evidence: Stated by Anker, 'after many attempts of convincing them how our system worked.'
- The NFT market went from a few million users to a few thousand. Evidence: Stated by Anker, based on their experience after launching their NFT app.
- Sonnet 3.5 enabled semi-reliable agentic change if sampled. Evidence: Anker's thesis during their mid-batch pivot, which led to significant improvements in resolution rates.
- Parahelp's initial customers, including Captions AI and Perplexity, were acquired via personalized cold emails. Evidence: Mass sent 700 cold emails, as they had few connections in Silicon Valley.
- Parahelp can build a 10x better solution by focusing solely on fast-moving software companies. Evidence: Stated by Mass, attributing it to their specific knowledge management problems and integration needs.
- Parahelp's AI agents don't just respond to tickets; they take action to solve problems. Evidence: Example given of processing refunds via Stripe, following complex instructions and company criteria.
- Parahelp has achieved a 99% success rate for certain billing actions for some customers. Evidence: Stated by Anker, highlighting their reliability.
- Parahelp's resolution rate increased from 20% (using simple RAG) to 50-60% (with Sonnet 3.5 tool calls) last year, and is now 'way higher' for certain customers. Evidence: Stated by Anker, detailing the impact of their agentic approach.
- Most AI-native companies find that default AI in ticket systems or custom-built solutions don't scale for knowledge management. Evidence: Stated by Anker, citing lack of deep integrations, maintenance, and built-in review systems.
- Parahelp handles thousands of customer support tickets daily. Evidence: Stated by Anker, indicating the scale of their operations.
- Some Parahelp customers entrust their agents with handling enterprise customers up to a thousand seats, representing six-figure accounts. Evidence: Stated by Anker, demonstrating a high level of trust and capability.
- Opus 4 had enough intelligence on Parahelp's internal benchmarks to generate evaluation sets on the fly. Evidence: Stated by Anker, which enabled them to rebuild their agent architecture and automate a critical engineering task.
- Since launching the new architecture, Parahelp has not written a single custom evaluation set. Evidence: Stated by Anker, as their AI agent now handles this automatically for customers.
- Parahelp's agent can easily work for 30-40 minutes optimizing its own policies and running tests. Evidence: Stated by Anker, illustrating the agent's autonomous capabilities.
- For Perplexity, the in-resolution rate increased by about 12-15 percentage points due to Parahelp's new flywheel system. Evidence: Stated by Anker, noting that Perplexity has made over 250 releases with the system.
- Parahelp can assist customers with customer success management, upselling, and churn prevention. Evidence: Description of the upcoming mass follow-up tool and discount/upsell link tools, leveraging historical customer data.
- Parahelp goes viral inside customer companies, becoming a knowledge brain for various departments. Evidence: Stated by Anker, with hundreds of pages of knowledge living in Parahelp and internal 'ask Parahelp' channels.
- Support for fast-growing software companies is the epicenter of customer and product knowledge. Evidence: Stated by Mass, explaining why Parahelp's insights are valuable across the organization.
- In Denmark, there is a culture of being very afraid of failing, despite having a strong social security net. Evidence: Stated by Anker, contrasting with Silicon Valley's 'just do it' mentality and highlighting a cultural barrier to entrepreneurship.
