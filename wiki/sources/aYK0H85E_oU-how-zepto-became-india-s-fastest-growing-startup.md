# How Zepto Became India’s Fastest Growing Startup

## Metadata

## Summary

Aadit Palicha, co-founder and CEO of Zepto, discusses the company's rapid growth as India's fastest-growing startup. He explains how Zepto disrupted the Indian grocery market by prioritizing a user-backward, 10-minute delivery model and full-stack logistics, contrasting this with competitors who focused on supply-chain-first, slower delivery models. The conversation covers Zepto's evolution from a pandemic-era WhatsApp group to a $5 billion unicorn, the importance of extreme execution, and the company's future as an 'internet supermarket' leveraging AI.

## Key Ideas

- Build user-backward rather than supply-chain-backward to achieve true product-market fit.
- Extreme speed (10-minute delivery) in India is a necessity driven by high-frequency, small-ticket consumption patterns, not just a convenience feature.
- Owning the full-stack logistics and dark store model is essential for controlling quality, selection, and price.
- Existential crises (like capital market downturns) can be powerful forcing functions for efficiency and operating leverage.
- The primary goal of a founder should be the love of building itself, rather than extrinsic rewards like money or fame.
- AI application strategy should focus on core business problems (ads, customer support, supply chain) while waiting for non-core solutions to be commoditized.

## Topics

- [[topics/quick-commerce|Quick Commerce]]: The model of delivering groceries and essentials in under 10 minutes, tailored to Indian consumption habits.
- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The iterative process of finding a model that users genuinely want, moving from subjective feedback to objective retention data.
- [[topics/startup-culture|Startup Culture]]: The importance of execution-focused, high-accountability environments for ambitious talent.
- [[topics/ai-in-e-commerce|AI in E-commerce]]: Applying generative AI and machine learning to core business functions like ad relevance, customer support, and supply chain forecasting.

## Notable Claims

- Zepto's 10-minute delivery model is more economically viable than 2-hour or 4-hour models. Evidence: Higher user stickiness, lower customer acquisition costs (CAC), and higher supply chain throughput.
- Zepto is evolving into an 'internet supermarket' rather than just a delivery service. Evidence: Expansion to 45,000-50,000 products including electronics, cosmetics, and the launch of Zepto Cafe.
- Over 50% of customer support tickets are solved dynamically by generative AI. Evidence: The company trained a generative chatbot on their specific data to handle common issues like delayed orders or damaged goods.
