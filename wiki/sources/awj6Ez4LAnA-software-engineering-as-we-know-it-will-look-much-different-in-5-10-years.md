# Software engineering as we know it will look much different in 5-10 years.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `awj6Ez4LAnA`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2025-05-15T17:33:53Z

## Summary

The speaker argues that software engineering is undergoing a fundamental transformation driven by AI, shifting from manual coding to managing AI-driven systems, similar to historical shifts in programming abstractions.

## Key Ideas

- Traditional software engineering roles will be unrecognizable in 5-10 years.
- The future of the profession lies in 'wrangling' AI coding machines rather than writing raw code.
- AI represents the next logical layer of abstraction in the history of computer programming.
- Historical shifts in programming (machine code to high-level languages) provide a precedent for current AI-driven changes.

## Notable Claims

- Current software engineering jobs will not exist in 5-10 years. Evidence: The speaker cites historical precedents where programming roles evolved significantly due to new abstractions.
- AI is simply another layer of abstraction for human developers. Evidence: Comparison to the transition from machine code and punch cards to modern object-oriented programming.

## Quotes

> I think there will be demand for smart people who know how to wrangle these AI coding machines.
> I think this new wave of AI is just another abstraction that allows the human to be a higher level agent rather than the person actually writing the code.
