# Why now? The key to million dollar startup ideas

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the 'Why Now' framework for evaluating startup viability, arguing that successful ventures typically emerge at the intersection of three major forces: technological breakthroughs, shifts in human behavior, and changes in government regulation.

## Key Ideas

- Startup success is often driven by timing, specifically identifying why a solution is viable now versus in the past.
- Technological progress often follows exponential curves, enabling solutions that were previously impossible or too expensive.
- Mass behavior change, such as smartphone adoption, creates new markets for services like grocery delivery and ride-sharing.
- Government regulation can act as a catalyst for new industries, either by creating mandates or by being navigated strategically.
- The best startup ideas often exist at the intersection of two or more massive, shifting forces.

## Notable Claims

- Orders of magnitude improvement (10x, 100x, 1000x) are the key to breakthrough startups. Evidence: Comparison of the 1994 General Magic device versus the 2007 iPhone.
- Government regulation is a powerful, double-edged sword for startups. Evidence: The success of Uber in DC and the growth of Zenefits/Gusto following the Affordable Care Act.
- Climate change is currently the most significant regulatory driver for new startups. Evidence: The Inflation Reduction Act and the rise of climate-tech companies like Pano.
