# By endurance we conquer

## Metadata

## Summary

Sam Altman reflects on Ernest Shackleton's motto 'By endurance we conquer' as a fundamental principle for startup success, emphasizing that persistence through adversity is often the deciding factor in a company's survival.

## Key Ideas

- Endurance is as critical to startup success as team, execution, and idea.
- Startups rarely fail due to competition; they usually fail due to internal issues or lack of product-market fit.
- The final 5% of effort at critical moments often determines the difference between success and failure.
- Persistence is required even when external feedback and internal circumstances are negative.

- [[entities/ernest-shackleton|Ernest Shackleton]] (person): Polar explorer known for his leadership and the Endurance expedition.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-resilience|Startup Resilience]]: The psychological and operational capacity to continue building a company despite significant setbacks and doubt.
- [[topics/competitive-advantage|Competitive Advantage]]: The role of sustained effort in outlasting competitors during critical business junctures.

## Notable Claims

- Most startups fail due to internal factors rather than external competition. Evidence: Altman cites internal implosion, founder burnout, and failure to build what people want as primary causes of death.
- The final 5% of effort is often the deciding factor in winning deals. Evidence: Altman suggests that pushing through exhaustion at critical moments allows startups to beat competitors.

## Quotes

> By endurance we conquer.
> It's very tough to keep going when everyone tells you your idea sucks and it will never work.
> Most startups don't die at the hands of a competitor. It's more often something like an internal implosion, the founders giving up, or not building something people want.
