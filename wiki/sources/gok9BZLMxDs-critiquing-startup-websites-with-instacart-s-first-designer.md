# Critiquing Startup Websites With Instacart's First Designer

## Summary

In this episode of Y Combinator's 'Design Review,' host Aaron Harris and guest Zayn Ali (first product designer at Instacart) critique the landing pages of three YC-funded startups: Hokali, Requestly, and Verdin. They provide actionable feedback on UI/UX, messaging clarity, the use of social proof, and the importance of focusing on user-centric benefits rather than just technical features.

## Key Ideas

- Avoid intrusive pop-ups that block the user's ability to understand the product value proposition.
- Headlines should clearly answer 'What is this?' and 'Is this for me?' immediately.
- Use customer language in headlines rather than internal jargon or vague marketing speak.
- Landing pages should focus on driving a single, primary call-to-action (CTA) rather than overwhelming users with too many choices.
- Screenshots should be high-quality and provide context; avoid small, cluttered, or confusing diagrams.
- Build trust by highlighting real user testimonials, founder stories, and social proof early in the page.
- Create dedicated landing pages for specific customer segments to improve SEO and conversion rates.

## Notable Claims

- Pop-ups that appear immediately upon landing often cause user fatigue and may lower conversion. Evidence: Zayn Ali notes that while they are a common growth hack, users often close them immediately without reading, and they obscure the core value proposition.
- Dedicated landing pages for specific services improve conversion rates. Evidence: Aaron Harris explains that sending users to a page tailored to their specific interest (e.g., surf lessons) rather than a generic homepage increases relevance and trust.
- Technical jargon in headlines can be beneficial if it effectively self-selects the target audience. Evidence: The hosts note that for developer tools like Requestly, specific terminology helps the right users identify the product's utility quickly.
