# Ro's solution to fixing the broken US healthcare system with Co-Founder Rob Schutz

## Summary

Rob Schutz, co-founder and Chief Growth Officer of Ro, discusses the company's evolution from a men's health startup into a $7 billion vertically integrated direct-to-patient healthcare platform. The conversation covers the importance of complementary co-founders, the 'jobs to be done' marketing framework, and the strategic shift toward building proprietary infrastructure to reduce healthcare costs and improve patient access.

## Key Ideas

- Healthcare is a massive, inefficient sector where direct-to-patient models can drive down costs by removing middlemen.
- Complementary co-founder skill sets are essential for early-stage success to avoid over-indexing on one area.
- The 'jobs to be done' framework focuses on marketing to specific patient anxieties rather than selling a generic 'everything' platform.
- Vertical integration (pharmacy, labs, in-home care) allows for a superior, data-driven patient experience.
- Growth strategy involves 'paying for education' by testing channels aggressively to determine if they are viable, then doubling down on winners.
- Land and expand: Start with a narrow, high-demand service and expand into holistic care as the patient relationship deepens.

- Ro can deliver 98% of patient orders via ground shipping, with 40% arriving overnight. Evidence: Internal logistics data provided by Rob Schutz.
