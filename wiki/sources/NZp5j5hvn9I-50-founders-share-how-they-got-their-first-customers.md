# 50 Founders Share How They Got Their First Customers

## Summary

A compilation of Y Combinator founders sharing diverse, practical strategies for acquiring their first customers, ranging from cold outreach and networking to leveraging social media and community platforms.

## Key Ideas

- Cold outreach (email, LinkedIn, door-to-door) remains a primary method for early traction.
- Leveraging personal networks, friends, and family is a common starting point for initial testing and feedback.
- Building in public on platforms like Reddit and Twitter can generate significant waitlists and early interest.
- Converting job inquiries into sales leads is a creative B2B strategy.
- Customer discovery calls are essential to validate pain points before building full-scale products.
- Speed is critical; some founders sold non-coded solutions or rough prototypes within days.

## Notable Claims

- Personal networks are the most common source for the first few customers. Evidence: Multiple founders cited friends, family, and former professional contacts as their initial customer base.
- Building a waitlist via social media can validate demand before a product is finished. Evidence: One founder reported gaining 1,000 signups in 24 hours by posting a mock-up on Reddit.
