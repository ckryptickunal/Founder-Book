## Summary

Founders Alex and Teddy discuss the growth and evolution of Magic Patterns, an AI-powered design tool that enables product managers and designers to rapidly create interactive prototypes through natural language prompting. The company recently raised a $6 million Series A from Standard Capital and emphasizes a 'customer-obsessed' approach, having pivoted multiple times from their origins as a text-message analytics app to their current focus on visual communication and rapid prototyping.

## Key Ideas

- Customer obsession and direct feedback are the primary drivers for product-market fit.
- Magic Patterns focuses on visual communication and prototyping rather than production-ready backend code.
- The tool allows users to iterate on existing brand components and designs rather than just starting from scratch.
- The product grew to $1 million ARR with only two employees by staying lean and solving a specific, high-value problem.
- Founders often need to pivot based on who is actually using the product versus who they initially targeted.
- Chaining AI tools (e.g., using ChatGPT to refine prompts for Magic Patterns) is a common workflow for power users.
