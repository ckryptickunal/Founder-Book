# Paul Graham: What are some common mistakes founders make?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `845O4RE1XDM`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2019-07-16T18:55:42Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=845O4RE1XDM

## Summary

Paul Graham outlines the primary mistake startup founders make: avoiding direct interaction with users in favor of building based on internal 'vision' and delaying product launches due to fear of negative feedback.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often prioritize their internal 'vision' over actual user needs.
- Avoiding sales and user feedback is a common but fatal mistake.
- The most effective way to build is to solve a specific problem for a specific person.
- Shipping unfinished products is essential for improvement, despite the fear of humiliation.
- Direct contact with the real world is the only way to validate and improve a product.

## Entities

- [[entities/paul-graham|Paul Graham]] (person): Co-founder of Y Combinator and venture capitalist.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): Startup accelerator.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-methodology|Startup Methodology]]: The process of building a company by focusing on user feedback rather than abstract vision.
- [[topics/product-development|Product Development]]: The necessity of shipping early and iterating based on real-world criticism.

## Notable Claims

- Founders often avoid talking to users because they find sales difficult and fear rejection. Evidence: Graham notes that founders prefer to sit in cafes thinking about their vision rather than facing the potential 'no' from customers.
- Shipping unfinished products is the only way to improve. Evidence: Graham argues that facing the 'humiliating' feedback from users is the only mechanism for refining a product.

## Quotes

> You will shrink from contact with the real world or contact with your users that's the mistake you will make.
> You'd be way better off finding someone anyone who has a problem that they will pay you to fix and fixing it.
