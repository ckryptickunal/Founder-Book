# The ERP for the AI Revolution is here

## Summary

John Glasgow, CEO and founder of Campfire, discusses his company's mission to provide an AI-native ERP solution for high-growth companies, automating finance and accounting tasks. He details Campfire's journey from a Google Sheets prototype through Y Combinator, challenging established incumbents like NetSuite by focusing on specific features for tech companies outgrowing QuickBooks. Glasgow emphasizes the importance of rapid product development, founder-led sales, and AI integration as key differentiators, aiming to build Campfire into a massive, durable public company.

## Key Ideas

- Campfire is an AI-native ERP designed for high-growth companies, automating finance and accounting functions.
- The established ERP market, dominated by legacy systems, was ripe for disruption due to outdated technology and unmet needs.
- Founder-market fit, stemming from John Glasgow's decade-long corporate finance career, provided crucial credibility and conviction.
- Rapid product iteration, exemplified by starting with a Google Sheets prototype, was essential for quickly acquiring paying customers and iterating based on feedback (following the YC mantra of 'build something people want').
- Campfire successfully targeted tech companies outgrowing QuickBooks by focusing on critical features like approval workflows and multi-entity accounting, which legacy systems often underutilized or lacked.
- Despite being a seed-stage startup, Campfire displaced NetSuite customers by offering a superior user interface, better APIs, and a commitment to continuous, fast product shipping.
- Founder-led sales are vital for early-stage companies to achieve product-market fit and effectively drive product development.
- The 'why now' for a new ERP was driven by the modernization of other finance tech stacks (e.g., payroll, spend management) and the emergence of AI, which made non-AI native incumbents seem less 'safe'.
- Campfire's competitive advantages include high product velocity, the ability to meet the rigorous reporting and audit requirements for public companies, and superior AI capabilities powered by its custom agent platform.
- The long-term ambition for Campfire is to become a massive, durable public company.
- Advice for founders includes enjoying the early journey, staying intimately close to customers, and personally owning the sales process until product-market fit is achieved.
