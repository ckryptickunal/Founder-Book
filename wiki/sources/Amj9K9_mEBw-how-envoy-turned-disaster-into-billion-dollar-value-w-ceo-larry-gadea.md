## Summary

Envoy CEO Larry Gadea discusses how the company pivoted its workplace management software during the COVID-19 pandemic, evolving from a visitor management tool to a comprehensive hybrid work platform, and shares insights on the importance of organizational adaptability and in-person collaboration.

## Key Ideas

- The pandemic forced a 'pivot or die' ultimatum, leading Envoy to build safety and hybrid work tools.
- Pain is a catalyst for business innovation; companies should chase problems others avoid.
- The 'Stockdale Paradox'—being realistic about current crises while maintaining unwavering hope—is essential for founders.
- In-person collaboration is a competitive advantage that drives innovation through spontaneous interaction.
- Hybrid work requires intentional organization (desk booking, meeting room management) to avoid employee frustration.
- Managers must be highly skilled to coordinate distributed teams and maintain culture.

## Topics

- [[topics/crisis-management|Crisis Management]]: How startups navigate existential threats by listening to customer needs and pivoting product roadmaps.
- [[topics/organizational-culture|Organizational Culture]]: The role of in-person interaction in fostering innovation, social bonding, and effective onboarding.

## Notable Claims

- In-person collaboration is essential for long-term innovation. Evidence: Gadea notes that spontaneous interactions at lunch tables lead to new ideas that are harder to replicate in fully remote environments.
- Hybrid work fails when it is unorganized. Evidence: Employees become discouraged if they commute to an office only to find it empty or lacking necessary resources like desks and meeting rooms.
