## Summary

In this Y Combinator design review, Glide CEO David Siegel evaluates four startup mobile apps—Pearls, Blue Dot, Duffel, and Eden Care—providing actionable feedback on UI/UX, navigation, accessibility, and platform-specific design standards.

## Key Ideas

- Prioritize platform standards and user context (environment and physical movement) when designing mobile apps.
- Avoid 'ugly software' by focusing on legibility, contrast, and thumb-friendly touch targets (minimum 60px).
- Get apps into the hands of real users as quickly as possible to identify friction points and 'aha' moments.
- Distinguish between a web app and a native app; if an app is purely content-heavy without unique mobile interactions, a website may be more appropriate.
- Avoid overwhelming users with excessive onboarding questions or gamification before they complete their first core task.
- Use animation to help users follow content changes rather than relying on hard cuts.

## Notable Claims

- Writing is a fundamental part of design. Evidence: The name of the app and the words used within the UI significantly impact user perception and usability.
- Native apps should leverage mobile-specific capabilities. Evidence: Apps that fail to use location services or native navigation feel like 'websites masquerading as apps'.
- Gamification can be a distraction if implemented too early. Evidence: Users should complete a successful transaction or core task before being pushed into rewards programs.
