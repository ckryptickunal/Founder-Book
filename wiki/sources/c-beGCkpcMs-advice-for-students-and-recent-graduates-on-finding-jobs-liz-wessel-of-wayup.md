## Summary

Liz Wessel, co-founder of WayUp, discusses her journey from a student entrepreneur at Penn to building a successful HR tech platform. She shares actionable advice for students and recent graduates on cold emailing, securing internships, choosing career paths, and the importance of finding work that aligns with personal passions.

## Key Ideas

- Treat career selection with the same level of scrutiny and importance as choosing a life partner.
- Use the 'student' status as a 'badge of pride' to get advice and networking opportunities while still in college.
- The 'REEF' model for interviews: Research, Enthusiasm, Ask questions, Follow-up.
- Cold emailing is highly effective if you do deep research, keep it short, and provide a clear reason for reaching out.
- Early career choices should prioritize learning and working with great people over immediate compensation.
- Scaling a company requires learning how to hire, manage, and delegate to people with diverse skill sets and personalities.

## Notable Claims

- People quit their managers before they quit their companies. Evidence: Anecdotal observation from Wessel's experience managing teams at WayUp.
- One in three people who apply on WayUp get hired. Evidence: Internal company metric cited by Wessel.
- Cold emailing is more effective if you research the recipient's personal interests. Evidence: Wessel's success in getting responses by mentioning shared experiences like water aerobics.
