# There's an Art to Getting Brilliant People to Surprise Themselves - Kevin Slavin of The Shed

## Summary

Kevin Slavin, a creative technologist and former MIT Media Lab faculty member, discusses his career trajectory, the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration, and his current role at The Shed. He emphasizes the value of 'drawing out' the best work from brilliant people, the necessity of cultural institutions to provide 'synchronizing signals' in a fragmented world, and his approach to building institutions that embrace risk and technological integration.

## Key Ideas

- The role of institutions is to provide a sense of continuity and connection between individuals and a broader set of ideas.
- The most effective way to lead brilliant people is to create an environment where they can surprise themselves.
- Cultural institutions should act as 'beacons' that provide live, synchronized experiences for global audiences rather than just local archives.
- True innovation often comes from being 'orthogonal' to existing disciplines and connecting experts from disparate fields.
- The 'art' of management involves valuing others' expertise and acting as a bridge between creative ideas and the practicalities of funding and scale.
- The future of cultural institutions lies in eliminating legacy infrastructure (like paper tickets) and leveraging high bandwidth for digital, live-streamed experiences.

## Notable Claims

- Higher education institutions are struggling because their traditional models are becoming obsolete. Evidence: Cites the high rate of university bankruptcies and the difficulty of changing institutional sensibilities compared to cities.
- The most valuable role for cultural institutions today is to provide a 'synchronizing signal'. Evidence: Argues that in a fragmented digital world, the shared experience of live, synchronized attention is a powerful, under-exploited commodity.
- The best way to build a successful project is to meet new technologies as they arrive rather than optimizing for a single known path. Evidence: Reflects on the success of Area Code by adapting to new platforms like GPS, iOS, and Facebook as they emerged.

## Quotes

> There's an art to getting brilliant people to surprise themselves.
> I have no illusions that the ideas that I have or the capabilities that I have or the knowledge that I have comes from me. It comes from being connected and embedded with an amazing group of people.
> What is valuable isn't just the performance and the event; what's valuable is that synchronizing signal.
> I think at a certain point I became aware that that's one of the only things that I'm good at [drawing out the best work in others] and so now it's very deliberate.
