## Summary

Jason Kilar, former CEO of Hulu and executive at Amazon and Disney, discusses the critical importance of scaling culture in organizations. He defines culture as how employees act when no one is looking and argues that successful scaling requires being explicit about values, walking the talk, and implementing repeatable mechanisms.

## Key Ideas

- Culture is defined by behavior when no one is looking, which is heavily influenced by the founder's actions.
- Scaling culture requires moving beyond 'good intentions' to implementing repeatable, scalable mechanisms.
- A strong culture acts as both a magnet for the right talent and a repellent for those who are not a good fit.
- Mechanisms (good processes) are essential for maintaining quality and values as a company grows from a small team to thousands of employees.
- Leadership principles should be explicit, challenging, and not just 'motherhood and apple pie' statements.
- Healthy organizations prioritize 'disagree and commit' over social cohesion to foster innovation.

## Topics

- [[topics/scaling-culture|Scaling Culture]]: The process of maintaining core values and behaviors as an organization grows in size.
- [[topics/mechanisms|Mechanisms]]: Repeatable processes that ensure consistent quality and behavior at scale.
- [[topics/leadership-principles|Leadership Principles]]: Explicit, written guidelines that define what a company values and how it operates.
- [[topics/cultural-magnets-and-repellents|Cultural Magnets and Repellents]]: The idea that a strong, specific culture attracts the right people while discouraging those who are not a good fit.

## Notable Claims

- Good intentions are insufficient for scaling culture beyond a small team. Evidence: Kilar notes that while good intentions work for 30 people, they fail at the scale of 10,000+ employees, necessitating formal mechanisms.
- A company's culture is a reflection of the founder's behavior when no one is looking. Evidence: Kilar observes that employees model their behavior after the founder, making the founder's private actions the standard for the company.
- Prioritizing social cohesion over healthy debate hinders innovation. Evidence: Kilar cites Amazon's 'disagree and commit' principle as a key factor in their success, noting that avoiding uncomfortable debate is a common failure point.

## Quotes

> Culture is basically it's how we act when no one is looking.
> When you're a team of 30 good intentions are good enough but when you're a team of a thousand or 10,000... good intentions are a recipe for disaster.
> If a culture is motherhood and apple pie it really isn't effective.
> You want to make sure [your culture] is a repellent as much as they are a magnet.
