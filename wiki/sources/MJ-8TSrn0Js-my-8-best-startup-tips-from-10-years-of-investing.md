# My 8 Best Startup tips from 10+ Years of Investing

## Summary

Garry Tan shares eight essential lessons for startup founders, covering topics from contrarian thinking and financial leverage to design principles, growth strategies, and the importance of retention metrics.

## Key Ideas

- Be contrarian and right to build a company that can scale to a billion people.
- Focus on leverage by building products that generate income while you sleep.
- When running out of gas, reduce speed to increase efficiency rather than accelerating.
- Design is about applying simple principles like empathy, clarity, and minimalism to solve user problems.
- Retention is the most critical metric; if you don't have it, spending on growth is a waste.
- A life authored by you requires self-awareness and the ability to distinguish your desires from the modeling of others.

## Notable Claims

- If you are running out of gas as a startup, you should coast rather than accelerate. Evidence: Reducing speed increases efficiency, providing more time to dial in product-market fit or pivot.
- 95% monthly retention leads to 73% retention after six months. Evidence: Calculated by raising the monthly retention rate (0.95) to the power of the number of months (6).
