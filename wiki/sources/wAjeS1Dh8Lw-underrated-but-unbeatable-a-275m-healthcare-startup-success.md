# Underrated but Unbeatable: A $275M healthcare startup success

## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Surbhi Sarna, a Y Combinator group partner and founder of Envision Medical, about her journey building a medical device startup that was acquired by Boston Scientific for over $275 million. Sarna shares how her personal health struggles with ovarian cysts inspired her to innovate in women's health, specifically focusing on early detection of ovarian cancer and fallopian tube diagnostics.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should orient their life decisions and career path toward the specific problem they intend to solve.
- Creativity in entrepreneurship is often about connecting existing concepts in new ways to solve unmet needs.
- Customer discovery in healthcare requires persistence, including unconventional methods to bypass gatekeepers.
- Scientific breakthroughs require iterative trial and error, often involving unconventional testing methods.
- Entrepreneurship is difficult and requires 'good indignation'—a deep-seated drive to solve a problem that transcends external validation.
- You do not need universal support to succeed; you only need a small group of believers to help move the needle.

- The fallopian tube is a primary site for early ovarian cancer detection, yet it is difficult to access. Evidence: Sarna's device successfully navigated the fallopian tube to capture cells for diagnostic testing.
- Entrepreneurship should not be pursued for its own sake due to the high number of setbacks. Evidence: Sarna highlights the intense emotional and physical toll of fundraising and R&D.
