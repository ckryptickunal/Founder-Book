# After PMF: People, Customers, Sales by Mathilde Collin

## Summary

Mathilde Collin, CEO and co-founder of Front, discusses the journey of building her company from the Y Combinator Summer 2014 batch to a successful enterprise software business. She emphasizes the importance of radical transparency, extreme discipline in focus, and the necessity of deep customer engagement to achieve product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Transparency is not just a moral choice but an efficient management tool to drive employee engagement and alignment.
- Discipline in focus—specifically tracking core metrics like revenue—is more critical than having a grand, fully-formed vision in the early stages.
- Founders should personally handle sales and customer support in the early days to deeply understand user workflows and pain points.
- Hiring should be based on high standards and cultural fit; never compromise on values, even when desperate for talent.
- Product-market fit is often found by solving a specific, painful workflow problem (e.g., shared email inboxes) rather than trying to disrupt a massive category immediately.
- Mental health and personal well-being are paramount; burnout is a risk that can be mitigated through practices like meditation.

## Notable Claims

- Transparency is the most efficient way to create engagement at scale. Evidence: Front shares revenue, churn, board decks, and internal challenges with all employees.
- You should not hire people to figure out things you haven't figured out yourself. Evidence: Mathilde argues that if the founder cannot figure out a function, it is unlikely a new hire will succeed in doing so.
- Discipline is more important than having a grand vision. Evidence: Front's success is attributed to rigorous focus on core targets like revenue growth.
