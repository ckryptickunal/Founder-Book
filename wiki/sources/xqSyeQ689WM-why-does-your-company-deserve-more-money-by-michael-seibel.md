# Why Does Your Company Deserve More Money? by Michael Seibel

## Summary

Michael Seibel discusses the critical importance of achieving product-market fit and revenue generation before seeking additional funding. He argues that founders often confuse 'means' (offices, teams) with 'ends' (customer value) and suggests that cutting burn to reach break-even is often a more effective strategy than raising more capital when a startup lacks traction.

## Key Ideas

- Founders should focus on solving user problems rather than optimizing for investor expectations.
- Having a team, office, or product is a means to an end, not evidence of success; wins and revenue are the only metrics that matter.
- Reaching break-even provides clarity and leverage, often making it easier to raise money later because the company is no longer desperate.
- Investors are more likely to invest when a company has demonstrated sustained growth and revenue, rather than just a 'snazzy' pitch.
- If a startup lacks product-market fit, cutting burn to extend runway is often superior to seeking further dilution.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The state where a product satisfies a strong market demand, which Seibel identifies as the primary prerequisite for further funding.
- [[topics/fundraising-strategy|Fundraising Strategy]]: The process of securing capital, which Seibel argues should be based on leverage (revenue/growth) rather than just a pitch.
- [[topics/burn-rate|Burn Rate]]: The rate at which a company spends its capital; reducing this is presented as a strategic alternative to raising more money.

## Notable Claims

- Reaching break-even is the most effective way to gain leverage and clarity in a startup. Evidence: Seibel cites his personal experience with Justin.tv, noting that the fear of failure vanished and the business strategy became clearer once they were self-sustaining.
- Founders often confuse 'means' with 'ends'. Evidence: Founders often point to having a team or a nice office as reasons they deserve more money, but these are just tools to achieve the actual goal of customer satisfaction and revenue.
