# Talk about doing things that don’t scale. From Doordash’s YC app in 2013.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `5-gDBnRSQwU`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-12-18T20:14:20Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5-gDBnRSQwU

## Summary

The founders of DoorDash describe their early days in 2013, explaining how they identified a market gap in Palo Alto where restaurants wanted to offer delivery but lacked the infrastructure, while drivers had excess capacity.

## Key Ideas

- Identify customer needs through extensive direct interviews before building software.
- Solve problems manually first to understand the operational complexities.
- Founders should perform the core service themselves to gain firsthand experience.
- Utilize existing inefficiencies in the market to create value for both sides of a marketplace.

## Entities

- [[entities/doordash|DoorDash]] (company): A food delivery platform that started as PaloAltoDelivery.com.
- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that hosted this presentation.

## Topics

- [[topics/market-research|Market Research]]: The importance of conducting over 100 interviews to validate a business problem.
- [[topics/operational-logistics|Operational Logistics]]: Using routing and batching algorithms to optimize delivery efficiency.
- [[topics/founding-strategy|Founding Strategy]]: The 'do things that don't scale' approach, where founders act as the initial delivery drivers.

## Notable Claims

- Restaurants in Palo Alto wanted to deliver but could not afford the infrastructure. Evidence: Founders conducted over 100 interviews with local small business owners.
- Founders can gain critical insights by performing the service themselves. Evidence: The four founders started as delivery drivers for their own service.

## Quotes

> We just went out and talked to all the small business owners we could find.
> The four of us actually started off as delivery drivers.
