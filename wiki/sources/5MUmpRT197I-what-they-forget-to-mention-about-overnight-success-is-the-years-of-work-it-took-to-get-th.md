# What they forget to mention about "overnight success" is the years of work it took to get there.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `5MUmpRT197I`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-07-11T17:18:30Z

## Summary

A discussion on the misconception of startup launches, contrasting the 'movie premiere' model of immediate hype with the reality of long-term, iterative effort required for success.

## Key Ideas

- Startup success is rarely an overnight event but rather the result of years of persistent, often ignored, work.
- The 'movie premiere' analogy is misleading because it suggests a singular, high-visibility launch event.
- Most startups experience a long period of obscurity before achieving widespread recognition.
- Founders must be prepared to launch repeatedly and iterate despite initial lack of public interest.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship.

## Topics

- [[topics/startup-culture|Startup Culture]]: The psychological and operational realities of building a company versus public perception.
- [[topics/iterative-development|Iterative Development]]: The necessity of continuous launching and improvement over time.

## Notable Claims

- Startups are the opposite of movie premieres. Evidence: Movie premieres generate immediate, short-lived hype, whereas startups typically face long periods of obscurity followed by gradual growth.

## Quotes

> Most people don't care most of the time and you just have to keep launching over and over and over again.
> A startup is the exact opposite of [a movie premiere]; it's like a whole bunch of nobody gives a [expletive] for a really long time and then some point 10 years from now everyone really cares.
