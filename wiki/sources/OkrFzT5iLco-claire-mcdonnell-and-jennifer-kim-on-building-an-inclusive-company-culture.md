# Claire McDonnell and Jennifer Kim on Building an Inclusive Company Culture

## Summary

In this Y Combinator podcast episode, host Kat Mañalac discusses the importance of building an intentional company culture and diversity and inclusion (D&I) initiatives with founders Claire McDonnell (True Link) and Jennifer Kim (formerly of Lever). They argue that culture is not a luxury but a strategic necessity for early-stage startups, serving as a competitive advantage for recruiting and retention. The guests provide actionable tactics for fostering inclusivity, such as implementing 'no-blame' policies, amplifying underrepresented voices, and ensuring cross-functional respect.

## Key Ideas

- Culture is a foundational skill for founders, similar to sales or engineering, and should be addressed from day one.
- Intentional culture acts as an insulator against the existential threats faced by startups.
- Diversity and inclusion are not just HR initiatives; they are critical for building a high-performing, sustainable team.
- Small, daily investments in culture—such as writing down policies and modeling inclusive behavior—are more effective than grand gestures.
- Prizing specific teams (like engineering) over others can inadvertently perpetuate societal biases regarding race and gender.
- Empowering team members to lead D&I initiatives allows the culture to scale beyond the founders' direct oversight.

## Notable Claims

- Good corporate culture correlates with long-term economic performance and revenue growth. Evidence: Reference to a 1992 HBS and Cotter Institute study of 200 companies.
- Prizing engineering teams over others is often a coded way of perpetuating societal biases. Evidence: Observation that engineering teams are often demographically skewed; treating them as superior reinforces existing power dynamics.
- Writing down policies reduces confusion and saves time. Evidence: Founders note that answering the same questions repeatedly indicates a need for documented, accessible internal wikis.
