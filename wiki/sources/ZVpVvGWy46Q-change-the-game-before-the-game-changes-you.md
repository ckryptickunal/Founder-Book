# Change the Game Before the Game Changes You

## Summary

Garry Tan explores the psychological and professional traps of success, arguing that high-achievers often fall victim to the 'games' of money, fame, and power. He advocates for defining one's own rules, prioritizing meaningful missions over external validation, and maintaining personal grounding to avoid burnout and self-destruction.

## Key Ideas

- Success often brings new, more difficult 'bosses' or challenges that cannot be defeated using the same rules that brought initial success.
- Money should be treated as a force multiplier for positive change rather than a scoreboard for personal worth.
- Chasing clout and engagement on social media is a trap that distracts from one's true mission.
- Hedonism and extreme neuroticism (e.g., excessive health optimization) are both symptoms of losing the plot.
- True success requires grounding practices and a mission that transcends personal rewards, as exemplified by Viktor Frankl's philosophy.

## Notable Claims

- Intelligence and awareness of the game are insufficient to prevent personal downfall. Evidence: Historical examples of highly intelligent founders like Tony Hsieh and Sam Bankman-Fried who still suffered catastrophic personal or professional failures.
- Virality is not a substitute for product-market fit. Evidence: The failure of Clinkle, which raised significant capital and went viral but failed to provide a useful product.
