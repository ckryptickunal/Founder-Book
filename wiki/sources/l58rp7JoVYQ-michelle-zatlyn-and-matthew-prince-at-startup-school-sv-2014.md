## Summary

Cloudflare co-founders Michelle Zatlyn and Matthew Prince share insights from their first five years of building the company, emphasizing the importance of team dynamics, choosing the right partners, and focusing on long-term momentum over short-term vanity metrics.

## Key Ideas

- Founding teams should cover diverse skill sets with minimal overlap to maximize surface area.
- Focus on 'mosquitoes' (small, critical operational details) rather than just 'sharks' (big, flashy threats).
- Titles are often unnecessary early on; focus on hiring people who are mission-driven rather than title-driven.
- Choose investors based on vision alignment and trust, even if it means accepting a lower valuation.
- Momentum is the greatest asset for a startup; prioritize progress over perfection.
- Diversity in a team (backgrounds, languages, experiences) leads to a richer product and unique perspectives.

## Notable Claims

- The average time to exit for a startup is eight years. Evidence: Cited as general industry data.
- It takes as much effort to build a significant infrastructure company as it does to build a trivial app. Evidence: Based on the founders' experience building Cloudflare versus observing smaller, less impactful projects.
- Titles are the cheapest variable to play with when hiring, but can be detrimental to culture. Evidence: Reflected on their decision to eliminate titles early on to ensure team members were there for the mission.
