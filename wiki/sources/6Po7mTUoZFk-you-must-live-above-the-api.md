# You must Live above the API!

## Metadata

- Video ID: `6Po7mTUoZFk`
- Channel: Garry Tan
- Published: 2024-03-18T16:00:03Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6Po7mTUoZFk

## Summary

Garry Tan explains the concept of living 'above the API' versus 'below the API,' arguing that economic value is derived from building and controlling the systems that govern human transactions rather than merely executing tasks dictated by those systems.

## Key Ideas

- Economic value is determined by the scarcity and uniqueness of one's skills.
- Living 'below the API' means following instructions provided by software systems, which limits leverage and compensation.
- Living 'above the API' involves creating, owning, and maintaining the systems that others use.
- Capitalism rewards those who build the infrastructure of modern business transactions.

## Topics

- [[topics/economic-leverage|Economic Leverage]]: The ability to control systems and platforms to generate outsized value compared to labor-based roles.
- [[topics/skill-valuation|Skill Valuation]]: The market's tendency to price skills based on scarcity and the ability to perform tasks others cannot.

## Notable Claims

- Skills that are rare and difficult to replicate are effectively priceless in a capitalist economy. Evidence: The speaker contrasts the commoditized nature of driving with the high-value nature of building software platforms.
- Working 'below the API' limits an individual's economic potential because the work is easily replaceable. Evidence: The example of an Uber driver following app directions demonstrates a lack of leverage compared to the platform owner.

## Quotes

> Not all skills are valued at the same price if you can do things nobody else can do you'll have skills that are literally Priceless.
> You can either live above the API or below the API.
> If you create Uber and own it you're above the API and you get to build and maintain the systems that give people what they want.
