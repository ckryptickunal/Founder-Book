# What do you do to help yourself prioritize and focus?

## Metadata

- Video ID: `rKp11Jyhor8`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2023-12-12T21:01:36Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rKp11Jyhor8

## Summary

A Y Combinator mentor explains the importance of extreme prioritization for startup founders, advocating for stack-ranking projects and focusing on a single, primary goal to avoid distraction.

## Key Ideas

- Limit active projects to the top three to ensure execution.
- Founders must constantly question the 'why' behind their tasks.
- A startup should focus on a single, primary metric or goal for Demo Day.
- Infinite to-do lists are often just distractions that hinder progress.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to startups.
- [[entities/demo-day|Demo Day]] (project): A high-stakes event where startups present their progress to investors.

## Topics

- [[topics/prioritization|Prioritization]]: The practice of stack-ranking tasks to identify and execute only the most impactful work.
- [[topics/focus|Focus]]: The necessity of narrowing a startup's objectives to a single, measurable goal.

## Notable Claims

- Founders who attempt to manage 10 projects will realistically only complete the top three. Evidence: The speaker notes that an infinite list of tasks is unsustainable and leads to distraction.
- Having a single goal for Demo Day is more effective than having multiple targets. Evidence: The speaker observes that focusing on one thing helps founders avoid the trap of managing too many disparate targets.

## Quotes

> If you have like 10 projects for the quarter stacking them one to 10... you probably will only do the top three.
> It kind of forces Founders to really start with why.
> Picking one helps you focus like you need that focus on one thing and only one thing.
