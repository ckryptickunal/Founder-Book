# How to Make Pittsburgh a Startup Hub

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-how-to-make-pittsburgh-a-startup-hub`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: April 2016
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-how-to-make-pittsburgh-a-startup-hub

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that Pittsburgh has the potential to become a major startup hub by leveraging its unique advantages: a growing population of young talent, affordable historic housing, a dense urban layout, and the presence of Carnegie Mellon University. He suggests that the city should focus on enhancing its livability—specifically through supporting local food culture, historic preservation, and pedestrian/bicycle infrastructure—rather than trying to artificially manufacture an innovation ecosystem.

## Key Ideas

- Startups are driven by people in their 20s who prefer living in dense, walkable urban environments.
- A city's 'personality'—defined by independent restaurants and cafes—is a key attractor for young talent.
- Historic preservation of solid, older buildings is more effective for attracting talent than new real estate developments.
- Universities contribute most to startup ecosystems by being world-class research institutions rather than creating 'entrepreneurship' programs.
- Tolerance for 'strangeness' is a prerequisite for a successful startup culture.
- The lack of a local investor community can be mitigated by the decreasing cost of starting companies and remote funding models like Y Combinator.

## Notable Claims

- The best thing a university can do to encourage startups is to be a great university. Evidence: Historical data shows successful startups grow organically from side projects, not from formal entrepreneurship programs.
- A city must be socially liberal to be a startup hub. Evidence: Startups are inherently 'strange' and require a culture that tolerates diversity and non-conformity to thrive.
- Pittsburgh is better positioned than Portland to be a startup hub. Evidence: Pittsburgh possesses a world-class research university (CMU) in addition to the lifestyle factors that attract young people.
