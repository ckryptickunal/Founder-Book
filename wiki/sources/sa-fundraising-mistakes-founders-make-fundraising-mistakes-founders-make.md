# Fundraising Mistakes Founders Make

## Summary

Sam Altman outlines common pitfalls founders encounter during the fundraising process, emphasizing that building a strong company is more effective than using 'fundraising jiu-jitsu' or game-playing tactics.

## Key Ideas

- Focus on building a great company rather than over-optimizing fundraising tactics.
- Run fundraising processes in parallel to create a competitive environment.
- Avoid chasing excessively high valuations to prevent future down rounds.
- Prioritize finding a lead investor to provide operational guidance and accountability.
- Be authentic and passionate during pitches rather than following rigid templates.
- Always perform reference checks on potential investors before signing.
- Know your company's key metrics cold to demonstrate execution quality.

## Notable Claims

- Running fundraising processes in parallel is essential for success. Evidence: It creates a competitive environment where multiple investors vie for space, leading to better terms.
- High valuations can be detrimental to a startup. Evidence: They increase the risk of a down round in the future and can push away good investors.
- Knowing key metrics is a predictor of execution quality. Evidence: Teams that execute well always know their numbers cold, demonstrating operational discipline.
