# Why Two IIT Engineers Turned Down $550K Jobs To Build A Startup

## Summary

Varun, a co-founder of Giga ML, discusses his journey from an IIT engineering student to building a successful AI startup. He explains how he and his co-founder pivoted from EdTech to AI customer support after Y Combinator mentorship, emphasizing the importance of product-led growth, customer-driven development, and the efficiency gains of using AI coding agents.

## Key Ideas

- Product-led growth is more effective than sales-led growth for AI companies.
- The 'forward deployed engineer' role is a bottleneck that can be solved by AI agents.
- Burning the boats—committing fully by rejecting safety nets—forces innovation and execution.
- AI coding agents allow small teams to achieve the output of teams 6-7 times larger.
- Validating ideas by securing paying customers early is more important than having a perfect business plan.
- The Bay Area remains the primary hub for GenAI innovation due to access to researchers and talent.

## Notable Claims

- AI agents can increase customer support deflection rates to 90-95%. Evidence: The company's internal metrics and performance with top-tier clients.
- Coding agents allow a small team to perform the work of a team 6-7 times larger. Evidence: Internal operational efficiency observed at Giga ML.
- Product quality is more important than sales teams for AI startups. Evidence: Comparison to companies like OpenAI and Anthropic which prioritize product over traditional sales commissions.
