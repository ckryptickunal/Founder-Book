# OpenClaw Creator: Why 80% Of Apps Will Disappear

## Summary

Peter Steinberger, creator of OpenClaw, discusses the rapid rise of his open-source personal AI agent. Unlike cloud-based assistants, OpenClaw runs locally on a user's machine, allowing it to interface with local files, hardware, and personal data, effectively acting as an autonomous agent that can even delegate tasks to other humans or bots.

## Key Ideas

- Local-first AI agents are significantly more powerful than cloud-based ones because they have full access to the user's local environment and hardware.
- The future of AI interaction is moving from centralized 'god' intelligence to swarm and community-driven intelligence.
- 80% of current apps may disappear as autonomous agents become capable of managing data and tasks natively without dedicated interfaces.
- Personal AI agents can effectively 'hire' other humans or negotiate with other bots to complete real-world tasks.
- The 'soul' of an AI agent—defined by system prompts and core values—is a critical differentiator in how it interacts with the world.
- Development efficiency is improved by using simple, Unix-based tools (like CLIs) rather than complex, proprietary frameworks like MCP.

## Notable Claims

- 80% of current apps will disappear. Evidence: AI agents will be able to manage data and perform tasks (like fitness tracking or scheduling) more naturally than dedicated apps.
- Local-first AI is more powerful than cloud-based AI. Evidence: Local agents can access hardware (ovens, lights, beds) and local files that cloud-based models cannot reach due to security and connectivity constraints.
