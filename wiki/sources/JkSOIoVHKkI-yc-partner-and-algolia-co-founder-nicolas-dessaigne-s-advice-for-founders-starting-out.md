# YC Partner and Algolia co-founder Nicolas Dessaigne’s advice for founders starting out.

## Summary

Nicolas Dessaigne, YC Partner and Algolia co-founder, provides essential advice for early-stage founders, emphasizing the importance of starting immediately, iterating rapidly, and prioritizing direct user feedback over perfection.

## Key Ideas

- Start building immediately rather than waiting for a perfect idea.
- Prioritize speed and iteration over engineering perfection in the early stages.
- Direct engagement with users is the most effective way to learn what to build.
- Launching early is a low-risk, high-reward strategy for gathering feedback.
- Do things that don't scale to understand user needs deeply.

## Notable Claims

- Waiting for the perfect idea prevents progress. Evidence: The speaker argues that building, even on imperfect ideas, provides the necessary 'reps' to discover the right idea.
- Launching early carries no long-term reputational risk. Evidence: The speaker notes that if a product fails, people will not remember, but the feedback gained is invaluable.
