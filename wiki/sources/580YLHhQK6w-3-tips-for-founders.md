# 3 Tips for founders

## Summary

Garry Tan provides three essential pieces of advice for startup founders: adopting a contrarian yet evidence-based mindset, prioritizing hands-on building, and mastering the art of persuasive communication.

## Key Ideas

- Identify fringe ideas that are fundamentally correct based on first principles or historical patterns.
- Minimize reliance on others by building the product yourself to increase credibility and momentum.
- Develop strong written communication skills to pitch effectively via email.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): CEO of Y Combinator and startup investor.

## Topics

- [[topics/contrarian-thinking|Contrarian Thinking]]: The practice of identifying unconventional ideas that are supported by logic or history.
- [[topics/founder-autonomy|Founder Autonomy]]: The importance of founders being able to build their own products to attract funding and talent.
- [[topics/communication-skills|Communication Skills]]: The necessity of writing compelling emails to effectively pitch a startup.

## Notable Claims

- Being a builder increases the likelihood of securing funding and attracting users. Evidence: Founders who do the work themselves leave less to chance and demonstrate higher capability.

## Quotes

> Be contrarian and right.
> The more you can do, the less you leave a chance and the more people will want to fund you, join you, or use your product or service.
