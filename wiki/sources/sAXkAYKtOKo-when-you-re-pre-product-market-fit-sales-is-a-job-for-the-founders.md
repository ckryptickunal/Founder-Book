# When you’re pre-product market fit, sales is a job for the founders.

## Metadata

- Video ID: `sAXkAYKtOKo`
- Channel: Y Combinator
- Published: 2024-07-10T15:21:09Z
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sAXkAYKtOKo

## Summary

This video explains why early-stage startup founders must personally handle sales before achieving product-market fit, arguing that the entrepreneurial nature of pre-PMF sales requires a level of vision and product feedback that hired salespeople cannot yet provide.

## Key Ideas

- Founders are the only ones capable of selling a product before product-market fit.
- Hiring a professional salesperson too early is a common mistake for founders.
- Pre-PMF sales is an entrepreneurial process requiring vision, experimentation, and a tight feedback loop with product development.
- Sales roles change fundamentally once product-market fit is established.

## Entities

- [[entities/y-combinator|Y Combinator]] (organization): A startup accelerator that provides seed funding and mentorship to early-stage companies.

## Topics

- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The stage at which a startup has identified a target customer base and serves them with a product that satisfies their needs.
- [[topics/startup-sales|Startup Sales]]: The process of acquiring initial customers for a new product, which requires a different approach before and after market validation.

## Notable Claims

- Founders cannot successfully outsource sales before achieving product-market fit. Evidence: Pre-PMF sales requires deep product vision, constant experimentation, and a direct feedback loop with the product team that external hires lack.

## Quotes

> If you aren't able to sell your product yourself at first, chances are you're not going to be able to hire somebody else to do it for you.
> Sales pre-PMF is fundamentally entrepreneurial; it requires vision and credibility with customers and lots of experimentation.
