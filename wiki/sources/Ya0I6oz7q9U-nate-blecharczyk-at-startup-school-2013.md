## Summary

Nate Blecharczyk, co-founder of Airbnb, shares the early, often difficult journey of the company, emphasizing that startup success is a long-term process requiring perseverance, strong co-founder relationships, and a willingness to do things that do not scale.

## Key Ideas

- Startup success is not an overnight phenomenon; it is a long, grueling journey similar to training for the Olympics.
- Choosing the right co-founders is the most critical decision, as you can pivot the product but not the partners.
- Early-stage startups should focus on 'ramen profitability' to survive and maintain focus.
- It is better to have a few users who love your product than a thousand who just like it.
- Doing things that do not scale—like manually visiting users to take photos and improve listings—is essential for early traction.
- Every experience, even failed projects or unfulfilling jobs, serves as a building block for future success.

## Notable Claims

- You can change your idea and pivot your company, but you cannot change your partners without starting over. Evidence: Blecharczyk's personal experience with the necessity of a stable, hardworking founding team.
- Doing things that don't scale is essential for early growth. Evidence: The founders manually visiting 40 users in New York to take professional photos and improve listings, which led to the first real bookings.
- Investors prefer 'billions' over 'millions' in projections. Evidence: Anecdote about changing a slide from $20 million to $200 million in revenue projections to match investor expectations.
