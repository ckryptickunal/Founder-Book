# You Don’t Have To Be A Billionaire To Launch Satellites

## Summary

Astranis co-founders John Gedmark and Ryan McLinko share how they transitioned from a small San Francisco apartment to building a multi-billion dollar aerospace company. By leveraging the Y Combinator accelerator, setting aggressive three-month milestones, and maintaining a scrappy, cost-effective approach to hardware development, they successfully launched commercial satellites into geostationary orbit.

## Key Ideas

- Hardtech startups do not require billionaire founders; domain expertise and a scrappy mindset are sufficient.
- Setting extreme, short-term goals (like building a prototype in three months) is essential for proving viability to investors.
- Minimum Viable Product (MVP) in aerospace involves simplifying requirements to prove core functionality before scaling.
- In-house testing facilities (vibration tables, thermal vacuum chambers) significantly reduce costs and development time.
- The 2016 Y Combinator batch served as a pivotal moment for the modern space startup ecosystem.
