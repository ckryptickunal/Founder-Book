# Tom Blomfield: How I Created Two Billion-Dollar Fintech Startups

## Summary

Tom Blomfield, co-founder of GoCardless and Monzo, discusses his journey from a law student to a serial fintech entrepreneur and Y Combinator partner. He details the extreme highs and lows of building billion-dollar companies, the psychological toll of leadership, the existential crisis faced during the COVID-19 pandemic, and his transition into supporting the next generation of founders.

## Key Ideas

- Choosing the path of a founder allows individuals to actively shape society rather than just following existing rules.
- Tackling 'hard' problems can be a strategic advantage, attracting top talent and generating significant public interest.
- The emotional volatility of startup life is extreme, often leading to burnout and identity crises for founders.
- Vulnerability and transparency with employees during crises can build immense organizational bonds.
- The transition from founder to investor allows for the application of hard-won experience to help others avoid past mistakes.

## Notable Claims

- Taking on a harder, more ambitious problem can make building a company easier. Evidence: It attracts smarter talent, generates more press, and creates a more compelling narrative for customers.
- Monzo's growth was driven entirely by word-of-mouth. Evidence: The company grew from zero to a million customers in 2.5 years without spending on advertising.
- The media cycle for startups is inherently fickle. Evidence: The press builds up newcomers until they reach a certain scale, then shifts to tearing them down.
