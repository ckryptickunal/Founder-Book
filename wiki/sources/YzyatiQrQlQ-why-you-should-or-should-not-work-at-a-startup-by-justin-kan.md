# Why You Should or Should Not Work at a Startup by Justin Kan

## Summary

Justin Kan, founder of multiple Y Combinator startups including Justin.tv, Twitch, and Atrium, provides an unfiltered perspective on the pros and cons of working at a startup, emphasizing that the primary value for employees is the accelerated rate of learning rather than financial gain or stability.

## Key Ideas

- Startups often have poor management and lack structured mentorship.
- Joining a startup is statistically unlikely to make an employee rich.
- Startups are not suitable for those seeking career stability or a clear five-year plan.
- Startups provide opportunities to take on roles and responsibilities for which one is technically unqualified.
- Working at a startup is an effective gateway to becoming a founder.
- The most significant benefit of a startup is the maximization of one's personal rate of learning, regardless of whether the company succeeds or fails.
- Career success is defined by one's 'slope' (rate of growth) rather than their 'y-intercept' (starting point).

## Notable Claims

- Management at early-stage startups is generally poor. Evidence: Anecdotal observation from the speaker's experience with multiple YC companies.
- You are more likely to learn from a failing startup than a successful corporate job. Evidence: Case study of Daniel Kan negotiating a difficult deal for a failing company, which prepared him for a billion-dollar exit later.
- Startups offer roles you are unqualified for. Evidence: Example of an engineer being promoted to run a major backend system within a year.
