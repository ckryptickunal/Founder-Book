# I funded Coinbase in 2012. Making 6000x on my best startup investment yet

## Summary

Garry Tan recounts his early investment in Coinbase, detailing how a $300,000 seed investment grew into a stake worth over $2.4 billion. He explains his decision-making process, highlighting the importance of being a builder, recognizing contrarian opportunities, and the role of Y Combinator in the company's early success.

## Key Ideas

- Early-stage investing requires identifying contrarian ideas that are fringe but fundamentally sound.
- Founders who are technical builders are more likely to succeed because they can execute their vision independently.
- Effective communication, such as Brian Armstrong's early demonstration of Bitcoin, is crucial for attracting investors.
- Personal preparation and intellectual curiosity (e.g., understanding fiat currency history) allow investors to recognize opportunities others miss.
- The importance of solving poor user experiences in emerging technologies to drive mass adoption.

## Notable Claims

- The difficulty of buying Bitcoin on early platforms like Mt. Gox was a major barrier to adoption. Evidence: Garry Tan's personal experience attempting to use the platform and finding the design and user experience 'terrible'.
- Brian Armstrong's background in anti-fraud at Airbnb made him uniquely qualified to build a secure crypto exchange. Evidence: Tan notes that protecting 'internet money' requires specialized software engineering skills similar to those used in high-stakes anti-fraud roles.
