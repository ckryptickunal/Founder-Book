## Summary

Ben Huh, founder of the Cheezburger Network, discusses the importance of therapy and CEO coaching for entrepreneurs, arguing that mental health support should be viewed as a tool for performance optimization rather than just a crisis intervention.

## Key Ideas

- Therapy should be proactive, not just a reaction to a crisis.
- CEO coaching and personal therapy serve different but complementary functions.
- Radical honesty is required for therapy to be effective.
- The 'So what?' mantra helps entrepreneurs detach from emotional reactions to focus on objective problem-solving.
- Investing in mental health provides a high return on investment (ROI) for startup founders.

## Topics

- [[topics/ceo-coaching|CEO Coaching]]: A framework focused on decision-making, leadership, and professional relationships.
- [[topics/mental-health-stigma|Mental Health Stigma]]: The cultural barrier that prevents people from seeking help until they are in a state of crisis.
- [[topics/performance-optimization|Performance Optimization]]: Using therapy to maintain an optimal mental state for high-stakes work.

## Notable Claims

- Waiting until a crisis to seek therapy is like waiting for a disease to become untreatable. Evidence: Analogy comparing mental health to physical health, such as waiting until an ankle injury is severe before seeing a doctor.
- Therapy and coaching provide a high ROI for entrepreneurs. Evidence: The cost of a few hundred dollars is negligible compared to the effort saved and improved decision-making capabilities.

## Quotes

> I think there's a lot of myth about what therapy is and a lot of people wait until there's a crisis.
> What is the reality of the situation and what are you going to do about it?
> A therapist here is like an employee or CEO or a service provider so that you want to maximize their time and your time.
