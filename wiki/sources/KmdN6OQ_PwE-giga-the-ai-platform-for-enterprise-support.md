## Summary

Giger, co-founded by Verun and Esher, builds AI agents for customer support, serving large companies like DoorDash. They differentiate themselves in a competitive market by offering a product-led approach that enables rapid deployment (weeks vs. months) and the ability to handle extremely complex, multi-party communication use cases at scale. Their core innovation is an "AI forward-deployed engineer" model, where AI writes Python code and translates business logic from natural language, making their product highly generalizable across enterprises without custom development. Giger's founders, with deep LLM fine-tuning expertise, emphasize solving the hardest problems and providing a superior customer experience (no hold times, faster resolution, multilingual support). Having recently raised a Series A, they plan to expand beyond customer support into other enterprise operations, aiming to build a platform for efficient ops automation.

## Key Ideas

- Giger's product-led approach enables rapid deployment and scalability for AI customer support agents, differentiating them from consultative competitors.
- The 'AI forward-deployed engineer' concept allows AI to translate natural language business logic into code (e.g., Python, JSON), making the product highly generalizable and reducing the need for custom development.
- Giger's AI agents can handle extremely complex, multi-party communication scenarios in customer support, surpassing human capabilities in efficiency and experience.
- AI agents offer significant improvements over human agents in customer support, including eliminating hold times, faster resolution, and robust multilingual/accent understanding.
- Customer support and programming are ideal verticals for current LLMs due to their relatively narrow context scope, making them ripe for AI optimization.
- Giger aims to expand beyond customer support to automate other 'opex heavy' processes within large enterprises, leveraging accumulated context and data.
- The company culture emphasizes hiring highly intelligent, mission-aligned, and impact-driven individuals, often attracting talent over larger tech giants by offering direct, large-scale impact.

## Notable Claims

- Giger differentiates itself from competitors like Sierra by building a product rather than taking a consultative, 'Palantir-type' approach. Evidence: Giger went live with DoorDash in a week, compared to competitors who took months for pilot deployment. Their internal rule is 'you don't build anything custom for any customer; everything has to be a part of the core product'.
- Giger's product can solve extremely complex customer support use cases at scale that other products cannot. Evidence: The example of DoorDash's multi-party communication for address changes (AI agent calling both dasher and customer simultaneously) is cited as a live, complex use case Giger solved.
- Giger's 'AI forward-deployed engineer' model allows AI to translate business logic into code, making the product highly generalizable. Evidence: Operations teams at DoorDash can talk to Giger's AI, which then writes Python code or converts JSON files to build complex use cases without significant engineer involvement. This makes the product 'by default extremely general'.
- Giger's AI agents provide a better customer support experience than human agents. Evidence: AI agents eliminate hold times, offer faster resolution, provide multilingual support with accent understanding, and can manage multi-party calls more efficiently than humans (e.g., simultaneously talking to dasher and customer).
- Customer support and programming are particularly well-suited for current LLM capabilities due to their narrow context scope. Evidence: The speaker explains that in these verticals, the necessary context (customer chat history, code files) is readily available, unlike a general manager's job, which requires broader, less defined context.
- Giger's success in sales and securing large contracts is due to a combination of market demand and a technically superior product. Evidence: The market is actively seeking AI strategies for customer support, and Giger's ability to demonstrate a working product that solves complex problems quickly (e.g., DoorDash's week-long pilot) generates strong word-of-mouth and C-level interest.
