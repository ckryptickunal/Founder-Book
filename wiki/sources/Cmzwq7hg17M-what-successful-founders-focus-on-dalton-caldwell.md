## Summary

Dalton Caldwell advises startup founders to ignore the 'noise' of the startup ecosystem—such as fundraising news and social media discourse—and instead prioritize building products, serving customers, and generating revenue.

## Key Ideas

- Founders often mistake consuming startup media for actual productivity.
- Excessive focus on fundraising news and VC discourse distracts from core business development.
- True progress is measured by product creation and revenue, not ecosystem influence.
- Curating information sources to prioritize 'maker' culture over 'meta' startup talk is essential.

## Notable Claims

- Consuming startup media and fundraising news often leads to a false sense of productivity. Evidence: Founders spend energy on 'meta' conversations instead of building products, resulting in zero actual progress.

## Quotes

> You can end up thinking that you're being productive and that you're an influencer or a good thinker on startups and the ecosystem and you're actually making no progress at all.
> I actually encourage people to reduce their consumption of media especially stuff around fundraising or the fundraising environment and to focus much more on building and making things.
