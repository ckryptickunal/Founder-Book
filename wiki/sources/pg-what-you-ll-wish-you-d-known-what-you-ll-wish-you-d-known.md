# What You'll Wish You'd Known

## Summary

Paul Graham offers advice to high school students on how to navigate their formative years by treating school as a 'day job,' focusing on curiosity, and working on hard, interesting problems rather than obsessing over long-term career plans or college admissions.

## Key Ideas

- Avoid 'premature optimization' by not committing to a fixed life plan early on.
- Stay 'upwind' by choosing paths that offer the most future options.
- Treat school as a day job: do what is required to get by, but focus your real energy on personal projects.
- Work on hard problems; if you aren't worried about failing, the work isn't hard enough.
- Curiosity is more powerful than 'passion' or 'discipline' for doing great work.
- Take intellectual responsibility for yourself rather than waiting for instructions.
- Avoid the trap of designing your life to impress college admissions officers.

## Notable Claims

- High school students should treat school as a day job. Evidence: It allows students to fulfill requirements while maintaining the autonomy to pursue real, self-directed work.
- The college admissions process is largely a charade. Evidence: Admissions officers are not always discerning, and the industry of prep schools exists to 'hack' the process.
- Great work is driven by curiosity, not discipline. Evidence: Many high achievers are procrastinators who only work hard when they are genuinely interested in a problem.
