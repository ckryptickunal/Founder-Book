# Moms shouldn't have to choose between family and career

## Summary

Garry Tan interviews Allison Robinson, founder of The Mom Project, a marketplace designed to reconnect mothers and caregivers with the workforce. The discussion highlights the systemic barriers preventing women from returning to work after having children and how the platform uses technology to match talent with companies that prioritize flexibility and family-friendly policies.

## Key Ideas

- Approximately 43% of American women leave the workforce for a period after having children.
- The Mom Project functions as a two-sided marketplace connecting skilled talent with companies that value flexibility.
- Retaining women in the workforce is a business imperative, not just a social one, representing a $570 billion economic opportunity.
- The platform uses matching algorithms to prioritize user preferences like commute, travel, and flexibility, rather than requiring users to search through job boards.
- The mission is expanding to include anyone who identifies with the need for flexible work, including fathers.
