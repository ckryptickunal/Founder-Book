# Why YC

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-why-yc`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: August 2009
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-why-yc

## Summary

Paul Graham explains the motivations behind founding Y Combinator, clarifying that while it involves elements of a 'band reunion' with his former colleagues, the primary driver was a hacker's desire to optimize the world's economy by enabling more startups to exist.

## Key Ideas

- Y Combinator was not founded primarily for profit or pure altruism.
- The founders viewed the creation of YC as a 'hack' to increase the efficiency of the startup ecosystem.
- Startups are inherently difficult ('schleps') and rarely designed purely for fun.
- The world's economy is viewed as an open-source system that can be improved through small, strategic interventions.

## Notable Claims

- All startups are mostly 'schleps' (difficult, tedious work). Evidence: Graham notes that Viaweb was rarely fun and suggests this is typical for the startup experience.
- The world's economy is an open-source system. Evidence: Graham uses this metaphor to describe how small, strategic tweaks can increase the efficiency of startup creation.

## Quotes

> The real reason we started Y Combinator is one probably only a hacker would understand. We did it because it seems such a great hack.
