# How Meesho Became India’s Biggest Shopping App

## Metadata

## Summary

Vidit Aatrey, co-founder of Meesho, discusses the company's 11-year evolution from a local fashion discovery tool to India's largest e-commerce platform, emphasizing the importance of customer obsession, pivoting based on market shifts, and leveraging new technologies like AI to reach the next billion users.

## Key Ideas

- Be rigid with the problem you are solving, but flexible with the solution.
- True product-market fit is characterized by organic growth and high retention without marketing spend.
- Successful companies must be willing to kill their existing business model when a paradigm shift (like the democratization of data) renders it obsolete.
- Customer obsession requires direct, on-the-ground observation rather than relying solely on user feedback.
- AI is the next major accessibility frontier, enabling voice-based, non-textual interfaces for non-tech-savvy populations.

## Topics

- [[topics/e-commerce-evolution|E-commerce Evolution]]: The transition from local, WhatsApp-based social commerce to a centralized, app-based mass-market platform.
- [[topics/product-market-fit|Product-Market Fit]]: The process of identifying core user pain points and iterating until a product achieves organic, high-retention growth.
- [[topics/ai-in-retail|AI in Retail]]: Using artificial intelligence to remove barriers to entry for non-tech-savvy users through voice and intuitive interfaces.

## Notable Claims

- Meesho has been the number one shopping app on the Android Play Store in India every single day since July 2021. Evidence: Stated by the CEO during the interview.
- The shift from a social commerce model to a direct-to-consumer app was necessary to survive the shift in data costs and consumer behavior. Evidence: The CEO explains that failing to pivot would have resulted in losing the market to competitors as data became free and users became more comfortable with apps.
