# Tablets

## Summary

Paul Graham argues that 'tablets' is the defining category for modern mobile computing devices, predicting that the trend of 'ephemeralization'—replacing physical objects with software apps—will continue to disrupt numerous industries.

## Key Ideas

- Tablets are the primary category for modern mobile devices, with phones being essentially small tablets.
- Ephemeralization is the driving force behind the replacement of physical, special-purpose objects with software.
- Startups should look for physical objects that can be rendered obsolete by tablet applications.
- Platform success depends on providing developers with versatile tools and hardware access to enable unforeseen innovations.
- There is a risk of a new client monoculture dominated by Apple, which can be countered by building more open or capable platforms.

## Topics

- [[topics/ephemeralization|Ephemeralization]]: The technological trend where physical machinery and objects are replaced by software.
- [[topics/platform-strategy|Platform Strategy]]: The importance of providing developers with hardware and software access to foster innovation.
- [[topics/client-monoculture|Client Monoculture]]: The concern regarding a single company controlling the dominant computing platform, similar to Microsoft in the 90s.

## Notable Claims

- Most special-purpose physical objects will eventually be replaced by apps on tablets. Evidence: Cites examples like GPS, music players, cameras, keys, and reading glasses.
- The iPhone is essentially a tablet small enough to hold to your ear. Evidence: Argues that if the iPad had been released first, the iPhone would be categorized as a small tablet rather than a phone.
