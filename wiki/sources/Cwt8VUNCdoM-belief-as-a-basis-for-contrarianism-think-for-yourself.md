# Belief as a basis for contrarianism – Think for yourself!

## Metadata

- Channel: Garry Tan
- Published: 2024-05-02T16:00:09Z

## Summary

Garry Tan discusses the necessity of deep, well-defined beliefs as the foundation for contrarian thinking and successful entrepreneurship, drawing on insights from Peter Thiel.

## Key Ideas

- Contrarianism requires having strong, well-defined beliefs rather than just being cynical.
- A lack of belief in society makes it difficult to identify bubbles or unique opportunities.
- Thinking for yourself is the primary mechanism for identifying high-potential ideas.
- Founders and investors must cultivate independent thought to distinguish good ideas from bad ones.

## Entities

- [[entities/garry-tan|Garry Tan]] (person): Investor and President/CEO of Y Combinator.
- [[entities/peter-thiel|Peter Thiel]] (person): Legendary investor, co-founder of PayPal and Palantir.

## Topics

- [[topics/contrarianism|Contrarianism]]: The practice of forming independent opinions that diverge from the consensus, rooted in deep personal belief.
- [[topics/entrepreneurial-decision-making|Entrepreneurial Decision Making]]: The process by which founders and investors evaluate and select high-value ideas.

## Notable Claims

- There are almost no bubbles left in the market because people lack strong beliefs. Evidence: Peter Thiel suggests that bubbles require a collective belief system, which is currently absent.

## Quotes

> To be contrarian in a world where people don't believe in anything is to actually think about things to have well-defined beliefs.
> The way to be contrarian is to think for yourself.
