## Summary

A guide from Y Combinator's Head of Outreach on why early-stage startups should abandon the 'big bang' launch mentality in favor of continuous, iterative launching to validate ideas and find product-market fit.

## Key Ideas

- Launch as early as possible, even if the product is unpolished.
- Focus on making a small group of users love your product rather than making a large group semi-happy.
- A clear, one-sentence pitch is essential for organic word-of-mouth growth.
- Avoid marketing jargon and 'why' stories in initial pitches; lead with 'what' you do.
- Launching is a continuous process, not a one-time event.
- Use diverse channels like online communities (Hacker News, Bookface), waitlists, and direct customer outreach to iterate.

## Notable Claims

- Launching too early is rarely fatal. Evidence: If no one cares, you can simply iterate and launch again; Airbnb launched three times.
- Press coverage is not a silver bullet for early-stage startups. Evidence: It is difficult to obtain without significant funding and does not guarantee product-market fit or sustained growth.
- The 'X for Y' pitch construction is often overused. Evidence: It should only be used if X is a household name, the analogy is clear, and Y is a massive market.
