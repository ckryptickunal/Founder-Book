## Summary

Jordan Gonen, CEO of Compound, provides a comprehensive guide for startup employees on understanding, managing, and negotiating equity compensation. He emphasizes that equity is a risky investment and encourages employees to treat it with the same rigor as professional investors, while also highlighting the importance of tax planning and understanding key terms like vesting, strike price, and dilution.

## Key Ideas

- Equity is a risky investment; treat it like a portfolio asset rather than a lottery ticket.
- Understanding the 'fully diluted shares outstanding' is essential to calculating actual ownership percentage.
- Tax implications (ISO vs. NSO, AMT, long-term vs. short-term capital gains) significantly impact the net value of equity.
- Negotiating equity is possible and should be approached with curiosity and a long-term partnership mindset.
- The post-termination exercise window is a critical factor for employee mobility and financial planning.
- Early exercise and 83(b) elections can be strategic tools to minimize tax liability.

## Notable Claims

- You can save 20 to 40 percent of the value of your work by optimizing for taxes. Evidence: Strategic timing of exercise and understanding tax treatments like long-term capital gains.
- Dilution is not inherently bad for employees. Evidence: Dilution often occurs because the company is raising capital to increase its overall valuation, potentially increasing the total value of the employee's holdings.
- Startups are not legally required to disclose fully diluted shares outstanding. Evidence: Current lack of regulation forces employees to rely on negotiation and curiosity to obtain this data.
