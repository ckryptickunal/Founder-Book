# The Founder Visa

## Metadata

- Video ID: `pg-the-founder-visa`
- Channel: Paul Graham Essays
- Published: April 2009
- URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pg-the-founder-visa

## Summary

Paul Graham proposes the creation of a specific 'founder visa' for startup entrepreneurs to enter the United States, arguing that immigration barriers are the primary constraint on startup growth and that such a policy would stimulate the economy at no cost to the government.

## Key Ideas

- The US should implement a dedicated visa class for startup founders to boost economic growth.
- Startup founders create jobs rather than taking them, as they are restricted to building new companies.
- The government can identify legitimate startups by leveraging the existing expertise of recognized venture capitalists and startup investors.
- The accreditation process for investors could mirror the existing system used for student visas.
- A quota of 10,000 founders per year would have a significant, visible impact on the US economy.

## Notable Claims

- The biggest constraint on the number of new startups in the US is the inability of foreign founders to enter the country. Evidence: The author asserts this is a greater barrier than tax policy, employment law, or regulatory burdens like Sarbanes-Oxley.
- Allowing 10,000 startup founders into the US annually would create 2,500 new companies per year. Evidence: Based on an assumption of 4 founders per startup.

## Quotes

> The single biggest thing the government could do to increase the number of startups in this country is a policy that would cost nothing: establish a new class of visa for startup founders.
> Startup investors work hard to find the best startups. The government could not do better than to piggyback on their expertise.
