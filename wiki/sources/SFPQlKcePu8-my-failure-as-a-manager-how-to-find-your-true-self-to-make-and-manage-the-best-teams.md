# My failure as a manager: How to find your true self to make and manage the best teams

## Summary

Garry Tan explores the concept of the 'multiple self' in leadership, drawing on neuroscience and developmental psychology to explain why managers often struggle with decision-making and conflict avoidance. By analyzing his own 360-degree review feedback, he illustrates how leaders can move from a socially-determined self to an 'authored' self, ultimately creating more cohesive and effective teams.

## Key Ideas

- Human consciousness is not a single, unified entity but a collection of competing 'agents' or 'selves'.
- The brain often rationalizes actions taken by subconscious processes after the fact.
- Robert Kegan's stages of development provide a framework for personal growth, with Stage 4 (self-authored) being the goal for effective leadership.
- Managers often struggle when they prioritize the perceived needs of others over their own authentic, authored values.
- Physiological awareness (noticing physical stress responses) is a key tool for identifying internal conflict and making better decisions.

## Notable Claims

- Leaders who rely on their team to express difficult opinions are failing to perform their core duties. Evidence: Personal anecdote from a 360-degree review where team feedback highlighted this specific avoidance behavior.
