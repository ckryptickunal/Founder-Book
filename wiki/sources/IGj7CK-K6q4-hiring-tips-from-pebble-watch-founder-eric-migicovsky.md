## Summary

Eric Migicovsky, Y Combinator partner and Pebble founder, outlines the three essential qualities founders should prioritize when making early-stage startup hires: flexibility, trust, and a multidisciplinary skill set.

## Key Ideas

- Early-stage startups require employees who can adapt to changing product directions rather than those with narrow, deep expertise.
- Trust is the most critical factor in hiring, ideally built through prior professional or academic relationships.
- Early employees must be multidisciplinary, capable of handling diverse tasks ranging from design to customer support.
- Avoid hiring deep specialists too early, as they may constrain the company's ability to pivot.

## Notable Claims

- Deep technical experts can be a liability in the early stages of a startup. Evidence: Early-stage startups often pivot their product direction, and highly specialized skills may limit the team's flexibility.
- Hiring people you have previously worked with is the best way to ensure trust. Evidence: Personal experience with school projects or previous jobs allows founders to understand a candidate's working style and reliability.

## Quotes

> If you don't [have trust], you're gonna end up having to do the work yourself.
