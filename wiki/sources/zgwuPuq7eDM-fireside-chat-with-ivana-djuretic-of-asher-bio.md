## Summary

Ivana Djuretic, founder and CEO of Asher Bio, discusses the company's journey from a YC Summer 2019 startup to a clinical-stage biotech firm. The company focuses on developing precise, selective immunotherapies using engineered cytokines to target specific immune cells, thereby minimizing systemic side effects.

## Key Ideas

- Asher Bio uses protein engineering to create 'targeted cytokines' that activate only specific immune cells, avoiding the systemic toxicity of traditional immunotherapies.
- The company successfully transitioned from an idea to in-vivo mouse efficacy data in just three months during their YC batch.
- Founders should work backwards from key milestones (like demo day) and take calculated risks, such as booking long-lead-time studies before the drug is fully finalized.
- Fundraising requires adapting pitch decks to the audience; technical data for scientists and high-level value propositions for general investors.
- Persistence is critical; negative data on a specific molecule does not mean the end of the company, but rather an opportunity to pivot to other candidates.

## Notable Claims

- Targeted cytokines can achieve better, safer, and more effective cancer treatment than systemic administration. Evidence: The company's pre-clinical data showed their molecules were 1,000 times more selective than state-of-the-art drugs.
- Small, agile teams can execute faster than large pharmaceutical companies. Evidence: Asher Bio went from an idea to in-vivo mouse efficacy data in three months, a pace difficult to match in a large corporate structure.
